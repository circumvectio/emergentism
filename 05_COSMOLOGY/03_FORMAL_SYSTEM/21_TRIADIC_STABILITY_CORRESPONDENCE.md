# EFR-21 — The Selected Projective Triad

There is no Emergentist “Triadic Stability Theorem.” The valid mathematics and
the selected symbolism are:

1. On `CP¹`, the involution `ι(z)=1/z` swaps `0` and `∞` and fixes `±1`.
2. The orbit of `{0,∞}` under `ι` is `{0,∞}`; fixed points are not generated
   from that orbit.
3. Their union `{−1,0,1,∞}` is a selected invariant four-point frame, not
   ordinary orbit closure.
4. Any three distinct points of `CP¹` may serve as a projective normalization;
   `{0,1,∞}` is standard and convenient, not uniquely forced.
5. A fourth point is not informationally empty: relative to a normalized triad,
   it carries cross-ratio data.

`0×∞=1` is emblematic only. It is not an arithmetic identity, a deeper
non-associative algebra, or a generator of the triad. Ordinary `0` and `1`
remain operands; `∞` is a projective point in the declared context.

The Titan roles `{0,1,∞}` therefore survive as an optional `[I]` frame:
ground-limit, finite unit, and horizon. They do not prove three substances,
three agents, three cosmic forces, or the D-ladder.

**Kill criterion.** If the selected triad adds no explanatory or mnemonic value,
replace it. Any argument that calls it forced, exhaustive, or produced by
closure fails this boundary.
