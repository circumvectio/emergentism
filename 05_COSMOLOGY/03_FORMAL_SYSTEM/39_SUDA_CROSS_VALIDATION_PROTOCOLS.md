# Suda Reciprocal Symmetry — Calibration Boundary

## What survives `[A/B]`

For `x>0`:

```text
E(x)=(log x)²,
E(1/x)=E(x),
B(x)=2x/(1+x²)=sech(|log x|).
```

These are analytic reciprocal-symmetry facts. Suda is retained as an attributed
source/crosswalk at `[B]`; attribution does not transfer empirical evidence or
ontology.

## The crack

The predecessor selected observations satisfying `V̂≈1/Φ̂` and then tested
whether reciprocal energy was equal. Because `E(1/x)=E(x)` identically, the
selection step manufactured the advertised result. It also used lowercase
`ν`-notation for an uppercase node estimate. That is calibration by conclusion,
not independent cross-validation.

## The only noncircular successor shape `[D/C]`

1. Define `Φ̂` and `V̂` independently using the
   [Operational Definitions](30_OPERATIONAL_DEFINITIONS.md).
2. Freeze normalization, population, horizon, exclusions, and uncertainty
   before inspecting the joint distribution.
3. Do **not** construct or match pairs by setting `V̂=1/Φ̂`.
4. Preregister the hypothesis, its tolerance, and serious rivals. A possible
   hypothesis is reciprocal symmetry of a stated observable under a naturally
   occurring or randomized transformation; another is held-out predictive
   performance of `Φ̂V̂` against other conjunctive aggregators.
5. Test on held-out data with independent custody.

Even a positive result would support only the stated node-level hypothesis. It
would not make real systems literal points on the reciprocal chart, prove a
μ-crossing, or turn `φν=1` into a world law.

## Kill criteria

- reciprocal pairs are selected before the symmetry test;
- either instrument is defined using the other;
- normalization is tuned on the evaluation set;
- chart notation and node notation are mixed; or
- a positive node result is reported as confirmation of an analytic identity.
