## What Is Confirmed `[A]`

### 1. Single photons exhibit non-classical behavior

Grangier, Roger, and Aspect report a triggered single-photon beam-splitter experiment in which coincidence counts fall below the classical wave lower bound, while a second experiment with the same source shows high-visibility interference. The stable claim is:

- light in the single-photon regime can show both indivisible detection behavior and interference behavior;
- the behavior is not captured by a simple classical wave model;
- complementarity belongs to experimental arrangement, not to a naive "the photon was already only a little billiard ball" picture.

### 2. Delayed-choice experiments confirm measurement-context dependence

Jacques et al. implement Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment with true single photons. The interferometer configuration is chosen after the photon has entered, using a quantum random number generator, with the choice spacelike-separated from the photon entering the interferometer. Closed configuration yields interference; open configuration yields which-way information.

The stable claim is:

- the observed wave-like or particle-like behavior depends on which experimental observable is actually measured;
- a naive pre-existing classical path story is not adequate;
- the experiment does not require the stronger claim that a human mind retroactively rewrites the past.

### 3. Bell-test experiments violate local hidden-variable bounds

Freedman and Clauser measured polarization correlations in calcium-cascade photons and found violation of Bell-type restrictions, in agreement with quantum mechanics. Aspect, Dalibard, and Roger repeated the Bell-test logic with time-varying analyzer settings and again found agreement with quantum mechanics and violation of Bell inequalities.

The stable claim is:

- the conjunction of local hidden variables, predetermined outcomes, and the auxiliary assumptions used in Bell tests cannot explain the observed correlations;
- quantum mechanics survives these tests where local hidden-variable models fail;
- "not locally real" is acceptable public shorthand only when immediately unpacked as Bell-inequality violation under the test assumptions.

### 4. The 2022 Nobel Prize ratified the experimental arc

The 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Alain Aspect, John Clauser, and Anton Zeilinger for experiments with entangled photons, establishing violation of Bell inequalities and pioneering quantum information science. This does not turn every interpretation of quantum mechanics into settled science. It does mark the Bell-test experimental arc as canonical modern physics.
