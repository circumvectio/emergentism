# Intelligence and the Potential Cone

> **The one sentence.** *Intelligence does not enlarge the physical light cone;
> it may let an actual D4 agent represent, distinguish, and reach more admissible
> futures. Those represented alternatives are D5 content.* Keep the physical
> bound, actual capacity, and possible content distinct.

---

## 1. The two cones (the distinction that makes the insight honest) `[A]` / `[I]`

On a Minkowski diagram a worldline carries a **future light cone**: the set of events it can causally reach. That cone is fixed by the speed of light `c`. **No amount of intelligence enlarges it.** `[A]` To claim otherwise — "more intelligence, a bigger light cone" read as relativistic reach — is a category error, and the framework does not make it.

What intelligence may enlarge is a different, model-relative object:

- **Physical light cone:** the spacetime-causal bound on possible influence,
  constrained by `c`. Its actual events and records are D4; the cone is not the
  definition of D4. `[A/I]`
- **Option cone:** the subset of admissible histories an actual D4 agent can
  model, distinguish, rank, coordinate around, and—given D4 means—attempt to
  reach. The represented alternatives are D5 content; the model, capacity,
  ranking, and attempt are D4. `[I]`

The light cone bounds what physics permits. The option cone is defined relative
to an agent, model, horizon, resources, and action set inside that physical
bound. More capable modeling may enlarge it, but intelligence alone need not:
missing energy, access, coordination, or authorization can leave an option
unreachable.

## 2. The published cognate — so this is not poetry `[B]`

The intuition has a real scientific formalisation, and it should be cited rather than asserted:

- **Causal Entropic Forces** — Wissner-Gross & Freer, *Phys. Rev. Lett.* **110**, 168702 (2013). Intelligence modelled as a force that **maximises the diversity of accessible future paths** — a drive to keep options open. `[B]`
- **Empowerment** — Klyubin, Polani & Nehaniv (2005): the channel capacity between an agent's *actions* and its *future perceptions* — formally, *how many distinguishable futures the agent can cause.* `[B]`

Both formalize related accessible-future quantities under their own assumptions.
The framework's contribution is an `[I]` type crosswalk: the represented future
set is D5 content, while the controller, channel, computation, intervention,
and measured capacity remain D4. The crosswalk is not an identity or a new
theorem.

## 3. "More light, a larger cone" — read correctly `[I]`

The evocative phrase may therefore be used only as an `[I]` compression: better
models and capabilities can illuminate more alternatives and sometimes enlarge
the option cone. “Light” is not photons, D5 is not an interior place occupied
by a witness, and the actual seeing, ranking, steering, and receipt remain D4.

## 4. Why this is the teleology, not a metaphor `[I]` / `[C]`

The normative proposal is to widen durable mutual option cones rather than
enlarge one agent's represented and reachable set by contracting another's.
That proposal is evaluated under the independent Justice envelope: affected
bearers, consent, custody, authorization, payer, beneficiary, reversibility,
contest, exit, and receipted consequence. Neither intelligence nor D5 content
derives the good. `[I]`

## 5. The fences `[S]`

- **The light cone is not the option cone.** `[A/I]` The physical cone is
  constrained by `c`; an option cone is model- and resource-relative. Never
  claim intelligence enlarges the relativistic cone.
- **Carrier is not content.** Actual models, ranking, computation, empowerment,
  action, and measurement are D4. Their represented alternatives may be D5.
- **Cognate, not derivation.** `[B]` for the cited formalisms; `[I]` for the
  option-cone crosswalk. `φν=1` derives none of them.
- **No crowning.** `[C]` Any claim that the geometry generates intelligence or
  solves mind is overreach.

## 6. Kill criterion `[S]`

The crosswalk dies if declared intelligence measures fail to predict a larger
reachable-and-distinguishable future set after controlling for means, horizon,
environment, and model complexity. It also dies if the D5 label adds no
discrimination beyond ordinary counterfactual and control language.
