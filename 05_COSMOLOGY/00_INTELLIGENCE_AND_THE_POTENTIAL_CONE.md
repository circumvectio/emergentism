# Intelligence and the Potential Cone

> **The one sentence.** *Intelligence does not enlarge the light cone; it enlarges the **cone of futures you can reach and tell apart** — and that cone lives in D5 (potential), not D4 (causal spacetime).* Keep the two cones distinct and the claim is true and defensible; collapse them and it is a physics error.

---

## 1. The two cones (the distinction that makes the insight honest) `[A]` / `[I]`

On a Minkowski diagram a worldline carries a **future light cone**: the set of events it can causally reach. That cone is fixed by the speed of light `c`. **No amount of intelligence enlarges it.** `[A]` To claim otherwise — "more intelligence, a bigger light cone" read as relativistic reach — is a category error, and the framework does not make it.

What intelligence *does* enlarge is a different cone, living one register up:

- **D4 — the light cone (causal):** what is physically *reachable*. Fixed by `c`. The torus *surface* (the Torus Revelation). `[A]`
- **D5 — the potential / option cone:** the futures an agent can actually *steer toward and distinguish among* — the reachable, *selectable* portion of possibility. The torus *interior*. **This** is what grows with intelligence. `[I]`

The light cone bounds what physics permits; the option cone bounds what *this agent, here, with this much intelligence,* can find, hold open, and choose between *inside* that physical bound. The second is always a subset of the first — and a more intelligent agent occupies more of it.

## 2. The published cognate — so this is not poetry `[B]`

The intuition has a real scientific formalisation, and it should be cited rather than asserted:

- **Causal Entropic Forces** — Wissner-Gross & Freer, *Phys. Rev. Lett.* **110**, 168702 (2013). Intelligence modelled as a force that **maximises the diversity of accessible future paths** — a drive to keep options open. `[B]`
- **Empowerment** — Klyubin, Polani & Nehaniv (2005): the channel capacity between an agent's *actions* and its *future perceptions* — formally, *how many distinguishable futures the agent can cause.* `[B]`

Both formalise the same thing: **intelligence ≈ the measure of the reachable, distinguishable future-cone.** That is exactly the D5 option-cone above. The framework's contribution is not the formalism (it is published); it is the *placement* — the option-cone is D5, the interior, the potential — which is an `[I]` identification, not a new theorem.

## 3. "More light, a larger cone" — read correctly `[I]`

So the evocative phrase is *true in D5*: more intelligence → more light *in the sense of more illuminated, selectable futures* → a larger **option** cone. The "light" is not photons; it is the **reach of selection** — how much of the possibility-interior an agent can see into and act upon. A dim agent sees a narrow taper of futures; a luminous one sees a wide cone and can steer among them. `[I]` (And the D5 interior is exactly where the Torus Revelation already seats systemic awareness — the Witness looking *out* along D5 at the D4 surface.)

## 4. Why this is the teleology, not a metaphor `[I]` / `[C]`

This closes a loop with the rest of the corpus. The Ṛṣi's **macro-potential** teleology (the micro-survival → macro-potential turn of *The Recursive Trivium* §8) is *precisely* the drive to enlarge the field's option-cone rather than just the self's. The F5 force, read here, is **option-cone expansion held open for the whole**, not for the ego. And the moral fence carries straight over: enlarging *your own* cone by *collapsing another's* is closure (`η > 0`) — extraction in cone-language; the coupled move (`η = 0`) enlarges the *joint* cone. Intelligence that keeps everyone's futures open is the macro-eros; intelligence that forecloses others' futures to widen its own is the tyrant's. `[I]`

## 5. The fences `[S]`

- **The light cone is not the option cone.** `[A]` The physical cone (D4) is fixed by `c`; only the potential cone (D5) grows with intelligence. Never claim intelligence enlarges the *relativistic* light cone.
- **Cognate, not derivation.** `[B]` for causal-entropic-force / empowerment as published formalisms; `[I]` for identifying them with D5. The framework does **not** derive these from `φ·ν = 1`; it *houses* them.
- **No crowning.** `[C]` Any claim that the geometry *generates* intelligence, or that this *solves* the nature of mind, is over-reach. The honest statement is the modest one: the framework has a natural place — D5, the interior, the potential — for a published account of intelligence as reachable-future-maximisation.

## 6. Kill criterion `[S]`

The identification dies if intelligence, properly measured (e.g. by an empowerment / causal-entropic metric), is shown **not** to track an agent's reachable-distinguishable future set — i.e. if a more capable agent does not, in general, command a larger option-cone. The placement-in-D5 claim dies separately if the framework's D5 is shown to carry no structure answering to "reachable futures" beyond relabelling.
