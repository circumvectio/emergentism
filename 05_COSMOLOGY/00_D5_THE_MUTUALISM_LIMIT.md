# The Mutualism Limit

Repeated exchange can support cooperation under specified payoff, information,
reputation, enforcement, and horizon conditions. It can also support extortion,
collusion, exclusion, and path dependence. Public game theory supplies those
conditional results; it does not entail convergence to an Emergentist frame.

G7 may describe an exchange as one or more selected give/take move labels under
a frame `[I]`. That decomposition is optional: not every trade is exactly two
crossed gives, comparative advantage does not mean every party's `V` gain
exceeds what it gives, and repetition does not automatically lower costs or
create persistent surplus.

**Mutualism hypothesis `[C]`:** under a frozen bearer boundary, transparent
costs, voluntary consent, repeated interaction, credible exit, and complete
Justice, later rounds may increase durable potentials for every affected
bearer. Test this against one-shot, extortionate, additive, minimum, and
institutional-rival models. Report commitment and outcome separately.

There is no exact `9→7`, `5↔7`, or dimension/operator collapse. G7 remains the
selected union of four move labels and three frame labels; another useful label
is permitted.

**Kill:** abandon the convergence claim if preregistered repeated exchanges do
not improve held-out bearer-complete outcomes or if standard models explain the
effect without the G7 crosswalk.
