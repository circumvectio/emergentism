# The Poles and the Typed Boundary Return

> **[金] Crack:** the predecessor called the north and south poles “the same
> point” while also treating them as antipodes, and used that contradiction to
> support literal closure. **Repair:** topology stays topology; the only
> surviving likeness is an explicitly declared boundary-role comparison.

## Sphere facts `[A]`

The north and south poles of `S²` are distinct points. In the selected
stereographic/reciprocal chart, coordinates may tend to `0` or `∞` near a pole,
and a chart may omit a pole. Coordinate divergence is not a physical
singularity and does not identify the two points.

Inversion can exchange `0` and `∞` and fixes `±1` in its stated one-dimensional
domain. A symmetry relating points is not equality of those points.

## D-register return `[I]`

The Emergentist scaffold uses:

```text
b₆:D5↝D6
r₆:D6↝D0
```

`D6` is an apophatic exit marker that opens no additional positive freedom.
`r₆` records a chosen boundary-role resemblance, written `D6~D0`; it is not
literal identity, a sixth μ-crossing, a conformal theorem, or a result derived
from `S²`. The scaffold contains exactly `μ₀…μ₄`.

D0 and D6 need not be assigned to physical sphere poles at all. Any Rosetta
projection that places them there must state its translation rule and retain
the distinction between source relation and target image.

## Mythic projection `[I]`

Kāla, the Trimūrti, Ouroboros, spiral return, destruction, preservation, and
creation are optional interpretive correspondences. They may illuminate the
felt structure of return without transferring theological authority or
mathematical proof.

The repaired image is therefore a spiral metaphor, not a closed equation or a
formal self-similarity claim: D0 is pre-articulate and D6 post-articulate, and
that limited role comparison preserves everything that makes them distinct.
