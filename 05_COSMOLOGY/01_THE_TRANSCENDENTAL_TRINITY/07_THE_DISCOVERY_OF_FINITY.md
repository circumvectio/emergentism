# Finity

> **[金] Crack.** The predecessor said that every mathematics student learns
> `0×∞=1` on the Riemann sphere, that the indeterminate form becomes
> determinate, and that this answered why there is something rather than
> nothing. Those are not consequences of projective geometry.
>
> **Repair.** *Finity* is Emergentism's name `[I]` for the selected finite unit
> or self-dual midpoint read between lower and upper boundary roles. The
> mathematics used is inherited; the emblem and ontological reading are ours
> to test, not discoveries that mathematics compels.

## What is established `[A]`

The Riemann sphere is the one-point compactification of the complex plane. A
Möbius transformation may be normalized by the three distinct projective
points `{0,1,∞}`. For the declared open reciprocal chart

```text
φ = cot(θ/2)
ν = tan(θ/2)
θ ∈ (0,π)
```

we have `φν=1`, with `φ=ν=1` at `θ=π/2`. These are ordinary analytic facts
inside the chosen chart. They do not generate the number `1`, resolve
division by zero, or establish an ontology.

## What Emergentism adds `[I]`

Emergentism calls the midpoint role **finity** and writes the glyph

```text
•   ⊙   ○
```

to picture finite presence as relation between a lower boundary and a horizon.
The `×` belongs to the emblem's frame grammar. It is not multiplication of the
ordinary operands `0` and `∞`; infinity is not a field element, so this is not
a field product. In limit calculus, `0·∞` may be an indeterminate form. The
glyph can therefore orient a reading without rewriting arithmetic.

## What remains a wager `[C]`

The claim that relational finitude is a deep feature of Being, or that the
selected midpoint helps explain why an actual world exists, is an ontological
wager. Projective geometry neither instantiates the world nor makes this
interpretation unique.

The wager weakens or dies if the emblem repeatedly causes field/frame
confusion, if a rival account explains the intended phenomena better, or if
the relational reading yields no independent discriminator. Removing this
document leaves the formal calculus unchanged.
