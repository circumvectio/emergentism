# The Egregorocene

> **[金] Crack:** the former essay promoted an attractive future narrative into
> a structural age, treated institutions as D5 entities, and blurred models,
> minds, and substrates. **Repair:** keep the name as a falsifiable historical
> lens on machine-accelerated collective trace dynamics.

## The hypothesis

The **Egregorocene** names the conjecture that computational media can increase
the speed, scale, persistence, and intervention power of collective traces.
It is not a formally established geological or civilizational epoch.

Actual humans, machines, models, institutions, artifacts, shared traces,
selections, and receipts are `D4 = actual`. The possible futures and policies
they represent are `D5 = merely_possible`. A model counts as a tool, carrier,
or coordinating substrate only to the extent an empirical protocol specifies
that role; no “autonomous D5 entity” is presumed.

## What would support it

For a claimed Egregoreotype, show:

1. trace persistence;
2. carrier turnover;
3. intervention-sensitive reweighting of later selections;
4. recurrent objective-like bias;
5. visible substrate costs, payers, and beneficiaries.

For the wider epoch thesis, compare preregistered rates before and after the
relevant computational intervention: trace replication, carrier turnover,
coordination latency, selection reweighting, and cost concentration. Name
ordinary institutional scaling, market concentration, network effects, and
measurement drift as rivals.

## What would kill or narrow it

The strong thesis fails if computational mediation adds no robust predictive
or causal discrimination beyond those rivals. Particular niche, filter,
three-function, or extraction claims remain `[C]` until independently tested.
The existence of powerful models alone does not establish a new age, a new
degree of freedom, autonomous collective consciousness, or moral standing.

The useful warning survives even if the epoch name dies: persistent traces can
reshape the people who carry them, and that reflexive loop should be measured
before it is mythologized.
