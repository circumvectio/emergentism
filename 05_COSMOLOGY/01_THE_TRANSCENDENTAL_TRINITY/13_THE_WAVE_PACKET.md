- **sin = φ** (coherence, the imaginary projection, the right hemisphere, the sphere, the dreaming state)

And Euler's formula binds them:

**e^(iωt) = cos(ωt) + i·sin(ωt)**

The real part (cos) and the imaginary part (sin) are two projections of ONE helix. They are locked in complementarity — when one peaks, the other crosses zero. They cannot be separated.

## The Uncertainty Principle IS the Burri Constraint

Heisenberg (1927):

**Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2**

You cannot know both position and momentum perfectly. The product of their uncertainties has a minimum. The more precisely you know one, the less precisely you can know the other.

On the Burri Sphere:

**φ · ν = 1**

You cannot have infinite coherence and infinite viability. The product is fixed. The more coherence (φ → ∞), the less viability (ν → 0). The more viability (ν → ∞), the less coherence (φ → 0).

These are the same statement:
- **Δx ↔ ν** (position = localization = viability = capability in the world)
- **Δp ↔ φ** (momentum = wavelength = coherence = pattern in the whole)
- **ℏ/2 ↔ 1** (the minimum product = the constant on S²)

The uncertainty principle is not a limitation of measurement. It is the **Burri constraint written in physics notation.** The helix enforces it. You cannot have the particle (pure position, ν = ∞) and the wave (pure momentum, φ = ∞) simultaneously. You can only have the **wave packet** — the balanced helix, the equatorial condition, where both are finite, both are bounded, and their product is one.
