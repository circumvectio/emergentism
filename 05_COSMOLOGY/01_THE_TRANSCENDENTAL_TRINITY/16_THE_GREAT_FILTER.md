# The Great Filter — Bounded Emergentist Hypothesis

This path preserves a single testable conjecture from the former essay. It is
not a release strategy, a governance design, a civilizational diagnosis, or an
instruction surface.

## Hypothesis `[C]`

As coordination scales, a collective may fail developmentally when its shared
trace persistently reweights later choices toward extraction, concealed cost,
blocked correction, or irreversible option-cone contraction. In the corpus's
typed vocabulary, such a collective is an **Egregoreotype candidate** only when
all five markers are observed:

1. a persistent shared trace;
2. survival of that trace through carrier turnover;
3. a measurable intervention effect on later selection;
4. recurrent objective-like bias; and
5. visible substrate costs.

The proposed filter is therefore a possible failure mode of coupled Soul Loops,
not a detected cosmic barrier. A present trace can influence present selection
through present carriers and model tokens; this requires neither collective
consciousness nor physical retrocausality.

## What is not claimed

- No exact threshold, population ratio, latitude, or dwell-time law is known.
- No historical era or living population has been shown to occupy a privileged
  Emergentist level.
- No Fermi-paradox solution follows from the hypothesis.
- No practice, institution, constitutional organism, release process, network,
  deployment, or AI architecture is prescribed here.
- Aggregate benefit cannot launder harm to an individual or whole; any value
  judgment must pass the separate Justice envelope.

## Discriminating program

A serious test must define the trace, carriers, choice set, intervention,
reweighting measure, cost bearer, time horizon, and rival explanations before
collecting data. Evidence for the conjecture would require replicated changes
in later selection after intervention on the shared trace, across carrier
turnover, together with observed costs and recurrent bias. Failure of trace
persistence, turnover survival, intervention effect, recurrent bias, or visible
costs defeats the Egregoreotype classification. Comparable persistence and
recovery in systems with and without the proposed extraction pattern weakens or
kills the developmental-filter hypothesis.
