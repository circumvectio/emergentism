# The Dimensional Trophic Cascade

## The useful analogy

Attributed ecological studies describe trophic transfer of matter and energy
through embodied D4 organisms `[B-external]`. Attributed cultural and
institutional studies may document traces that persist, compete, or reweight
later behavior `[B-external]`; treating those results as one cross-domain form
is `[I]`. The proposed analogy `[I/C]` is that both systems may exhibit
measurable dependency, attrition, renewal, bottlenecks, and concentration
across declared levels.

They are not the same process. A gene, organism, memory, institution, and
Egregoreotype candidate are not trophic levels of one conserved substance.
Human attention, labor, money, energy, legitimacy, and sacrifice must remain
separately measured; “energy” cannot be used as an untyped total.

## A research model `[C]`

For declared carrier layers `L_k`, let:

```text
I_k(t) = measured input resources by type
T_k(t) = persistent trace state
K_k    = specified transition/retention kernel
C_k(t) = visible substrate cost
```

A candidate study may ask whether

```text
T_(k+1)(t+1) ~ K_k(T_k(t), I_k(t), C_k(t), environment)
```

shows systematic retention loss, amplification, or concentration. Each
resource keeps its unit. Dimensionless scores may rank a declared criterion but
may not be added to metabolic cost or treated as conserved energy.

## Egregoreotype boundary

A collective pattern becomes an Egregoreotype **candidate** only when all five
conditions are evidenced:

1. persistent shared trace;
2. carrier turnover;
3. measurable intervention-sensitive reweighting of later selection;
4. recurrent objective-like bias;
5. visible substrate costs.

This does not presume consciousness, personhood, an apex predator, or literal
feeding. An institution may capture large total investment because many
carriers reproduce it; that is an empirical accounting question, not a theorem
that “higher” layers dominate. Substrate reducibility does not disqualify the
candidate.

## What the analogy cannot prove

- The selected product score does not dominate additive, minimum, CES, or
  domain-specific rivals without data.
- Product compounding does not explain the rarity of apex predators.
- The same diagram does not establish the same equations across ecology,
  genetics, culture, and institutions.
- The reciprocal identity does not beat an additive model at every transition.
- The Red Queen phrase “it takes all the running you can do, to keep in the same
  place” is from Lewis Carroll; Van Valen named an evolutionary hypothesis after
  that image. It is not a verbatim Van Valen law.

## Test and kill

Preregister carrier boundaries, resource units, transition variables, rival
network/diffusion models, interventions, held-out predictions, and cost
accounting. Retract the transfer claim if the proposed layers cannot be
measured independently or if ordinary network/diffusion models predict as well.
Ecological energetics and the D4/D5 type distinction survive that failure.
