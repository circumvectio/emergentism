# Co-Emergence

> **[金] Crack:** the predecessor made balance, `η`, and D5 carry ontology and
> morality. **Repair:** co-emergence names a testable relation among actual
> systems, not a proof that relation is good or irreducible.

Two processes co-emerge when interventions on each alter the other's reachable
trajectories across a declared boundary and neither unidirectional model
predicts the observations as well. A valid claim states variables, temporal
order, coupling mechanism, comparator, uncertainty, recovery, and kill
criterion.

Actual organisms, agents, awareness, models, institutions, interactions, and
receipts are `D4 = actual`. Their represented alternatives are
`D5 = merely_possible`. Co-emergence is not an autonomous D5 entity, quantum
measurement, physical retrocausality, or proof of strong emergence.

Mutual dependence also does not prove Justice. A parasite and host can be
tightly coupled. Moral, ethical, or syntropic classification separately
requires complete bearer accounting and receipted consequence;
`etaObserved=0` is necessary but insufficient.

The higher-level relation remains useful if its constraint variable adds
intervention-sensitive predictive power while respecting lower-law
admissibility. If a simpler one-way or common-cause model performs equally
well, retire the co-emergence claim for that case.
