# The Titans — Canonical Mathematical Boundary

The framework selects the tokens `{0,1,∞}` as **Titans** because they form a
useful projective normalization and a compact symbolic grammar:

```text
• = 0     additive origin / absence token
⊙ = 1     multiplicative unit / finity token
○ = ∞     projective horizon / unbounded token
```

The selection is `[I]`. It is not a forced three-element closure, a new theorem,
an exhaustive ontology of number, or a generator of the D-ladder. This document
is a lens on mathematics, never the Ground.

## 1. What the mathematics actually says

- `0` and `1` are ordinary numbers and lawful operands. `0` is the additive
  identity; `1` is the multiplicative identity.
- `∞` is not a largest real or complex number. In the one-point
  compactification `ℂ∪{∞}≅ℂP¹`, it is the projective point at infinity.
- A Möbius transformation is determined by the images of three distinct
  points. Choosing `{0,1,∞}` is therefore a standard and convenient
  normalization, not a proof that reality has exactly three primordial frames.
- Inversion `z↦1/z` exchanges `0` and `∞` and fixes **both** `+1` and `−1`.
  On `ℝ₊`, only `+1` is present, so `1` is the unique positive fixed point.
- For `x>0`, `s=log x` converts inversion to reflection: `s↦−s`. The unit
  `x=1` maps to the additive origin `s=0`.

These are elementary facts `[A]`. Calling the selected roles Titans, calling
the positive unit *finity*, and reading the triad philosophically are
Emergentist interpretations `[I]`.

## 2. The Kintsugi seam: why the triad is selected, not forced

The retired owner said that `{0,1,∞}` were “not numbers,” “never operands,” and
the forced structural closure of the number field. Formal audit broke those
claims:

1. `0` and `1` plainly participate in ordinary arithmetic.
2. Inversion does not uniquely close the poles with `+1`; it fixes `±1`.
3. Projective three-point normalization permits **any** three distinct points;
   `{0,1,∞}` is canonical by convention and usefulness, not necessity.
4. Nothing in that normalization derives an ontology, an ethic, or seven
   dimensions.

The beauty survives as a declared coordinate choice. The false necessity does
not. This is the gold seam: the symbolism becomes stronger because its type is
now explicit.

## 3. Finity and the reciprocal chart

On the positive reals, let `x>0` and `s=log x`. Then:

```text
x ↦ 1/x          corresponds to          s ↦ −s
x = 1            corresponds to          s = 0
E(x)=(log x)²    has its unique minimum at x=1
```

For the selected spherical chart

```text
θ∈(0,π),   ν=tan(θ/2),   φ=cot(θ/2)=1/ν,
```

the equator `θ=π/2` gives `φ=ν=1`, while the excluded pole limits exchange
`0` and `∞`. The exact chart identities are `[A]`; “balance,” “presence,” and
“finity” are interpretations `[I]`. The unit is not thereby proved morally
optimal or ontologically fundamental.

## 4. Division by zero: precise scope

In the extended complex plane, the meromorphic map `z↦N/z` for finite
`N≠0` has value `∞` at `z=0`. It is acceptable, **when this projective context
is named**, to write:

```text
N / 0 = ∞     (N finite and nonzero; extended-complex/projective context)
```

This does not totalize ordinary field division. In ordinary real or complex
arithmetic, division by zero remains undefined. The forms `0/0` and `∞/∞`
remain indeterminate; no Titan doctrine assigns them a value. Ordinary
`1/1=1` remains valid because `1` is an ordinary number.

## 5. `1 = 0 × ∞` is an emblem only

In field arithmetic and standard limit calculus, `0·∞` is indeterminate. The
framework may display

```text
⊙ = • × ○
1 = 0 × ∞
```

only as a typed symbolic emblem `[I]`. The `×` in the emblem is not field
multiplication and creates no arithmetic result.

A quick falsifier shows why. If ordinary multiplication were extended with
`0×∞=1` while keeping the usual finite rules and the extended rule
`a×∞=∞` for finite nonzero `a`, associativity would fail:

```text
(0×∞)×2 = 1×2 = 2
0×(∞×2) = 0×∞ = 1
```

Therefore the emblem cannot be cited as algebraic proof, as a definition of
the reciprocal chart, or as evidence for the finite-node model.

## 6. Two useful faces of number

- The additive line is appropriate for order, signed magnitude, limits, and
  linear comparison. It distinguishes `−∞` and `+∞` in the two-point extended
  order.
- The multiplicative/projective chart is appropriate for ratios, reciprocal
  symmetry, conformal geometry, and one-point compactification.
- `log` and `exp` connect positive multiplication with addition.

Neither geometry is the one complete ontology of number. Rosetta may project
between them, but projection transfers structure—not proof.
