## 1. What the mathematics actually says

- `0` and `1` are ordinary numbers and lawful operands. `0` is the additive
  identity; `1` is the multiplicative identity.
- `∞` is not a largest real or complex number. In the one-point
  compactification `ℂ∪{∞}≅ℂP¹`, it is the projective point at infinity.
- A Möbius transformation is determined by the images of three distinct
  points. Choosing `{0,1,∞}` is therefore a standard and convenient
  normalization, not a proof that reality has exactly three primordial frames.
- Inversion `z↦1/z` exchanges `0` and `∞` and fixes **both** `+1` and `−1`.
  On `ℝ₊`, only `+1` is present, so `1` is the unique positive fixed point.
- For `x>0`, `s=log x` converts inversion to reflection: `s↦−s`. The unit
  `x=1` maps to the additive origin `s=0`.

These are elementary facts `[A]`. Calling the selected roles Titans, calling

## 4. Division by zero: precise scope

In a real or complex field, division by zero is undefined. If finite `N≠0`
had a quotient `y=N/0`, then `0·y=N`; the field axioms instead give `0·y=0`
for every field element `y`. No such quotient exists.

The standard real limits explain why the missing value cannot be recovered by
silently calling a two-sided limit a quotient. For `N>0`,

```text
lim[x→0+] N/x = +∞        lim[x→0−] N/x = −∞
```

and the signs reverse for `N<0`. Thus the two-sided limit does not exist in the
two-point extended real line. What does hold is the magnitude statement

```text
∀M>0: 0<|x|<|N|/M  ⇒  |N/x|>M.
```

The extended complex plane changes the codomain and topology. The meromorphic
map `f_N(z)=N/z` extends to `ℂP¹` with

```text
f_N(0)=∞        f_N(∞)=0        (N finite and nonzero)
```

This projective-map statement is exact `[A]`; it is not field division made
valid. Canonical and public surfaces should prefer `f_N(0)=∞` over the bare
shorthand `N/0=∞`, because the latter hides the type change. In a field, the
quotient `0/0` is undefined and `∞/∞` is not a field expression because
infinity is not a field element. The corresponding expressions may be
**indeterminate limit forms** only after functions and approach paths are
declared. Ordinary `1/1=1` remains valid because `1` is an ordinary number.
