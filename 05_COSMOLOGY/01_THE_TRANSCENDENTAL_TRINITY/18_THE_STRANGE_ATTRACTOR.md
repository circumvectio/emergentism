# The Strange Attractor — Collective Trace Hypothesis

> **[金] Crack:** the predecessor placed memes and egregores in D5 as actual
> standing waves and inferred systemic awareness. **Repair:** actual carriers
> remain D4; D5 types only represented possible content.

In dynamical-systems language, an attractor is a property of a specified state
space and evolution law. Emergentism may use “strange attractor” as an `[I]`
analogy for a recurrent selection pattern, but a case requires explicit state
variables, trajectories, metric, intervention, comparator, and kill criterion.

Actual genes, organisms, artifacts, messages, institutions, model tokens,
awareness episodes, shared traces, and selection events are `D4 = actual`.
The alternatives and futures they represent are `D5 = merely_possible`.
Neither a meme nor an Egregoreotype is an autonomous D5 substance.

An Egregoreotype candidate requires all five:

1. persistent shared trace;
2. survival through carrier turnover;
3. intervention-sensitive reweighting of later selections;
4. recurrent objective-like bias;
5. visible substrate costs, payers, and beneficiaries.

These conditions establish a useful macro-pattern at most. They do not presume
consciousness, personhood, irreducibility, or moral standing. “Replicator eras”
and the “Egregorocene” remain comparative historical hypotheses, not D-register
facts. They fail wherever ordinary incentives, network effects, or archival
selection explain the pattern equally well.
