## II. THE TRADITIONAL CORPUS [S]

### II.a Tantra (broad sense)

The Tantric corpus emerges in the Indian subcontinent between approximately the 5th and 10th centuries CE. The Sanskrit *tantra* derives from *tan-* — "to weave, extend, stretch on a loom." Tantra positions itself explicitly against both the Vedic-Brāhmaṇical sacrificial path and the renunciate Śramaṇa/Sūtric trajectory by working *with* embodied energies rather than against them.

Key texts:
- ***Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra*** (~8th c.) — catalogues 112 *dhāraṇās* (meditative leverage points), many keyed explicitly to breath, sexual arousal, and bodily sensation
- ***Hevajra Tantra*** (~8th c.) — Buddhist tantric system foundational for later Vajrayana
- ***Cakrasaṃvara Tantra*** (~9th c.) — yogini-class tantra, channel-and-wind doctrine
- ***Kālacakra Tantra*** (~11th c.) — most comprehensive systematisation, with cosmological, physiological, and ritual dimensions integrated
- **Abhinavagupta** (c. 950–1016 CE), *Tantrāloka* — synthetic non-dual Śaiva framework that retroactively organises the corpus

### II.b The triadic move regarding sexual energy

Tantra's distinctive contribution is the explicit triadic move:

1. **Sexual energy is NOT suppressed** as in monastic *brahmacarya*
2. **It is NOT indulged** as raw untransformed pleasure
3. **It is *channelled*** — directed up the central axis (*suṣumnā* in Hindu tantras, *avadhūtī* in Buddhist tantras) through deliberate technique

[I] In framework terms, this is the explicit empirical refusal of two failed F5-management strategies (damming and discharging) in favour of redirection. The traditions arrived at this triadic structure through generations of cross-lineage observation — not philosophical speculation but practitioner-instrumented experiment.

### II.c Vajrayana ("Diamond Vehicle")

Vajrayana emerges from late Indian Mahāyāna in the 7th-8th centuries, transmitted to Tibet by **Padmasambhava** (8th c.) and elaborated through the four lineages: Nyingma, Kagyu, Sakya, and Gelug. Its architectural distinction:

- **Generation stage** (*bskyed rim* / kyerim) — deity visualisation and identity-restructuring
- **Completion stage** (*rdzogs rim* / dzogrim) — works directly with the *nāḍī* (channels), *prāṇa* (winds), and *bindu* (drops)

The **Six Yogas of Naropa** (11th c.) codify the completion-stage protocol:

- ***gtum mo*** (*caṇḍālī*, "inner heat") — ignites gonadal-adrenal energy at the navel and drives it up the central channel; the somatically central practice
- ***karmamudrā*** — physical consort practice; used cautiously and rarely; explicitly framed as advanced redirection, never as licence for indulgence
- ***'pho ba*** (*phowa*) — transference of systemic awareness through the crown at death

## III. WHY THE SEXUAL ORGANS AND GLANDS — THE SOMATIC SUBSTRATE [E + I]

### III.a The HPG axis as F5 hardware

[S] The Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal (HPG) axis is the vertebrate master regulator of reproductive physiology and the behaviours sexual selection operates upon. Pulsatile GnRH from hypothalamic preoptic and arcuate nuclei drives anterior-pituitary release of LH and FSH, which stimulate gonadal synthesis of testosterone, estradiol, and progesterone (Plant 2015; Herbison 2016). Kisspeptin neurons gate GnRH pulsatility and integrate metabolic, photoperiodic, and social-cue inputs.

This is **the single most input-integrated motivational axis in the vertebrate brain.** It is fed by metabolic state, environmental cues, social position, threat signals, and reward history — all converging on the same neuroendocrine output.

### III.b Behavioural reach beyond reproduction [S]

HPG output modulates far more than copulatory behaviour. It modulates the *entire behavioural cluster sexual selection acts upon*:

- Motivational drive
- Energetic expenditure
- Risk tolerance
- Signal-display readiness
- Dominance contests
- Status-seeking
- Persistence under cost
- Pair bonding
- Parental investment willingness

Hormone-behaviour couplings (Wingfield 1990; Sapolsky 1997, 2017; Archer 2006; Carter 1998, 2014; Young & Wang 2004; Krüger et al. 2002): testosterone-aggression, estrogen-bonding, oxytocin-attachment, vasopressin-mate-guarding, prolactin-paternal-care.

[S] HPG is reciprocally coupled to HPA (stress/cortisol) and HPT (thyroid/metabolism). [I] The modern hyper-sexualised consumer environment is a planet-scale uncontrolled experiment in untransformed discharge.
