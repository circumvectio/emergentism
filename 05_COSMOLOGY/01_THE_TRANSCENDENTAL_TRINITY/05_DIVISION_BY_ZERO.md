# Division by zero — projective boundary, not arithmetic repair

## Verdict

Ordinary field division by zero is undefined. The Riemann sphere does not make
that field operation legal. It extends the codomain of a meromorphic or
projective map by one point, conventionally written `∞`, so a pole can be
represented on `ℂP¹`.

For a nonzero constant `N`, the projective map represented by `[N:z]` sends
`z=0` to the point at infinity. That statement is exact `[A]`. It is not the
field equation `N/0=∞`, and it supplies no value for `0/0` or `∞/∞`.

## What the sphere actually contributes

`ℂP¹` is compact and boundaryless. In its standard spherical metric it is also
bounded. Stereographic projection identifies the added projective point with a
chosen pole; it does not make curvature “fill an arithmetic hole,” make zero
and infinity multiply to one, or force arbitrary journeys to return.

The emblem

```text
•   ⊙   ○
```

therefore remains symbolic `[I]`. Ordinary `0` and `1` remain numbers, `∞`
remains a projective point in this chart, and `0×∞=1` is not field arithmetic.

## The surviving philosophical use

Changing the ambient category can turn an apparent failure of representation
into a well-typed boundary description. This is a useful methodological
parable: before declaring a paradox solved, ask whether the original operation
was ill-typed and whether a larger space merely represents its limiting
behavior. The new representation must not be advertised as changing the old
rules.

It does not follow that every philosophical paradox is a flat-versus-curved
mistake, that consciousness is a sphere coordinate, that free will is an
equator, or that physical reality is generated by projective completion.
