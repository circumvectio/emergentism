# The Soul Loop

The Soul Loop is the operational Rosetta stone of agency: a finite agent holds
a fallible model, represents alternatives, commits through actual means, meets
a world it does not control, and lets the resulting receipts change both its
map and its way of choosing.

It is a feedback grammar, not a soul substance, a quantum collapse mechanism,
or evidence that future events send information backward through time.

## 1. The type boundary

```text
D3 = probability-bearing quantum-state content
D4 = actual carrier, model token, ranking event, selection event,
     attempted action, outcome, and receipt
D5 = merely possible counterfactual content represented by D4 tokens
```

An actual model is not D5. It is a D4 token **about** D5 content:

```text
m_t:ModeledFutureToken(D4,actual)
  ── represents ──▶ p:D5(merely_possible)
```

The distinction prevents a forecast from becoming a fact merely because it is
vivid, ranked, authorized, or selected.

## 2. Selection produces commitment, not consequence

Let `X_t` be the available actual state, `Ω_t` the represented alternative
contents, `M_t` the present fallible model, `V_t` usable D4 means, `U_t` the
authorization and normative assessment, and `G_t` the actual selection policy:

```text
χ_t:(X_t,Ω_t,M_t,V_t,U_t,G_t) → (a_t,q_t)
a_t ∈ Action ∪ {⊥}
```

`q_t` is a **CommitmentReceipt**. It records the option considered, actor,
means, authorization status, expected consequence, and whether an action was
attempted, refused, or unavailable. It is not evidence that the expected
outcome occurred.

The environment answers separately:

```text
(X_(t+1),r_(t+1)) ~ K_t(· | X_t,a_t,E_t)
```

`K_t` includes other agents, physical constraints, noise, and conditions
outside the selector. `r_(t+1)` is the **OutcomeReceipt**. The selector may not
manufacture it.

If `a_t=⊥`, the outcome receipt is null unless an ambient observation is made;
ambient change must not be attributed to a non-action. An unauthorized action
that physically occurs remains causally real and must be receipted as
unauthorized. Normative invalidity never makes an event disappear.

## 3. The return

```text
(M_(t+1),G_(t+1)) = Loop(M_t,G_t,q_t,r_(t+1))
```

The loop may update:

- the model of the territory;
- the weights, habits, or policy used to select;
- the declared uncertainty and evidence tier;
- the available option cone after costs and consequences.

A null or non-informative receipt may legitimately produce no update. A loop
that must report improvement regardless of receipt is self-sealing and fails.

## 4. Three gaps

| Gap | Comparison | Primary record |
|---|---|---|
| cognitive | territory versus model | prediction/model audit |
| execution | intended selection versus performed attempt | `q_t` |
| outcome | expected versus observed consequence | `r_(t+1)` |

Negative feedback means a declared error decreases; positive feedback means
divergence or self-confirmation increases. These signs are dynamical, not
moral. A reinforcing loop can be beneficial or destructive; a corrective loop
can preserve or suppress.

This crosswalk credits George Soros's fallibility, cognitive and participating
functions, and reinforcing/corrective feedback. Emergentism adds the explicit
commitment/outcome receipt split. Soros supplies no evidence for quantum
collapse, physical retrocausality, or the D-register ontology.

## 5. Modeled futures influence the present

The public mnemonic is:

```text
F = M × A
```

Its formal form is typed coupling:

```text
M⋆A : ModelState × PhysicallyFeasibleActionField → ActionWeights
```

Changing a represented future can change a present action distribution. The
causal carrier is the present D4 model token embodied in a brain, diagram,
conversation, institution, or program. The future content does not travel
backward. The realized future still depends on the world.

Emergentism may call this **model-mediated retrocausality** as shorthand. In
standard causal language it is anticipatory or future-guided control. The
shorthand fails if it is used to claim a physical arrow from a future event to
an earlier one.

## 6. Authorization is a separate assessment

Consequential commitment is evaluated through:

```text
AuthorizationEnvelope := {
  principal, mandate, scope, consent, custody,
  expiry_or_revocation, contest_path, actor, consequence_bearer_set
}
```

The envelope says who may expose whom to which consequence under what mandate.
It does not make a claim true, make an action physically possible, or guarantee
an outcome. A governed selector may fail closed; the descriptive world model
must still represent accidental, coerced, or unauthorized acts that occur.

## 7. Option cone, not physical light cone

```text
OptionCone_t(A)
  = histories A can model, rank, coordinate, and plausibly reach
  ⊆ PhysicallyAdmissibleHistories(X_t)
```

Symbolic, social, institutional, and intergenerational modeling can give
humans wide option cones. It does not widen the relativistic light cone,
exceed `c`, or confer greater intrinsic worth.

## 8. Editorial and contemplative projections

The same topology can be projected onto revision:

```text
read → model → edit → inspect result → revise map and method
```

That is a Rosetta projection, not a new ontology. The older three-pass and
L-level editing vocabularies remain optional methods; they do not define the
Soul Loop, the D-registers, or evidence. Contemplative language is likewise an
interpretive practice, never a substitute for the two receipts.

## 9. Tests and kill criteria

The operational contract must represent:

1. a selected option without sufficient means;
2. an authorized attempt vetoed or altered by the environment;
3. an unauthorized but causally effective attempt;
4. distinct commitment and outcome receipts;
5. a null/non-informative update;
6. two agents with the same physical cone but different option cones;
7. an intervention on represented futures that may change present selection.

Kill or contract the model-mediated influence claim if controlled changes to
represented futures add no predictive value beyond fair present-state
baselines. Retire the loop for a scope if it cannot distinguish its own
commitment from the world's consequence or cannot update under adverse
receipts.
