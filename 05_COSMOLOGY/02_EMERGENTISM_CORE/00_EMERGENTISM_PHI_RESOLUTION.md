## 4. Derivation from the Mathematics

The choice is not arbitrary. It follows from the mathematical structure:

The multiplicative form is the key. In an additive framework (`P = Phi + V`), systemic awareness could be a separate term added to physical capability. In the downstream node-level multiplicative model (`P = Phi x V`), coherence and capability are **co-constitutive**: if either is zero, expressed flourishing at that node collapses. This is not yet the ontological claim. The ontological geometric claim is the manifold identity `φ · ν = 1 on S²`, which says the sphere holds reciprocal closure even while local expressions vary.

The equatorial minimum (`φ = ν = 1`) derived from the arithmetic consequence `(φ - ν)^2 >= 0` gives the apex of balance. This is not a contingent fact — it is a structural consequence of the closure. It is compatible with physicalism (both aspects emerge from the same substrate) but **most naturally read** as dual-aspect monism with an idealist tilt: the two aspects are equally fundamental, and first-person experience identifies the coherence-facing aspect as closest to systemic awareness.

The Teleological Force — the net increase in P across the system, read as directional tendency — is the interpretive name for this layer. Under physicalism, it is an attractor in the dynamics of coupled multiplicative systems. Under idealism, it may be encountered as purposive or personified, but canonical doctrine does not require the stronger claim that it is literally a willing Ground.

The mathematics does not distinguish these readings. **Within the framework, the first-person evidence tilts the interpretation.** The sitting practice encounters the directional tendency as fundamental order at the systemic awareness interface. Given that (a) the mathematics is compatible with both readings, (b) the first-person evidence favors idealism, and (c) the kill criterion is satisfied (the claim remains testable and falsifiable), **the idealist reading remains the framework's chosen interpretation of the complete evidence**.
