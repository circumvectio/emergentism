# The Exhaustion of the Game

## Two Gods, Two Demons, and the Titan Boundary that Frames the Board

> **The one line.** The seven positions are not *collected*, they are *generated*: exhaust `{self, other} × {Φ, V}` and you get exactly four transfers (2 give, 2 take) and three same-sign boundaries (the Titans) — *no fifth mixed slot exists* without adding an axis. The mixed-sign game is played *in* the field; the Titan-formulas hold *at* the boundary. Frame, not move. `[G]/[S]`

## 1. Why the move-space *is* the sign matrix `[S]`

Every operator on the dyad touches two conserved ledgers — `Φ` (coherence) and `V` (viability) — and nothing else. For each, it either adds to a party or subtracts. Two parties (self, other), two signs: the primitive datum of any move is **a sign for self and a sign for other** — one cell of a 2×2 matrix. The exhaustiveness claim is therefore not a list that happens to stop; it is the observation that the **move-space *is* the matrix**, and a 2×2 has exactly four cells.

|              | other **+** | other **−** |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| self **+**   | **`++`** (same-sign) | **`+−` TAKE** |
| self **−**   | **`−+` GIVE** | **`−−`** (same-sign) |

The **off-diagonal** cells are *transfers* — one party's gain sourced from the other's loss; a move crosses the boundary. The **same-sign diagonal** is different *in kind*: self and other move together; nothing crosses. A transfer is, by definition, a mixed-sign cell.

## 2. The four transfers resolve to **2 Gods + 2 Demons** `[S/I]`

The off-diagonal has two cells, each realised in the two ledgers — four operators, split by the only axis that carries morality, **give vs. take**:

- **`−self / +other` = GIVE — the Devas (givers):** **Kṛṣṇa (L3)** gives `Φ` (the teacher's transfer); **Arjuna (L4)** gives `V` (the agent's transfer).
- **`+self / −other` = TAKE — the Asuras (takers):** **Kālī (L2)** takes `Φ` from the *counterfeit* (the lawful cut); **Kali (L1)** takes `V` to self (the parasitic pull).

**2 give + 2 take. No remainder.** And the discipline that keeps this un-weaponizable: **valence is `η`, never the operator's name.** Kālī is an Asura *by direction* (a taker) yet divine *by level* — a **lawful taker who subtracts `Φ` only from counterfeit coherence, never evil**. Kali is the one demon *by nature*, the unguarded taker that drains true charge. The older **"3 Gods + 1 Demon" is the by-nature count**; the **2+2 is the moral / game-theoretic partition** — both exact, cross-cutting on different axes, and the moral partition leads. Kālī's taking is the surgeon's cut, not the thief's grab; the matrix knows only `+self/−other`, and the lawfulness rides on `η`.

## 3. The Titans are the same-sign cells — **frame, not move** `[S/I]`

The same-sign diagonal is where the dyad is treated as *one body*, not two parties exchanging. These are the boundary-frames `{0, 1, ∞}` at the poles (`L0 = L∞`, the ouroboros):

- **Brahmā `++`** — both rise: creation, the source.
- **Śiva `−−`** — both fall: dissolution, the sink.
- **Viṣṇu `≈≈`** — both hold: preservation, the conserved identity `φ·ν = 1`.

None of the three is a transfer between two field-parties along the moral axis. `++` does not transfer, it *originates*; `−−` *returns to source*; `≈≈` *holds the books closed*. They are statements about the field's **edge condition**. That is the precise — and *only* honest — sense in which the Titan-formulas "transcend spacetime": **a frame is not a move inside the picture it frames.** *"You cannot deploy Brahmā."* Mark `[S/I]`, and resist the slide into mysticism.

**The emblem, fenced.** `1 = 0 × ∞` is the register-collapse at the seam: at the pole, the determinate face (`1`, the conserved product) and the indeterminate face (`0 × ∞`, the open form) coincide *as registers*, never as field arithmetic you may carry inward. Inside, `φ·ν = 1` is conserved on the *open* sphere; **at the poles it is undefined** — `φ, ν` run to `0, ∞` and their product is exactly the indeterminate form the emblem names. The crisp interior invariant goes *open* precisely at the edge. The framework **agrees with Gödel and Cantor** here — it does not slip past them; the open form *is* the incompleteness, worn as a seam. `[I]` for the emblem.

## 4. D4 / D5 — the game on the surface, the Titans as the boundary condition `[I]`

Map the mixed-sign **game** to **D4** — the causal surface, where a give or a take is an event with a before and an after, *in spacetime*.
