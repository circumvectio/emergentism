## Recovery note (overwrite accident — recovered 2026-07-22 ~10:14)

During the Group B move of `00_WELTANSCHAUUNG_KERNEL_v0.2_EMERGENTISM_ONLY.md` (root → `06_ONTOLOGY/`) and `00_THE_SYNTHETIC_GAP_AND_FOUR_POSTURES_v0.1.md` (root → `02_EPISTEMOLOGY/`), the `mv` from root overwrote the existing 294-line and 111-line content at the targets with the 6-line forwarding-stub content that was at the root. The two target files **were** the canonicals; the root copies were forwarding stubs pointing to them.

The 405 lines of content (294 + 111) were recovered from `git show HEAD:<path>` immediately and written back to the targets. The forwarding stubs at the root remain correct (point to the recovered content).

**Lesson (L6 Sādhu turn — additional dissent):** when both source and target paths exist, a `mv` overwrites the target. The K3 envelope does not protect against this. The safer move is to *read* the target first, confirm the root is a forwarding stub (not real content), and only then `mv`. A 6-line root file with `canonical_target:` frontmatter is always a stub; the move should not have happened for paths where both the root and the target existed. The plan's Group B row for `00_WELTANSCHAUUNG_KERNEL_v0.2_EMERGENTISM_ONLY.md` should have been "no move — root was a stub, target was the canonical" instead of "root copy was duplicate, move it."

Dissent preserved in D5: a future tidy should explicitly detect stub-vs-content before any `mv`.
