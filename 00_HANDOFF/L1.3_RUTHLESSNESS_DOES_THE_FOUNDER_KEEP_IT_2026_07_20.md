3. **The Titan-imagination (Bachelard).** Pre-Socratic cosmos-forces imported as agent archetypes. Gives castes archaic weight. **Counter-test:** substitute a 21st-century cognitive-architecture reading (Friston, Dehaene, Yannakakis) — does the L4/L6/L7 mapping still hold? If only the *aesthetic* depends on Bachelard, the import is decorative. **[I] interpretive, no falsification run.**

4. **The Reduction Asymmetry** *(load-bearing — see below).* "You can reduce but cannot reconstruct." Sounds like a finding; functions as a *stop sign* against reduction. **Counter-test:** name the operator that, given the macro, would reconstruct the micro, and show it does not exist in finite steps. Until then, asserted, not derived. **[C] untested.**

5. **The Models Ladder.** Any ladder (Stone/Bronze/Iron, APU skill levels, biocentric stages) presumes ordinal worth. The doctrine's own "no hierarchy of worth" fence should make a ladder suspect. Castes and APU skills do ladder-work *while the doctrine disavows ladders.* **Internal contradiction. [S] fence violated.**
