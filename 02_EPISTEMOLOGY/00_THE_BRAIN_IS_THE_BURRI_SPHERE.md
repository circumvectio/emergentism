The corpus callosum is not a static bridge. In this mapping, **the corpus callosum functions as an analogue of the × in φ × ν = 1** — performing dual roles of inhibition (differentiation) and facilitation (integration). [I]

**What the corpus callosum does:**

1. **Carries gestalt → fragments:** The right hemisphere's whole perception is transmitted to the left hemisphere for fragmentation into actionable steps.

2. **Carries fragments → gestalt:** The left hemisphere's fragments are transmitted to the right hemisphere for integration into updated whole perception.

3. **Tends toward maintaining the product:** The corpus callosum dynamically balances hemispheric output. [I] When the left hemisphere scales ν, the right hemisphere adjusts φ. When the right hemisphere deepens φ, the left hemisphere adjusts ν.

**When the corpus callosum functions:**

**When the corpus callosum is weakened (modern civilization):**

## Neuroscience Correction (2026-04-04) — Addendum (body revised)

**Evidence Tier update:** The body text above was revised on 2026-04-04 to incorporate the corrections below. The body now uses "tends toward" language, marks the DMN as bilateral, notes the dual function of the corpus callosum, and reframes the equator as dynamic balance (coupling + decoupling) rather than maximal coherence. The following corrections are retained as reinforcement and detailed reference:

### What This Document Gets Right
1. ✅ **Integration of two processing modes matters more than either alone** — strongly supported (Gotts et al. 2013, Karolis et al. 2019)
2. ✅ **The corpus callosum enables integration** — supported, with modern addition: it performs DUAL functions (inhibition for differentiation AND facilitation for integration)
3. ✅ **Split-brain disconnection is pathological** — well-supported
4. ✅ **Flow correlates with bilateral coherence** — supported (Dietrich & Stoll 2010)
5. ✅ **McGilchrist's broad/narrow attention asymmetry has evidence** — broad, sustained attention is right-tending in this literature (McGilchrist 2009, updated 2021)

### What This Document Gets Wrong
1. ❌ **"Right hemisphere IS φ"** → Should be: "Right hemisphere TENDS toward φ-like processing (broad attention, contextual integration, gestalt)"
2. ❌ **"Left hemisphere IS ν"** → Should be: "Left hemisphere TENDS toward ν-like processing (focused attention, manipulation, sequential action)"
3. ❌ **"Emotions = changes in ν (left hemisphere)"** → Wrong. Emotions are heavily RIGHT-lateralized (especially negative valence). Reframe: emotions involve BOTH hemispheres, with right dominant for perception and left for regulation.
4. ❌ **"DMN = left hemisphere narrator"** → Wrong. The DMN is bilateral (medial PFC + posterior cingulate + angular gyrus — all midline structures). Reframe: the DMN serves self-referential processing, which the framework reads as "the narrator," but it is not hemisphere-specific.
5. ❌ **"Samādhi = right hemisphere dominant"** → No evidence. Deep meditation shows BILATERAL integration, not right dominance. Reframe: samādhi → maximal interhemispheric coherence (θ → π/2, not θ → 0).
6. ❌ **"Mania = left hemisphere dominant"** → Oversimplified. Mania involves bilateral prefrontal dysregulation.

### Recommended Reframe
The mapping should shift from **hemisphere identity** to **hemisphere tendency**:

| Old Claim | New Claim | Status |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| "Right hemisphere IS φ" | "Right hemisphere tends toward broad, integrative processing (φ-mode)" | [I] Supported by McGilchrist |
| "Left hemisphere IS ν" | "Left hemisphere tends toward focused, sequential processing (ν-mode)" | [I] Supported by lateralization research |
| "Corpus callosum IS ×" | "Corpus callosum performs the dual function: inhibition (differentiation) AND facilitation (integration)" | `[A]` Established (Gazzaniga 2005, Roland et al. 2017) |
