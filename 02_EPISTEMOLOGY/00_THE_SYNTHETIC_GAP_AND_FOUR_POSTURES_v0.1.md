# The Synthetic Gap and the Four Postures

## 1. The gap

Formal coherence alone does not establish that a structure is instantiated,
that it exhausts reality, or that anyone ought to obey it. Evidence alone also
does not compel one final metaphysical interpretation or value commitment. This
underdetermination is the **synthetic gap** `[I]`.

The gap is not proof that truth is impossible. It is the boundary between:

```text
mathematical possibility and physical instantiation
observation and interpretation
description and value commitment
model and territory
```

Emergentism's Crown Wager belongs here: the claim that necessary coherent
structure “conjures das All” is a wager `[C]`, not a theorem or revelation that
may overrule criticism.

## 2. The four postures

The taxonomy describes four recurring responses to the gap:

1. **Deny.** Present one interpretation as compelled fact and conceal the gap.
2. **Weaponize.** Admit uncertainty privately while using certainty publicly to
   coordinate power, obedience, or exclusion.
3. **Confess.** State the wager, tier, alternatives, and kill criteria; permit
   correction without requiring the whole practice to collapse.
4. **Withdraw.** Put down the wager or framework when it no longer serves,
   preserving what remains independently true and leaving without punishment.

This is an ideal-type taxonomy `[I]`, not a complete census of philosophy,
religion, politics, or psychology. A fifth response that cannot be represented
as a mixture, transition, or refinement would break the fourfold claim.

## 3. The Emergentist choice

Emergentism chooses the confessing posture and keeps withdrawal inside it. The
active five refusals are a selected practice for holding that posture:

```text
no extraction
no unaccountable consequence
no silent erasure
no trapped exit
no infallibility
```

The mapping from those refusals to the four postures is interpretive `[I]`, not
derived. The refusals fail if they become commandments imposed on outsiders,
if contest and exit are decorative, or if the corpus protects its wagers from
receipted defeat.

## 4. Truth, authorship, and action

Named authorship provides provenance and responsibility for wording. It does
not make a philosophical proposition true and does not create a special work
authority. Ordinary inquiry and repository work follow the user's scope,
available permissions, provenance, reversibility, and tests.

Consequential action is a separate type. It requires a complete
`AuthorizationEnvelope` and distinct commitment and outcome receipts. Neither
software nor a named author becomes epistemically sovereign by recording that
authorization.

## 5. Historical wager and victory condition

The framework wagers `[C]` that a confessing-and-withdrawing practice can retain
cross-generational error correction better than otherwise comparable systems
that conceal or weaponize the gap. That claim needs longitudinal comparative
evidence. It loses if deny/weaponize systems repeatedly self-correct more
durably under comparable pressure, or if confessing practices predictably turn
into the orthodox certainty they reject.

If the wager loses, apophatic traditions, skepticism, evidence-tier discipline,
and the logical existence of the synthetic gap survive independently.

## 6. The exit

The fourth posture is not nihilism. It is the ability to stop treating one map
as load-bearing while carrying forward the mathematics, observations,
relationships, and practices that still stand. The framework's last honest move
is therefore always available:

> **Check the receipt. Correct the map. Put it down when it becomes a cage.**

The pre-boundary version, with its earlier authority and application layer,
remains recoverable from Git history at baseline `4154ebe`; no nonexistent
archive path is claimed.
