# The Honest Position

> **[金] Kintsugi seam — the ledger, not the sales deck.** The predecessor
> accumulated research programs, applications, historical claims, and stronger
> rhetoric until the evidence owner became difficult to audit. This successor
> restores one job: define how a claim earns, keeps, loses, and reports its
> standing. It does not promote a proposition by listing it.

## 1. The six evidence tiers

| Tier | Meaning | What it does not mean |
|---|---|---|
| `[A]` | Analytic theorem, identity, counterproof, or established formal result inside a named system and domain | empirical truth, world applicability, framework ownership, or moral authority |
| `[B]` | Custodied observation, measurement, source attribution, built artifact, executed test, repository fact, or dated receipt | generalization beyond the observation, independent replication unless named, deployment, or universal truth |
| `[S]` | Structural consequence inside explicit premises, definitions, types, or rules | that the premises describe nature |
| `[I]` | Interpretation, analogy, translation, synthesis, or chosen normative reading | proof, measurement, or discovered universality |
| `[D]` | Draft, demonstration, proposed schema, or staged construction | adoption, operation, or calibration |
| `[C]` | Conjecture with a stated route to possible defeat | revelation, license to ignore counterevidence, or a future fact |

`[A]` and `[B]` are different evidence types, not higher and lower versions of
one claim. A theorem may be `[A]`; an experiment, attributed paper, direct
measurement, or deterministic renderer run may be `[B]`, qualified as internal,
external, or independently replicated where relevant. Neither automatically
evidences the other's domain. Canonical status, publication, beauty, adoption,
survival, and authority are not evidence tiers.

## 2. The minimum claim record

A load-bearing claim must declare:

```text
id and exact proposition
claim type and scope
current tier and justification
sources and custody
strongest live rival
prediction or operational consequence
kill or downgrade criterion
what survives failure
owner and last adjudication
```

If the claim cannot state its boundary or a possible defeat, it may remain a
definition, wager, metaphor, or practice. It may not masquerade as an empirical
discovery.

## 3. Translation cannot transfer proof

The Rosetta projection `ρ_domain` may preserve a topology while changing the
domain vocabulary. It never transfers proof, evidence, mechanism, or tier.
Cross-domain resemblance is `[I]` until each domain supplies its own sources,
measurements, alternatives, and kill criterion.

One source shown in many columns remains one source. One mathematical identity
rendered through many traditions remains one identity plus several readings,
not several independent confirmations.

## 5. Direct and first-person disclosure

First-person experience can orient inquiry, disclose salience, and motivate a
wager. Sincerity is not public calibration. A direct experience becomes a
public candidate claim only when its report, boundary, alternatives, method,
and possible defeat are explicit. Instruments and witnesses have calibration
profiles; no identity is intrinsically sovereign, independent, or reliable.

## 6. Commitment is not outcome

An action proposal, performed action, and world consequence are distinct:

```text
model -> intention
selector -> action + commitment receipt
environment -> outcome receipt
receipts -> update
```

A valid authorization does not make a proposition true. A well-intended act
can have a harmful outcome. An observation made without authority can still be
factually correct. Epistemic standing, permission, performance, and consequence
must therefore be audited separately.

## 7. Change and failure law

- No silent tier change, draft→receipt, receipt→deployment, or
  internal→independent custody claim. Formal `C→A` requires proof; empirical
  `C→B` requires its matching observation or test.
- A result changes only the exact preregistered proposition it tests.
- A failed construct, missing custody, post-hoc criterion, or rival that wins
  causes narrowing, retyping, retirement, or redesign; a new interpretation cannot rescue
  the old result retroactively.
- Successful tests support bounded `[B]` claims. Independent replication remains
  `[B]` with stronger custody and scope; it does not become analytic `[A]`.
  External calibration requires suitable independent contact with the territory.
- A correction preserves the wound and the predecessor's recoverable custody.
