# D5 — The Mutualism Limit

## Where Positive-Sum Exchange Lives in the Seven-Slot Grammar

> **The objection this answers.** The four transfers are conjugate-paying (every give spends the giver), and the `++` frame is non-deployable ("you cannot deploy Brahmā"). So where does *trade* live — the Pareto-improving, both-parties-rise exchange that `η = 0` cooperation is supposed to produce, and that the entire ΣΔP_node > 0 program depends on? If the grammar has no cell for the healthiest transaction in economics, the grammar fails exactly where the organism needs it most.
>
> **The answer in one line.** Single-shot trade is **two crossed gives** (no new operator); its *gains* are why the crossing is worth it `[E]`; and under iteration at `η = 0` the pair **converges to the Brahmā frame** — creation is not a move you deploy but the frame you *earn*. `[S/I]`, with the fences in §5.

---

## 1. Single-shot trade = crossed gives `[S]`

Let A and B trade. In the dyadic grammar each side runs a giving transfer toward the other, simultaneously:

```
A:  −dΦ_A , +dV_B      (a Kṛṣṇa-signature give toward B)
B:  −dΦ_B , +dV_A      (a Kṛṣṇa-signature give toward A)
```

Per node, net single-shot signature: `(−dΦ, +dV)` — each party spends coherence (attention, negotiation, verification, contract — the *coordination spend*) and gains means. **No eighth operator is needed**: trade is a composition of two existing slots, exactly as the Exhaustion note requires ("no fifth mixed slot exists without adding an axis").

Two public-register anchors `[E]`:
- The V-gains exceed the V given up — **comparative advantage** (Ricardo): the crossing is positive-sum in means even when each transfer alone is conjugate-paying.
- The Φ-spend is the **transaction cost** (Coase, North): real, and the thing institutions exist to economize.

## 2. The Mutualism Limit `[S/I]`

Iterate the crossed gives under `η = 0`. The public literature is unambiguous that repetition changes the game (`[E]`: Axelrod's repeated-game cooperation; North on institutions as amortized coordination; reputation and trust as capital):

- The per-round Φ-spend **falls**: norms form, verification thins, contracts shorten, trust substitutes for enforcement. The coordination cost amortizes across the relationship.
- The V-surplus **persists** each round (comparative advantage does not deplete).
- Additionally the *relationship itself* accrues coherence: shared expectations, common language, institutional memory — `Φ` rising at the pair level.

In the limit, the per-round signature of each node crosses from `(−dΦ, +dV)` to `(+dΦ, +dV)`:

> **The pair enters the Brahmā frame.** Sustained equatorial exchange at `η = 0` *is* the approach to `++`. Creation is not transacted; it is **converged to**. "You cannot deploy Brahmā" survives intact — you can only earn his frame, round by round, by keeping the crossing clean.

This also states the converse discipline: one extractive round (`η > 0`) re-inflates the Φ-spend (verification returns, trust collapses) — the pair falls out of the frame far faster than it entered. The asymmetry is the empirical signature to test (§5).

## 3. Why this is the organism's own transaction `[I]`

`K* = 0` toward cooperators, ΣΔP_node > 0 across the widest boundary, fees only on value created — the constitutional economics *presuppose* a transaction whose iterated form leaves both parties risen. §1–2 name it inside the grammar instead of leaving it implicit: **the Skyzai exchange is a crossed give under η = 0, run to its Brahmā limit.** The DAC's job, in this language, is to keep the Φ-spend of crossing low (receipts, K0) and the `η = 0` fence hard, so the limit is reachable.

## 5. Fences and kill criteria

1. **E2/V3 fence.** At *reflexive contact* (mutual modeling, depth-vs-depth) the E2/V3 program found **no conserved product** — the coupling is positional, competed to zero at equilibrium. The Mutualism Limit is a claim about **iterated dyadic exchange under enforcement**, not about reflexive depth games. Do not cite this note as overturning V3; the two registers are disjoint by §4.
2. **Not a conservation claim.** The limit claim is directional (`per-round Φ-spend falls; pair-level Φ rises`), not a conserved-quantity claim. `P∞ = 1` is untouched; this is `P_node` register throughout.
3. **Kill criterion (empirical).** In repeated-exchange data — lab trust/PD games `[E]`, or the organism's own receipts once live — if sustained `η = 0` interaction fails to (a) reduce per-round coordination cost and (b) raise both parties' Φ-proxies (trust, verification-thinning, dispute rate), the Brahmā-limit reading dies and trade stays a mere crossed give. The crossed-gives decomposition (§1) survives either way.
4. **Kill criterion (structural).** Exhibit a genuinely positive-sum *single-shot* dyadic move that is not decomposable into crossed transfers and adds no third axis — then the `4 + 3` closure itself fails (this is the Seven Actions' own kill criterion, inherited).
