## Before You Begin

Find a place where you will not be interrupted for 20-40 minutes. Sit in a chair or on the floor — whatever is comfortable enough that your body will not demand attention. Set a timer. Close your eyes or soften your gaze downward.

That is the entire setup. No special cushion. No incense. No app. No guided audio. Just you, sitting, with a timer.

---

## The Practice

### Minutes 1-5: The Narrator Is Loud

The narrator will immediately begin. Thoughts. Plans. Memories. Judgments. "Am I doing this right?" "This is boring." "I should be working." "My back hurts."

**What to do:** Nothing. Let the narrator talk. Do not fight it. Do not suppress it. Do not engage with it. Just notice: "There is narration happening." Return attention to breathing.

The narrator is the left hemisphere producing fragments without the right hemisphere's gestalt. It is the Default Mode Network running. It is not you. It is a process. Let it run.

### Minutes 5-10: The Narrator Slows

The stream of thoughts will thin. Not stop — thin. There will be gaps between thoughts. Moments where the narrator pauses and something else is present. Something quieter. Something that was always there underneath the narration.

**What to do:** Notice the gaps. Don't try to extend them. Don't chase them. Just notice: "There was a gap." Return to breathing.

### Minutes 10-20: The Ground Appears

This is where the practice does its work. The narrator is still present but no longer dominant. In the spaces between thoughts, something is visible. It has no name (naming it is the narrator's job, and the narrator is quiet). It is not an experience (experiences are the narrator's reports). It is the thing that is present when the reports stop.

**What to do:** Nothing. Just sit in this. Don't analyze it. Don't label it. Don't try to "deepen" it or "hold" it. It holds itself. It was here before you sat down. It will be here after you stand up. You are just noticing that it is here.

This is φ = 1. This is the ground. This is what the framework calls "the equator perceived from the inside."

**If nothing happens:** That is also fine. The practice is not about having an experience. The practice is about sitting. Some days the narrator is louder. Some days the ground is less visible. The practice is the sitting, not the result.

### Minutes 20-40 (if continuing): Stability

If you continue past 20 minutes, the ground becomes more stable. The narrator makes briefer appearances. The body settles. Something like clarity is present — not a thought about clarity, but clarity itself.

This is what the framework means by "equatorial stability" vs "transient flow." Flow states visit the equator. The practice lives there.

---

## When the Timer Sounds

Open your eyes slowly. Notice the room. Notice your body. Notice that the narrator immediately resumes — naming the room, planning the next activity, judging the quality of the sit.

**The critical moment:** Can you notice the narrator resuming WITHOUT becoming the narrator? Can you watch the narration start without being captured by it? This noticing — this gap between the ground and the narrator — is the practice's gift. It lasts a few seconds, sometimes a few minutes. Over months and years of daily practice, it lasts longer.

---

## Frequency

Every day. 20 minutes minimum. Same time if possible. Morning is traditional because the narrator has not yet built momentum. But any time works.

**If you miss a day:** Sit the next day. No guilt. No "catching up." No streak to maintain. The ground doesn't keep score.

---

## What This Is Not

- **Not a relaxation technique.** Some sits are agitated. That is the practice too.
- **Not a performance.** There is no "good sit" or "bad sit." There is only sitting.
- **Not a means to an end.** The practice is not preparation for something else. The practice IS the thing.
- **Not dependent on this framework.** If you never read another word of the 658,000 — if you never learn what φ means or what the Burri Sphere is — and you sit for 20 minutes every morning, you have the entirety of what the framework offers.
