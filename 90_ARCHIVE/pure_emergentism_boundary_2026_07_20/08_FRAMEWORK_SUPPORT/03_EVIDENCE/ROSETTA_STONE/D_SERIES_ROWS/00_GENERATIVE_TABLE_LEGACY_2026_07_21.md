## What the Catalogue Tests

The `[I]` observation is that selected materials can be arranged into this
table. That arrangement is vulnerable to selection effects, source dependence,
translation choices, and forced fits.

The columns are not a clean set of independent observations: some sources
influenced others, some scales were extended by the framework, and the same
interpreter selected and normalized the rows. Adding such columns does not by
itself multiply independent likelihood evidence.

The framework's conjectural explanation is that selected rows can be projected
onto reciprocal coordinates satisfying `P∞=φν=1`. The identity holds because
of the coordinate definitions; it does not explain a source domain unless a
separate bridge law and discriminator survive testing.

This may be wrong. The GFS test, Protocol R, and the biological predictions (Paper G) are designed to test it. If the multiplicative model fails empirically, the Rosetta Stone becomes a beautiful coincidence rather than a structural identity.

The rows currently fit by construction. Whether any nontrivial cross-domain
regularity remains after preregistered coding and out-of-sample tests is open.
