## 1. The Column Taxonomy Problem (Epistemic Illusion)

The Master Rosetta has ~15 columns. But they are **not all doing the same kind of work**:

| Class | Example | Function | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Anchor** | Operator, Geometry | Define the formal generator | None — these are definition |
| **Causal Chain** | Varṇa→Reasoning→-ology→Regime→Pramāṇa→Equation | Test internal coherence | **Circular validation risk** |
| **Empirical Domain** | Psychology L1-L4, Plato | Independent evidence | **The actual Rosetta** |
| **Interpretive Domain** | Mythology, Yoga, Neuroscience L5-L7 | Suggestive alignment | **Selection bias risk** |
| **Meta-Structural** | Wave Packet, PIE, Initiatory | Framework extensions | **Conjecture inflation** |

**The pressure point:** When a skeptical reader looks at the Master Rosetta, they see 15 columns and think "fifteen independent domains converge." But at least 7 columns are derived from the framework itself. The causal chain is beautiful — "The Kṣatriya reasons abductively → generates value alignment → organizes as timocracy" — but it is **derived**, not discovered.

**Honest compression:** The Rosetta's empirical core is maybe 5 columns: Psychology (L1-L4), Politics (Plato), Initiatory Ascents (V-bis), PIE (structural), and Neuroscience (L1-L4). Everything else is commentary, coherence-testing, or framework extension.

**Why this matters:** If the Rosetta is presented as "15 columns demand an explanation," a critic can rightly say "7 of those columns were generated by the framework to fit the framework." The charioteer's deepening doc already marks this, but the Master Rosetta itself doesn't distinguish column classes.

## 2. The Mirror Symmetry as Falsification Engine

The most powerful structural prediction of the Rosetta is not "7 levels." It is **mirror symmetry around L4**:

- L1 ↔ L7 (boundary pressure ↔ boundary closure)
- L2 ↔ L6 (first social contract ↔ renunciation of contract)
- L3 ↔ L5 (productive building ↔ contemplative holding)

This symmetry is **not optional**. It is a geometric necessity on S²: if φ = cot(θ/2) and ν = tan(θ/2), then for every θ there exists a complementary angle that gives the reciprocal coordinate pair.

**Falsification protocol:** For any NEW domain column, predict the mirror pairs before looking at the data. If the domain has a 7-stage structure but L2 and L6 are NOT functional mirrors, either:
- The domain doesn't fit (column rejected)
- The geometry is wrong (framework wounded)
- The mapping is forced (cell rejected)

**Example:** In the initiatory ascents (V-bis), does the Mithraic "Nymphus (Bridegroom)" at L2 mirror "Heliodromus (Sun-Runner)" at L6? L2 is lunar, receptive, first union. L6 is solar, active, running toward the source. The mirror is there but it's **interpretive** — not obvious without framework glasses.

**Harder example:** In neuroscience, does "limbic / alpha / social-emotional" at L2 mirror "DMN / theta / witness" at L6? The framework says yes. But DMN is bilateral, not purely "right-tendency." The neuroscience column already had a correction (2026-04-04) that hemisphere = tendency, not identity. The mirror is suggestive but not clean.

## 3. The L4 Center Problem

Every column claims to place a "center" at L4. But what makes something an L4?

The framework's functional definition: L4 is where **integrated agency at maximum balance** occurs. But this is a framework-imposed reading, not a domain-native category.

| Domain | L4 Claim | Domain-Native Reading | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Psychology | Post-conventional / self-actualization | Kohlberg's Stage 5-6 is "social contract / universal ethical principles" | The domain doesn't naturally distinguish L4 as "center" — it distinguishes pre-conventional→conventional→post-conventional as a progression |
| Politics | Timocracy | Plato's timocracy is the SECOND-BEST regime, not a center | Plato's center is Aristocracy (L5). The framework inverts Plato to make L4 central |
| Yoga | Karma / Anāhata | Anāhata IS the heart center in chakra systems — this is domain-native | Strong fit |
| Mythology | Thor / Ares-Athena | Thor is a major god but not "central" in Norse system architecture | Interpretive |
| Computation | Gradient descent | GD is an algorithm, not a "center" of computation | Weak — selected to fit |
| Initiatory | Leo / Tiferet / Mutma'inna | Tiferet IS the central sefirah on the Tree of Life | Strong fit |
| Game Theory | Pareto optimal | Pareto optimality is a solution concept, not a "center" of strategy | Structural but not native |

**The pressure point:** The strongest L4 fits (Anāhata, Tiferet) come from traditions that **already have a center concept**. The weakest fits come from domains without native centering (computation, game theory, politics). This suggests that L4-as-center is **real in center-oriented traditions** but **projected onto non-centered domains**.

**Implication:** The Rosetta's L4 claim is strongest where the domain already has an L4. It is weakest where the domain is linear or acyclic. This is selection bias, not convergence.

The Rosetta is ambitious but incomplete. Conspicuous absences:

- L3: Market exchange / price mechanism
- L4: Pareto-efficient allocation / mechanism design
- L5: Planned economy / central optimization
- L6: Commons-based peer production / post-scarcity
- L7: ?

This column would test whether economic organization follows the 7-pattern. But note: it's dangerously close to "derived."

**B. Biology (embryonic development)**
- Zygote→morula→blastula→gastrula→neurula→organogenesis→fetus
- 7 stages, but the functional mapping to operators is unclear

**C. Chemistry (periodic table / electron shells)**
- s, p, d, f orbitals don't map to 7
- But **periods** 1-7 do map to 7 rows
- Period 4 (transition metals) is the "center" in terms of chemical complexity

**D. Music theory (scales, harmonic series)**
- 7 notes in the diatonic scale
- 4th / 5th as "perfect" intervals (center?)
- Highly speculative but independently structured

**E. Architecture / design (pattern languages)**
- Christopher Alexander's "A Pattern Language" has 253 patterns but no obvious 7-level structure

**F. Linguistics (beyond PIE)**
- Semitic root pattern evolution

**The pressure point:** If the Rosetta is a "calibration table," it should be tested against domains that were NOT selected because they fit. The current columns were mostly chosen because they already have 7-ish stages. This is the **file-drawer problem** — we don't see the domains that were tried and failed to fit.

**Proposed column (honest):** `00_SELECTION_METHODOLOGY.md` already mentions this. But the Rosetta should have a **FAILED MAPPINGS** appendix — domains that were examined and did NOT fit the 7-pattern. This would strengthen the honest position dramatically.

**Can this be turned into a falsifiable prediction?**

Test: Examine East Asian initiatory systems (Daoist 內丹, Buddhist 10 bhūmis compressed to 7). Does the 4-3 split appear? If yes, the prediction strengthens. If no, the split may be Indo-European artifact.

## 6. Row Compression: Are There Really 7?

The framework is honest: "The number 7 is a design choice." But we can ask: what is the **minimal** row set?

| Compressed | Rows | Geometry |
|---|---|---|
| **3 rows** | Boundary (L0/L∞), Equator (L4), Interior (L2-L3-L5-L6) | Too coarse |
| **5 rows** | L1 (demon), L2/L6 (half-balance), L3/L5 (high-balance), L4 (equator), L7 (seer) | Functional compression |
| **7 rows** | Full resolution | Current choice |
| **9 rows** | Including L0 and L∞ as distinct | Mathematical completion |

**Observation:** Many domain columns naturally have 5 stages, not 7. Plato has 5 regimes. Piaget has 4 stages (with substages). Maslow has 5 needs (with self-actualization+). The "7" often comes from splitting the top and bottom.

**Pressure point:** If many domains naturally have 5 stages, and the framework forces them to 7 by splitting L2/L6 and L3/L5, then the convergence is partially artifactual.

**Counter:** The mirror symmetry demands that if L3 exists, L5 exists as its mirror. If the domain only has "productive building" (L3) and no "contemplative holding" (L5), the domain may be incomplete, not the framework. But this is a framework-favorable reading.

**Pressure point:** This is a **derived column**, not an empirical one. The DAC was designed using the Rosetta as a template. Of course it fits — it was built to fit.

**But:** If the DAC functions well as an organization, it provides **pragmatic validation** — the Rosetta is not just descriptive but **generative**. A generative framework that produces working structures is stronger than a descriptive framework that only matches existing structures.

**Test:** Does the DAC's L4 (uplink / compressed routing) actually function as an equatorial balance between L3 (body/production) and L5 (public/contemplation)? This is a live empirical question about the organism, not a corpus question.
