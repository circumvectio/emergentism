# 07 (TYSON/KO) — The boundary the surveys can't see

> **Origin.** Owner statement, 2026-07-13: *"Our conjecture says that like Tyson in his prime V and PHI getting punched in the face foes KO because he looses conciousness to zero."* This doc stages the conjecture formally, with explicit scope, kill criteria, and the falsifier that any test would have to clear.
>
> **Why this is staged now.** The GFS study (Wave 1 April 2026, Wave 2 AND-class July 2026) was the framework's third-person test of the multiplicative form. The owner retracted it 2026-07-13 because GFS measured the wrong register: it tested whether flourishing survey aggregates fit a multiplicative model — but the framework's load-bearing multiplicative claim is **the Zero-Factor Catastrophe at the boundary** (P = Φ × V → 0 when V → 0), which a population survey cannot measure. Without a boundary test, the multiplicative wager is empirically adrift. This doc stages the boundary test as the correct empirical target.

## 0. The conjecture, stated

**Interior (Tyson in his prime):** Under the agency gloss (D5 Register, 2026-06-12), `φ = worldline-foresight / knowing-how` and `ν = means-at-the-boundary (body, tool, energy, access, execution)`. In the normal operating range, the framework's multiplicative form `P = Φ × V` (action-capacity, task output) holds, with both Φ and V active. The product is bounded above by the smaller of the two (when one saturates), and the system behaves as if `φ·ν = 1` after normalization (the chart tautology). The agent at this point is *Tyson in his prime* — Φ and V are both dominant, the product carries the agency register, the system nearly-unbeatable in its normal range.

**Boundary (the punch):** When V → 0 — the punch lands, the body fails, the tool breaks, the access is removed, the execution collapses — `P = Φ × V → 0` *regardless of Φ*. The plan (Φ) can be intact; the agent still knows what to do, still has the foresight. The product is annihilated because the *and* is a hard and, not a soft and. Φ·0 = 0. KO.

> "Everyone has a plan until they get punched." — popular gloss, accurate.

**The conjecture, in one sentence:** *The multiplicative form P = Φ × V is empirically tested at the V=0 boundary, not at the interior; the boundary behavior is total collapse to zero, not gradual degradation.*

## 1. Why the boundary, not the interior

Three reasons the boundary is the right test:

1. **The zero-factor catastrophe is the only place the multiplicative form makes a *unique* prediction.** The interior (Φ, V both positive) is dominated by `min(Φ, V)` (Liebig/O-ring), Cobb-Douglas `Φ^a · V^b`, and CES — all of which collapse alike at the boundary. The zero-factor law selects a *whole AND-class* of forms, not the product. **The product's distinguishing test is what happens at the boundary, not in the interior.** A flourishing survey sees only the interior. A knockout sees the boundary.

2. **The agency register (D5) was always about action-capacity under failure, not welfare under success.** The framework's agency gloss maps φ/ν to *foresight/means*, not *meaning/health*. A wellbeing-survey operationalization (Wave 1's GFS mapping: φ → meaning items, ν → health items, outcome → life-satisfaction) was a category error from the start. The right test asks: when the means collapse, does the action-capacity collapse with it, regardless of the foresight?

3. **The Tyson image encodes a non-trivial claim about coupling.** In the interior, Φ and V *complement* (a chainsaw is higher V but demands more Φ — Φ gates usable V). At the boundary, the complementarity doesn't soften the *and*; it hardens it. The complementarity is a *threshold/min* structure; the catastrophe is a *product/zero-factor* structure. Both can be true. The boundary is the one place the product's signature is unique.

## 2. The falsifier (K-T1)

A test of the Tyson/KO conjecture must answer: **does a controlled V → 0 event produce P → 0 with Φ held non-zero, faster or more completely than the additive or min-Liebig models predict?**

**Pre-registered kill criterion (K-T1):** If, in a controlled V → 0 event (e.g., sudden loss of access, body failure, tool break, with Φ held non-zero by experimental design), the observed `P` is better predicted by `P = Φ + V - |Φ - V|` (Liebig/min), `P = Φ^a · V^b` (Cobb-Douglas) with a > 0 and b > 0, or any additive form, than by `P = Φ × V` evaluated at the boundary, **the conjecture loses its empirical signature and the AND-class ambiguity returns.**

**Differential diagnostic (what separates the product from min and from Cobb-Douglas at the boundary):**
- Product: P → 0 *asymptotically fast* (linear in V near zero, multiplied by Φ).
- Cobb-Douglas with a, b < 1: P → 0 *with sub-linear tail* (V^b with b<1 means small V still produces fractional P).
- Min / Liebig: P → 0 *linearly with V*, regardless of Φ (no multiplicative amplification).
- Additive: P → Φ (the foresight is the only thing that survives).

**The product's signature is: P → 0 at a rate strictly faster than min, strictly slower than additive, with the rate scaling with Φ.** If the rate does not scale with Φ (i.e., a small-Φ agent and a large-Φ agent collapse at the same rate when V → 0), the product's signature is absent.

**Important boundary condition:** Φ must be *measured* to be non-zero, not assumed. If the punch also collapses Φ (the agent *loses* their plan), the test is degenerate. The controlled design must include a Φ-measurement pre- and post-event to confirm Φ is held non-zero across the V → 0 transition.

## 3. What this conjecture is NOT claiming

- **Not claiming** Φ·ν = 1 in the boundary regime. The chart tautology holds in the interior (after normalization); the boundary is a different regime and the product form `P = Φ × V` evaluated at the boundary returns 0 for any Φ when V = 0. The identity *is* the catastrophe; the conjecture is about which test the identity faces.
- **Not claiming** the product is universally the right model. The Zero-Factor Catastrophe selects a *whole AND-class* (min, CES, Cobb-Douglas, product, geometric mean) that all collapse alike at the boundary. The product's distinguishing test is the *rate* of collapse with Φ held non-zero, not the *fact* of collapse.
- **Not claiming** consciousness → 0 is the only V → 0 path. The conjecture generalizes: any *V → 0* (loss of means, body failure, tool break, access removed) triggers the catastrophe. Consciousness is one V; the framework's agency gloss includes body, tool, energy, access, execution as V-candidates.
- **Not claiming** the GFS result was wrong because the data was bad. The GFS data was real; the test was mis-targeted (interior, not boundary). See `90_ARCHIVE/2026_07_13_gfs_retraction/` and receipt 119 §"The GFS correction".
