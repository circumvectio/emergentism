## 2 · What the catalog does NOT cover

The catalog is *load-bearing dead forms*, not *every strong-form claim*. Three categories of strong-form claims are intentionally excluded:

### 2.1 · Truly dead with no rescue (3 rows)

Per L2's false-coherence check, these three forms are dead at the strong form and have no useful narrower reading that survives:

- **#7 (the squid)** — false-to-fact, no biological survivor; η=0 gate lives via A4-1 (Model A), not via any animal witness
- **#17 (civilizational physics)** — the analogy is fine at `[C]`, but "physics" tier is gone; rescuing "civilizational `[C]` analogy" would dilute the halāhala
- **#18 (dissolves Hard Problem)** — the lens survives, but the lens does not claim to dissolve; the dissolution-claim is the whole dead form

### 2.2 · Borderline synthetic-gap items (3)

L1 flagged these as potential additions, but the catalog excludes them on the principle that a "dead list" is for *proof-claim retractions*, not for *strong-form-killed identifications*:

- **C5/C22/C24 (model → ethics readings)** — strong form "formal model IS ethical content" is dead at `[I]`-permanent, but it's a synthetic-gap wager, not a proof-claim retraction
- **TF-2/7/8 (self-dual point IS moral midpoint)** — the math (`+1` fixed point) survives; the *identification* is `[I]`-permanent
- **A1-C7 (`{0,1,∞} → S²` presupposes the continuum)** — borderline; subsumed under row 9 (Forced Titan-3) if C21 is read broadly

These three are noted but not in the 22. They live in receipt 126 §3 as `[I]`-permanent wagers, where the strong form is already named and the standing rule applies (no upgrade above `[I]`).

### 2.3 · Out of scope (load-bearing items from the un-enumerated 52 BROKEN)

The worksheet states the 52 "follow the same patterns (mostly 2A overclaim)" and the 14 enumerated rows are exemplars. The un-named 52 are sub-bullets of named categories (DSIC iff, equator-as-attractor, Green-Laffont misapplication, A3-5b/5c, etc.) — implicitly covered by the 2A/2B patterns. They are not in this catalog because they are not load-bearing enough to name individually; the audit's run `journal.jsonl` carries the per-claim verdicts.
