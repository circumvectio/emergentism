# T-N2 Transfer-Test — Theology-Side Signature Envelope

> **The one line.** The claim "output peaks at Φ=ν and declines with imbalance"
> (`B = sin θ`; the equator-optimum) is a **theorem of a class, not a universal
> law** — it binds exactly the finite systems that are conservation-coupled,
> complementary, and symmetrically γ-priced in excess. The ruling below decides
> whether the framework may say so as conditional canon, must keep holding, or
> must strike equator-optimum language to the boundary everywhere.

## 1. The question for signature

May the framework speak of a **balance-optimum at the equator** — as a
*conditional* theorem with its class test attached — or may every surface speak
**only of the zero-factor boundary** (imbalance kills the dyad) and never of an
optimum?

Formally: the **T-N2 transfer test** — the instrument that decides whether a
given finite system `P_node = Φ × V` sits on the Burrisphere (pays the
γ-price) and therefore inherits the sphere's equator-optimum — is staged
upstream as **[D] PENDING-K2** (2026-07-03,
[`05_COSMOLOGY/00_THE_BALANCE_OPTIMUM_IS_CONDITIONAL.md`](../05_COSMOLOGY/00_THE_BALANCE_OPTIMUM_IS_CONDITIONAL.md);
lens status row, TITAN ledger: *"T-N2 — equator-telos binds only
conservation-coupled, complementary, γ-symmetric nodes"*). K2 now disposes of
it. Per the 2026-07-19 disposition packet (F9), this is the **upstream call**:
it gates F10 (P-score sphere/band/kill-clock), F19/CIRCLE placement tags,
EVONET R4, and SPECTRE ④.

## 2. The theorem's exact class boundary

The equator-optimum is a theorem of the **class of systems satisfying all four
conditions**; drop any one and the claim either weakens, tilts, or inverts
`[S]`:

| # | Condition (the probe) | What it buys | If it fails |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(i)** | **Conservation-coupled** — finite budget; a `γ`-like price is *really paid* for imbalance (`φ·ν = 1` as the premise of sphere membership) | AM-GM downslope: excess of one factor *is* the deficit of the other `[A]` | Free surplus → **plateau** (imbalance costs nothing) |
| **(ii)** | **Complementary** — the task needs both factors *jointly*; they do not substitute | Imbalance starves the scarce factor | Substitutability → ratio barely matters |
| **(iii-a)** | **Costly excess (friction)** — unmatched capacity becomes clutter/maintenance/paralysis, not inert store | Surplus turns from asset to burden | Storable excess → no downslope |
| **(iii-b)** | **Symmetric γ-price** — the imbalance metric is the even ratio-metric `γ = (φ+ν)/2` (payoff `B = 2/(φ+ν)`), penalizing over-Φ and over-ν one-for-one | The optimum sits **at** `φ = ν` — *the equator specifically* | Asymmetric cost → **tilted optimum** (Cobb-Douglas `a/b`, off-equator); loose-Leontief → plateau |

**The seam is (iii-b).** `(i)+(ii)+(iii-a)` are necessary but not sufficient for
the framework's *specific* claim; they deliver at most "*an* optimum exists
somewhere" — and the asymmetric Munnell fit (`a = 0.61 ≠ b = 0.44`) shows that
when one exists off the sphere's premise, it is off-equator. Only the symmetric
γ-price pins the peak to the equator `[S]` (folded in from the propagation-packet
adversarial pass, 2026-07-03).

**On the sphere itself the theorem is unconditional `[A]`:** given `φ·ν = 1`,
`B = sin θ = 2/(φ+ν)` maxes at `φ = ν` by AM-GM; `γ = (φ+ν)/2` prices imbalance
monotonically. The conditionality is **entirely in the transfer** — the step
from `P∞` (sphere identity) to `P_node` (an organism, task, economy). A finite
system inherits the equator-optimum **iff it actually satisfies the sphere's
premise**: conditions (i)–(iii-b). The negation class is witnessed empirically:
unconstrained, substitutable, free-surplus aggregate production prices imbalance
*negatively* — specialization pays; balance shows a **trough** (d² > 0), not a
peak ([VERDICT_BALANCE.md](../11_UPLINK/25_EXPERIMENTS/2026-07-02_production_function_form/VERDICT_BALANCE.md)).

The honest form, as staged: *"For a finite agent under a binding budget,
performing a task whose factors are complementary and whose excess is costly
and symmetrically priced, the payoff is maximized at balance and declines with
imbalance. The equator is the optimum of the constrained mortal, not of the
cosmos."* `[S]` conditional; `[A]` the geometric core; `[I]` the domain mapping.

## 3. Options

**(a) Ratify as conditional canon `[S]`.** The transfer test becomes the
standing gate instrument (disposition packet F9-A). The 2026-07-03 conditional
document and the Burrisphere scope clause promote from `[D]` to conditional
canon: any surface may speak of the equator-optimum **only with the class test
attached**; unscoped universalizations ("balance is optimal, everywhere") remain
retracted. Downstream riders execute as scoped repairs (§4). Nothing in the
negation class is touched — aggregate specialization doctrine stands.

**(b) Hold `[D]`.** No ruling. All current fences stay exactly as they are:
the instrument remains PENDING-K2; AGENTZ R4's two-factor lint stays
boundary-only; every downstream rider stays parked; DHARMA_YUDDHA:108–124 and
kindred surfaces keep their missing-banner state. Cost: F10, F19 placement
tags, EVONET R4, and SPECTRE ④ remain gated indefinitely.

**(c) Reject.** Strike equator-optimum language to `[I]`/boundary-only
everywhere (disposition packet F9-B). Surfaces may assert only the zero-factor
boundary (imbalance zeroes the dyad); "optimum at the equator" survives solely
as model-internal on-sphere geometry `[A]` with **no normative transfer** to
any finite system. SPECTRE/EVONET/CIRCLE/REFU/ARCH riders close as
strike-to-`[I]` repairs; the holobiont action test loses its equator anchor and
reverts to boundary-only form.

## 5. Kill criteria

The staged theorem's own three kill criteria carry with the canon if ratified
`[S]`:

1. Exhibit a **finite, budget-bound, complementary-task** agent for whom
   **imbalance outperforms balance**, with no specialization/depth reward and
   no free surplus → the scoped downslope dies.
2. Exhibit an **unconstrained, substitutable** system where **balance strictly
   beats specialization** → the "specialization wins when conditions fail"
   clause dies.
3. Show that `φ·ν = 1` conservation does **not** license AM-GM (e.g. the
   factors are not positive reals) → the geometric core dies.

*Envelope-added (derived, `[S]`):* exhibit a system satisfying **all of
(i)–(iii-b)** whose payoff peaks **off** the equator → the class theorem dies
outright, since (iii-b) plus AM-GM forces the equator peak.
