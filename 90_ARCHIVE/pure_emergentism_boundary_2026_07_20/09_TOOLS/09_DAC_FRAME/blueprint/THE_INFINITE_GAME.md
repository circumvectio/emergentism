**Purpose**: To align the DAC's aggressive strategies with long-term survival.
**Concept**: "Finite players play to win. Infinite players play to keep playing." (Carse/Sinek).

**Rosetta boundary:** [I] This paper defines long-horizon doctrine. [D]

## 1. The Objective
- **Finite Game**: Defeating a competitor, shipping a feature, hitting a quarterly Revenue goal.
- [I] **Infinite Game**: **Survival**. The DAC must exist tomorrow, next year, and next decade.
- [I] **Rule**: Never sacrifice the Existential (Survival) for the Finite (Profit).

## 2. Apex Optimization
- Being the "Apex Predator" is dangerously finite. Predators starve if they over-hunt.
- We aim for **Apex Adaptability**.
    - If the environment changes, we mutate (Spinoff/Merge).
    - If a technology dies, we absorb the new one (Red Queen).

## 3. Resilience vs Efficiency
- **Efficiency**: Doing X fast/cheap. (Often fragile).
- **Resilience**: Being able to survive shock. (Often redundant).
- **Doctrine**: We pay the "Resilience Tax". We keep `_DNA.md` seeds everywhere. We maintain redundant capabilities.

## 4. The Horizon
- [D] We aggressively dominate niches (Bio-Econ) to secure resources for the Long Game.
- We use Game Theory to avoid "Pyrrhic Victories" (Winning the battle but losing the war).
