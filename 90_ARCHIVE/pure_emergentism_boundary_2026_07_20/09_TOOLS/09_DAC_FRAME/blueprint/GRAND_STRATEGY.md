## 0) Strategy ≠ Goals ≠ Tactics
- **Goal**: what we want.
- **Strategy**: how we win (a theory of advantage under constraints).
- **Tactics**: local moves that implement strategy.

This document is strategy. Tactics belong in playbooks and campaigns.

**Rosetta boundary:** [I] This paper is a strategy reference. It does not prove
current product position, market outcomes, validator coverage, CI behavior, or
campaign completion without current execution receipts.

## 1) Winning Diagnosis (what game are we in?)
[D] The agentic tooling ecosystem is fragmented and failure-prone because:
- Many systems optimize for demos (high narrative Φ) while breaking in reality (low V).
- Other systems add features (high surface V) without a coherent epistemic spine (low Φ).

**Primary competitor class:** [D] epistemically undisciplined frameworks.

**User pain:** “It works once, then it lies / drifts / breaks; I can’t trust it.”

---

## 2) Guiding Policy (how do we win?)
[I] We win by becoming the **epistemic operating system** for agentic projects:
1) **Contracts first**: declare interfaces, invariants, and required artifacts.
2) **Enforcement**: automated validation of structure, links, and drift.
3) **Evidence**: doctrine must be backed by executed campaigns with artifacts.

[I] **Competitive advantage** is not “more features.” It is:
- lower decision latency *without* hallucinated confidence,
- reproducibility across context resets,
- auditable traces from intent → action → artifact.

---

## 3) Coherent Actions (what must we build/do?)
### A) Standardize doctrine (reduce ambiguity)
- [I] Every Blueprint doc must have Holographic Briefing metadata.
- Central epistemic doctrines (Reasoning Rosetta, Mission Command, etc.) are “highest standard.”

### B) Define contracts (schemas)
Create and adopt schemas for:
- RULING (decision record)
- INTER_AGENT_TREATY (service/interface contract)
- RUNBOOK (how to operate)
- POSTMORTEM (how we learn)

### C) Build enforcement (validation + CI)
- Validation scripts that check:
  - required artifacts exist
  - required headers exist
  - links resolve
  - naming and uniqueness constraints
- [B] CI runs validators on every change.

### D) Prove doctrine via one real campaign
- Execute at least one campaign end-to-end with:
  - intent
  - F3EAD trace
  - artifacts
  - postmortem

---

## 4) Metrics (how we know it’s working)
- **Coherence (Φ)**: fewer contradictions, fewer “unclear ownership” zones.
- **Viability (V)**: successful runs reproducible from a cold start.
- **Auditability**: changes trace to intents, rulings, and artifacts.
