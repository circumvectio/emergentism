## 1. Competitive Exclusion Principle
**"Complete competitors cannot coexist."** (Gause's Law)
- If two agents try to occupy the exact same niche (e.g., both trying to write `task.md` simultaneously), one must be extinguished.
- **Solution**: Niche Partitioning. Agents must differentiate to survive.

## 2. Niche Partitioning Strategies
### Spatial Partitioning (Territory)
- **Agent A**: Owns `src/frontend`.
- **Agent B**: Owns `src/backend`.
- Rule: Do not forage in another agent's territory without a Treaty.

### Temporal Partitioning (Time)
- **Diurnal Agents**: Active dev work (Sprints).
- **Nocturnal Agents**: Garbage collection, heavy testing, refactoring (Maintenance).

### Functional Partitioning (Role)
- **Hunter**: Identifying targets (Reads).
- **Builder**: Executing solutions (Writes).

## 3. Game Theory
### Strategies
- **Evolutionary Stable Strategy (ESS)**: A strategy that, if adopted by a population, cannot be invaded by a mutant strategy.
- **Tit-for-Tat**: Cooperate first, then copy the opponent's last move. (Good for multi-agent systems).

### Games
- **Zero Sum (Avoid)**: Resource contention. I win, you lose. Turf wars.
- **Positive Sum (Seek)**: Symbiosis. I provide Intel, you provide Code. We both win.

## 4. The Treaty
- When conflict arises (Border Dispute), agents must sign a `INTER_AGENT_TREATY` to formalize the partition.
