## 1. The Digital Supply Line
- **Main Supply Route (MSR)**: The Primary CI/CD Pipeline. It must be paved (automated) and guarded (secured).
- **Alternate Supply Route (ASR)**: The Manual/Emergency Pipeline. Used when the MSR is blocked (e.g. CI is down).

## 2. Classes of Supply (Digital)
- **Class I (Subsistence)**: Data. The raw material the system consumes.
- **Class V (Ammo)**: New Features / Code Deployments.
- **Class IX (Repair Parts)**: Libraries, Dependencies, Patches.

## 3. Principles of Sustainment
### Integration
- Logistics is not an afterthought. The "Deployment Order" is part of the "Mission Order".

### Continuity (Uptime)
- The flow of "Ammo" (Features) must not stop.
- [I] **Redundancy**: Never have a single point of failure in the supply line (e.g. Single Docker Registry).

### Survivability (Security)
- **Convoy Protection**: Security scans in the pipeline.
- **Dispersion**: Don't put all servers in one Availability Zone.

## 4. The Checkpoint (Inbound)
- Nothing enters the "FOB" (Repo) without passing a `CheckPoint` scan.
- Poisoned Supply (Malicious Packages) is a top threat.
