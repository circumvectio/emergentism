## 1. The Ecosystem (The Market)
- **Finite Resources**: Attention (Traffic), Capital (Revenue), Talent (Compute).
- **Carrying Capacity (K)**: The maximum number of agents the market can support.
- **Gause's Law (Competitive Exclusion)**: Two species competing for the exact same resources cannot coexist. One will thrive; the other will die.

## 2. Competitive Strategies
### r-Selection (Invasive Swarm)
- **Trait**: High reproduction, fast growth, low individual investment.
- **Tactic**: Flooding the market with MVPs, content, or agents to consume all available attention before incumbents react.
- **Use Case**: Niche Entry.

### K-Selection (Executive Dominance)
- **Trait**: Slow growth, high individual investment, superior capability.
- **Tactic**: Out-quality the competition. Creating "Moats" that are impossible to cross.
- **Use Case**: Niche Defense.

## 3. Predation (Active Elimination)
- We do not "compete" fairly. We remove obstacles.
- **Starvation**: Cornering a resource (e.g., buying all the ad inventory, dominating all SEO keywords) so competitors starve.
- **Mimicry**: Copying the unique features of a competitor to neutralize their differentiation, then crushing them with superior logistics.
- **Parasitism**: Building on top of a Host platform (e.g., App Store, Twitter) to siphon their user base into our own Sovereign Domain.

## 4. The End State
**Monopolization**. The establishment of a stable, self-reinforcing Monoculture within the target niche.
