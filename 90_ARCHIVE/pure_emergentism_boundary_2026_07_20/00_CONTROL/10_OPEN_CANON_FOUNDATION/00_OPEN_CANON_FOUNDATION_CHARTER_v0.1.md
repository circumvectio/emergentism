## §1 · Purpose

The Open Canon Foundation exists to hold, in perpetuity, the public-benefit artifacts of the Emergentism corpus:

1. **The canon** — the open-source framework, its axioms, the synthetic gap doctrine, the 4-posture taxonomy, the strategy for the spread, the fables, the trial record, the practice curriculum.
2. **The trial record** — the permanent, public, versioned record of every line of the canon that has been attacked by an adversary and what survived or fell. The record is the receipt of A7 self-correction.
3. **The practice curriculum** — the sitting (Pratyakṣa), the personal trial record (№017), the dyadic check. Transmissible in an afternoon; valuable independently of every metaphysical claim.
4. **The constitutional red team** — an annual adversarial audit of the canon, funded by the foundation. The trial record is the output.
5. **The trademark** — the name and mark of the framework, held for the public benefit, defended against appropriation, licensed to the commercial organs under the published membrane (§5).

The foundation's `η=0` is not a slogan; it is the constitutional sentence. The foundation **cannot profit**. Any surplus above operating costs is rolled back into the public-benefit mission (the red team, the practice curriculum, the trial record, the open-source infrastructure). This is the same sentence that the legal non-inurement requirement enforces natively; the ethic and the law are the same constraint, which makes compliance a constitutional matter, not a performed one.

The foundation's mission is **not** to spread the framework. Spreading is the doctrine of §1 in the strategy (users, not believers); the foundation's role is to *hold the public-benefit artifacts* that make spreading possible without conversion.
