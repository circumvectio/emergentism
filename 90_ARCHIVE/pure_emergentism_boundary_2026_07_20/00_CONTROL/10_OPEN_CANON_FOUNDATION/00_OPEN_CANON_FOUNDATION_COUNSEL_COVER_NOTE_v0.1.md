Dear *[counsel name]*,

**Context.** A small group has produced an open-source philosophical/educational corpus (the "canon"), a public audit ledger (the "trial record"), and a teachable practice (a sitting practice, a personal error-ledger practice, a decision heuristic). They wish to place the canon, its trademark, and the funding of ongoing independent audits into a Swiss public-benefit entity. Several commercial companies, separately owned and separately governed, build products informed by the corpus and would pay published license fees to the entity. The entity must never be classifiable as a religious community, must qualify for tax exemption on the education/research track, and must be constitutionally incapable of channeling value to its founders or to the commercial companies.

**Attachments.** Two documents are attached to make a first consultation maximally concrete:

1. **Charter draft** (`00_OPEN_CANON_FOUNDATION_CHARTER_DRAFT_v0.1.md`) — the intended entity in draft-statute form: purposes, non-inurement clause, the membrane separating the entity from the commercial companies, immune-system clauses (forkability of the corpus, a locked audit budget, a standing public-ledger rule), governance with enumerated signature acts, and dissolution.
2. **Questions memo** (`00_OPEN_CANON_FOUNDATION_COUNSEL_QUESTIONS_v0.1.md`) — the specific questions we ask you to answer, organized Q1–Q10: vehicle choice (Verein vs Stiftung), Gemeinnützigkeit qualification, trademark holding, the membrane and related-party flows, the religious-activity boundary, non-inurement mechanics, statute drafting exclusions, dissolution, cross-border, and engagement logistics.

**The ask.** We ask for:

1. **A vehicle recommendation** — Verein (ZGB 60 ff.) or Stiftung (ZGB 80 ff.) for this purpose, with reasoning (the questions memo, Q1, stages the trade-offs we see).
2. **An estimate for obtaining an exemption pre-ruling** — a preliminary ruling / advance confirmation from the tax authority on the exemption and on the license-flow treatment before formation, including expected cost and timeline (questions memo, Q10.2).

**The boundary — what we are not asking.** We are **not** seeking legal advice on the philosophy, the corpus, or the practice, and we do not ask you to validate or endorse them. The engagement is scoped to **the vehicle, the exemption, the membrane, and the religious-activity boundary** — nothing else. Securities, tokens, and financial regulation are out of scope; the commercial companies carry their own counsel.

We would be glad to provide any further documents you need (corpus sample, audit-ledger sample, license-fee schedule draft, founder/related-party map — Q10.3 lists what we can prepare). Please let us know your availability for an initial consultation.

With thanks,

*[signature — K2 to sign]*
