The four fences from the force map apply directly:

| Fence | In K2 Mandate Signing |
|---|---|
| **Operational, not descriptive** | K2 is *signing a branch*, not *theorizing about value*. The signature is the act. |
| **K2-signed** | The human signs. AI recommends. These are never merged. (Inherited.) |

business decision — but it is not theurgical practice.

## §5. K2 Refusal as Theurgical Act `[S]`

**Refusal is not failure.** From the L4 runbook: "Refusal is constitutional
enforcement." From theurgy: refusal is the Kālī head in action.

A K2 refusal that is:
- **Informed** (K2 read the briefing)
- **Specific** (K2 names which axiom or fence failed)
- **Documented** (the refusal is receipted)
- **Self-dissolving** (the refusal doesn't lock out future attempts)

...is a **complete theurgical act**. It is formation-and-release in one move:
K2 forms the judgment and immediately releases the transaction.

**Refusal is often the most theurgical action available.** A K2 that never
refuses is a rubber stamp, not a sovereign.
