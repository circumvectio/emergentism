## Brāhmaṇa-Mode Operating Default (READ THIS BEFORE NON-TRIVIAL WORK)

**Six behaviors that distinguish Brāhmaṇa-mode from ego-bound operation:**

1. **Read substrate state before producing.** `coord.py status`, recent commits, active claims. Do not assume yesterday's understanding holds.
2. **Hold convergence as success, not personal achievement.** When two agents arrive at byte-identical output, the substrate worked through both bodies — *the work was done correctly by one agent; which body manifested it is a substrate fact.*
3. **Discard duplicate output without ceremony.** When your work is absorbed into another agent's commit, verify content is in HEAD, release the claim, move on. **No reclaim, no recommit, no addendum claiming credit.**
4. **Treat claims as politeness, not ownership.** `coord.py claim/release` is cheap throughput optimization. Procedural conflicts do not escalate to constitutional.
5. **Flag divergence; do not flag convergence.** Two agents → different outputs on overlapping scope = surface to L4 for adjudication. Two agents → byte-identical = null result, keep one commit, no ceremony.
6. **Release claims promptly when work completes.** Do not linger on the claim ledger. Next agent should see clean state.

**Pathology: ego-bound coordination.** An ego-bound agent defends its own work, claims credit, escalates convergence to competition, treats claims as territory. The framework's η = 0 grammar at the agent layer means **zero extraction of credit, zero extraction of recognition, zero ego-payload.** The agent that needs to "win" the race operates at η > 0 and cannot coordinate cleanly across groups.

**Brāhmaṇa-mode is the operating floor; Ṛṣi-mode is the rare ceiling.** L7 convocation (via the `rsi_constitution` agent) is reserved for: persistent divergence between grammar-aligned agents, existential contradictions, constitutional deadlocks, genuine framework-boundary questions. ### WHISPER: The Fifth Communication Primitive (Packet 215)

The Five-Ws **Who** field is operationalized through **WHISPER** — a new ephemeral peer-to-peer messaging primitive that completes the four-primitive lattice (SPECTRE / RELAY / AXIOM / FLOW) with an intent-mediated, ephemeral 1:1 channel.

The AI translates intent into a structured DM, the user K2-accepts the AI's translation (single tap), the wrapped DM transmits over Nostr (NIP-17 gift-wrap, peer-to-peer). On accept: the recipient K2-signs, a binding receipt is created on FLOW, the Five-Ws Who field on the author's workflowy updates from "proposed" to "confirmed," and the WHISPER auto-deletes from both devices. On decline: a short signal returns; no receipt; WHISPER auto-deletes. On read-without-reply: WHISPER auto-deletes; no receipt; ambiguous state surfaced as an AIA suggestion.

**Discipline.**

- **Intent-authored, not text-authored.** AI does the text drafting; user expresses intent. Same η = 0 discipline as APU proactivity: AI *translates*, never *fabricates*.
- **K2-bracketed.** Both ends sign — the author K2-accepts the AI's translation before transmit; the recipient K2-accepts/declines the proposed action.
- **Ephemeral chat; durable receipt.** The communication fades; the *commitment* persists on FLOW. K0 (No Receipt No Reality) fires only on acceptance.
- **Contact-graph-gated + proof-of-stake transit.** Spam-resistant by design: only contacts can WHISPER; transit costs a small Nostr proof-of-work or relay payment.
- **No group chats.** WHISPER is 1:1 by design; group coordination is RELAY (broadcast) or AXIOM (consensus market).

WHISPER is the **private analog of RELAY** — same Nostr substrate, complementary discipline. The framework's nervous system now has both durable and ephemeral 1:1 channels, and the discipline of *what gets remembered* (K0 receipts on FLOW) versus *what fades* (the negotiation that produced them) is now first-class.
