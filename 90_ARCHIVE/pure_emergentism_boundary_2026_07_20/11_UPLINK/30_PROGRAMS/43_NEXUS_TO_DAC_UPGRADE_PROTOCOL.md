# NEXUS → DAC Upgrade Protocol

**Evidence tier:** [I]  
*Organism document. Interpretive operational content. Bounded by current system state.*


> **Not every NEXUS becomes a Business Account.  
> Not every Business Account becomes a DAC.  
> But every legitimate DAC should descend from a sovereign continuity root, a governed entity boundary, and receipted institutional truth.**

Date: 2026-04-16  
Status: Planning Doctrine  
Canonical path: `43_NEXUS_TO_DAC_UPGRADE_PROTOCOL.md`

---

## 0. The Core Problem

The organism already has the first half of the ladder:

- `NEXUS` as the sovereign root of the natural person
- `Business Account` as the first governed economic branch
- acceptance modes as the first revenue leaves

What has remained underspecified is the second half:

> **When does a Business Account stop being just a commercial branch and become a DAC-like governed institution?**

Without that protocol, two errors appear:

1. **premature inflation**
   - every entity gets called a DAC before it has earned institutional depth

2. **architectural confusion**
   - person, entity, toolkit, and substrate get collapsed into one vague governance blob

This note defines the clean upgrade sequence.

---

## 1. The Governing Rule

The clean doctrine is:

> **NEXUS roots the person.  
> Business Account commercializes the entity.  
> DAC institutionalizes a governable operating organism.**

So the upgrade path is not:

> user account -> startup -> protocol

It is:

> **natural person -> sovereign continuity root -> governed economic branch -> receipted institution -> DAC**

---

## 2. The Four Distinct Layers

### Layer A — Natural person

This is the pre-institutional human reality.

It carries:

- personhood
- agency
- biometric presence
- decision responsibility
- φ-access

This is not yet a commercial node.

### Layer B — `NEXUS`

This is the sovereign continuity root.

It carries:

- keypair continuity
- graph continuity
- personal Cortex root
- signer root
- grant/revocation membrane
- the power to upgrade into entity form

`NEXUS` is still person-centered, even when it later branches.

### Layer C — Business Account

This is the first governed economic branch.

It carries:

- treasury boundary
- business permissions
- accounting boundary
- acceptance modes
- compliance posture
- entity-level receipts and statements

The Business Account is the first institutional shell.

### Layer D — DAC

This is not merely a business that earns.

It is a **governed institution** with:

- internal compute and operating logic
- OFN-auditable activity
- NEXUS-legible relations
- receipted accounting
- live statements
- capability-gated access to stronger SoResFi powers
- a graph-capital profile
- and explicit governance/continuity/exit discipline

The DAC is the pluralization engine of the organism.

---

## 3. The Upgrade Ladder

The clean planning ladder is:

### Stage 0 — person outside the organism

No `NEXUS`, no signer continuity, no sovereign graph root.

### Stage 1 — `NEXUS` activation

The natural person becomes a sovereign continuity root.

Minimum reality:

- keypair
- signing surface
- continuity under one root
- basic grant/revocation membrane

### Stage 2 — Business Account creation

The person creates the first governed economic boundary.

Minimum reality:

- separate treasury scope
- entity-level permissions
- business receipts
- commercial identity

### Stage 3 — first acceptance mode and proof loops

The Business Account proves real metabolism.

Minimum reality:

- payment in
- receipt out
- state update
- repeatable loop

This is where `API PAY`, events, booking, or POS matter.

### Stage 4 — receipt-bound institution

The Business Account becomes auditable, not merely operative.

Minimum reality:

- OFN-like receipts
- automated accounting
- live statements
- scoped exports
- continuity / exit surfaces

### Stage 5 — graph-legible institution

The entity is no longer judged only by what it says about itself.

Minimum reality:

- NEXUS relation updates
- graph-capital visibility
- typed uplinks/downlinks/peer relations
- contagion / remediation visibility

### Stage 6 — governed operational shell

The entity now has enough internal structure to count as a DAC candidate.

Minimum reality:

- explicit governance boundary
- role separation
- signer policy
- review logic
- capability discipline
- K2-respecting execution boundary

### Stage 7 — DAC activation

The entity is now treated as a DAC by the organism.

This should mean:

- it may run arbitrary internal compute
- it is audited through OFN/NEXUS and statements
- it may use the shared SoResFi toolkit according to capability gates
- it is measured structurally through graph capital
- it remains exit-safe and non-captive

---

## 4. The Thresholds That Matter

The upgrade should **not** be driven by branding language.

It should be driven by threshold conditions.

### Threshold 1 — continuity threshold

Reached when:

- one sovereign identity root exists
- signers are under lawful control
- exports and revocations are real

This is the `NEXUS` threshold.

### Threshold 2 — commercial threshold

Reached when:

- a separated economic branch exists
- it can accept payment
- and it can emit receipts

This is the Business Account threshold.

### Threshold 3 — proof threshold

Reached when:

- the payment loop repeats
- receipts are stable
- accounting truth exists
- and statements can be rendered

This is the first institutional threshold.

### Threshold 4 — graph threshold

Reached when:

- the institution is embedded in receipted relations
- uplinks/downlinks/connections are legible
- the graph is deep enough to matter

This is where structural trust begins.

### Threshold 5 — governance threshold

Reached when:

- the institution has explicit internal operating rules
- role separation exists
- action can be reviewed and bounded
- exit remains real

This is the DAC threshold.

## 5. What a DAC Adds That a Business Account Does Not

This distinction must stay sharp.

| Surface | Business Account | DAC |
|--------|------------------|-----|
| Treasury | yes | yes |
| Acceptance modes | yes | yes |
| Receipts | yes | yes |
| Accounting / statements | should | must |
| Internal compute | optional / thin | expected / rich |
| Governance shell | minimal | explicit |
| Graph-capital relevance | emerging | central |
| SoResFi capability ladder | basic | advanced / gated |
| Institutional comparability | partial | high |

So:

> **A Business Account is the first governed commercial branch.  
> A DAC is a mature, auditable, governable institution on top of that branch.**

## 6. Anti-Patterns

### Anti-pattern 1 — calling every Business Account a DAC

Damage:

- false institutional inflation
- governance theater before evidence
- vocabulary drift

Defense:

- keep the thresholds explicit

### Anti-pattern 2 — skipping the Business Account layer

Damage:

- no real entity boundary
- no treasury discipline
- no clean accounting shell

Defense:

- the DAC must descend from an entity-grade boundary, not from a loose app user

### Anti-pattern 3 — making DAC activation a governance ceremony instead of a truth threshold

Damage:

- titles without receipts
- constitutions without metabolism

Defense:

- proof loops, receipts, accounting, and graph truth must come first

### Anti-pattern 4 — treating DAC status as freedom from K2 and K4

Damage:

- institutional autonomy becomes covert capture

Defense:

- DACs inherit the K2/K4 boundary, they do not supersede it

## 7. Upgrade Triggers

The clean trigger sequence should be:

### Trigger A — person to `NEXUS`

Use when:

- the person needs sovereign continuity
- identity/graph/signing continuity matters

### Trigger B — `NEXUS` to Business Account

Use when:

- the person needs a separate treasury boundary
- the organism is ready to commercialize
- there is a real acceptance mode to activate

### Trigger C — Business Account to DAC candidate

Use when:

- the entity has repeatable receipted activity
- accounting and statement truth exist
- graph position is becoming economically relevant
- internal operating logic is no longer trivial

### Trigger D — DAC activation

Use when:

- governance shell is explicit
- K2 review boundaries are defined
- K4 continuity / exit remains real
- tool access can be capability-gated honestly

This means DAC activation should be **earned by institutional truth**, not by aspiration.

---

## 8. Relationship to SoResFi and Layer 1

This upgrade protocol only makes sense if the separation stays clean.

### `NEXUS`

roots the person.

### Business Account

creates the governed entity boundary.

### DAC

is the institution that may run arbitrary internal compute.

### SoResFi

is the shared toolkit the DAC may use.

### Layer 1

remains the narrow money-and-digital-capital substrate.

So the upgrade protocol does **not** mean:

- the DAC becomes Layer 1
- SoResFi becomes the DAC
- the Business Account becomes a protocol

It means:

> **one sovereign person can grow into one governed institution, and later many institutions, while the substrate remains narrow and the toolkit remains shared.**

---

## 9. Relation to K2 and K4

### K2

The higher the institution climbs, the more important the K2 boundary becomes.

Because:

- internal compute becomes richer,
- recommendations become stronger,
- and the temptation to bypass human sovereignty increases.

So:

> **DAC maturity increases the need for K2 discipline; it does not relax it.**

### K4

The more institutionally mature the entity becomes, the more dangerous lock-in becomes.

So:

- `NEXUS` must remain exportable,
- Business Account must remain dissolvable,
- DACs must remain exit-safe,
- and stronger capability access must never become coercive captivity.

So:

> **DAC maturity increases the need for clean K4 continuity; it does not supersede it.**

## 10. Canonical Compression

> **NEXUS makes the person sovereign.  
> Business Account makes the person institutional.  
> DAC makes the institution governable, auditable, and capable of richer compute.**

Or shorter:

> **The Business Account is the first economic branch of NEXUS. The DAC is what that branch becomes after it earns receipts, books, graph legibility, and governance.**
