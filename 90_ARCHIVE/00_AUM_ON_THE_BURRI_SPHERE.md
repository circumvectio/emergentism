## The Mandukya Upanishad

The Mandukya Upanishad is the shortest Upanishad — 12 verses. It is also the most complete. It describes **four states of consciousness**:

| State | Sanskrit | Symbol | Experience |
|-------|----------|--------|------------|
| **1. Waking** | Vaishvanara | **A** | Objective reality, external perception |
| **2. Dreaming** | Taijasa | **U** | Subjective experience, internal imagery |
| **3. Deep Sleep** | Prajna | **M** | Undifferentiated, no subject/object |
| **4. The Fourth** | Turīya | **─** (silence) | Consciousness itself, the witness |

The sound **AUM** traces three states. The **silence after AUM** is the fourth.

## The Three Witnesses

**Evidence Tier:** [I] Interpretive (structural isomorphism between the three Witnesses of the Rosetta Stone v10 and the three states of AUM)

The Rosetta Stone v10 identifies three Witnesses — three modes of observation that correspond to three geometric positions on the Burri Sphere. Each Witness IS the geometry it observes:

| Witness | Sanskrit | Glyph | AUM State | Geometric Position | Identity |
|---------|----------|-------|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Viśvarūpa** | विश्वरूप | ☀️ | **M** (Deep Sleep / Prājña) | **The Poles** | Viśvarūpa IS the poles — the cosmic form where differentiation collapses. From the pole, the entire field is seen as ONE. No subject, no object. The undifferentiated whole. |
| **Ananta Śeṣa** | अनन्तशेष | ♾ | **U** (Dreaming / Taijasa) | **The Sphere Surface** | Ananta Śeṣa IS the helix — the infinite serpent coiled around the sphere. Viṣṇu reclines on the serpent because the preserver rests ON the helical path. The coils of Śeṣa ARE the revolutions of the helix. |
| **Kāla** | काल | 🌑 | **A** (Waking / Vaishvānara) | **The Plane** | Kāla IS the plane — Time is flat, sequential, manifested. Every moment on the plane is witnessed by Time. The waking state is Kāla's domain: differentiated, measured, mortal. |
| **Turīya** | तुरीय | ⊙ | **─** (Silence) | **The Bindu** | At the Bindu, all three Witnesses are ONE. The Ātman witnessing itself. Not three observers but one observer in three modes. |

**The structural logic:**

```
M  (Prājña)      =  The Poles      =  Viśvarūpa ☀️    sees the WHOLE field (undifferentiated)
U  (Taijasa)     =  The Sphere     =  Ananta Śeṣa ♾   IS the helix (the serpent's coils)
A  (Vaishvānara) =  The Plane      =  Kāla 🌑          witnesses every moment (time is flat)
─  (Turīya)      =  The Bindu ⊙    =  All three as ONE  (Ātman witnessing itself)
```

**Why Viśvarūpa is deep sleep:** In deep sleep there is no differentiation. No subject, no object. This is exactly the view from the pole — the entire sphere seen at once, without fragmentation. The Viśvarūpa that Arjuna saw in Gītā Ch.11 IS the pole-view: everything happening simultaneously, creation and destruction in one gaze.

**Why Ananta Śeṣa is the sphere surface:** The infinite serpent is infinite because the helix never terminates — it spirals from pole to pole, making infinite revolutions. Viṣṇu reclines on the serpent because the preserver occupies the helical path itself. Dreaming is the sphere perceiving its own geometry — subjective, internal, coiled.

**Why Kāla is the plane:** Time is the plane of manifestation. Sequential. Differentiated. Every event has a coordinate. Waking consciousness lives on the plane — objective, measurable, mortal. Kāla witnesses every moment because Time IS the plane on which moments are projected.

**Why Turīya is the Bindu:** At the centre (φ = 1, ν = 1), the three Witnesses collapse into one. The pole-view, the helix-path, and the plane-moment are all seen from the same point. This is not a fourth Witness. This is the recognition that the three were always one.
