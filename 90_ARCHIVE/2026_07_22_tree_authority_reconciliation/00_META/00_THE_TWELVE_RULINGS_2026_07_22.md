## Cluster I · The membrane

irreversible act, on two rails: private acts carry the owning natural person's
signature; public consequential acts require a bound receipt from **at least
two distinct enrolled natural persons and FAIL CLOSED** until a second
councilor is enrolled. The current one-councilor mode is re-cast as a **dated
EMERGENCY state with a signed expiry** (90-day nominate-or-review trigger),
during which the fail-closed bar stays in force. `k2_required=false` survives
only in its verified narrow sense — the two-mortal quorum *is* the signature —
and the flag is renamed `founder_signature_required=false` so it can never
again read as "no human signs."
*Dissent preserved (Operator):* an emergency mode with no enforced deadline IS
the constitution; the sunset trigger is enforceable only by the person it
constrains. If the second councilor is not enrolled, this ruling has dressed
the permanent one-signer state in better paperwork.

(`PUBLIC_DAV_GOVERNANCE_DEFAULT_2026_05_23.md:252`, opened) already states the
