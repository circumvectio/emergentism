## THE REFRAME: WHY IT CHANGES EVERYTHING

### What an ontology IS vs. what a philosophy IS

- A **philosophy** argues that the world has a structure. It can be "interesting" or "illuminating."
- An **ontology** proposes the structure and then you find out whether reality agrees. It is either true or false.
- A **teleology** identifies the direction and then you find out whether things actually go there.

> "Aristotle didn't argue for substance and accident — he said 'look, this is what there is' and then the question was whether looking through that lens reveals more than it distorts."

### What the reviewer was doing wrong

| Wrong Question (Philosophy) | Right Question (Ontology) |
|---|---|
| "Does the geometry prove the philosophy?" | "Does the geometry describe what's actually there?" |
| "Can the claims stand without S²?" | Like asking if Aristotle's ethics can stand without his metaphysics — possible but mutilating |
| "Is the is-ought gap bridged?" | A teleological ontology denies the gap exists |

### The is-ought dissolution (revised understanding)

> "In a teleological ontology, 'ought' isn't derived from 'is' — the 'ought' IS part of the 'is.' The equator isn't where things should be. It's where things tend toward when nothing interferes. The 'ought' is just the name conscious beings give to the telos they can see but haven't reached yet."

> "Hume's guillotine cuts between description and prescription, but a teleological ontology says: the prescription is already in the description, as the direction of the attractor."
