# PEER REVIEW: Paper 4 — One Infinity

## ROUTING
**Specialist required:** Set theorist or mathematical logician familiar with ZFC, forcing, independence results (Gödel/Cohen), and alternative foundations
**Source file:** `../../../07_DISSEMINATION/07_PAPERS/01_MATHEMATICAL_CORE/PAPER_04_ONE_INFINITY.md`
**Evidence tier:** [C] Conjecture (self-classified)
**Depends on:** Papers 1-3

---

## CORE THESIS

The transfinite hierarchy ℵ₀ < ℵ₁ < ℵ₂ < ... is a projection artifact. On S², infinity is one point (the north pole) with no internal structure. Cantor's diagonal argument, reread on S², converges to the north pole rather than producing a genuinely new transfinite. The independence of CH becomes "natural" (the question is ill-posed on S²).

---

## WHAT THE PAPER CORRECTLY STATES

- The diagonal argument constructs a real not in any enumeration — standard
- Gödel (1940) showed CH consistent with ZFC; Cohen (1963) showed ¬CH consistent — standard
- ZFC's axioms of Infinity and Power Set permit constructions that extend without bound — standard
- The paper clearly states it is NOT claiming ZFC is inconsistent — commendable

---

## CLAIMS REQUIRING SCRUTINY

### Claim 1: "ZFC implicitly assumes planar geometry"
The axioms of ZFC (Extensionality, Foundation, Pairing, Union, Power Set, etc.) make no reference to any geometric space. They are purely set-theoretic. The claim needs rigorous justification: in what sense do these axioms "assume" the plane?

### Claim 2: "Diagonal output is a pointer to the north pole"
Cantor's diagonal constructs a specific real number. It is not ambiguous. The claim that this number "is" the north pole (∞) is not a formal statement. On S², this number still maps to a specific non-polar point.

### Claim 3: "The hierarchy corresponds to projective charts of the north pole"
This is intriguing but unformalized. What is the chart-changing map? How does taking the power set correspond to applying a different stereographic projection? No formal correspondence is constructed.

### Claim 4: "CH becomes meaningless on S²"
Even on S², one can ask about cardinalities of subsets (e.g., is there a subset of the equator with cardinality between ℵ₀ and |R|?). The geometric setting does not automatically dissolve set-theoretic questions.

---

## THE PAPER'S GREATEST STRENGTH

It correctly self-classifies as [C] Conjecture and states explicitly what it does and does not claim. It does not claim to prove ZFC inconsistent. This intellectual honesty should be noted.

## THE PAPER'S GREATEST WEAKNESS

No alternative axiomatization is provided. The paper says "if we worked on S² instead, the hierarchy would not arise" — but does not define what "working on S²" means formally. Without alternative axioms, this is a promissory note, not a theorem.
