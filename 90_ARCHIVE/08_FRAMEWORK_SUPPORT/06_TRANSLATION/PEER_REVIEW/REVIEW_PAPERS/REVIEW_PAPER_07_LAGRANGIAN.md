# REVIEW GUIDE: Paper 7 — The Lagrangian-Burri Bridge

## Specific Review Questions
1. Is the identification ν ↔ T (kinetic energy) and φ ↔ V (potential energy) physically justified or merely structural analogy?
2. The AM-GM proof that H = φ + 1/φ is minimized at φ=1 is elementary. Does it have physical content beyond the mathematical identity?
3. Is the restoring force interpretation (Great Filter = Lagrangian correction) falsifiable?
4. Does the constraint φ·ν = 1 correspond to any known physical constraint between T and V?
5. The virial theorem relates T and V in bound systems. Does φ·ν = 1 reduce to the virial theorem in any limit?

## What a Skeptic Would Say
- "In real Lagrangian mechanics, T and V are independent — they are not reciprocally constrained"
- "AM-GM is trivial — you cannot derive physics from an algebraic identity"
- "The megalithic evidence for L≈0 civilizations is historical speculation, not physics"
- "Calling the Great Filter a 'Lagrangian restoring force' is metaphor dressed as physics"

## Definitions to Scrutinize
- "Ground state" applied to a civilization — is this well-defined?
- The ν ↔ T mapping — what units does this operate in?
- "Stationary action" at L=0 — in standard mechanics, δS=0 does not mean L=0

## Kill Criteria
- The ν↔T identification produces false physical predictions in any testable domain
- A physical system satisfying φ·ν=1 is found where H is NOT minimized at the equator (impossible by AM-GM)
- The restoring force interpretation is shown to be inconsistent with known Lagrangian dynamics

## Evidence Tier
[E] for the AM-GM proof itself. [I] for the ν↔T mapping. [C] for "this IS Lagrangian mechanics."
