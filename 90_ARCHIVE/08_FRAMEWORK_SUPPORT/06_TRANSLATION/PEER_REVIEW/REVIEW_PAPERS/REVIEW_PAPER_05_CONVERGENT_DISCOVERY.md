# REVIEW GUIDE: Paper 5 — Convergent Discovery Across Seven Traditions

## Specific Review Questions
1. Are the seven tradition mappings structurally independent, or could selection bias produce the same pattern?
2. Is the Egyptian weighing-of-the-heart mapping (Ma'at = φ, heart = ν) historically justified or retrofitted?
3. Does the German etymological argument (Anmut/Demut sharing root Mut) hold under professional linguistic analysis?
4. Are the megalithic coordination claims (Göbekli Tepe at L≈0) archaeologically defensible?
5. Could a tradition be found that encodes an INCOMPATIBLE two-axis structure?

## What a Skeptic Would Say
- "You're cherry-picking traditions that fit and ignoring ones that don't"
- "Etymology is not philosophy — Anmut/Demut sharing a root is coincidence"
- "The megalithic builders had hierarchies — there IS archaeological evidence of elites"
- "Seven traditions is not statistically significant for convergent discovery"

## Definitions to Scrutinize
- "Convergent discovery" — is this a well-defined concept or ad hoc?
- "Independent encoding" — how independent are traditions that had contact?
- "Structural resonance" — is this falsifiable?

## Kill Criteria
- Exhibit a major tradition that encodes an incompatible structure
- Show that the tradition mappings require forced interpretation
- Demonstrate historical contact that explains the convergence without geometry
