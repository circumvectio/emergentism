## CORE THESIS

Three values {0, 1, ∞} constitute the irreducible foundation of arithmetic. These emerge co-instantaneously with the Riemann sphere S² itself. The expression 0 × ∞ = 1 is "resolved" as a geometric identity on S². All numbers derive from this ground via the unit 1. The dimensional hierarchy D0→D6 closes upon itself.

## ESTABLISHED MATHEMATICS USED (verify correctness)

- S² ≅ CP¹ (Riemann sphere as complex projective line) — standard
- Stereographic projection from north and south poles — standard
- The inversion z ↦ 1/z on S² has the unit circle |z| = 1 as fixed locus — standard
- The three points {0, 1, ∞} form a projective frame for CP¹ — standard
- Möbius transformations act triply transitively on CP¹ — standard

## CLAIMS THAT REQUIRE SPECIALIST SCRUTINY

### Claim 1: "0 × ∞ = 1 is resolved on S²"
The paper claims the indeterminate form 0 × ∞ becomes a geometric identity on S². However, on S², 0 and ∞ are points, not numbers with multiplication defined between them. The fixed locus of z ↦ 1/z is the unit circle, which is a 1-manifold — not the number 1. **This equivocation between the circle and the number is the paper's central move.** The reviewer should assess whether this constitutes a mathematical resolution or a suggestive metaphor.

### Claim 2: "The inflation parameter α ∈ [0°, 90°]"
Introduced as a conceptual parameter describing "sphere inflation." This is not a standard topological construction. Is it a homotopy? A parametric family of metrics? A Riemannian deformation? Its mathematical status is undefined in the paper.

### Claim 3: "The dimensional hierarchy D0→D6 closes"
The D-levels are assigned to the Burri Sphere but their formal definitions rely on the inflation parameter α. If α is not rigorously defined, the D-level assignments are also informal. The closure claim D6 = D0 rests on the topological observation that pushing ν → ∞ returns to a pole — but this is a coordinate chart issue, not a dimensional closure.

### Claim 4: Theorem 4.1 (Triadic Stability)
Referenced but not proved in the paper. The proof is in the separate document (Proof 1). The paper's use of this theorem is valid if and only if the external proof stands. <!-- [I] -->

## WHAT A SKEPTICAL REVIEWER WOULD SAY

"The paper correctly describes the Riemann sphere and stereographic projection. These are established mathematics. Everything beyond that — the interpretation of {0, 1, ∞} as 'transcendentals,' the resolution of 0 × ∞ = 1, the dimensional hierarchy — is philosophical interpretation grafted onto standard geometry. The Riemann sphere has been known since the 1850s; calling it a 'Burri Sphere' does not constitute a new mathematical result."

The key question: **does the paper claim mathematical discovery or philosophical reinterpretation?** If the former, the claims don't hold. If the latter, the paper should be routed to a philosophy of mathematics venue, not an analysis journal.
