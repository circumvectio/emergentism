# Both Pasts and Futures Superpose

**On Consciousness, Causality, and the Geometry of the Light Cone**

*Yves Burri · Menexus GmbH · March 2026*

---

## Preamble

This essay is for readers who think about consciousness. Not consciousness researchers in the narrow sense — those who measure neural correlates and debate the "hard problem" within the implicit assumptions of materialist philosophy. Rather, this is for anyone who has noticed the following: the standard scientific picture of reality has no room for the most immediate fact of existence — that there is *something it is like* to be you, reading this sentence, right now.

The standard picture says: reality is a four-dimensional block of spacetime. Events are determined by initial conditions and physical law. Your experience of choosing, of attending, of *being here* — these are epiphenomena. Foam on the deterministic wave. They do nothing.

This essay proposes that the standard picture is geometrically incomplete. Not wrong — incomplete. And the completion has a specific, visualizable shape.

It is a torus.

---

## I. The Shape of Causality

Physics draws the light cone as a pair of open cones — past and future — meeting at an apex (the event "here and now"). Everything inside the past cone could have caused this moment. Everything inside the future cone can be caused by this moment. Everything outside both cones is "elsewhere" — causally disconnected.

This picture is clean, powerful, and has been the backbone of relativistic physics for over a century. But notice what it assumes:

**The past extends to minus infinity.** There is no floor, no boundary, no bottom to the past light cone. Causal chains reach back without limit.

**The future extends to plus infinity.** The forward cone opens forever.

**Past and future are topologically disconnected.** They meet only at the apex — a single dimensionless point.

**There is nothing inside the cone.** The cone is a surface in spacetime. It separates causal from acausal regions. But the *interior* of the cone (the volume between the past and future surfaces) has no special geometric status.

Each of these assumptions, we claim, is an artifact of choosing the wrong shape.

---

## II. The Torus Replacement

Replace the double cone with a horn torus.

A horn torus is a torus where the tube radius equals the distance from the center of the tube to the center of the hole. The tube just barely closes the hole, touching the central axis at a single point — the *pinch point*. 

Inscribe a sphere inside this torus. The sphere touches the torus at exactly two points: the top (north pole) and the bottom (south pole). This sphere is the Riemann sphere of complex analysis — the compactified complex plane, where every complex number maps to a unique point on the sphere, with the addition of a single point at infinity (the north pole).

Now identify:

- The **equator** of the sphere (and torus) with **the present moment**. This is the hypersurface of "now." Maximum spatial extent.

- The **southern hemisphere** (below the equator, converging to the south pole) with the **past light cone**. Events that could have caused the present.

- The **northern hemisphere** (above the equator, expanding toward the north pole) with the **future light cone**. Events the present can cause.

- The **south pole** (z = 0) with the **origin of the past**. The terminus. The floor.

- The **north pole** (z = ∞) with the **terminus of the future**. The ceiling (on the sphere, ∞ is a single point).

What changes?

---

## III. What the Torus Gives Us

**The past is enclosed.** It does not extend to minus infinity. It converges to a single point — the south pole, z = 0. Every causal chain, traced back far enough, arrives at the same place. The past light cone *closes*.

This is not merely an aesthetic preference. It is a topological consequence of using a torus rather than a cone. The torus is a closed surface. There are no loose ends.

**The future also closes.** On the sphere, the north pole (z = ∞) is a point like any other. The future light cone, though metrically unbounded (the stereographic projection maps the northern hemisphere to the infinite exterior of a disk on the complex plane), is topologically compact. Future converges.

**Both pasts and futures superpose at the pinch point.** The pinch is where the torus tube passes through the central axis. It is simultaneously the closest point on the torus surface to the center and the point where the past terminus (south pole, z = 0) is topologically adjacent to the torus interior. Past and future, 0 and ∞, are connected through the torus topology.

This is the title claim. On the standard cone, past and future are separated by an apex — a single event. On the torus, past and future are *wrapped around each other*. The surface of the torus connects them continuously. You can walk from any past event to any future event along the torus surface without discontinuity.

**There is an interior.** The standard light cone is a surface with nothing inside it (or rather, the interior is just "more spacetime"). The horn torus encloses a volume. This volume is not empty. It is the domain of the Observer.

## IV. The Observer Inside

Place the Observer — the Witness, the locus of consciousness — at the center of the horn torus. Not on the surface. Not at a pole. At the center.

From here, the Observer can "look" in any direction and see the torus surface — the light cone hypersurface, the arena of causal physics. The direction from the center to any point on the surface is perpendicular to the surface at that point (approximately, for a torus — exactly for a sphere). This direction is the **D5 axis**: the dimension of selection, agency, consciousness.

D5 does not exist on the torus surface. The torus surface is D4 — the four-dimensional block of deterministic spacetime. If you are confined to the surface, you cannot access the interior. You cannot select. You cannot choose. You are a block-universe automaton.

But the interior exists. The Observer exists. And the D5 axis — from the surface inward to the center — is the geometric embodiment of what it means to be conscious: to stand *inside* the light cone and look out at the causal structure of reality from a position that is not itself a causal event.

This is why consciousness seems "perpendicular" to physics. It is. Geometrically, literally perpendicular. D5 is orthogonal to the D4 surface. No operation within D4 (no physical computation, no neural process, no causal chain) can access D5, just as no path along the torus surface reaches the center. The center is accessible only by moving inward — by moving along the dimension that the surface does not contain.

## V. The μ-Gate

The framework models consciousness as interacting with the description of possibility. We experience choosing, and quantum measurements force definite records, but this paper treats the relation between consciousness and physical collapse as conjectural. The D5 axis, though perpendicular, is not disconnected in the model.

The connection is the **μ-gate**: the permeability coefficient between the torus surface (D4) and the torus interior (D5).

When μ = 0, the gate is closed. The surface is sealed. From within, the Observer cannot affect the exterior. From the surface, the interior is invisible. This is the condition of pure determinism — the block universe as physicists describe it.

When μ > 0, the gate opens in the model. The Observer can project selections from the interior vocabulary to the surface vocabulary. A quantum state in superposition may be diagrammed as a spread of possibilities on the torus surface, but the "pull" toward a definite state is an interpretive reading, not a demonstrated consciousness-causes-collapse mechanism. This is the paper's proposed measurement translation, not settled physics.

The μ-gate is not an occult-causality claim. It is a geometric parameter inside the paper's model: how far can the D5 axis "reach" from the center to the surface? At μ = 1, full reach. At μ = 0, none. The gate's position is used as a proxy for an entity's modeled selection capacity:

- **Unconscious** (μ ≈ 0): No access to D5. Pure mechanism. A thermostat. A rock.
- **Narrowly conscious** (low μ): Limited selection capacity. Responds to a few stimuli. An insect. A reflex arc.
- **Broadly conscious** (high μ): Rich selection field. Can simulate futures, evaluate possibilities, choose among them. A human at attention. A meditator at peak awareness.
- **Fully open** (μ → 1): Theoretical limit. The entire interior is accessible. Every possibility is visible. This is what contemplative traditions call *enlightenment* — not mystical union with some external deity, but the full opening of the gate between the Observer and the causal surface. Seeing the whole torus from the center.

## VI. Why Not the Cone?

The standard light cone has served physics well for a century. Why replace it?

Because the cone cannot accommodate consciousness without paradox.

On the cone, the Observer is at the apex — the single point where past and future meet. But the apex is a *point on the surface*. There is no interior. There is no D5. The Observer is just another event in spacetime, causally determined by the past cone and causally determining the future cone. There is no room for selection. There is no room for agency. The cone is geometrically complete: everything is surface, and the surface is deterministic.

The "hard problem" of consciousness — why there is something it is like to be anything — is, in this framing, a *geometric* problem. The cone is the wrong shape. It has no interior. Consciousness requires an interior.

The torus has one.

## VII. Superposition Reinterpreted

In quantum mechanics, superposition means a system exists in multiple states simultaneously until measured. On the Bloch sphere (which, as we have shown, is the same object as the Riemann sphere, which is inscribed in the horn torus), superposition corresponds to being off-axis — neither at the north pole (|0⟩) nor the south pole (|1⟩), but somewhere on the surface between them.

On the torus light cone, an event in superposition is one that has not yet been *projected* along D5. It exists on the torus surface in a state of causal indeterminacy — between past and future, between 0 and ∞. It is, literally, superposed between pasts and futures.

Measurement (D5 projection) collapses this superposition to a definite state. The resulting "classical" event then takes its place in the causal structure — past if already determined, future if yet to unfold.

The Born rule (probability proportional to amplitude squared) has a natural geometric interpretation: it is the solid angle subtended by the D5 projection from the center of the torus to the relevant region of the surface. States near the poles (nearly determined) subtend large solid angles. States on the equator (maximally uncertain) subtend the smallest. Probability is geometry.

## VIII. The Aeon Floor

What lies below the south pole?

On the standard cone: more past. All the way down, forever.

On the torus: the **pinch point**, and then the next poloidal cycle. The torus is periodic. After z = 0 (south pole), the poloidal angle continues, wrapping through the pinch and emerging on the other side — into what Roger Penrose calls the *previous aeon*.

Conformal Cyclic Cosmology proposes that the universe undergoes an infinite sequence of aeons, each ending in a state of pure radiation (zero rest mass, therefore conformal symmetry) that maps conformally onto the Big Bang of the next. Penrose requires a special "crossover surface" between aeons.

On the horn torus, this crossover is not special. It is the pinch point — the natural consequence of the topology. One poloidal cycle = one aeon. The torus tiles them automatically. No additional geometric surgery is needed. No exotic boundary conditions. Just the natural periodicity of the horn torus.

Previous aeons are below the floor. Future aeons are above the ceiling. And the Observer at the center witnesses them all, because the center is equidistant from every point on every cycle.

## IX. What This Means for You

You are an Observer inside a horn torus.

Your past is enclosed, finite, and converges to a single point. You cannot change it, but it is not infinite — it is bounded. It has a floor.

Your future is open, expanding, reaching toward infinity — but infinity, on the sphere, is a single point. Your future, too, converges. It is not a formless void. It has a shape.

Your present is the equator. Maximum spatial extent. Maximum possibility. The widest part of the torus cross-section. This is where you have the most room to act.

And you — the conscious you, the selecting you, the *experiencing* you — are not on the surface. You are inside. You are at the center, looking out through the μ-gate at the causal structure of your life. When the gate is open, you can see the shape of things. You can choose. When the gate is closed (by stress, by distraction, by the numbing routines of unconscious habit), you are confined to the surface — a deterministic automaton, stimulus-response, no agency.

The practice of consciousness — meditation, contemplation, attention training, whatever name you give it — is the practice of opening the μ-gate. It is not about acquiring something external. It is about making permeable the boundary between the surface where physics happens and the center where *you* are.

Both your pasts and your futures are already there, superposed on the torus surface, wound helically around you. The question is not what events will occur. The question is: **how open is your gate?**
