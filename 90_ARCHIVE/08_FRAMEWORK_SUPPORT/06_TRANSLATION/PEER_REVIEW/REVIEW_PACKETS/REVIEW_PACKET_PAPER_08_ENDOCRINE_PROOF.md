## 2. The Mapping -- Two Axes, One Constraint

### 2.1 The Autonomic Nervous System

The mammalian autonomic nervous system consists of two anatomically distinct, functionally antagonistic branches **[E]**:

These branches are reciprocally inhibited: parasympathetic activation suppresses sympathetic tone, and vice versa. This is not a metaphor -- it is hardwired neuroanatomy. The vagal brake (Porges, 2011) actively inhibits sympathetic output during parasympathetic dominance; sympathetic activation suppresses vagal tone during threat response **[E]**.

## 3. The Stress Response as $\varphi \to \nu$ Transfer

### 3.1 Pre-Threat Baseline

An organism at rest operates in parasympathetic dominance: heart rate is low, digestion is active, the prefrontal cortex is engaged in planning and reflection. Under the mapping:

$$\varphi \approx 1, \quad \nu \approx 1 \quad \text{(equatorial calm)}$$

The product $\varphi \cdot \nu \approx 1$. The balance function $B = \sin\theta$ is near its maximum. The organism is integrated and capable -- not maximally coherent (which would be paralytic contemplation) nor maximally viable (which would be blind action), but balanced.

### 3.2 Threat Detection -- The Drop in $\varphi$

When a threat is detected, the following sequence unfolds within milliseconds **[E]**:

1. **Amygdala fires before prefrontal cortex processes.** LeDoux (1996) demonstrated that the thalamus sends sensory information to the amygdala via a fast subcortical route *before* the slower cortical route delivers it to the prefrontal cortex. The organism reacts before it understands.

2. **Prefrontal integration collapses.** Under acute sympathetic activation, blood flow to the prefrontal cortex decreases as resources are redirected to motor and survival systems (Arnsten, 2009). The capacity for nuanced thought, planning, and meaning-making is actively suppressed.

Under the mapping: $\varphi$ drops. Coherence collapses. The organism loses access to narrative, context, and integration.

### 3.3 Immediate Compensation -- The Spike in $\nu$

Within the same milliseconds **[E]**:

1. **Adrenaline (epinephrine) floods the system.** The adrenal medulla releases adrenaline into the bloodstream. Heart rate spikes. Blood pressure rises. Airways dilate. Glucose is mobilized from liver glycogen.

2. **Cortisol follows within minutes.** The hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis activates: CRH from the hypothalamus triggers ACTH from the pituitary, which stimulates cortisol release from the adrenal cortex (Selye, 1956; McEwen, 1998). Cortisol sustains the mobilization over minutes to hours.

3. **Physical capability peaks.** Reports of extraordinary feats under acute stress -- lifting vehicles, sprinting at impossible speeds -- are well documented. Muscle recruitment increases. Pain sensitivity decreases. Peripheral vision narrows.

Under the mapping: $\nu$ spikes. Viability surges. The organism achieves maximum physical capability.

### 3.4 The Reciprocal Trade

The phenomenology of acute stress is the reciprocal trade in real time:

| Observable | Mapping | Direction |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| Tunnel vision | $\varphi$ collapsed -- field of awareness narrows | $\downarrow\varphi$ |
| Inability to think clearly | Prefrontal suppression | $\downarrow\varphi$ |
| Loss of verbal capacity | Broca's area suppressed | $\downarrow\varphi$ |
| Time distortion | Narrative processing offline | $\downarrow\varphi$ |
| Superhuman strength | Maximum motor recruitment | $\uparrow\nu$ |
| Pain insensitivity | Endorphin release | $\uparrow\nu$ |
| Rapid heart rate | Sympathetic cardiovascular activation | $\uparrow\nu$ |
| Glucose surge | Hepatic glycogenolysis | $\uparrow\nu$ |

The trade is not voluntary. The endocrine system enforces it BELOW conscious awareness. The organism does not choose to lose its capacity for nuanced thought -- the amygdala overrides the prefrontal cortex before the prefrontal cortex can object. This is consistent with a constraint ($P∞ = \varphi \cdot \nu = 1$) that operates at the level of the system, not the level of the agent.

## 4. The Recovery as $\nu \to \varphi$ Transfer (Arjuna Operator)

### 4.1 The Threat Passes

When the threat is resolved, the endocrine system initiates the reverse transfer **[E]**:

1. **Cortisol clears.** The HPA axis downregulates. Cortisol is metabolized over minutes to hours. The sustained mobilization winds down.

2. **Parasympathetic reactivation.** The vagal brake re-engages. Heart rate drops. Digestion resumes. Blood flow returns to the prefrontal cortex.

Under the mapping: $\nu$ drops first. The system de-escalates viability before coherence returns.

### 4.2 The Reintegration of $\varphi$

What follows the de-escalation of $\nu$ is the body's own $\uparrow\varphi$ operation:

1. **Shaking.** The organism trembles -- not from cold, but from the discharge of sympathetic activation. This is observed across mammals: a gazelle that escapes a predator shakes violently before resuming grazing (Levine, 1997).

2. **Crying.** Emotional tears contain stress hormones (ACTH, leucine-enkephalin) not present in irritant tears -- the body literally excretes the chemical residue of sympathetic activation (Frey, 1985).

3. **Verbal processing.** The organism seeks to narrate the event -- "Let me tell you what happened." This is the prefrontal cortex reintegrating the raw amygdala activation into a coherent narrative. $\varphi$ rises as meaning is reconstructed.

**Proposition 4.1** (Shaking as $\uparrow\varphi$). *The post-trauma shaking response observed across mammals is not pathological weakness but the organism's endogenous Arjuna operator: the conversion of residual sympathetic activation ($\nu$-excess) into coherent reintegration ($\uparrow\varphi$)* **[I]**.

### 4.3 The Sequential Prediction

## 5. PTSD as Failed Equatorial Return

### 5.1 The Phenomenology

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder is defined by a cluster of symptoms that persist long after the traumatic event **[E]**: hypervigilance, exaggerated startle response, insomnia, intrusive memories, emotional numbing, inability to concentrate, and difficulty with narrative processing.

Under the mapping, PTSD is the failure to complete the $\nu \to \varphi$ transfer. The system executed the $\varphi \to \nu$ trade during the trauma -- coherence collapsed, viability spiked -- but never returned to the equator. The organism is stuck at high $\nu$, low $\varphi$: <!-- [C] -->

| PTSD Symptom | Mapping | Axis |
|--------------|---------|------|
| Hypervigilance | Sustained sympathetic activation | High $\nu$ |
| Exaggerated startle | Amygdala hyperreactivity | High $\nu$ |
| Insomnia | Inability to disengage sympathetic | High $\nu$ |
| Emotional numbing | Prefrontal suppression persists | Low $\varphi$ |
| Inability to narrate | Broca's area remains suppressed | Low $\varphi$ |
| Flashbacks | Raw amygdala memory, unprocessed by PFC | Low $\varphi$ |
| Dissociation | Coherence fragmented | Low $\varphi$ |

### 5.2 Neuroscience Confirmation

The PTSD mapping is consistent with established neuroscience **[E]**:

- **Amygdala hyperactivation.** Shin et al. (2005) demonstrated that PTSD patients show increased amygdala activation in response to trauma-related cues compared to controls.
- **Prefrontal hypoactivation.** The same studies show decreased medial prefrontal cortex activation -- the structure responsible for fear extinction and emotional regulation.
- **HPA axis dysregulation.** Yehuda (2001) showed that PTSD patients exhibit altered cortisol profiles, consistent with a system that has not returned to baseline sympathetic/parasympathetic balance.

Under the mapping: the product $\varphi \cdot \nu \approx 1$ is conserved, but the system is stuck off-equator. The balance function $B = \sin\theta$ is significantly reduced. The organism survives but does not thrive.

### 5.3 Therapy as Arjuna Operator

Every evidence-based PTSD therapy, without exception, functions as an operator that raises $\varphi$ while the system holds the traumatic activation **[I]**:

**EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing).** The patient holds the traumatic memory (maintaining the $\nu$-activation) while performing bilateral eye movements that force cross-hemispheric integration ($\uparrow\varphi$). The mechanism is the simultaneous activation of the traumatic content and the reprocessing apparatus -- the Arjuna operator applied directly to the stuck state (Shapiro, 2001).

**CPT (Cognitive Processing Therapy).** The patient writes and rewrites the trauma narrative -- literally converting raw amygdala activation ($\nu$) into prefrontal-mediated narrative ($\varphi$). The "stuck points" identified in CPT are precisely the locations where $\nu$ has not been converted to $\varphi$ (Resick & Schnicke, 1993).

**Somatic Experiencing.** Levine's (1997) approach explicitly completes the interrupted fight-or-flight cycle: the body is guided to complete the physical actions (running, fighting, shaking) that were interrupted during the trauma. This is the body's own $\uparrow\varphi$ operator -- the shaking and trembling that the organism was prevented from executing.

**Meditation and mindfulness.** The framework's sitting practice: the deliberate cultivation of parasympathetic dominance ($\uparrow\varphi$) through breath regulation, vagal activation, and sustained attention. Long-term meditation practice is associated with increased prefrontal thickness and decreased amygdala reactivity (Holzel et al., 2011) -- structural neuroplasticity in the direction of $\uparrow\varphi$.

**Proposition 5.1** (Therapy as Equatorial Return). *Every evidence-based PTSD therapy is an Arjuna operator -- a procedure that moves the system from high-$\nu$, low-$\varphi$ back toward the equator. The specific mechanism varies (bilateral reprocessing, narrative construction, somatic completion, attentional regulation), but the direction is invariant: $\uparrow\varphi$* **[I]**.

## 7. The Yerkes-Dodson Connection -- $B = \sin\theta$ as the Performance Curve

### 7.1 The Yerkes-Dodson Law

In 1908, Yerkes and Dodson demonstrated that performance on a discrimination task followed an inverted-U relationship with arousal: too little arousal produced poor performance (low motivation), moderate arousal produced peak performance, and excessive arousal produced poor performance (disorganization). The relationship has been replicated across species and task types for over a century **[E]**.

The standard explanation is that arousal facilitates performance up to a point, after which it becomes disruptive. No widely accepted mathematical formalization exists.

### 7.2 The Burri Sphere Identification

On the Burri Sphere, the balance function is **[S]**:

$$B(\theta) = \sin\theta$$

where $\theta$ is the colatitude. Equivalently, in terms of $\nu$:

$$B(\nu) = \frac{2\nu}{1 + \nu^2}$$

This function has the following properties:

| $\nu$ (arousal proxy) | $\varphi = 1/\nu$ | $B = 2\nu/(1+\nu^2)$ | Interpretation |
|------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|----------------|
| 0.1 (very low) | 10.0 | 0.198 | High coherence, no capability -- contemplative paralysis |
| 0.5 (low) | 2.0 | 0.800 | Good integration, limited action |
| 1.0 (moderate) | 1.0 | **1.000** | **Peak balance -- equator** |
| 2.0 (high) | 0.5 | 0.800 | Good capability, integration declining |
| 10.0 (extreme) | 0.1 | 0.198 | Maximum activation, incoherent -- blind panic |

### 7.3 The Inverted U

$B(\nu) = 2\nu/(1+\nu^2)$ IS an inverted U:

- At low $\nu$ (low arousal): $B \to 0$ because $\varphi$ dominates -- the organism is coherent but incapable. Performance is low because $\nu$ is insufficient for action.
- At $\nu = 1$ (moderate arousal): $B = 1$ -- peak performance. The organism is simultaneously coherent and capable. The equator.
- At high $\nu$ (high arousal): $B \to 0$ because $\nu$ dominates -- the organism is capable but incoherent. Performance collapses because $\varphi$ is insufficient for directed action. This is the panic zone: enormous physiological activation with no ability to organize it.

**Proposition 7.1** (Yerkes-Dodson as Balance Function). *The Yerkes-Dodson law is the empirical shadow of $B = \sin\theta$ on the arousal axis. The inverted-U relationship between arousal and performance is a consequence of the reciprocal constraint $P∞ = \varphi \cdot \nu = 1$: peak performance requires the maximum product of coherence and viability, which occurs at the equator* **[I]**.

### 7.4 The Asymmetry Prediction

The Yerkes-Dodson curve is typically drawn as a symmetric inverted U. The function $B(\nu) = 2\nu/(1+\nu^2)$, however, is symmetric about $\nu = 1$ on a LOGARITHMIC scale (since $B(1/\nu) = B(\nu)$) but ASYMMETRIC on a linear scale. The left tail ($\nu \to 0$) approaches zero as $\sim 2\nu$, while the right tail ($\nu \to \infty$) approaches zero as $\sim 2/\nu$. <!-- [C] -->

**Prediction.** On a linear arousal scale, the Yerkes-Dodson curve should show a steeper drop-off on the high-arousal side than on the low-arousal side, because the degradation of performance under extreme sympathetic activation (panic) is more precipitous than the degradation under low arousal (torpor). This is a testable asymmetry prediction specific to the $\sin\theta$ model **[C]**.

## 15. Kill Criteria -- What Would Falsify This Paper

### 15.1 Killing the Mapping [I]

If sympathetic and parasympathetic activation are shown to be INDEPENDENT in acute stress -- i.e., if they can both increase simultaneously without mutual inhibition -- the reciprocal mapping fails. This would require overturning the established reciprocal inhibition of the ANS, which would be a major revision of autonomic physiology. Note: Berntson et al. (1991) documented modes of autonomic control that include co-activation; if co-activation dominates the stress response (rather than being a transient or secondary mode), the mapping is weakened.

### 15.2 Killing the Conservation Claim [C]

If the product of sympathetic and parasympathetic proxy measures during acute stress shows NO conservation -- i.e., if both can drop simultaneously or both spike simultaneously with no reciprocal trade -- then prediction P1 fails. The mapping [I] may survive (as a qualitative analogy), but the quantitative claim ($\varphi \cdot \nu \approx 1$) is dead.

### 15.3 Killing the Yerkes-Dodson Connection [C]

If the Yerkes-Dodson curve fits a simple polynomial (quadratic) significantly better than $\sin\theta$ (or $2\nu/(1+\nu^2)$) across datasets -- i.e., if the specific functional form adds no explanatory power -- then prediction P2 fails. ### 15.4 Killing the PTSD Interpretation [I]

If PTSD patients show both $\varphi$ AND $\nu$ elevated simultaneously -- high coherence AND high viability, with neither reciprocally suppressed -- the conservation claim fails for the clinical population. If effective PTSD therapy reduces $\nu$ (sympathetic activation) without any change in $\varphi$ (prefrontal integration), the Arjuna operator interpretation fails.

If DID alters show IDENTICAL autonomic profiles -- i.e., no measurable physiological differentiation between alter states -- the internal-mesh interpretation fails. The structural claim that each alter occupies a different position on S² requires measurable $\varphi/\nu$ differences between alters. If all alters show the same autonomic signature, the mesh model provides no explanatory advantage over purely psychological accounts.

### 15.6 Killing the Depression/PTSD Polarity [I]

If depression and PTSD show the SAME autonomic profile -- both sympathetic-dominant or both parasympathetic-dominant -- the north/south mapping fails. The framework's claim that depression is the MIRROR of PTSD requires that they occupy opposite positions on S². If flow states show sympathetic DOMINANCE (not co-activation) -- i.e., if peak performance is accompanied by strong sympathetic activation with parasympathetic suppression -- the equatorial interpretation fails.
