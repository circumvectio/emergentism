### 1.2 Motivation

Division by zero is the oldest prohibition in arithmetic. Every student learns that 1/0 is "undefined," that 0 × ∞ is "indeterminate," and that these expressions must be avoided. Yet the Riemann Sphere S², introduced by Bernhard Riemann in 1857 and now standard in complex analysis, algebraic geometry, and theoretical physics, provides a rigorous geometric setting in which the point at infinity is a well-defined member of the extended complex plane **[A]**.

On S², the Möbius involution z ↦ 1/z is a smooth automorphism that sends 0 to ∞ and ∞ to 0, with fixed locus the unit circle |z| = 1 **[A]**. The "singularity" at z = 0 is not a pathology but a pole — a point where the function achieves its value at the north pole of the sphere. The operation 1/0 = ∞ is not a violation of arithmetic; it is the natural expression of the sphere's geometry **[A]**.

This paper asks a philosophical question: if the Riemann Sphere provides a geometric home for 0, 1, and ∞ as distinguished points, what does this structure suggest about the conceptual foundations of arithmetic? Our answer — and this is the interpretive contribution — is that these three values are not ordinary numbers but *transcendentals* in the philosophical sense: the irreducible conceptual ground from which arithmetic meaning is generated **[I]**.

## 2. The Co-Emergence Argument

### 2.1 Stereographic Projection (Established)

Let S² be the unit sphere in ℝ³, and let N = (0, 0, 1) be its north pole. Stereographic projection π: S² \ {N} → ℂ maps each point P on the sphere to the point where the line NP intersects the equatorial plane **[A]**. The inverse map π⁻¹ embeds ℂ into S², and we extend the map by defining π⁻¹(∞) = N, completing the Riemann Sphere Ĉ = ℂ ∪ {∞} ≅ ℂP¹ **[A]**.

These constructions are entirely standard. The Riemann Sphere Ĉ is a compact complex manifold of dimension 1, diffeomorphic to S². It admits the structure of the complex projective line ℂP¹ **[A]**.

### 2.2 The Inflation Parameter (Pedagogical)

We introduce a pedagogical parameter α ∈ [0°, 90°] to visualize the "opening angle" of the stereographic projection — the angle between the polar axis and the line from N to a point on the equator **[I]**.

**Note on formal status.** The parameter α is a pedagogical and visual device, not a formal mathematical object. It is not defined via Ricci flow, homotopy, or deformation retract. Its purpose is to make vivid the conceptual relationship between the collapsed point and the fully extended sphere. Readers seeking formal rigor should interpret the argument below in terms of the standard one-point compactification of ℂ, not as a claim about continuous deformation.

**Definition 2.1** (Inflation parameter — pedagogical). Let α parameterize the geometric visualization as follows:

- At α = 0°: conceptually, the sphere has collapsed to a single point. There is no sphere, no plane, no projection, and no division. The north pole, south pole, and equator coincide. There is only the undifferentiated point **[I]**.

- At α > 0°: the sphere exists to support the projection. The south pole (0), the north pole (∞), and the equator (the unit circle, containing 1) are simultaneously present **[I]**.

**Proposition 2.1** (Co-emergence — interpretive). *We interpret the relationship between 1/0 and S² as co-emergent: the instant the question "what is 1/0?" becomes meaningful is the same instant the geometric structure provides the answer* **[I]**.

*Interpretive justification.* At the conceptual "α = 0°," there is no division, hence no division by zero, hence no problem. The expression 1/0 presupposes the existence of 1, 0, and the operation of division — all of which require the differentiated geometry of the sphere (α > 0°). But at α > 0°, the sphere already contains the point at infinity as its north pole. Therefore one cannot formulate 1/0 without simultaneously having access to its value ∞. This is a philosophical observation about the conceptual structure of the Riemann Sphere, not a theorem **[I]**. ∎

$$P∞ = \varphi \cdot \nu = 1$$

*Justification (mathematical).* On S², the map z ↦ 1/z is a conformal automorphism. It sends 0 ↦ ∞ and ∞ ↦ 0, and it preserves the unit circle |z| = 1 **[A]**. The Burri coordinates φ = cot(θ/2) and ν = tan(θ/2) satisfy φ · ν = cot(θ/2) · tan(θ/2) = 1 for all θ ∈ (0, π) **[S]**.

## 5. Only 1/0 = ∞ Is Fundamental

### 5.1 One Zero, One Infinity, One Unit

**Proposition 5.1** (Uniqueness of the poles). *There is one zero, one infinity, and one unit circle on S²* **[A]**.

The Riemann Sphere has exactly one south pole, one north pole, and one equator. The extended complex plane Ĉ = ℂ ∪ {∞} adjoins exactly *one* point at infinity **[A]**. This is the defining property of the one-point compactification of ℂ.

### 5.2 Cantor's Hierarchy — An Interpretive Remark

Georg Cantor's transfinite hierarchy — ℵ₀, ℵ₁, ℵ₂, ... — establishes an infinite tower of infinities of increasing cardinality **[A]**. Within the framework, we offer the following reinterpretation:

**Proposition 5.2** (Single-point infinity — interpretive). *On S², infinity is one point. The Emergentist framework interprets Cantor's hierarchy of infinities as a projection artifact: the differentiated structure that appears when one geometric point is viewed through the lens of the flat plane* **[I]**.

*Interpretive justification.* Stereographic projection maps the single north pole N to the single point ∞ ∈ Ĉ. The apparent "sizes" of infinity (countable, uncountable, etc.) arise from the structure of the *plane* onto which the sphere is projected, not from the structure of the pole itself. The north pole is one point, regardless of how richly the plane below it is structured **[I]**.

*Caveat.* This is a philosophical reinterpretation, not a claim that Cantor's theorem is incorrect. The cardinality distinctions are valid within set theory. The interpretive claim is that from the projective-geometric viewpoint, all infinities are aspects of a single geometric point **[I]**. ∎

**Proposition 5.3** (1/0 as the unique fundamental division by zero). *Every other "division by zero" is derived from 1/0 = ∞* **[S]**.

*Justification.* On the Riemann Sphere, the map z ↦ 1/z sends 0 to ∞ **[A]**. For any n ∈ ℕ, n/0 = n · (1/0) = n · ∞. On the Riemann Sphere, any non-zero constant times ∞ equals ∞ (the north pole absorbs finite scaling) **[A]**. Every numerator n is a sum of 1s: n = 1 + 1 + ... + 1 (n times). Therefore the only *fundamental* division by zero is 1/0 = ∞; all others are derived by the prior construction of n from the unit **[S]**. ∎

### 5.4 Every Number Is a Product of 1s

**Proposition 5.4.** *Every natural number n is the n-fold sum of the unit: n = 1 + 1 + ... + 1. All numbers are generated from the transcendental unit* **[I]**.

This is arithmetically trivial **[A]** but philosophically suggestive: every number is a repetition of the unit, and the unit itself is the fixed point of the exchange between nothing and everything **[I]**.

**Proposition 7.1** (Bit-to-qubit transition — interpretive). *The Emergentist framework interprets the emergence from classical to quantum computation as the inflation of the point into the sphere* **[I]**.
