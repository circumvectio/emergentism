## 2. The Mapping -- $\nu \leftrightarrow T$, $\varphi \leftrightarrow V$ <!-- [I] -->

### 2.1 Kinetic and Potential Energy in Classical Mechanics

Analytical mechanics describes the evolution of physical systems through two energy functions **[A]**:

1. **Kinetic energy** $T$: the energy of motion, extension, force, and expression -- what a system *does*.
2. **Potential energy** $V$: the energy of configuration, structure, position, and rest -- what a system *is*.

The Hamiltonian is their sum: $H = T + V$ (total energy). The Lagrangian is their difference: $L = T - V$ (the quantity whose integral is stationary along physical trajectories).

### 2.2 The Burri Sphere Coordinates

On the Burri Sphere, the two coordinates are **[S]**:

1. **Viability** $\nu$: the capacity of a system to act, extend, express, and manifest -- what a system *can do*.
2. **Coherence** $\varphi$: the structural configuration, meaning, and ground condition of a system -- what a system *is*.

The constraint $P∞ = \varphi \cdot \nu = 1$ ensures that these are reciprocal: $\nu = 1/\varphi$. <!-- [I] -->

### 2.3 The Identification

**Proposition 2.1** (The Lagrangian-Burri Identification). *We identify* **[I]**:

$$\nu \leftrightarrow T \quad \text{(viability is kinetic energy)}$$
$$\varphi \leftrightarrow V \quad \text{(coherence is potential energy)}$$

*Justification.* The identification is motivated by the following structural parallels:

| Property | Kinetic Energy $T$ | Viability $\nu$ |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Character | Energy of motion and expression | Capacity to act and manifest |

In standard mechanics, $T$ and $V$ are independent variables bounded only by total energy conservation in closed systems. On the Burri Sphere, they are identically linked **[S]**:

## 3. The Hamiltonian Minimum -- $H(\varphi) = \varphi + 1/\varphi$

### 3.1 Construction

Substituting the reciprocal constraint into the Hamiltonian:

$$H = T + V = \nu + \varphi = \frac{1}{\varphi} + \varphi$$

This is a function of a single variable $\varphi > 0$.

### 3.2 The Minimum

**Theorem 3.1** (Absolute Energy Minimum). *Under the constraint $P∞ = \varphi \cdot \nu = 1$ with $\varphi > 0$, the total energy $H(\varphi) = \varphi + 1/\varphi$ is absolutely minimized at $\varphi = 1$, with $H_{\min} = 2$* **[A]**.

**Proof (Calculus).**

First derivative:
$$H'(\varphi) = 1 - \frac{1}{\varphi^2}$$

Setting $H'(\varphi) = 0$:
$$1 - \frac{1}{\varphi^2} = 0 \implies \varphi^2 = 1 \implies \varphi = 1 \quad (\text{since } \varphi > 0)$$ <!-- [I] -->

Second derivative:
$$H''(\varphi) = \frac{2}{\varphi^3}$$

Evaluating at the critical point:
$$H''(1) = 2 > 0$$

The second derivative is strictly positive, confirming that $\varphi = 1$ is a local minimum. Since $H(\varphi) \to \infty$ as $\varphi \to 0^+$ and $H(\varphi) \to \infty$ as $\varphi \to \infty$, and the function is continuous on $(0, \infty)$ with a unique critical point, the minimum is absolute. <!-- [I] -->

Evaluating:
$$H(1) = 1 + 1 = 2 \quad \blacksquare$$

**Proof (AM-GM Inequality).**

The arithmetic mean -- geometric mean inequality states that for positive reals $a, b$ **[A]**:

$$\frac{a + b}{2} \geq \sqrt{ab}$$

with equality if and only if $a = b$. Setting $a = \varphi$, $b = \nu = 1/\varphi$: <!-- [I] -->

$$\frac{\varphi + 1/\varphi}{2} \geq \sqrt{\varphi \cdot \frac{1}{\varphi}} = \sqrt{1} = 1$$

$$\varphi + \frac{1}{\varphi} \geq 2$$

Equality holds if and only if $\varphi = 1/\varphi$, i.e., $\varphi = 1$ (since $\varphi > 0$). $\blacksquare$ <!-- [I] -->

### 3.3 Interpretation

**Corollary 3.1** (Ground State). *The equator $\varphi = \nu = 1$ is the ground state of the Burri Sphere. Any deviation from the equator requires the injection of excess energy $\Delta H = H(\varphi) - 2 > 0$* **[S]**.

### 4.1 Construction

$$L = T - V = \nu - \varphi = \frac{1}{\varphi} - \varphi$$

$$\frac{1}{\varphi} - \varphi = 0 \implies \frac{1}{\varphi} = \varphi \implies \varphi^2 = 1 \implies \varphi = 1 \quad (\text{since } \varphi > 0)$$
