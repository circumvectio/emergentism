## REVIEW OF PROOF 21: TRIADIC STABILITY THEOREM

### Status: ✅ **APPROVED** (with clarifications)

**Evidence Tier Assessment:** [S] — Correctly classified

### Summary of Claim
[S] The theorem proves that the pair {0, ∞} under the Möbius involution φ(z) = 1/z generates the frame {0, 1, ∞}, where 1 emerges as the unique positive real fixed point of the involution.

### Detailed Analysis

#### ✅ Correct Elements
1. **Mathematical structure is valid.** The construction of cl({0, ∞}, φ) = {−1, 0, 1, ∞} with distinguished subset {0, 1, ∞} is mathematically correct.

2. **Fixed point calculation is correct.** For φ(z) = 1/z, solving z = 1/z yields z² = 1, so Fix(φ) = {1, −1}. The selection of 1 as the positive real fixed point is natural.

3. **Uniqueness up to Möbius isomorphism follows** from the transitivity of PSL(2, ℂ) on ordered triples.

**W1.1:** The relation to "emergence" in the philosophical sense should be distinguished from mathematical emergence. The fixed point 1 "emerges" in the sense that it's not in the generating set but is in the closure — this is a technical sense of emergence, not the philosophical sense of strong emergence.

**W1.2:** The η corollary is correctly labeled [I] (Interpretive). The claim that log₂(1) = 0 bits represents "zero selection complexity" is a framework-specific interpretation, not standard Kolmogorov complexity.

#### Verdict
**Code: A1** (Approved as written)  
The proof is mathematically sound. The [S] classification is appropriate.

## REVIEW OF PROOF 22: POWER-MAX LEMMA

### Status: ✅ **APPROVED** (with minor clarification)

**Evidence Tier Assessment:** [S] — Correctly classified

### Summary of Claim
In the balance maximization game Γ(N, λ), the strategy νᵢ = 1 (equatorial position) is a strictly dominant strategy for all players, yielding harmonic incentive alignment with Price of Anarchy = 1.

### Detailed Analysis

#### ✅ Correct Elements
1. **The game structure is well-defined.** Payoffs Πᵢ = (1−λ)Bᵢ + λB̄ with B(ν) = 2ν/(1+ν²).

2. **Strict dominance proof is correct.** Since ∂²Πᵢ/∂νᵢ∂νⱼ = 0 for i ≠ j, each player's optimization is separable. The unique maximum of B(ν) at ν = 1 implies strict dominance.

3. **Price of Anarchy = 1 follows** from the fact that the dominant strategy equilibrium maximizes social welfare (sum of balances).

4. **Concavity analysis is nuanced and correct.** B(θ) = sin(θ) is strictly concave on (0, π). B(ν) = 2ν/(1+ν²) is strictly pseudo-concave (unimodal) with unique maximum at ν = 1.

#### ⚠️ Clarification Needed (W1)

**W1.1:** The "game" is strategically trivial (Lemma 0: zero strategic interaction). This is correctly noted but the philosophical significance — that the geometry "dissolves" rather than "resolves" the cooperation problem — should be flagged as interpretive [I].

#### Verdict
**Code: A1** (Approved as written)  
The proof is game-theoretically sound. The [S] classification is appropriate.

### Status: 🟡 **APPROVED WITH CLARIFICATIONS**

**Evidence Tier Assessment:** Mixed — [E] and [S] claims correct; [I] and [C] appropriately flagged

### Summary of Claim

#### ✅ Correct Elements (E and S)

1. **Theorem 3.1 (Hamiltonian Minimum) — [E]:**

   - **Verdict:** Mathematically unassailable.

2. **Theorem 4.1 (Lagrangian Zero-Point) — [E]:**
   - L(φ) = 1/φ − φ = 0 ⟹ φ = 1
   - Unique solution on positive reals
   - **Verdict:** Correct.

3. **Proposition 5.1 (Restoring Force) — [E]:**
   - F(φ) = −1/φ² − 1 < 0 for all φ > 0
   - Divergence as φ→0⁺: lim F(φ) = −∞
   - **Verdict:** Correct calculation.

4. **Theorem 7.1 (Triple Optimality) — [S]:**
   - Convergence of H-min, L-zero, B-max at φ=1
   - **Verdict:** Correctly derived from the constraint.

#### ⚠️ Critical Clarifications (Already Implemented in v2.0)

The paper v2.0 correctly addresses three critical distinctions:

1. **T·V = 1 vs. Standard Mechanics:**
   - Correctly noted: "No known physical system satisfies T·V = 1"
   - The identification is **[I]** (interpretive), not identity

2. **Pointwise L=0 vs. Principle of Least Action:**
   - Correctly distinguished: L(φ=1) = 0 is a pointwise value
