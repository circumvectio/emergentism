## Distillation law

- The kernel receives; it does not invent.
- One doctrine has one owner. Other live documents are evidence, elaboration,
  route, or projection; superseded bodies are archive or visible tombstone.
- Assembly status never upgrades claim status. `[C]` remains `[C]` after
  adoption, publication, deployment, or applause.
- Emergentism stands alone. External products, ventures, companies, runtimes,
  and governance systems are applications at most—never premises or authorities.
- AI work follows the scoped request, repository permissions, provenance,
  reversibility, and tests. Consequential action uses a complete
  `AuthorizationEnvelope`; private financial or contractual signatures do not
  govern editorial or repository work.
- The Rosetta translates structure while preserving type and tier. It transfers
  no proof.
- Exit stays on the inside. A reader may use, test, reject, or put down the map.

## Stranger test

A stranger should be able to read the Door, then K-1 through K-7, in one
sitting and distinguish:

1. what is analytic;
2. what the framework defines;
3. what it interprets;
4. what it wagers;
5. what the repository records;
6. what would kill each stronger claim; and
7. what only external contact can decide.

If that distinction fails, repair the route or wording; do not add an eighth
surface to explain the confusion.
