# Apophatic Ground and Practice

> This is a subordinate companion about epistemic restraint and optional
> practice. It does not own ontology, prove a Ground, certify privileged access,
> or derive the D-scaffold.

## 1. What “Ground” Means Here

In this companion, **Ground** names the remainder that no present description
is licensed to claim it exhausts. That is an apophatic posture `[I]`, not a
discovery that a particular metaphysical entity exists behind appearances.

The posture has one practical use: it prevents a model from certifying itself
as the whole territory. Silence, negation, and humility may mark the limit of a
claim; they do not supply missing evidence.

The current K-2 owner is the
[Degrees-of-Freedom Ontology](02_THE_DEGREES_OF_FREEDOM_ONTOLOGY.md). If this
companion conflicts with that owner, the owner controls.

## 2. The Sphere Is a Selected Lens

The reciprocal chart and `S²` may diagram limits, inversion, and a selected
balance coordinate. Their mathematical identities have only the scope granted
by the [Canonical Formula Block](../05_COSMOLOGY/00_CANONICAL_FORMULA_BLOCK.md).

Using the sphere as an ontological picture is a selected correspondence `[I]`.
It is **not** a theorem that:

- `S²` is reality's substrate or first closure;
- the chart is the unique or minimal manifold for Being;
- its poles are literal modes of existence;
- its equator confers truth, goodness, or spiritual attainment; or
- an analytic identity establishes an ethic or cosmology.

The lens survives only while it clarifies more than its rivals at acceptable
cost. A better lens may replace it without loss of the apophatic discipline.

## 3. D6 Is a Chosen Boundary Reading

The D0–D6 scaffold is a selected interpretive architecture. D6 opens no positive
freedom and is not formally necessary. In the current Burri grammar, `b₆` marks
the refusal to assert another positive register, while `r₆ : D6 ↝ D0` records a
proposed similarity of boundary roles `[I/C]`. Neither relation is identity,
proof of recurrence, nor a sixth μ-crossing.

The useful lesson is modest: an explanation may be powerful without being
exhaustive. That lesson remains available even if the D6 notation is retired.

## 4. Practice Produces Reports, Not Ontological Proof

Quiet sitting is an optional practice. A practitioner may report changes in
attention, narration, self-boundaries, affect, or salience. Such reports are
first-person observations whose reliability, interpretation, and generality
remain open to ordinary epistemic scrutiny.

Sitting does not by itself:

- establish direct access to Ground;
- locate a person at `φ=1` or at the equator;
- bypass perception, reasoning, action, or the Rosetta levels;
- discriminate among phenomenological, psychological, neurological, and
  metaphysical explanations; or
- prove any doctrine in this corpus.

The [historical sitting-practice text](../90_ARCHIVE/00_THE_SITTING_PRACTICE.md)
is therefore a practice source, not an epistemic authority. Participation is
voluntary, and a person may stop without philosophical penalty.

## 5. The Kintsugi Seam

The earlier version of this file called `S²` an ontological substrate, described
the sphere-product structure as internally derived, treated sitting as direct
Ground access, and claimed that practice bypassed seven levels. Those claims
exceeded their warrant and are retracted here.

What survives is more useful:

1. **Humility:** no symbol, diagram, experience, or institution possesses the
   territory it addresses.
2. **Correction:** every positive claim remains tiered, contestable, and open to
   replacement by better evidence.
3. **Practice:** experience may inform inquiry without becoming proof.
4. **Exit:** a worldview must remain put-downable.

## 6. Exit

The framework succeeds as a lens only when a reader remains free to question,
revise, or leave it. Exit does not validate the worldview; it prevents the
worldview from making continued mediation a condition of agency or meaning.

Use the map while it helps. Compare it with alternatives. Correct it when it
fails. Put it down when it obscures the territory.

## Reading Discipline

1. Route ontology claims to the K-2 owner.
2. Treat `S²` as a selected `[I]` lens, never a substrate theorem.
3. Treat practice reports as evidence of reported experience, not proof of
   Ground or doctrine.
4. Treat D6 as an optional boundary reading, not formal necessity.
5. Preserve correction, contestability, and exit.
