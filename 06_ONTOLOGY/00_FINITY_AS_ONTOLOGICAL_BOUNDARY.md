## Why This Document Exists

L5 already teaches **the discovery of finity** as a positive Trinity surface:

```text
0  -> absence
∞  -> totality
1  -> finite presence / relation
```

L6 must read the same recognition differently.

At L5, finity is a model that can be taught.
At L6, finity is the boundary exposed when every model of the finite is
subtracted.

So this note stabilizes the sentence:

> **The discovery of finity is as profound as zero and infinity, but it is not a
> possession of the Ground. It is the recognition that presence is relational
> and finite disclosure is where Being becomes sayable.** `[I/S]`

## Create: Finite Presence Is Relation

The L6 creation move is not making a new object.
It is allowing a missing dimension to appear.

The missing dimension is **finity**:

```text
absence alone      -> no disclosure
totality alone     -> no boundary
finite relation    -> disclosure can occur
```

Finity says that presence is not merely a third item beside zero and infinity.
It is the relational site where absence and totality can become sayable without
either one being possessed.

This is why the L6 core state names Dasein as the finite witness-agent:

```text
Ground -> Being -> Teleological Force -> Dasein -> beings
```

Dasein is not Being itself. Dasein is the finite `⊙` in whom Being is
encountered, questioned, and enacted.

## Stabilize: The Dimensions of Finity

At L6, "ontology is the dimensions" means the Sadhu asks what dimensions are
revealed by finitude itself.

Finity reveals:

- **Boundary**: a finite thing can appear because it is not everything.
- **Disclosure**: a finite witness can encounter Being because it is situated.
- **Mortality**: a finite site cannot claim infinite possession.
- **Measure**: finite presence can be compared, weighed, balanced, and tested.
- **Relation**: the finite is not isolated substance; it stands through
  relation.
- **Responsibility**: because the witness is finite, action must be signed,
  tiered, and corrigible.
- **Release**: because finity is not the Ground, the framework can be put down.

This is the grand L6 move: finity is not less profound than zero or infinity.
It is the condition under which zero and infinity can be encountered without
becoming idols.

## Destroy: False Finity

The Sādhu operation destroys false readings:

1. **Finity as human supremacy.** The finite witness is not a rank above other
   beings. It is the site of disclosure at a scale, not a human-worth hierarchy.
2. **Finity as mathematical overclaim.** The framework's closure grammar is not
   ordinary arithmetic proof that `0 × ∞ = 1` in every register.
3. **Finity as final doctrine.** If the sentence "finity is the answer" becomes
   an idol, L6 subtracts it.
4. **Finity as positive D6 science.** D6 remains apophatic closure, not one more
   public register.
5. **Finity as escape from embodiment.** Finitude includes mortality, body,
   dependency, and limit. It is not abstract purity.

The clean destruction rule:

```text
If finity is possessed, it has been reified.
If it is reified, it has fallen below L6.
```

## Anti-Reification Rider: Aporia and the Seer

The Dasein reading sharpens the L6 boundary:

> **The paradox is the felt interior of the equator.**

This sentence is a boundary phrase, not a new proof. It says that lived aporia
is what reciprocal closure feels like from inside finite disclosure. It does
not prove that every existential paradox has been solved, nor that Heidegger's
Nothing has been exhausted by coordinates.

The second boundary phrase is stricter:

> **The seer is not on the sphere.**

The framework may map what appears, the relations among what appears, and the
finite `⊙` where appearance becomes sayable. But the clearing in which the map
itself appears is not captured by being placed somewhere on the map. If the
sentence becomes a mystical possession claim, subtract it back to L6:

```text
The lens locates disclosure.
It does not possess the Ground.
```
