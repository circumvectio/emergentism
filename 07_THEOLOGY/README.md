# 07_THEOLOGY — Symbol Teaches and Dissolves

Theology is Emergentism's public-symbol translation surface. It receives
source truth from ontology, epistemology, methodology, cosmology, and axiology;
it does not govern them and cannot upgrade an evidence tier.

## Mandate

Symbols, stories, rites, and categories may help finite agents coordinate and
remember. Their valid use is reproducible, ungated, contestable, discardable,
and self-dissolving: success means the learner no longer needs the symbol.

Theology must not:

- identify a symbol with the territory;
- claim privileged access to Ground;
- convert role, caste, age, polarity, or developmental stage into human worth;
- demand belief, sacrifice, obedience, or membership;
- conceal payer, beneficiary, custody, or consequence;
- turn a private legal or financial convention into spiritual or AI-work
  authority.

## Stewardship boundary

Symbolic coordination is permitted only within the Justice envelope: informed
consent, accountable authorization for consequential acts, named custody,
visible payer and beneficiary, reversibility where possible, contest, remedy,
and Grace Exit. Ordinary reading, comparison, drafting, and criticism require
no special signature beyond the user's scope and tool permissions.

## Claim boundaries

- Symbolic correspondences are `[I]`, not demonstrations of historical or
  physical identity.
- Coordination claims are `[S]` only within explicit premises.
- Civilizational and noosphere-scale projections remain `[C]`.
- `φν=1` is an analytic chart identity. It does not prove ontology, ethics, or
  a conservation law.
- A myth may carry a pattern without being evidence that the pattern is
  universal.

## Kill criteria

A theological surface must be repaired or withdrawn if its value depends on
exclusive authority, hidden coercion, unmarked tier inflation, a branded
organization, or a claim that cannot survive removal of its symbol.
