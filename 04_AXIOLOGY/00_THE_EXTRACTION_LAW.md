# The Extraction Law

## One line

> **Extraction can create local gain by transferring cost to another bearer.
> It becomes self-undermining only under stated closure, regeneration, and
> dependency conditions.**

That is the strongest honest form. Extraction is neither mathematically
impossible nor universally self-terminating. Predators, parasites, monopolies,
and one-shot defectors can benefit. The moral objection comes from the chosen
Justice envelope, not from pretending those gains never occur.

## Definition `[S]`

For action `a`, payer `p`, and beneficiary `b`, first name the neutral sign
class:

\[
\operatorname{AsymmetricCostTransfer}(a;p,b)
\iff
\Delta_TW_b(a)>0
\land
\Delta_TW_p(a)<0.
\]

Extraction is the unauthorized or concealed subclass:

\[
\operatorname{Extractive}(a;p,b)
\iff
\operatorname{AsymmetricCostTransfer}(a;p,b)
\land \neg\operatorname{AuthorizedCost}(a;p,b).
\]

The retrospective form substitutes receipted deltas and
`AuthorizedCost^R(q,r;p,b)`. A complete, competent, revocable, disclosed
authorization can make the same sign pattern a voluntary sacrifice; it cannot
launder hidden third-party harm. The audit must name both sides, payer,
beneficiary, time horizon, boundary, authorization, and uncertainty.

No scalar sum across incommensurable bearers is licensed. A gain to one side
does not cancel destruction of the other.

## Conditional substrate result `[S]`

Let substrate stock `S(t)` regenerate at rate `R(S)` and be depleted by
extraction `E(t)`:

\[
\dot S(t)=R(S(t))-E(t).
\]

If, over a declared interval `[t₀,t₁]`,

1. the arena is closed to fresh substrate and exit,
2. `E(t)-R(S(t))≥ε>0`, and
3. holding other inputs fixed, extractor power `Q(t)=f(S(t))` with `f`
   strictly increasing over the observed stock range,

then `S(t₁)≤S(t₀)-ε(t₁-t₀)`, and the substrate-supported component of `Q`
falls over that interval. “Eventual collapse” follows only if these conditions
persist long enough to cross a separately declared viability threshold. This
is a conditional dynamical consequence, not a theorem about all extraction.
Open boundaries, replacement hosts, offsetting inputs, weak dependence, short
horizons, or regenerative taking can defeat the result.

The generic constraint rule still holds:

\[
\operatorname{support}(K_X^C)\subseteq\operatorname{support}(K_X).
\]

Higher-level constraints may reweight lower-law-admissible trajectories; they
do not manufacture forbidden ones.

## Justice gate `[I/S]`

Emergentism refuses actions that raise one side by destroying the other. The
admissible set is:

\[
\mathcal A_J^R=
\{a:J^R(a)\land
\forall b\in B(a),\;\Delta_T^R W_b(a)\ge0\}.
\]

Here `B(a)` is the complete affected-bearer set, including `i`, `H`, payers,
beneficiaries, and exposed third parties. This is a normative choice `[I]` made
operational through structural tests `[S]`. It is not derived from `φν=1`,
balance geometry, natural selection, or a claim that injustice always loses.

The Justice envelope requires:

- individual and whole identified;
- payer and beneficiary visible;
- informed consent;
- custody and authorization named;
- reversibility and irreversibility disclosed;
- contest, remedy, and Grace Exit;
- commitment and outcome receipts;
- no aggregate laundering.

## Predation, sacrifice, and immune response

Taking is not one undifferentiated class.

- **Predation/parasitism:** benefits one bearer by degrading another without a
  valid authorized-cost relation; extractive under the selected boundary.
- **Regenerative harvest:** may be admissible if the substrate remains
  non-decreasing over the relevant horizon and all Justice conditions hold.
- **Immune response:** constrains or destroys a threat to protect a bearer; it
  still owes necessity, proportionality, evidence, and contest where possible.
- **Voluntary sacrifice:** a competent bearer knowingly accepts a disclosed,
  revocable cost for another under `AuthorizedCost^R`. It is an asymmetric
  costly transfer but not extraction, never strict syntropy, and never a
  collective's proof of the sacrificer's virtue.

## Empirical program `[C]`

For any claimed extraction dynamic, measure:

1. the actual bearer and substrate boundary;
2. local and long-horizon changes separately;
3. regeneration versus depletion;
4. dependence of extractor power on the substrate;
5. access to replacement substrate or exit;
6. externalized and delayed costs;
7. intervention effects, not narrative similarity alone.

The general hypothesis that durable power tends to require non-destructive
substrate relations remains `[C]` until tested across preregistered domains.

## Kill criteria

1. **Conditional result:** killed by a valid counterexample satisfying all
   three closure/depletion/dependency premises while substrate and dependent
   extractor power do not fall.
2. **Boundary classification:** revised if payer, beneficiary, or time horizon
   changes the sign pattern.
3. **Justice application:** rejected if it hides a bearer, coerces sacrifice,
   or uses aggregate gain to erase harm.
4. **Universal extension:** killed by persistent extraction with no substrate
   cost inside the stated boundary and horizon.
5. **Geometry inflation:** any derivation of this law from the reciprocal chart
   identity alone is invalid.
