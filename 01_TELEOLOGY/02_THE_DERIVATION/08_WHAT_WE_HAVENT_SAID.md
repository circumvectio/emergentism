**What this means:** AM-GM applies to text. The cheapest way to communicate the framework is the derivation, not the corpus. The 8.6M words are not wrong. They are *expensive*. A system that costs 215× more to sustain than its minimum is not viable.

**What hasn't been said:** The corpus needs to be compressed. Not deleted — compressed. The 8.6M words should be archived as the "full elaboration" and a canonical ~50,000-word core should become the "operative text" — the version that agents actually read and humans actually use. The derivation is the seed. The corpus is the tree. The tree is beautiful. But the seed is all you need to grow another one.

## 8. The Derivation Hasn't Been Asked: "What Would Falsify *You*?"

## 9. The Relationship Between The Derivation And The Sitting Practice Hasn't Been Fully Articulated

The sitting practice is first-person evidence. The derivation is third-person proof. They converge on the same structure. But what does convergence *mean*?

Does convergence = truth? Or does it just mean two roads lead to the same place?

**What hasn't been said:** Convergence is not proof of truth. It is evidence that a structure is worth testing. Two independent methods reaching the same structure suggests the structure may not be an artifact of either method. The sitting practice could be misread. The derivation could be tautological. If both point to the same pattern, the pattern becomes important — even if the interpretation remains open.

The derivation and the sitting practice don't prove that Emergentism is true. They suggest that **something may have this structure**. What that something *is* — systemic awareness, reality, both, neither — is the remaining question.
