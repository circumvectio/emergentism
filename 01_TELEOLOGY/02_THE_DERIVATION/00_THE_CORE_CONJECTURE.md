## 1. One sentence

> Finite agents and some adaptive systems may tend to preserve or widen their
> viable option cones; the Emergentist normative proposal is to pursue durable
> **mutual** option-cone widening under Beauty, Truth, and Justice.

The first clause is empirical `[C]`. The second is a declared value orientation
`[I]`. Neither is proved by the reciprocal chart.

## 2. Definitions

### Physical admissibility

Let `ℋ_phys(X_t)` be the histories physically admissible from actual state
`X_t` under a named physical model.

### Agent option cone

\[
\operatorname{OptionCone}_x(t)
=\{h\in\mathcal H_{\mathrm{phys}}(X_t):
x\text{ can model, distinguish, rank, coordinate, and reach }h\}.
\]

The option cone is agent-relative and remains inside physical admissibility. It
is not a relativistic light cone and cannot exceed `c`. Authorization defines
the separate normative subset
`AuthorizedOptionCone_x(t)⊆OptionCone_x(t)`; it does not erase unauthorized,
coerced, criminal, or accidental actions from causal description.

### Durable potential

For a declared measure `P_x(t)`:

\[
W_x(T)=\int_0^T P_x(t)\,dt.
\]

Emergentism may select `P_x=Φ_xV_x` as a normalized conjunctive instance, but
the product is not unique and must be compared with alternative aggregators.

### Viable widening

An option-cone increase is **viable** only if counted histories remain
physically reachable, actual costs are included, and later receipts do not show
that the added options were purchased by destroying the agent's own supporting
conditions.

The normative form adds Justice: the widening must preserve each independent
bearer, expose payer and beneficiary, and pass consent, custody, contest,
authorization, and exit tests.

## 3. Three different claims

### C1 — reflective agent claim `[C]`

Agents capable of modeling counterfactual futures will, under some conditions,
prefer actions that preserve or enlarge a measure of later attainable and
distinguishable outcomes.

This has cognates in empowerment, causal-entropic models, planning, and real
options. It is not universal: agents can be myopic, self-destructive,
misinformed, coerced, committed to terminal values, or willing to sacrifice.

### C2 — adaptive-system claim `[C]`

Across specified environments, selection or control may favor systems that
retain adaptive degrees of freedom and avoid irreversible loss of viability.

This is an as-if teleonomic claim. It does not require the system to represent
a future, possess a goal, or be conscious. Natural selection, homeostasis,
allostasis, and control can generate future-looking descriptions without a
future event causing the present.

### C3 — universal extension `[C]`

The strongest poetic conjecture says reality as a whole tends toward viable
completion or increasing attainable possibility. No current derivation or
evidence in the corpus establishes this universal quantifier. It remains
optional, removable, and unable to carry ethics or physics.

`F5` or **Will to Potential** may name this poetic/teleological extension. It is
not an established fifth physical interaction, a force slot derived from D5, or
a substitute for specific mechanisms.

\[
M\star A:
\operatorname{ModelState}\times\operatorname{PhysicallyFeasibleActionField}
\to\operatorname{ActionWeights}.
\]

Changing a represented possible future can change present action weights while
actual means, authorization, observations, and incentives are controlled. The

retrocausality.

Authorization defines a separate normative subset of physical feasibility. It
does not make an unauthorized attempt causally impossible or exempt it from
receipts.

The decisive test intervenes on represented futures and observes present
selection distributions.

## 5. Justice-constrained objective

Let `i` be an acting part and `H` its sustaining whole. Prospective selection
uses modeled changes:

\[
\mathcal A_J=
\{a:J(a;i,H)
\land\widehat{\Delta}_TW_i(a;M_t)\ge0
\land\widehat{\Delta}_TW_H(a;M_t)\ge0
\land\forall b\in\mathcal B(a),\widehat{\Delta}_TW_b(a;M_t)\ge0\}.
\]

If the field is finite, or compact with an upper-semicontinuous objective,
Power-Max may choose exactly:

\[
a^\star\in\operatorname*{arg\,max}_{a\in\mathcal A_J}
\mathbb E_{M_t}[W_i(T)\mid a].
\]

Otherwise, for a nonempty field with finite supremum, it must declare
`ε>0` and choose an `ε`-optimal action. A nonempty open field need not attain
its supremum.

Here `\mathcal B(a)` is the complete finite set of materially affected bearers;
omitting or harming a third bearer makes `J` false. This is the project's
chosen compass, not a law of behavior. Justice defines
the action field before optimization. Aggregate gain cannot compensate for a
negative bearer, and voluntary sacrifice stays a separate class outside the
ordinary nonnegative frontier.

After action, commitment and outcome receipts determine whether the move was
actually moral, ethical, syntropic, extractive, or a failed prediction.

## 8. Discriminating research program

### Test A — represented-future intervention

Hold actual means, observations, authorization, and current incentives fixed.
Vary only future content in the agent's model. Measure the change in action
weights and later receipts.

### Test B — option preservation

Compare an option-preserving model against simpler reward, survival, energy,
fitness, and habit baselines. Pre-register the option metric and test out of
sample.

### Test C — aggregator comparison

Compare product, minimum, harmonic, and Cobb–Douglas conjunctive forms. Include
cases where they reverse rankings. The selected product survives only where it
predicts receipts competitively.

### Test D — Justice intervention

Compare otherwise similar decision systems with and without enforced payer,
beneficiary, authorization, consent, custody, contest, exit, and separate
receipt visibility. Measure extraction, durability, and mutual option-cone
effects.

### Test E — cross-scale limit

Identify precisely which mechanisms recur across organisms, agents,
institutions, and Egregoreotype candidates. A Rosetta match counts only when the
same intervention structure and prediction survive, not when vocabulary alone
can be aligned.

## 9. Kill criteria

Narrow or reject the conjecture for a scope when:

1. option-preserving models do not outperform simpler baselines;
2. more capable agents do not command more reachable and distinguishable
   histories under a non-gameable metric;
3. interventions on represented futures do not change present selection;
4. systems systematically choose irreversible option loss even when model,
   means, and coercion are controlled;
5. the Justice-constrained objective produces worse receipted durability and
   contestability than a named rival;
6. product aggregation predicts worse than alternatives;
7. cross-domain mechanisms differ enough that the shared grammar adds no
   discriminator;
8. the universal claim requires unfalsifiable redefinition of "option" or
   "completion."

The universal extension is rejected if it cannot be made more precise than
"whatever persists counts as viable completion."
