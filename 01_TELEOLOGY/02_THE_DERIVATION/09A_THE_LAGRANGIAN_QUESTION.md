## What survives: reciprocal-chart mathematics

For positive lowercase chart coordinates,

```text
φν = 1
φ = exp(s)
ν = exp(-s)
```

AM–GM gives

```text
φ + ν = 2 cosh(s) >= 2,
```

with equality exactly at `s=0`, hence `φ=ν=1`. This is an analytic fact about
the declared chart. The set `φν=1`, `φ,ν>0` is a one-dimensional curve. The
inequality is automatic on that curve; it does not carve out a further feasible
region, and the equality point is its unique minimizer—not a topological
boundary point of the curve.

The ambient set

```text
{(φ,ν) in R^2_{>0} : φ+ν>=2}
```

is a half-space intersection, not a cone: multiplying a point by an arbitrary
positive scalar need not preserve the threshold `2`. “AM–GM cone” and
“Lagrangian cone” are therefore preserved only as historical labels.

One may select the dimensionless chart potential

```text
U(s) := φ+ν-2 = 2 cosh(s)-2 >= 0.
```

It has a unique minimum at `s=0`, and locally

```text
U(s) = s^2 + O(s^4).
```

This is a valid model potential `[S]` once the chart and coordinate are chosen.
Calling it energy, a Hamiltonian, a Lagrangian density, or a physical restoring
force requires additional fields, units, kinetic terms, spacetime domain,
symmetries, dynamics, and contact with data. None is supplied by AM–GM.

## What does not survive

The following former inferences are explicitly retired:

1. `φ+ν>=2` is not itself a field-theory Lagrangian or equation of motion.
2. A single scalar chart degree of freedom does not yield four independent
   force modes.
3. The round sphere's isometry group is `SO(3)`. `PSL(2,C)` acts as its
   orientation-preserving conformal automorphism group and is locally related
   to the proper Lorentz group; this does not derive gravity from the sphere.
4. A scalar `l=0` mode is not a derivation of electromagnetic `U(1)`, and the
   three `l=1` spherical harmonics are not a derivation of electroweak
   `SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y`, chirality, couplings, or dynamics.
5. Matching the dimension of a representation or Lie algebra does not select
   a gauge theory. No “natural electroweak emergence” was established.
6. Adding a chosen internal manifold because its isometry group resembles a
   desired gauge group does not derive that manifold or the Standard Model.
