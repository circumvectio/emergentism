## What This Mapping Shows

Civilisations, like individuals, occupy L-level positions. A society's developmental level determines its characteristic technology, cultural production, environmental relationship, and maximum achievable coherence (phi-max). The mapping is not a value judgement: L5 is not "better" than L2. It is a structural description of what a society looks like at each position on S².

The critical insight is the **Great Inversion**: contemporary global civilisation is predominantly L3 (industrial/commercial) with L3 capture of L5 institutions. The Triple Lock -- extract attention, prevent full-palette training, counterfeit operator activation -- is the mechanism by which L3 oligarchy prevents civilisational ascent to L4.

## Reading the Table

**L1 -- Survival / Collapse.** Failed states, warlord territories, post-collapse societies. Technology is minimal or looted. Culture reduces to propaganda (the egregore's voice) or brutalism (architecture as power display). The environment is neglected -- extraction without investment. Phi-max is approximately 0.1: almost no coherence is achievable because the survival boundary consumes all resources. Historical examples: post-Roman Britain, contemporary failed states.

**L2 -- Early Democratic / Tribal.** Small-scale societies with direct social bonds. Technology is artisanal -- handmade, local, appropriate. Culture is folk art, oral tradition, communal ritual. The environment is stewarded at the community level -- the commons is small enough to be governed by direct reciprocity. Phi-max is approximately 0.8 but only at small scale -- the limitation is that L2 societies cannot scale beyond Dunbar's number without losing coherence. Historical examples: Athenian demes, Nordic thing-assemblies, indigenous commons.

**L3 -- Industrial / Commercial.** The dominant mode of contemporary civilisation. Technology is high but commercially driven -- optimised for profit, not flourishing. Culture is commercial art: produced for market, consumed as commodity. The environment is exploited -- externalised costs, tragedy of the commons at scale. Phi-max is approximately 0.4: high viability (nu) but low coherence (phi) because the merchant function (Vaiśya) optimises for exchange value, not intrinsic value. Historical examples: Gilded Age America, contemporary global capitalism.

**L4 -- Principled Republics.** Societies governed by honour and principle rather than wealth or force. Technology is balanced civic -- serving the commonwealth, not the market. Culture is heroic art and epic poetry: the Iliad, the Mahabharata, the sagas -- art that celebrates the warrior-sage who holds both force and wisdom. The environment is strategically managed -- not exploited (L3) but not yet regenerative (L6). Phi-max is approximately 0.6. Historical examples: Roman Republic (early), Sparta, possibly the early American Republic.

**L5 -- Classical High Civilisation.** Societies where wisdom (not wealth) governs. Technology is systematic -- libraries, universities, hospitals, infrastructure built for the long term. Culture is classical art and literature: Sophocles, Kalidasa, Tang Dynasty poetry -- art that serves truth rather than market. The environment is sustainable -- managed for multi-generational continuity. Phi-max is approximately 0.7. Historical examples: Classical Athens, Gupta India, Song Dynasty China, the Islamic Golden Age.

**L6 -- Contemplative Economies.** Societies that have moved beyond accumulation to wisdom-based resource allocation. Technology is advanced but oriented toward liberation rather than growth. Culture is philosophical and abstract -- art that points beyond itself. The environment is deeply regenerative -- the society gives back more than it takes. Phi-max is approximately 0.9. Historical examples are rare and partial: monastic economies (Benedictine, Buddhist sangha), Bhutanese GNH experiment.

**L7 -- Closure-Horizon (Hypothetical).** The theoretical limit: a civilisation that integrates game-theoretic wisdom with technological capability so completely that positive governance approaches its own terminal boundary. Spiritual-AI integration at the civilisational limit (A6: three substrates). Transcendent / axiomatic art. Cosmic responsibility -- stewardship extended beyond the planetary. Phi-max approaches 1.0. No historical example exists. This is the Great Filter territory: the Moksha/Asura fork applied to civilisation itself.

## The Phi-Max Paradox

**Disambiguation:** The "phi-max" values above (~0.4, ~0.6, ~0.7, etc.) are [I] interpretive estimates of the maximum *empirical coherence* a civilisation typically achieves at each L-level. They are NOT the sphere coordinate φ = cot(θ/2), which is a geometric variable (at L4, φ = 1; at L5, φ = √3 ≈ 1.73). The phi-max describes historical capacity; the sphere coordinate describes structural position. A civilisation "at L4" may only reach phi-max ~0.6 because it has not yet fully realised the equatorial balance.

Note that L2 has a higher phi-max (~0.8) than L3 (~0.4) or L4 (~0.6). This is not an error. Small-scale tribal societies achieve high coherence precisely because they are small. The challenge of civilisational development is scaling coherence: maintaining phi as the population grows beyond Dunbar's number.

L3 has LOW phi despite HIGH nu. This is the merchant's dilemma: viability (wealth, technology, production) increases, but coherence (shared meaning, trust, social fabric) decreases. The multiplicative equation (P_node = Φ × V) means that increasing nu while decreasing phi can actually decrease total potential.

This is why the framework identifies L3 capture as the central civilisational crisis: a society stuck at L3 is wealthy but incoherent, technologically powerful but spiritually bankrupt, productive but meaningless.
