# RESEARCH BRIEF: The Three-Stage Process Psychology Convergence Claim

**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Scope:** Empirical assessment of the claim that Piaget, Kohlberg, and Maslow independently converge on a 7-level developmental structure
**Method:** Systematic web research across published literature, meta-analyses, and cross-cultural studies
**Author:** Research compilation for Emergentism framework audit

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The convergence claim is **partially supported but significantly overstated** in its current form. The three theories do share a sequential, stage-based structure and were developed in intellectual contact with each other (Kohlberg built explicitly on Piaget). However, aligning them into exactly seven rows requires extension beyond what each theorist originally proposed -- Piaget has 4 stages, Kohlberg has 6 (in 3 levels), and Maslow has 5 (later 7-8). The alignment is the framework's own interpretive contribution, not something found in the published literature. Additional developmental theories (Kegan, Loevinger, Cook-Greuter, Commons) do show structural convergence, which strengthens the claim that hierarchical development is real -- but the specific number 7 is not a finding; it is a design choice.

**Recommended evidence tier: [S] Structural** for the convergence pattern itself. Downgrade to [I] Interpretive for the specific 7-level alignment and for L5-L7 mappings.

## 1. IS THE CONVERGENCE REAL OR FORCED?

### 1.1 What the published literature says

**No published paper maps Piaget, Kohlberg, and Maslow onto exactly seven shared levels.** The three-way convergence is the framework's own contribution.

What does exist in the literature:

- **Piaget-Kohlberg connection:** Well-established. Kohlberg built directly on Piaget's work. Both propose sequential, qualitatively distinct stages where progression is invariant in order and driven by active child-centered processes. Kohlberg explicitly posited that moral reasoning develops as a function of cognitive growth (Kohlberg & Hersh, 1977). This is not independent convergence -- it is intellectual descent. [B]

- **Piaget-Maslow connection:** Oliver Kress published "A new approach to cognitive development: ontogenesis and the process of initiation," connecting Piaget's genetic data science to Maslow's concept of self-actualization. However, this is a single paper exploring the connection, not a demonstration of structural identity. [I]

- **Comparative stage charts:** Multiple educational resources (Gross, HuffPost; Washington University developmental stages table) place Freud, Erikson, Piaget, and Kohlberg side-by-side. These comparisons align stages by approximate age range, not by structural equivalence. They typically compare 4 theorists, not the specific Piaget-Kohlberg-Maslow triad. [S]

- **Neo-Piagetian convergence research:** The structural developmental psychology literature (Commons et al., 1998; Cook-Greuter, 1999; Loevinger & Blasi, 1976) shows that most stage sequences "map onto one another," suggesting structural alignment across frameworks. This is the strongest published support for a general convergence claim. [S]

### 1.2 The stage-count problem

The three theories have DIFFERENT native stage counts:

| Theory | Native stages | Required for 7-level mapping |
|--------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| Piaget | 4 (sensorimotor, preoperational, concrete operational, formal operational) | Extend to "formal+," "post-formal," and "beyond formal" -- none of which Piaget himself defined |
| Kohlberg | 6 stages in 3 levels (pre-conventional, conventional, post-conventional) | Collapse some, extend "universal principles" into L6-L7 territory |
| Maslow | 5 (original) / 7-8 (late revision with cognitive, aesthetic, self-transcendence) | The late revision to 7-8 levels actually fits best, but this version is the least empirically validated |

**Verdict:** The convergence at L1-L4 is defensible. The convergence at L5-L7 requires extensions that go beyond what the original theorists established. This should be marked honestly.

### 1.3 Intellectual independence

The three theories are NOT fully independent:

- Kohlberg studied under Lawrence Kohlberg at Harvard, who was deeply influenced by Piaget. Kohlberg's moral stages presuppose Piaget's cognitive stages as necessary (but not sufficient) conditions.
- Maslow operated in a different tradition (humanistic psychology) and did not build on Piaget. The Maslow-Piaget connection is weaker and more genuinely independent.
- All three worked within mid-20th-century Western academic psychology, sharing cultural assumptions about development as upward progress.

## 2. MODERN STATUS OF EACH THEORY

### 2.1 Piaget -- Still foundational, significantly revised

**Current status: [B] Established as foundational, but with major qualifications.**

Piaget's theory remains a cornerstone of developmental psychology with over 20,000 texts based on his work. However:

- **Underestimation of infant competence:** Infants demonstrate capacities far earlier than Piaget predicted. Object permanence, numerical reasoning, and causal understanding appear months before his stages predict (Baillargeon, 1987; Spelke, 1992).
- **Inconsistent staging:** Children at a given "stage" show diverse thinking across tasks, contradicting the prediction that thinking should be similar across tasks within a stage.
- **Cultural and social factors neglected:** Piaget studied his own three children -- a severe sampling limitation. He underweighted the role of instruction and social interaction (Vygotsky's critique).
- **Children learn faster with instruction:** Brief instruction can teach concepts Piaget considered developmentally impossible at a given age.
- **A 2023 PMC article on "fuzzy structuralism"** proposes that stages are better understood as probabilistic patterns than rigid thresholds.

**For the Three-Stage Process:** Piaget's 4 stages are [B] established. The extension to "post-formal" and beyond is [I] interpretive -- Piaget never described these stages himself.

### 2.2 Kohlberg -- Under significant pressure

**Current status: [B] for the basic stage sequence, [I] for universality of higher stages.**

- **The 2024 Kohlberg Memorial Lecture** (Moody-Adams, 2025, Journal of Moral Education) identified three fundamental flaws: Kohlberg fails to show that moral theory study is prerequisite for moral maturity; he neglects sympathetic imagination; and he provides no compelling evidence the path is linear, invariant, or hierarchical.
- **Judgment-action gap:** People's moral reasoning does not reliably predict their moral behavior. This is a fundamental problem for any theory claiming moral "stages."
- **Cross-cultural findings (Snarey, 1985):** Review of 45 studies in 27 countries found the basic stage sequence holds cross-culturally, BUT: (a) urban populations reach higher stages than traditional/folk societies, (b) the highest stages (post-conventional) are consistently absent in some cultural groups, suggesting cultural specificity rather than universality.
- **Gender bias (Gilligan, 1982):** Kohlberg's justice-orientation may reflect male moral reasoning; Gilligan argued for an equally valid "ethics of care" orientation that Kohlberg's framework cannot capture.
- **Meta-analyses of educational interventions** (2020-2025) do show that moral reasoning can be promoted through Kohlberg's stages, suggesting the stages have practical utility even if theoretically incomplete.

**For the Three-Stage Process:** L1-L4 Kohlberg mappings are [B/S]. L5-L7 are [I] -- Kohlberg's own Stage 6 was empirically nearly empty, and he considered dropping it late in his career.

### 2.3 Maslow -- Widely known, weakly validated

**Current status: [I] Interpretive. Possibly the weakest of the three.**

- **Lack of empirical grounding:** A 1976 review found little evidence for the specific ranking or the existence of a definite hierarchy at all. This has not been remedied in subsequent decades.
- **Non-sequential need fulfillment:** The Tay & Diener (2011) study of 60,865 participants across 123 countries using Gallup World Poll data found that needs are universal and important for well-being, BUT people pursue multiple needs simultaneously and do not follow a strict hierarchy. Basic needs do not need to be fully met before higher needs are pursued.
- **Methodological problems:** Maslow relied on personal observations and clinical experiences rather than rigorous scientific method. His sample of "self-actualized" individuals was hand-picked and overwhelmingly Western (Lincoln, Einstein, Eleanor Roosevelt).
- **Self-transcendence is genuine but late:** Maslow did amend his model in 1969 to place self-transcendence above self-actualization (Koltko-Rivera, 2006, Review of General Psychology). This supports the L6-L7 extension. However, this late revision is the least empirically validated part of the theory.
- **Cultural bias:** In collectivist cultures, belonging may be foundational rather than mid-hierarchy. A "double-Y model" (Gambrel & Cianci) proposes separate need hierarchies for individualist and collectivist societies.
- **Oversimplification:** Human motivation is far more complex than a single hierarchy can capture.

**For the Three-Stage Process:** Maslow's categories as descriptors of motivational territory are [S]. The hierarchical ordering is [I]. The self-transcendence extension is [C] conjecture based on Maslow's unpublished late writings.

## 3. ADDITIONAL DEVELOPMENTAL THEORIES -- DO THEY CONVERGE?

### 3.1 Robert Kegan -- Constructive Developmental Theory (5 stages)

**Verdict: Partially supports convergence.**

Kegan describes 5 stages of adult meaning-making based on subject-object transitions. Research using the Subject-Object Interview (Lahey et al., 1988) shows:

- 43-46% of adults age 19-55 are at Stage 3 or 3/4 transition
- Most adults plateau at Stage 3 (socialized mind)
- Stage 5 (inter-individual) is extremely rare

Kegan's 5 stages map reasonably to L2-L6 of the framework but do NOT have a clean 7-level structure. His stages 1-5 could be aligned, but it requires compressing or expanding. The subject-object mechanism is empirically supported. [B] for the mechanism, [S] for alignment to 7 levels.

### 3.2 Jane Loevinger -- Ego Development (9 stages)

**Verdict: Supports the existence of hierarchical development; complicates the 7-level claim.**

Loevinger identified 9 sequential stages measured by the Washington University Sentence Completion Test (WUSCT). This instrument has:

- Strong interrater reliability across many populations
- Demonstrated longitudinal growth (~1.5 stages over 9 years)
- Been validated cross-culturally

**The problem for the framework:** Loevinger has 9 levels, not 7. You can collapse some (e.g., merge impulsive/self-protective, or merge individualistic/autonomous), but any such compression is an interpretive choice. [S]

### 3.3 Susanne Cook-Greuter -- Ego Development Extended (9 levels)

**Verdict: Strongest support for post-conventional stages; confirms something exists above L4.**

Cook-Greuter (Harvard doctoral thesis, 1999) extended Loevinger's work into "post-autonomous" territory. Key findings:

- Post-conventional stages constitute ~10% of the US adult population
- The "Construct-aware" stage (her Stage 9) involves awareness of the constructed nature of reality itself, and of ego as a process
- This maps well to the framework's L6 (axiomatic/dissolution)

However, Cook-Greuter's 9 levels again resist compression to exactly 7. [S] for the existence of post-conventional development. [I] for specific alignment to the 7-level scheme.

### 3.4 Commons -- Model of Hierarchical Complexity (15+ orders)

**Verdict: Most empirically rigorous stage model. Confirms that hierarchical development is real. Does NOT converge on 7.**

The MHC (Commons et al., 1998) uses mathematical principles to define task complexity. Empirical findings:

- r = 0.92 correlation between task complexity and performance stage
- Rasch analysis shows genuine gaps between stages (they are not continuous)
- Stages are equally spaced mathematically
- 15 orders of hierarchical complexity, with the last 4 being "post-formal"

**This is the strongest empirical evidence that developmental stages are real, discrete, and sequential.** However, 15 orders is very different from 7 levels. The framework would need to argue that its 7 levels represent a meaningful grouping of these finer-grained stages. [B] for the reality of stages. [I] for mapping to 7.

### 3.5 James Fowler -- Stages of Faith (7 stages including Stage 0)

**Verdict: Directly has 7 stages. Weak empirical support.**

Fowler's 7 stages (0-6) align most naturally with the 7-level framework. However:

- Empirical evidence has NOT supported the assumption that development is linear and irreversible
- Multiple stages may be apparent within the same person simultaneously
- Streib's "Religious Styles" model has been proposed as a more empirically defensible replacement
- The Faith Development Scale (Leak) provides some measurement validity

Fowler explicitly drew on Piaget and Kohlberg, so this is NOT independent convergence. [I]

### 3.6 Spiral Dynamics -- (8 value memes)

**Verdict: No scientific validity. Should NOT be used as supporting evidence.**

- [I] This brief did not find Graves' original research in a peer-reviewed journal (appeared in The Futurist, 1974)
- [I] This brief did not find independent replication of the auditing method
- Original data reportedly lost, precluding verification
- No standardized, psychometrically validated assessment instrument exists
- Patrick Vermeren: Spiral Dynamics is "in blatant contradiction to scientific facts"
- Critics dismissed as expressing "lower-level memes" -- a textbook unfalsifiable claim (Popper's criterion for non-science)

**The framework should distance itself from Spiral Dynamics.** Using it as supporting evidence weakens the overall case. [C] at best.

## 4. THE "MAGIC NUMBER 7" -- STRUCTURAL OR COGNITIVE?

### 4.1 Miller's original finding

George Miller's (1956) "The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two" proposed that working memory holds ~7 chunks. Key updates:

- Miller himself used the number semi-humorously; it is not "magical"
- Subsequent research (Cowan, 2001) revised the estimate downward to **4 +/- 1**
- The number refers to working memory capacity, not to structural features of reality

### 4.2 Does 7 reflect a structural constraint on development?

**The evidence suggests: NO.** Different developmental theories produce different numbers of stages:

| Theory | Stage count |
|--------|------------|
| Piaget | 4 |
| Kohlberg | 6 (in 3 levels) |
| Maslow | 5 (original) / 7-8 (late) |
| Kegan | 5 |
| Loevinger | 9 |
| Cook-Greuter | 9 |
| Commons (MHC) | 15 |
| Fowler | 7 |
| Erikson | 8 |

The range is 4-15. There is no natural clustering around 7. The framework's choice of 7 is defensible as a useful granularity, but it is a design choice, not a discovered constant.

### 4.3 What IS structurally constrained?

The evidence does support that:

- Development IS hierarchical (Commons MHC: r = 0.92)
- Stages ARE discrete, not continuous (Rasch analysis shows gaps)
- The sequence IS invariant (you cannot skip stages)
- Higher stages integrate lower ones (subject-object transitions in Kegan)

These are structural findings. The NUMBER of stages is a function of measurement granularity.

## 5. CROSS-CULTURAL VALIDITY

### 5.1 Piaget

- Basic sensorimotor and concrete operational stages replicate cross-culturally
- Formal operational thought appears culturally variable -- not all cultures produce it universally
- Cultural and instructional factors play a larger role than Piaget acknowledged

### 5.2 Kohlberg

- **Snarey (1985):** 45 studies, 27 countries. Basic stage sequence confirmed. But:
  - Higher stages are more common in urban/industrial societies
  - Post-conventional stages are absent in some cultural groups
  - Snarey proposed "culturally inclusive elaboration" of highest stages
- **Gibbs (revisionist):** Basic moral judgment development converges across cultures; post-conventional stages remain debated
- **Gilligan (1982):** Justice-only framework misses care-oriented moral reasoning
- **2021 PMC review:** Multimethod convergence found for common moral values and basic moral judgment stages across cultures, despite ongoing questions

### 5.3 Maslow

- **Tay & Diener (2011):** 123 countries, 60,865 participants. Needs are universal. Hierarchy is not.
- **Collectivist cultures:** Belonging may be foundational, not mid-hierarchy. Self-actualization as individual achievement is distinctly Western.
- **Alternative models:** Double-Y model separates individualist and collectivist need hierarchies

### 5.4 Overall cross-cultural verdict

**Lower stages (L1-L3) have reasonable cross-cultural validity.** Basic cognitive development, pre-conventional morality, and physiological/safety/belonging needs appear universal.

**Higher stages (L5-L7) are culturally specific in their expression and may reflect Western, individualistic, WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) assumptions.** The framework should acknowledge this limitation explicitly.

## 8. RECOMMENDED CHANGES TO ROSETTA_PSYCHOLOGY.md

1. **Acknowledge that Kohlberg built on Piaget.** The convergence is not fully "independent." Two of three theories share intellectual lineage. Maslow is the genuinely independent one.

2. **Mark L5-L7 explicitly as framework extensions.** Piaget did not describe post-formal cognition. Kohlberg's Stage 6 was empirically nearly empty. Maslow's self-transcendence comes from unpublished late writings.

3. **Add the additional theories as supporting evidence.** Kegan (5 stages), Loevinger (9 stages), Cook-Greuter (9 stages), and especially Commons (MHC, 15 orders) all confirm that hierarchical development is real and sequential. This strengthens the structural claim even though they do not converge on exactly 7.

4. **Add the cross-cultural limitation.** The mapping is most valid for WEIRD populations. Collectivist cultures may organize motivational needs differently, and post-conventional moral stages are culturally specific.

5. **Reframe the evidence claim.** From "three established scales independently discovered the same structure" to "three major developmental traditions, two sharing intellectual lineage, describe qualitatively similar progressions that can be meaningfully aligned at a 7-level granularity -- though this specific granularity is the framework's contribution, not a finding inherent to the source theories."

6. **Distance from Spiral Dynamics.** If Spiral Dynamics appears anywhere as supporting evidence in the Rosetta Stone, it should be removed or marked [C] with a note about its lack of scientific validity.

## 9. THE HONEST POSITION

**What is strong:**
- Hierarchical development IS real. The MHC demonstrates it with r = 0.92 correlation and Rasch-verified gaps between stages. [B]
- The basic progression from pre-conventional to conventional to post-conventional appears in cognition, morality, and motivation. [B/S]
- Multiple independent research traditions (Piaget, Maslow, Kegan, Loevinger, Cook-Greuter, Commons) all find hierarchical, sequential, integrative development. [B]
- Something exists beyond conventional stages (L5+). Cook-Greuter's post-autonomous work confirms this. [S]

**What is weak:**
- The specific number 7 is not a structural constant. Theories range from 4 to 15 stages. [I]
- Piaget and Kohlberg are not independent -- they share lineage. [B]
- Maslow's hierarchy as a HIERARCHY (strict sequential ordering) is not supported by the largest cross-cultural study (Tay & Diener, N=60,865). [B against]
- L5-L7 mappings extend all three theories beyond what their originators established. [I]
- Cross-cultural validity degrades at higher levels. [S]

**The framework's genuine contribution:**
The alignment itself. Nobody else has mapped these three traditions onto a single 7-level structure anchored to a geometric object (S²). This is an original theoretical contribution. It should be marked [S] Structural -- a pattern observed and proposed, not a finding established by controlled experiment.
