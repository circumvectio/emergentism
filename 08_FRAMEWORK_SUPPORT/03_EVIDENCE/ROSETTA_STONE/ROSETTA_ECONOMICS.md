## 2. Reading the Rows

### L1 — Subsistence / Scarcity (Kali)
- **Problem:** Existence at the margin. No surplus. Every calorie is fought for.
- **Mechanism:** Extractive. Might makes right. The strongest takes.
- **Economic form:** Hunting-gathering in marginal environments; famine; war economies; slavery as extraction.
- **Pathology:** Tyranny of scarcity. The zero-sum trap.

### L2 — Gift Economy (Kālī — immune function)
- **Problem:** How do we prevent defection in a group with no surplus to tax?
- **Mechanism:** Social debt. The gift creates obligation. Reputation replaces currency.
- **Economic form:** Potlatch; kula ring; blood wealth; tribal redistribution.
- **Pathology:** Status competition through destruction; obligation traps; honor killings as debt enforcement.
- **Key insight:** The gift economy is NOT altruism. It is **immune surveillance via social debt.** Kālī cuts false coherence (cheaters lose reputation).

### L3 — Market Exchange (Kṛṣṇa — productive)
- **Problem:** How do we scale cooperation beyond kinship?
- **Mechanism:** Price as information. Voluntary exchange. Property rights.
- **Economic form:** Medieval markets; early capitalism; merchant banking; joint-stock companies.
- **Pathology:** Oligarchy. Wealth concentration. The merchant captures the state.
- **Key insight:** Markets build viability (ν) by enabling specialization. Kṛṣṇa gives capability to the other.

### L4 — Mechanism Design / Strategic Allocation (Arjuna)
- **Problem:** Markets fail (externalities, public goods, coordination problems). How do we design institutions that align individual and collective rationality?
- **Mechanism:** Mechanism design. Auction theory. Constitutional economics. Game-theoretic institution-building. K2-signed transactions (human validates every critical economic decision).
- **Economic form:** Myerson optimal auctions; Vickrey-Clarke-Groves mechanisms; market design (school choice, kidney exchange); Skyzai's SoResFi layer with φ-split (61.8/38.2).
- **Pathology:** Over-optimization. The planner's hubris. Mechanism design without mortal wisdom becomes technocracy.
- **Key insight:** This is the **equator of economics.** Not capitalism, not socialism, but **constitutional design** — the rules that let markets and planning coexist. Skyzai lives here: η = 0 (no extraction), K2 (human signs), K4 (grace exit).

### L5 — Planned / Information Economy (Brahmā)
- **Problem:** Information is the ultimate resource. How do we optimize at system scale?
- **Mechanism:** Algorithmic allocation. Centralized information processing. Knowledge hierarchies.
- **Economic form:** Soviet planning (failed); modern central banking; AI-driven supply chains; Google's ad market; Facebook's attention market.
- **Pathology:** The Great Inversion. Information power captures truth. The algorithm preserves the cage, not liberation. Brahmā stuck = overextension.
- **Key insight:** Planning works when information is complete and preferences are stable. It fails when information is local and tacit (Hayek). L5 is viable ONLY when L4 (constitutional constraints) is strong.

### L6 — Commons / Post-Scarcity (Śiva)
- **Problem:** Rival goods create scarcity artificially. What if we build anti-rival systems?
- **Mechanism:** Peer production. Non-exclusive access. Regenerative circularity. Dissolution of property boundaries.
- **Economic form:** Wikipedia; Linux; Creative Commons; circular economy; degrowth; basic income experiments.
- **Pathology:** Nihilism. "Everything should be free" → nothing gets maintained. The commons collapses without L4 governance.
- **Key insight:** Śiva dissolves false structure. Open source works when the "dissolution" is matched by L3 productive energy (corporate sponsorship, foundation funding). L6 without L3-L4 = tragedy of the commons.

### L7 — Gift / Transcendent Economy (Viṣṇu)
- **Problem:** Accounting itself is a limitation. What if circulation is the only function?
- **Mechanism:** Pure gift. No ledger. Dāna. Circulation without accumulation.
- **Economic form:** Monastery economies (Buddhist dāna); theoretical post-monetary systems; total-system integration where value is inseparable from flow.
- **Pathology:** Theocracy. Divine mandate applied to economics. "The economy will provide" = fanaticism. L7 without L6-L5 grounding = bypassing.
- **Key insight:** This is the boundary. Not a practical economy but a **limit concept.** Like the north pole of S² — you can approach it, but you cannot live there. The Ṛṣi seer glimpses L7 but returns to L4 to act.
