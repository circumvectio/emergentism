## 1. Mesoamerican mystery traditions

### 1.1 What is documented

Mesoamerican religious-cosmological systems are well-documented but show **NON-seven structural numerology**:

| Tradition | Documented structure | Match to 7? |
|---|---|---|
| **Aztec/Mexica cosmos** | 13 levels of heaven (Tonatiuh-Ihuicac through Omeyocan); 9 levels of underworld (Mictlān) | **No** — the canonical numerology is 13 + 9 = 22 |
| **Aztec ritual calendar** | 260-day Tonalpohualli (20 × 13); 365-day Xiuhpohualli (18 × 20 + 5); Calendar Round = 52 years | **No** — base numerologies are 13, 20, 18 |
| **Maya cosmos** | 13 heavens (Oxlahuntiku) + 9 underworlds (Bolontiku); Long Count cycles | **No** — 13 + 9 |
| **Maya calendar systems** | Tzolkin (260-day), Haab (365-day), Long Count (baktun cycles) | **No** — base numerologies are 13, 20, 18 |
| **Toltec/Nahua initiation** | Priestly castes: Tlamatini (sage), Tlamacazqui (priest), Tlatoani (ruler), Cuauhtli/Ocelotl (warrior orders) | **No** — caste structure (4-5 classes) |
| **Quetzalcoatl / Tezcatlipoca duality** | The Plumed Serpent and the Smoking Mirror as polar deities; four Tezcatlipocas at four cardinal directions | **No** — 4-fold quadripartite, not 7-fold |

### 1.2 The closest 7-fold candidate (interpretive)

Some scholars (e.g., Maffie, *Aztec Philosophy* 2014) note **the Aztec teotl-system architecture has a quincunx** (four quarters + center = 5) extended by *above* and *below* axes, giving **7 cosmological points** (4 cardinal + center + above + below). This is **interpretive numerology**, not a documented graded ascent.

| Center (Yollotl, the heart) | L4 |
| Below (Mictlān, underworld) | L1 |

| West (Cihuatlampa, women's land) | L6 |

This is **forced interpretive mapping**, not a Mesoamerican-native 7-grade ascent. Tier [I]→[C].

### 1.3 Verdict

**Mesoamerican traditions FAIL the universal-7 claim.** They have richer structures (13/9 system architecture, 4-fold cardinal quadripartite, calendar cycles) that don't reduce to 7 stages without forcing. **Charioteer recommendation:** add to the FAILED MAPPINGS appendix; do NOT add to V-bis canonical.

## 2. Andean Q'ero priesthood

### 2.1 What is documented

The Q'ero are the Quechua-speaking *paqos* (priest-shamans) of the high Andes (Paucartambo region, Peru), considered the surviving Inca priestly lineage. Their initiation tradition is documented in fragmentary form.

| Source | Documented stages |
|---|---|
| Joan Parisi Wilcox, *Masters of the Living Energy* (2004) | 3 stages: Pampamisayoq (lower paqo, valley/community), Altomisayoq (high paqo, mountain spirits), Kurak Akulleq (master of masters) |
| Jorge Luis Delgado, *The Andean Code of Wisdom* | Some descriptions add intermediate stages (Layqa, Hampeq) bringing total to 5 |
| Field reports from various traveler-anthropologists | Variation between 3, 5, and 7 stages depending on lineage and informant |

### 2.2 The 7-stage claim

Some New Age / modern reconstructions report a **7-level paqo hierarchy**, sometimes citing connection to chakra system architecture. Scholarly Andean anthropology generally treats this as a **post-1970s synthesis** influenced by global esoteric movements rather than pre-contact Inca tradition.

### 2.3 Verdict

**Andean Q'ero priesthood: documentation is inadequate to make a confident 7-stage claim.** The 3-stage Pampamisayoq → Altomisayoq → Kurak Akulleq core is well-attested; the 7-stage extension is contested. Tier [I]→[C]. **Charioteer recommendation:** flag as research-pending; do NOT add to V-bis canonical until Andean scholars publish authoritative stage counts.

## 3. Iroquois Great Law of Peace / Condolence Council

### 3.1 What is documented

The Iroquois Confederacy (Haudenosaunee) preserves the Great Law of Peace (*Kayanere'kó:wa*) and the Condolence Council ceremony. The Great Law has been transcribed in various forms, the most-cited being Arthur Parker's *The Constitution of the Five Nations* (1916).

| Element | Documented count |
|---|---|
| Founding nations of the Confederacy | 5 (Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, Seneca; later Tuscarora = 6) |
| Articles of the Great Law | Variously 117 articles in Parker / different counts in other transcriptions |
| Condolence Council ritual stages | Variably reported as 14-19 stages depending on transcription |
| Sacred Tree of Peace | Singular symbol; not a graded structure |

### 3.2 The 7-stage claim

There is no clean 7-grade structure documented in the Great Law or Condolence Council. The *Three Sisters* (corn, beans, squash) trinity is sometimes structurally significant but is 3-fold, not 7. The *Hiawatha Wampum Belt* shows 5 nations linked, not 7 stages.

### 3.3 Verdict

**Iroquois traditions FAIL the universal-7 claim.** Confederacy structure is 5-fold (later 6); Condolence Council is 14-19 ritual stages; Great Law is multi-article constitutional text without a 7-grade ascent. **Charioteer recommendation:** add to FAILED MAPPINGS; do NOT add to V-bis canonical.

## 4. Tahitian Arioi Society — verification

### 4.1 What is documented

The arioi were a religious-performative society of pre-contact Tahiti (Society Islands, French Polynesia), members of which traveled inter-island performing songs, dances, plays, and rituals dedicated to Oro, the war and fertility god. The society was abolished in the 1820s under missionary pressure.

### 4.2 The 7-grade claim

**Primary source:** William Ellis, *Polynesian Researches* (1829), II.214–217. Ellis was a London Missionary Society missionary who interviewed arioi members and former members in the 1810s-1820s. His report describes **seven grades**:

| # | Grade name (Ellis spelling, ≈Reo Tahiti) | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Avae-parai | Newest entrants; legs painted |
| 2 | Otiore | Body painted on both arms |
| 3 | Harotea | Body painted at sides |
| 4 | Hua | Trunk painted from waist up |
| 5 | Atoro | Stripes on left side |
| 6 | Painted entirely from feet to mid-back ("a lobster") | (named differently in different sources) |
| 7 | Avae fati / hia (highest grade) | Master arioi |

The system is body-painting-based (visual marking of attainment) and progressively covers the body upward, suggesting a vertical-ascending structure.

### 4.3 Scholarly contest

| Source | Position |
|---|---|
| Ellis (1829) | 7-grade system explicit |
| **Bengt Danielsson, *Love in the South Seas* (1956)** | Confirms 7-grade structure based on Ellis + later French ethnography |
| **Douglas Oliver, *Ancient Tahitian Society* (1974)** | The most comprehensive modern scholarly survey; **partially confirms** the 7-grade structure but notes Ellis's account is colored by missionary interpretation; some grade-counts vary across the islands |
| **Anne Salmond, *Aphrodite's Island* (2009)** | More skeptical; treats Ellis as one source among several; emphasizes inter-island variation |
| **Niel Gunson, *Messengers of Grace* (1978)** | Notes that Ellis's account may have been influenced by his own theological framing of the arioi as a corrupting institution |

### 4.4 The tentative L-mapping

If the 7 grades are accepted (with Ellis as primary):

| L | Arioi grade | Body-painting state | Functional reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | Avae-parai | Legs painted | Initial commitment; feet/legs symbolize grounding/entry |
| L2 | Otiore | Both arms painted | Capability for action; hands engaged |
| L3 | Harotea | Sides painted | Lateral expansion; productive engagement |
| L4 | Hua | Trunk waist-up | Core/heart marked; commitment full at the center |
| L5 | Atoro | Left side striped | Specialized depth on one side |
| L6 | (Whole body except face?) | Feet to mid-back | Full-body adornment less the head |
| L7 | Avae fati / hia | Highest, fully marked | Master grade; full attainment |

### 4.5 Mirror analysis

- **L1 ↔ L7 (Avae-parai feet ↔ Avae fati master):** Both involve the *avae* (foot/leg) terminology. L1 paints just the feet; L7 is the "broken/raised foot" master. ◐ PARTIAL — etymological link suggests structural pairing but the inversion isn't immediate.
- **L2 ↔ L6 (arms ↔ near-full body):** Both about *body coverage breadth*. L2 marks both arms; L6 marks most of body. Not a clean inversion — more a continuum.
- **L3 ↔ L5 (sides ↔ left side):** Both about *lateral marking*. L3 both sides, L5 left only. Not a clean inversion — more a refinement.

**Mirror score:** 1 PARTIAL, 2 inconclusive (not clean fail, not clean strong). Body-painting progression is **monotonic accumulation** rather than mirror-symmetric.

### 4.6 Verdict

**Tahitian arioi is ambiguous.** A 7-grade structure is documented (Ellis/Danielsson/Oliver) but the underlying logic is **monotonic body-coverage**, not mirror-symmetric inversion. The arioi system is more like a **belt-rank system** (martial-arts grading) than a mirror-symmetric initiatory ascent.

**Charioteer recommendation:**
- **DO add Tahitian arioi to FAILED MAPPINGS** as a "candidate that is 7-graded but not mirror-symmetric" — this is a structurally distinct case worth documenting.
- **Do NOT add to V-bis canonical** — V-bis requires both 7-grade AND mirror-symmetric structure.
- **Note the structural finding:** the framework's geometric claim distinguishes between *7-graded* traditions (which exist in many forms — including monotonic accumulation systems like belt ranks) and *7-graded mirror-symmetric* traditions (which are the cleanly-S²-projecting subset). Tahitian arioi is the former, not the latter.
