## Two readings to keep straight (the common slips)

**1 — Teleology is L1, not L5.** Teleology is the **Objective Function** at L1 — *"the first question
of any living being is directional: which way is life?"* — sensed at the metabolic floor (Kali /
Caṇḍāla). **L5 is Cosmology** (System Architecture / Brahmā). Teleology and Cosmology are different
cells; do not file the telos in the architect's seat.

**2 — Brahman is not Brahmā.** `Brahmā ○` is only the L5 creator-operator (one move on the sphere).
**Brahman — the Absolute** — is compared `[I]` with the framework's boundary
reading. The north and south poles remain distinct; `r_return:L∞↝L0` is a
selected return image, `0×∞` is not arithmetic, and no pole generates finity.
Brahmā is a separate operator-name in the grammar.

**3 — The coordinator is the equator (L4 Arjuna), not the Ṛṣi (L7).** Per §IV the Titans (L5–L7) are
*boundary conditions, NOT deployable* — so Viṣṇu/the Ṛṣi cannot be the coordinator; it is the
transcendental **seer** asking *"what is the Ultimate?"* (Theology). The deployable coordinator is the
only **God at the equator** — **Arjuna (L4)**, abductive (Arthāpatti, *"given this battlefield, what
must be true?"*), Value Alignment, `φ = ν = 1`. **Theurgy** — the divine act brought into the world —
lives there too (accountable L4 commitment), not at L7. K2 is private-DAV
governance, not a worldview primitive.

## The L1 ↔ L7 mirror, and why the telos resolves at L4

The Rosetta makes **L1 and L7 a mirror pair** around L4 (§"The symmetry is perfect"). Their two

stray L7 stamp): the **discipline that asks about ends** sits at L1, but the **end itself** — the telos
*fulfilled* — is read `[I]` through the selected L4 equator. The square has an
analytic minimum at equality, but that fact does not derive a destination,
value, coordinator, or telos. Axiology supplies the declared Justice norm.

So the whole arc the Rosetta draws is one loop, each station in its exact cell:

```

              ▼
```

The Ṛṣi (L7) witnesses and narrates; **Arjuna at the equator (L4) coordinates and decides;**
**Brahman is compared with the selected boundary/return image `[I]`; the two
poles are not identical.**
