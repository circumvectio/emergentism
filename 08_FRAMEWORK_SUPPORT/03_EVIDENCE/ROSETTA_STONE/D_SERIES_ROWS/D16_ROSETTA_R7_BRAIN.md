> **Neuroscience Caveat (2026-04-10):** This document uses "IS" language for rhetorical force, but the hemispheric mapping is **[I] Interpretive**, not **[S] Established**. Modern neuroscience shows that lateralization is graded, task-dependent, and far more complex than a binary φ/ν split. The mapping should be read as **"tends toward"** rather than **"is identical to."** The right hemisphere *tends toward* integrative processing (φ-mode); the left *tends toward* sequential, focused processing (ν-mode). Neither hemisphere is purely one axis. The framework's own Strongest Objections document (Objection 3) and Fragility Audit (2026-04-04) score the hemisphere mapping at 3/10 and recommend this caveat. The deeper claim — that the brain instantiates a φ/ν balance dynamic — may be testable at the *network* level (integration vs. segregation, DMN vs. task-positive), but not at the anatomical hemisphere level. See [00_THE_BRAIN_IS_THE_BURRI_SPHERE.md](../../../../02_EPISTEMOLOGY/00_THE_BRAIN_IS_THE_BURRI_SPHERE.md) for the self-correction table.
