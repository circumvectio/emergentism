## Core Convergence

### The Critical One

Suda's key contribution is the "critical-one" hypothesis: reciprocal symmetry is centered at `1`, not at `0`. On the positive ray, the reciprocal map fixes `1` and swaps the two poles:

```text
0 <-> infinity
1 -> 1
```

In logarithmic coordinate:

```text
s = log x
I: s -> -s
```

This is exactly the mirror geometry the Finity theory needs for `/1`: `1` is not merely the first counting number. It is the unique positive fixed point of inversion, the mirror where reciprocal sides meet.

### The Energy Well

Suda's invariant:

```text
E(x) = (log x)^2
```

is minimized uniquely at `x = 1` and diverges toward both `0` and `infinity`. This gives a precise way to state the doctrine:

```text
zero and infinity are symmetric extremes in reciprocal distance from one
```

The Finity Papers add the sphere bridge:

```text
B = sin theta = sech(log x)
```

So Suda provides the reciprocal energy; the Finity Papers connect that energy to equatorial balance on the Riemann sphere.

### The Egg Coordinate

Suda's hinge coordinate:

```text
u = (x - 1)/(x + 1)
```

compresses the positive ray into a bounded interval:

```text
0        -> -1
1        ->  0
infinity -> +1
```

and turns inversion into:

```text
u -> -u
```

This is the formal version of Suda's "egg of infinity." Emergentism can use it as a clean public bridge: the egg is not just metaphor; it is a coordinate in which the two poles sit on the rim and `1` sits at the center.

## Relation to the Riemann Sphere

Suda mainly works with the positive ray, the projective line, and Mobius/half-twist language. Emergentism's public geometry uses the Riemann sphere:

```text
phi = cot(theta/2)
nu  = tan(theta/2)
phi * nu = 1
```

The careful statement is:

```text
Suda's reciprocal map is a one-dimensional reciprocal chart compatible with the Riemann-sphere coordinates used by Emergentism.
```

Do not overstate this as proof-authority for the Burrisphere, or as an identity claim between the Mobius band and the sphere. The projective/reciprocal involution is shared; the topological objects and doctrine scopes differ.
