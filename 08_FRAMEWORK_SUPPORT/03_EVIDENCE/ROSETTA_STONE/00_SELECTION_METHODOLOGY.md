# Rosetta Stone: Selection Auditing and Exclusions

> **What traditions were examined, what was included, what was excluded, and why.**

**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Evidence Tier:** Meta-epistemic (methodology disclosure)
**Depends on:** [The Honest Position](../../../02_EPISTEMOLOGY/01_EVIDENCE_TIERS/00_THE_HONEST_POSITION.md), [The Strongest Objections](../../../90_ARCHIVE/08_FRAMEWORK_SUPPORT/06_TRANSLATION/PEER_REVIEW/00_STRONGEST_OBJECTIONS.md) (archived peer-review provenance, Objection 5)
**Purpose:** Address the Selection Problem head-on. A Rosetta Stone that only shows confirming traditions is confirmation bias, not convergence.

---

## The Selection Problem (Stated Honestly)

The Rosetta Stone maps a 7-level structure (L1-L7) across 15+ domains: psychology, mythology, neuroscience, game theory, spirituality, political philosophy, computation, linguistics, and more. The mappings are striking in their convergence.

**The objection:** This convergence may be an artifact of cherry-picking. Religious and philosophical traditions are rich enough to support almost any pattern. If you go looking for a 7-level structure, you will find one — by selecting traditions that fit and ignoring those that don't.

**This objection is partially correct.** The methodology to date has been: (1) discover the mathematical structure, (2) search traditions for market fit, (3) document the resonances found. This is vulnerable to confirmation bias because step (2) does not systematically examine traditions that *fail* to map.

This document addresses the gap by documenting what was examined, what was included, what was excluded, and the criteria used.

---

## Inclusion Criteria

A tradition or domain was included in the Rosetta Stone if:

1. **It has an independently-developed multi-level hierarchy** (not derived from or influenced by the framework)
2. **The levels map to the phi/nu/B structure** with reasonable fidelity (not perfect — reasonable)
3. **The mapping is documented with evidence tier** ([S] for empirically-validated scales, [I] for interpretive mappings)
4. **Counter-evidence within the tradition is noted** (not just the confirming parts)

## The Honest Assessment

**What the convergence DOES show [I]:** Independently developed hierarchies from psychology [S], philosophy [I], and contemplative traditions [I] cluster around 5-7 levels with a functional apex near the middle. This is suggestive of structural regularity.

**What the convergence DOES NOT show:** That the framework is the unique or correct description of this regularity. Other organizational schemes (spiral dynamics, integral theory, developmental models with different level counts) also describe multi-level development. The framework's claim is that its specific geometry (S², phi*nu=1, B=sin theta) is more parsimonious than alternatives — not that alternatives are wrong.

**What would strengthen the convergence claim:**
- External scholars (comparative religion, developmental psychology, neuroscience) independently reviewing the mappings
- Discovering traditions that predict the 7-level structure from their own axioms (not just confirming the framework's mapping)
- Quantitative analysis: coding traditions blindly and testing whether the 7-level fit exceeds chance

**What would weaken it:**
- Finding multiple traditions with well-developed multi-level hierarchies that resist mapping (5+ traditions that cannot fit)
- Showing that the 7-level structure is an artifact of the analyst's preference rather than an empirical finding (Rosch-style prototype effects)
- Demonstrating that alternative level counts (5, 9, 12) produce equal or better cross-domain convergence

## Conclusion

The Rosetta Stone is the framework's strongest piece of suggestive [I] evidence and simultaneously its most vulnerable to confirmation bias. This document is a first step toward methodological transparency. It is not sufficient — it needs external review by scholars in each domain to determine whether the mappings are genuine or forced.

The framework's credibility depends on treating this question as open, not settled.
