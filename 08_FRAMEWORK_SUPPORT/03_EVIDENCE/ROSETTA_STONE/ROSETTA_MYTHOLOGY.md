## What This Mapping Shows

[I] Six parallel columns are compared here: Greek, Norse, Sumerian, Tarot Major Arcana, Jungian archetype, and Animal totem. Each column assigns a figure to each L-level based on structural function, not surface similarity. The question asked is always: "What does operator X look like when projected into tradition Y?"

The mapping is not etymological or historical. Hades and Hel are not the "same god." They occupy the same structural position: the underworld sovereign, the L1 boundary condition where phi approaches 0, the encounter with annihilation that is the precondition for any ascent.

## Reading the Table

**L1 -- The Underworld Boundary.** Many traditions place a sovereign of the dead at the threshold. Hades, Hel, Ereshkigal: the place where phi approaches zero in this reading. The Shadow (Jung) is the psychic equivalent. The Wolf is the predator at the survival edge. The Death card in Tarot is transformation through annihilation. This is an interpretive L1 translation, not proof that the traditions share a single hidden doctrine.

**L2 -- The Social Bond.** Aphrodite, Freya, Inanna: the goddess of love, connection, fertility. The Lovers card. The Companion archetype. The Dolphin -- social, playful, bonded. L2 is where isolated survival becomes relationship.

**L3 -- The Maker.** Hephaestus (the smith), Freyr (the harvest god), Ea (the craftsman-god of water and wisdom). The Magician card. The Creator archetype. The Bee -- collective production, the hive. L3 is where relationship becomes productive civilisation.

**L4 -- The Warrior.** The equatorial position. Ares/Athena hold both war and wisdom (the Greek split is itself informative -- Ares is war without wisdom, Athena is war with wisdom; L4 requires both). Thor is the protector. Ninurta is the champion. Strength/Justice in Tarot. The Lion -- sovereign, balanced, holding territory. L4 is phi = nu = 1, maximum balance.

**L5 -- The Sovereign.** Athena in her wisdom aspect (not war), Frigg (the all-seeing queen), Enlil (lord of wind and decree). The Hierophant -- keeper of mission-critical tradition. The Ruler archetype. The Owl -- vision in darkness, contemplative perception.

**L6 -- The Dissolver.** Kronos (time, the devourer), Mimir (the severed head of wisdom), Enki (the trickster-sage). The Hermit -- withdrawal from the world. The Wise Old Man -- wisdom through renunciation. The Serpent -- shedding skin, dissolution, kundalini.

**L7 -- The Boundary Above.** Zeus (the sky-father), Odin (the all-father who sacrificed an eye for wisdom), Anu (the heaven-god). The World card -- completion of the cycle. The Self (Jung) -- the totality. The Eagle -- highest perspective, the view from the pole. L7 is the Moksha/Asura fork: transcendence or catastrophic inversion.

**PIE / F5 reading:** At L7, the PIE reconstruction names Dyḗus Ph₂tḗr — the bright-sky-father, a luminous face of perceived order. This is not merely "Zeus's ancestor"; the framework reads it as one of the oldest reconstructable Indo-European names for the bright-sky face that can be mapped to F5 [I/C]. The framework reads Dyḗus as the phenomenological gestalt of F5 at paleolithic perceptual scale: bright (Φ most visible in clarity), sky (vertical axis = forward-light-cone direction), father (generative source, not competitor). L7 figures across traditions — Zeus, Jupiter, Odin, Anu — can be read as daughter-faces of this perception without proving one-field identity. See packet 138 for full Indo-European lineage; packet 140 for modern and esoteric F5-sensing (Nick Land, occult initiates, perennialists, quantum retrocausality claims [C], Teilhard).

## The Pattern Across Traditions

No tradition invented the seven levels by consulting another. The Greek, Norse, and Sumerian pantheons developed independently across millennia and continents. Jung's archetypes were derived from clinical observation and comparative mythology. The Tarot Major Arcana emerged from Renaissance esoteric tradition.

The framework's claim: these are not coincidences but projections of the same seven structural positions on S² onto different cultural substrates. The operator at each position determines the mythological figure that each tradition independently discovers.

This claim is [I] -- interpretive, not empirically testable. It is falsifiable in principle: if a sufficiently detailed analysis of a new mythological tradition (e.g., Aztec, Yoruba, Aboriginal Dreamtime) cannot be mapped onto the seven levels without forcing, the universality claim weakens.
