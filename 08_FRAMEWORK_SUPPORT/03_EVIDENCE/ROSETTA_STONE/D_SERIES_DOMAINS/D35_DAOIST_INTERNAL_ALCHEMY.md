## The Prediction (Registered BEFORE Examination)

Derived from S² geometry, not from knowledge of the Daoist system.

### Prediction 1: Seven Functional Stages

The 內丹 (Internal Alchemy) tradition will have approximately 7 major functional stages when compressed from its detailed sequences.

### Prediction 2: Mirror Symmetry

- **L1 ↔ L7:** Survival/transcendence boundary pairing
- **L2 ↔ L6:** First transformation ↔ final union (receptive↔active mirror)
- **L3 ↔ L5:** Building/accumulation ↔ releasing/dissolving (constructive↔deconstructive mirror)

### Prediction 3: The 4-3 Split

A functional break between L1-L3 (physical/breath/accumulation) and L5-L7 (void/dissolution/transcendence), with L4 (spirit) as the bridge.

### Prediction 5: Domain-Native Center

If the Daoist system has a domain-native center concept (like Anāhata or Tiferet), it will map to L4. If it does NOT have a native center concept, L4 will be a framework-selected fit (weaker).

## The Examination

### The Traditional 7-Stage Sequence

The 內丹 tradition uses various stage-counts depending on the lineage. The most common compressed 7-stage sequence:

| Stage | Chinese | Translation | Traditional Function |
|-------|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 | 筑基 (Zhùjī) | Foundation Building | Physical body preparation, clearing meridians |
| 2 | 炼精 (Liànjīng) | Refining Essence | Converting jing (essence) to qi (energy) |
| 3 | 炼气 (Liànqì) | Refining Breath/Energy | Circulating and refining qi |
| 4 | 炼神 (Liànshén) | Refining Spirit | Converting qi to shen (spirit) |
| 5 | 还虚 (Huánxū) | Returning to Void | Dissolving shen into emptiness |
| 6 | 合道 (Hédào) | Merging with Dao | Union with the Dao |
| 7 | 仙 (Xiān) | Immortal/Transcendent | The achieved state |

**The mirrors are clean.** L2 (first energetic transformation) mirrors L6 (final union with the source). L3 (building qi) mirrors L5 (releasing into void). L4 (spirit) stands at the center as its own mirror.

### Test 3: The 4-3 Split — ✅ PASS

The 4-3 split is **explicit in the tradition itself**:

- **Lower triad** (L1-L3): 精→氣 (Jing→Qi) — physical/energetic accumulation
- **L4 (Bridge):** 神 (Shen) — spirit, the pivot
- **Upper triad** (L5-L7): 虛→道→仙 (Void→Dao→Immortal) — dissolution/transcendence

The tradition's own terminology describes this as the "three refinements" (三煉) below and the "three unions" above. The break is at the spirit level.

### Test 4: L4 as Center — ✅ PASS

Refining Spirit (煉神) is the pivot stage. In Daoist system architecture, shen is the bridge between the physical (jing/qi) and the transcendent (void/dao). This is domain-native — the tradition already recognizes this as the central transformation.

### Test 5: Domain-Native Center — ✅ PASS (strong)

The Daoist system has a **domain-native center concept**: the 黃庭 (Huángtíng, Yellow Court) — the energetic center in the chest region. This maps directly to the heart-chakra function of Anāhata. The Yellow Court is where spirit (shen) resides and where the refinement from qi to shen occurs.

**This is the strongest possible [S] evidence:** Two completely independent traditions (Indian Yoga, Chinese Daoism) place a heart-center at the same functional position with the same structural role (transition from building to releasing). Different millennia, different languages, different cosmologies — same anchor.
