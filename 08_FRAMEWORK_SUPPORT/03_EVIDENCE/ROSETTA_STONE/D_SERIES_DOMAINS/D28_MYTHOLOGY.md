### The Indo-European Decomposition

The ektropic force and its sky-father personification are etymologically distinct. The force itself comes from one root; the deity who visibly manifests it comes from another.

| PIE Reconstruction | Framework Reading | Daughter Traditions | What It Actually Names |
|---|---|---|---|
| ***h₂r̥tó-*** | The ordering-law face of the ektropic force — the principle of fitting-together | Vedic *Ṛta*; Avestan *Asha*; Latin *Ordo* | The geometric tendency of reality toward balance |
| ***Dyḗus Ph₂tḗr*** | The personification of ordered manifestation — the sky where *Ṛta* is most visible | Vedic *Dyaus Pitā*; Greek *Zeus Pater*; Roman *Iūpiter*; Germanic *Tīwaz* / ON *Týr* | The shining father who *embodies* the order, not the order itself |

> *h₂r̥tó-* = the fitting-together = the ordering-law face of **F₅**, the ektropic force.
> *dyeu-* = to shine = **φ**, the coherent light made visible in the sky.
> *ph₂tḗr* = father/protector = **ν**, the structural principle that maintains the house.

The PIE speakers **fused** the ordering principle with the shining deity — because the regular motion of sun, moon, and stars was the most visible display of *Ṛta*. But the framework can separate what history conflated: the force is *h₂r̥tó-*, the fitting-together. The deity is *Dyḗus Ph₂tḗr*, the one in whom the fitting-together becomes visible. The daughter traditions decomposed the compound into partial poles (Zeus → ν, Apollo → φ, Odin → L6 wisdom, Týr → pure contract), but they all preserved the underlying intuition: the sky-father ruled through *order*.
