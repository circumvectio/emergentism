# PD-12: The Measurement Problem — Correspondence Boundary

Emergentism does not dissolve the quantum measurement problem.

The former text called measurement a μ-limit mechanism, used an invalid
scalar-accumulation formula, treated a detector as a Finitor, and made D5→D4 a
candidate collapse mechanism. Those claims are withdrawn. The complete prior
text remains recoverable from Git blob
`4cae980a7e02cebe24cf3d30818262f1baa63b0a`.

## Physics boundary

- Everett's relative-state formulation has no fundamental collapse.
- Copenhagen-family actualization, objective-collapse models, Bohmian
  mechanics, relational accounts, and other interpretations type measurement
  differently.
- Decoherence explains suppression of interference in appropriate reduced
  descriptions; what further account of definite records is required remains
  interpretation-dependent.
- Neither a D-register nor a Burri operator is part of standard quantum theory.

## Typed D3→D4 interface `[A/I]`

A D3 state `ρ` and declared quantum instrument `{𝓘_k}` yield
`p_k=Tr[𝓘_k(ρ)]`. An actual preparation, apparatus interaction, outcome token,
and provenance-bearing record are D4. Emergentism names that state-to-record
boundary `μ₃` `[I]`; standard quantum theory supplies the operational
state/instrument/probability contract `[A/B]`. The label supplies no additional
physical mechanism.

The conditional update `ρ'_k=𝓘_k(ρ)/p_k` is operationally valid when `p_k>0`.
Whether it is a fundamental collapse, observer-relative conditioning, or part
of an uncollapsed relative-state description remains interpretation-specific.

`D4 → μ₄ → D5` is separately the framework's candidate emergence of counterfactual
capacity. `D5 → commitment → D4` is a finite agent using actual means to enact
an option. Neither operation is quantum measurement, and D5 is not quantum
branch space. A laboratory outcome is returned by the physical interaction and
environment, not manufactured by an agent selector.

## Kill criterion

Recast the crosswalk whenever it obscures the standard formalism, implies
physical collapse by consciousness or authorization, or makes the operational
Burri calculus depend on a quantum interpretation. Removing interpretation
commentary must leave the D3 density-operator and instrument calculus intact.

*The problem remains a quantum-foundations problem. The Rosetta may compare; it
may not claim the solution.*
