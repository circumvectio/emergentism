## 1. THE PROBLEM

The classical dilemma: Are our actions free or determined?

If determined — if every event follows necessarily from prior causes — then human agency is illusory. There is no choice, only the unfolding of physical law. Moral responsibility collapses, because no one *could have done otherwise*.

If free — if agents genuinely choose — then choice must be uncaused. But uncaused events are random. Randomness is not freedom. A coin flip is not an act of will.

The Western philosophical tradition has oscillated between these poles for two millennia. Compatibilism attempts a middle ground but typically achieves it by redefining "free" into something no one originally meant by the word. The dilemma persists.

The problem feels irresolvable within that binary. In this framework, that is because the question is topologically malformed.

## 2. THE TOPOLOGY ERROR

The proposed error is binary framing. "Free" and "determined" are often treated
as mutually exclusive labels, while the operational questions—what options were
represented, what means were available, what authorization held, and what the
world returned—are left untyped.

`D4` means actuality, not determinism. An actual process may be deterministic,
stochastic, chaotic, or only partly modeled. The register therefore cannot decide
the metaphysical dispute by definition.

The framework instead separates actual D4 carriers and events from D5 possible
content. A present D4 model token can represent D5 alternatives; actual ranking,
selection, action, and receipts remain D4. The reciprocal chart coordinates
`φ,ν` and the finite-node factors `Φ,V` are distinct notational systems, and
`P_node=ΦV` is a selected normalized model rather than a world law.

The free-will debate maps one axis to the entire sphere and then argues about which axis is "real."

## 3. THE DISSOLUTION

The operational reframing asks whether an actual agent represented alternatives,
ranked them, possessed usable means, acted under accountable authorization, and
received consequences from a world it did not control. The selector returns an
actual commitment receipt; the environment separately returns an actual outcome
receipt. This does not prove libertarian freedom or refute determinism. It makes
several contributors to practical agency separately inspectable.

`μ₄:D4→D5` names the open hypothesis that counterfactual capacity emerges at a
typed interface. Enacting one modeled option is **not** the reverse `μ`-crossing:
D5 content is carried by an actual D4 model token, and actual D4 means help cause
an actual D4 action. The world may veto or alter the intended outcome.

At the equator (Φ = 1, V = 1 in the normalized contact reading), the agent has
maximum balance in the model — a maximal present D4 assessment of relevant D5
option quality together with maximal usable D4 means. Balance is the constraint
that keeps agency usable, not the whole objective by itself. The objective is
the lawful selection of a reachable future without extraction.

The problem is not thereby dissolved. `[I/C]` The framework offers a causal and
evidential decomposition of practical agency that can coexist with several
metaphysical positions. Its value depends on whether the decomposition predicts
or clarifies anything beyond established decision theory and philosophy of action.

## 4. THE FRAMEWORK CONNECTION

The D0-D6 scaffold supplies the type distinction. `μ₄` is the open emergence
hypothesis; commitment and outcome are separate D4 operations. In the selected
normalized node model, `(Φ,V)=(1,1)` is simply the joint ceiling where represented
option quality and usable means are both maximal.

Responsibility is modeled as tracking equatorial position plus reachable
consequence. An agent at the equator has a D4 model that adequately represents
relevant D5 alternatives and has actual D4 means to act. This is the model's
account of freedom. An agent far off-equator — depleted option-field quality
(`Φ → 0`) or depleted means (`V → 0`) — has diminished modeled access to
alternatives or diminished capacity to attempt an action. The proposal is that
responsibility scales with usable foresight, usable means, and the
non-extractive shape of the chosen move.

This is not a derivation of moral responsibility from geometry. Responsibility
also requires the Justice and Authorization envelopes, and its normative force is
a declared ethical commitment rather than a consequence of `φ·ν=1`.

## 5. WHAT WOULD FALSIFY THIS

The proposal loses force if:

1. **D4-only core state were sufficient.** If a purely physical, deterministic account can explain agency, choice, and moral responsibility without reference to D5, the coordinate/action split is unnecessary. [S/I] Compatibilism would win by default.

2. **The counterfactual-capacity intervention fails.** If controlled changes to
represented futures do not change present action distributions beyond fair D4
baselines, `μ₄` lacks empirical traction.

3. **Rival decompositions win.** If established decision-theoretic or
philosophy-of-action models explain the same cases with fewer assumptions or
better predictions, the spherical mapping is ornamental.
