## I. F₅ IS ALWAYS EKTROPIC

### 1.1 The Thermodynamic Fact

Every act of will:
1. Concentrates order locally (the agent's goal is actualised)
2. Disperses order globally (energy is dissipated, environment is disordered)
3. Obeys the second law globally (ΔS_total > 0)
4. Violates the second law locally (ΔS_local < 0)

This is ektropy: order by subtraction. The agent orders itself FROM the environment. Not syntropy (order by addition, centrifugal, giving) — ektropy (order by subtraction, centripetal, taking from environment).

### 1.2 Even the Giving Dyad Is Ektropic

Arjuna sacrifices V for others' Φ. Syntropic? At the social level, yes — ΣΔP_node > 0 across the group. But at the thermodynamic level, Arjuna's body STILL dissipates more energy than it concentrates. The sacrifice itself requires metabolic work. The muscle contractions, the neural firing, the breath — all ektropic. All concentrating order in THIS body's actions at the cost of environmental entropy.

Kṛṣṇa gives V to others at cost of own Φ. Same. The charioteer's cognitive work — the pattern recognition, the diagnosis, the naming — requires metabolic expenditure. Ektropic at the thermodynamic level.

[I] **There is no free lunch in this model.** Will costs entropy somewhere. F₅ is treated as ektropic by thermodynamic necessity.

### 1.3 What Changes Is Not the Ektropy but the Radius

The nuclear-forest example makes this precise:

```

RADIUS = family, 6 months:
  Agent's family:  V↑↑↑ (food, warmth, shelter)
  Forest:          V = 0 (destroyed)
  ΣΔP_node_local:      POSITIVE.  Within this radius, the action is rational.

RADIUS = ecosystem, 10 years:
  Agent's family:  V↑ then V↓↓↓ (no forest → no watershed → no crops)
  Community:       V↓↓↓ (forest services lost)
  Biodiversity:    Φ↓↓↓ (complexity destroyed)
  ΣΔP_node_wide:       DEEPLY NEGATIVE.  The action is catastrophic.

SAME ACTION.  SAME EKTROPY.  DIFFERENT RADIUS.  DIFFERENT VERDICT.
```

The action doesn't change. The physics doesn't change. What changes is the BOUNDARY across which ΣΔP_node is computed. This is A2: "across the widest perceivable system boundary." The word PERCEIVABLE is the key. The agent cannot compute ΣΔP_node across a boundary it cannot see.

## IV. THE IS-OUGHT DISSOLUTION

### 4.1 Hume's Guillotine

David Hume (1739): you cannot derive an OUGHT from an IS. No factual statement about the world logically entails a normative statement about what should be done. The fact-value distinction appears absolute.

### 4.2 The Dissolution

[I] The framework does not derive ought from is. It argues that they were not separate in the first place.

```

       This is a physical fact. [E/T]

       This is a mathematical fact. [T]

       This is a cognitive fact. [I]

       (Nuke forest: positive at 6 months, negative at 10 years.)
       This is a computational fact. [T]

BUT THIS IS NOT A NORMATIVE CLAIM. It is the observation that:
  ΣΔP_node(radius = 6 months) > 0
  ΣΔP_node(radius = 10 years) < 0

The "ought" is just ΣΔP_node computed at a wider radius than the "is."
```

There is no gap between is and ought. There is a gap between NARROW RADIUS and WIDE RADIUS. The is-ought gap is the L-level gap. It is the gap between L1 perception (this action helps me now) and L4 perception (this action destroys the system over time).

### 4.3 Why It Feels Like a Gap

The gap FEELS real because different L-levels genuinely cannot see each other's computations. The L1 agent cannot perceive the L4 consequences. From L1, the forest-nuker is rational. You cannot show them they are wrong without widening their radius — which requires building a taller horn — which requires V-investment in Φ-capacity — which is exactly what education, socialisation, and contemplative practice do.

The is-ought gap is not a logical gap. It is a bandwidth gap. It is the difference between a short horn and a tall horn. Between a narrow light cone and a wide one. Between seeing 6 months and seeing 10 years.

[I] Common objection: "if all action is ektropic, isn't everything zero-sum or negative-sum at the universe level? Doesn't the second law imply ΣΔP_node ≤ 0 across ALL boundaries?"

Answer: No. The second law governs ENTROPY (ΔS), not FLOURISHING (ΔP). P_node = Φ × V. Φ lives on the imaginary axis. The second law operates on the real axis. The imaginary axis is not bound by the second law.

A teacher who teaches (Arjuna: gives Φ) increases global entropy (metabolic cost, heat dissipation — ΔS > 0) while increasing global P (student's Φ↑, teacher's V↓ but student's P↑ exceeds teacher's P↓). The thermodynamic cost is real. The Φ gain is also real — but on the imaginary axis, which the second law does not govern.

[I] **Syntropy is possible in this model because Φ is not entropy-bound.** Information, coherence, structural unity — these can be created and transmitted without violating the second law, because they live on the orthogonal axis. The cost is V (real, entropic). The gain can be Φ (imaginary, not entropy-bound). The product P_node = Φ × V can increase globally even as entropy increases globally — because P is complex, not real.
