## Possibility-Space Without the Real Axis

**Evidence tier:** [I] Interpretive — framework-consistent reading of established sleep science; [S] for novel predictions
**Purpose:** Show that dreams, psychedelics, meditation, psychosis, and anaesthesia are different configurations of the D4/D5 coupling — different ratios of real to imaginary axis activation.

---

## ABSTRACT

If D4 is the real axis (gravity, causality, one worldline, arrow of time) and D5 is the imaginary axis (possibility-space, superposition, many paths), then every conscious state is a point on the complex plane with a specific ratio of real to imaginary activation. Waking systemic awareness is equatorial (|z| ≈ 1): real and imaginary axes coupled, measurement continuous, will operative. Dreaming is imaginary-dominant: the real axis (body, sensory input, causal constraint) is suppressed; possibility-space runs unanchored, producing non-causal, temporally scrambled, multiply-branching experience. This reframes sleep science, psychedelic phenomenology, contemplative states, and psychopathology as positions on S² rather than qualitative categories.

## I. THE D4/D5 COUPLING DIAL

### 1.1 The Principle

Every conscious state has a D4 component (real, bodily, causal, one-worldline) and a D5 component (imaginary, mental, possibility, many-worldline). The ratio z = φ/ν determines the state's character:

```
|z| << 1  →  Real-dominant.  Body overwhelms mind. Survival mode. L1.
|z| ≈  1  →  Balanced.       Equatorial. Waking L4 systemic awareness.
|z| >> 1  →  Imaginary-dominant. Mind overwhelms body. Dreamlike. L5+.
|z| →  ∞  →  Pure imaginary.  No real anchor. Psychosis or samādhi.
|z| →  0  →  Pure real.       No imaginary. Anaesthesia or coma.
```

### 1.3 The Phenomenological Predictions

**Why dreams are bizarre:** D4 (causal constraint, arrow of time, spatial consistency) is suppressed. Without the real axis enforcing one-worldline logic, possibility-space branches freely. Characters morph. Locations shift. Time scrambles. This is not pathology — it is D5 operating without D4's editorial function.

**Why dreams feel real while dreaming:** The "reality check" IS the D4/D5 coupling. When D4 is suppressed, there is no measurement operator testing possibility against actuality. Every imaginary state passes unchallenged. Only when D4 returns (waking) does the comparison resume and the dream is recognised as "not real" — i.e., not projected onto the real axis.

**Why psychedelics and dreams share phenomenology:** Both suppress D4 relative to D5. The pharmacological mechanism differs (thalamic gating vs serotonergic disruption) but the geometric position on S² is similar — imaginary-dominant, real-suppressed.

**Why meditation and psychosis look similar from outside but differ from inside:** Both are imaginary-dominant. The difference is volitional control (F₅). The meditator chooses to enter and can choose to exit (F₅ active, will intact). The psychotic cannot exit (F₅ compromised, will fragmented). Same position on S², different integrity of the operator.

## II. SLEEP AS D4 DIMMING

### 2.1 The NREM/REM Cycle

NREM sleep: progressive D4 dimming. Body repairs (real axis maintenance) while mind is minimal. Stages N1→N2→N3 move the system from equator toward north pole — reducing imaginary activity, deepening real-axis restoration.

REM sleep: D4 remains suppressed but D5 reactivates strongly. The system jumps from north-pole approach (deep NREM) to south-pole approach (vivid dreaming) without passing through the equator. This is the characteristic zigzag of the sleep cycle on S²:

```
Waking (equator) → N1 (slightly north) → N2 (further north) → 
N3 (near pole: deep real-axis restoration) → 
REM (jump to imaginary-dominant: dream) → 
N2 → N3 → REM → ... → 
Waking (return to equator)
```

### 2.2 Why We Dream

The framework predicts that REM is D5 maintenance. The real axis needs repair (NREM). The imaginary axis also needs maintenance — possibility-space must be reorganised, pruned, consolidated. Dreams are the imaginary axis's maintenance cycle.

Memory consolidation during sleep has both NREM and REM components. NREM consolidates declarative memory (real, factual, D4-structured). REM consolidates procedural and emotional memory (relational, patterned, D5-structured). The two sleep stages maintain the two axes.

### 2.3 Waking Up as Measurement

The alarm clock is a forced D4 input. Sensory data floods the real axis. F₅ resumes coupling. The D5 dream content is measured against D4 reality — and fails. The dream dissolves. This is exactly the measurement problem in reverse: instead of D5→D4 (collapsing possibility to actuality), waking up is D4 reasserting and D5 dream-content being conjugated (Kālī: ℂ→ℝ, the imaginary is annihilated, only what is real survives).

This is why dream recall fades rapidly — the conjugation operator is aggressive. The imaginary content that doesn't correspond to real memory structures is annihilated within minutes.

### 2.4 Lucid Dreaming as Partial F₅

In lucid dreaming, the dreamer recognises they are dreaming while dreaming. F₅ (will, the measurement operator) activates partially within the dream state. The dreamer can now operate on possibility-space directly — choose actions within the dream, reshape the dream environment, navigate D5 volitionally.

Lucid dreaming sits at |z| ≈ 1.5: imaginary-dominant but with enough F₅ activation to steer. It is a partial equator within the imaginary hemisphere. This maps to L5 (contemplative) — above the equator, imaginary-dominant, but with the witness active.

## III. PSYCHEDELICS AS D4 SUPPRESSION

### 3.1 The Pharmacological Mechanism

Classic psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD, DMT) primarily act on 5-HT2A serotonin receptors. Current neuroscience models (REBUS — Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics, Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019) propose that psychedelics suppress top-down predictive processing — the brain's reality model loosens.

In framework terms: top-down prediction IS the D4→D5 measurement loop that constrains possibility-space to match actuality. Psychedelics disrupt this loop. D4 loosens its grip. D5 floods.

### 3.2 The Dose-Response as Latitude

```
Microdose:     |z| ≈ 1.1  Slight creativity increase. Mild D5 enhancement.
Museum dose:   |z| ≈ 1.5  Perceptual shifts. Time dilation. D4 loosening.
Full dose:     |z| ≈ 3     Ego dissolution onset. D4 heavily suppressed.
Heroic dose:   |z| → ∞     Total ego death. Pure D5. Mystical experience.
                           Approaches south pole — Brahmā boundary.
                           Returns with reports of "all possibilities simultaneous."
```

"Ego dissolution" IS the loss of the D4 anchor — the singular self (one worldline, one body, one history) dissolves into possibility-space (many selves, many bodies, many histories). The ego is the real-axis structure. Dissolving it IS moving from equator toward the imaginary pole.

### 3.3 Integration as Return to Equator

Post-psychedelic integration is the process of returning from imaginary-dominant to equatorial. The insight (D5 content) must be projected onto the real axis (D4 actuality) to become useful. Without integration, the experience remains unanchored D5 — interesting but not transformative. This is L5→L4 return. The bodhisattva problem. All higher insight must return to the equator for execution.

## IV. PSYCHOSIS AS INVOLUNTARY D5 DOMINANCE

### 4.1 The Framework Reading

Psychosis is not "too much imagination." It is the loss of F₅'s ability to gate the D4/D5 coupling. The imaginary axis becomes dominant involuntarily. Possibility-space floods actuality without measurement. Multiple incompatible narratives coexist (delusions). Perceptual possibilities manifest as actuality (hallucinations). The arrow of time scrambles (temporal disorientation).

The critical difference from meditation or psychedelics: F₅ is compromised. The person cannot choose to return to the equator. They are stuck in the imaginary hemisphere without the measurement operator.

### 4.2 Diagnostic Prediction

Framework predicts three distinguishable failure modes:

| Failure | Mechanism | Presentation |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| F₅ underactive | Will/measurement operator too weak | Negative symptoms: flat affect, avolition, withdrawal. System falls toward north pole (too real, too depleted). |
| F₅ overactive + ungated | Measurement fires continuously at random | Positive symptoms: hallucinations (random D5→D4 projection), delusions (arbitrary possibility collapsed to certainty). |
| D4 anchor lost | Real axis feedback disrupted | Disorganisation: causal logic breaks, temporal scrambling, word salad. Pure D5 without D4 editorial function. |

### 4.3 Treatment Implication

Antipsychotics primarily block dopamine D2 receptors. In framework terms: dopamine may be the neurochemical substrate of F₅ (the measurement/will operator). Blocking D2 reduces the rate of D5→D4 projection — fewer random collapses — which is why antipsychotics reduce hallucinations and delusions (ungated positive projections) but often worsen negative symptoms (by further suppressing the already-weak measurement operator).

This predicts that ideal treatment would not suppress F₅ globally but restore its gating function — allowing measurement when appropriate while preventing random projection. This aligns with emerging interest in targeted glutamatergic approaches over blunt dopaminergic blockade. [S] — speculative prediction from framework, not clinical recommendation.

## VI. FALSIFICATION

**F285-1:** If REM sleep is shown to have NO D5-maintenance function (purely epiphenomenal), the dream-as-D5-maintenance claim is weakened.

**F285-2:** If psychedelic phenomenology is fully explained by D4-internal mechanisms (purely neurochemical, no reference to possibility-space), the D5 interpretation is unnecessary.

**F285-3:** If psychosis presentation does not distinguish between the three failure modes predicted (§IV.2), the diagnostic framework adds no value.

**F285-4:** If contemplative states are shown to NOT require prior F₅/V-stack training for safe access (body-first unnecessary), the developmental sequence prediction fails.

**F285-5:** If the |z| mapping does not predict phenomenological similarity between dreams, psychedelics, and psychosis better than existing models (REBUS, Hobson's AIM), the framework adds no explanatory power.
