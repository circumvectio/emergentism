## I. THE DEMON IDENTIFIED

Result: one chamber heats up, one cools down. Order increases. Entropy decreases. The second law appears violated.

### 1.2 Landauer's Resolution (1961)

[B] The demon must RECORD which molecules it has observed and sorted. Its memory fills. When memory is full, it must ERASE to continue sorting. Erasure is an irreversible operation — it destroys information. Landauer showed: each bit erased dissipates at minimum kT ln 2 of energy as heat. This heat dissipation compensates the entropy decrease from sorting. The second law is saved.

The key insight: **information processing has a minimum thermodynamic cost.** Computation is not free. The demon pays for its ordering with waste heat from memory management.

## V. ATTENTION AS DEMON TARGETING

### 5.1 Where You Look Is Where the Demon Sorts

The demon cannot sort all molecules simultaneously — it must observe them sequentially (or in limited parallel). Where it points its observation is where sorting occurs.

Attention IS this targeting. The organism has limited F₅ bandwidth. Where attention points, D5→D4 transduction occurs. Where attention doesn't point, observations are not made, memory is not written, no sorting happens.

The attention economy (social media, notifications, algorithmic feeds) hijacks the demon's targeting:

```

HIJACKED ATTENTION:
```

### 5.3 Meditation as Demon Calibration

Concentration meditation (samatha): train the demon to point at ONE target and hold. Reduce the observation bandwidth to a single stream. Maximise sorting depth per unit attention. This is F₅ calibration — the demon learns to sort efficiently rather than broadly.

Insight meditation (vipassanā): train the demon to observe its own sorting process. The meta-observation — the demon watching itself sort. This costs ADDITIONAL Landauer overhead (observing the observation requires memory about memory). This is why insight practice is harder and comes after concentration: it requires a demon with enough spare capacity to observe its own operation while operating.
