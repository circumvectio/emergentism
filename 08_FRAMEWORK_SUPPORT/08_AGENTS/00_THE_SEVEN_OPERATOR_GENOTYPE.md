## 0. The source of agency (why an agent can act at all)

Before the seven moves, there is the capacity to make a move. The repaired
interface keeps emergence and enactment separate:

- `D4` is causal actuality: actual model tokens, performed ranking/selection
  events, embodied means, attempted action, and record.
- `D5` is the counterfactual content those D4 tokens may represent: possible
  futures and option relations, not an acting or selecting substance.
- `μ₀…μ₅` name candidate emergence crossings. Enacting an option ranked
  by a D4 event over D5 content is instead a commitment through D4 means.
  Authorization separately evaluates
  normative validity; unauthorized attempts remain possible and receipted.
- The selector emits an immediate commitment receipt; the environment, other
  agents, and constraints separately determine the outcome receipt.

**[S] The seven operators below are routing profiles inside that interface, not
the source of sovereignty.** An agent team brings a decision to the L4 commit
route cleanly. Normatively valid acts sit inside an `AuthorizationEnvelope` that identifies the
principal, mandate, scope, consent, custody, revocation or expiry, contest path,
actor, and consequence bearer. K2 remains a private-DAV governance instance,
not a primitive of agency.

## 2. The competence tree branches from the transfer signatures

Each operator is a configurable **routing profile**. Its competence bundle is
selected and tested against the work it is meant to route; it is not dictated
by a mythic name, Varṇa label, or sign sketch.

| Operator | The move it makes | Competence branch (the lineage) |
|---|---|---|
| **Kali** (firewall) | classify evidence sufficiency and contradiction surfaces; do not act on a bearer | boundary / firewall: typed intake {−1,0,+1}, spam/prompt-injection/adversarial detection, ambiguity stop, evidence packet |
| **Kālī** (explorer) | expand candidates and surface suspected false coherence for audit; do not excise it | exploration / analogy: candidate generation, counterexamples, evidence-tier disclosure, handoff to ranking |
| **Kṛṣṇa** (auditor) | rank and test candidates; export a decision-ready packet without choosing | audit / enable: inference checks, uncertainty, capability export, the charioteer that steers but does not fight |
| **Arjuna** (executor) | assess prospective Justice, validate authorization, and propose the smallest commitment | decide / protect / execute: boundary-setting, accountable D4 commitment/action receipt, followed by a world-issued outcome receipt |
| **Brahmā** (architect boundary) | propose alternative architectures without mutation authority | create / architect: systematize options, owners, failure modes, and kill criteria in a read-only redesign packet |
| **Śiva** (compressor boundary) | propose archive-first compression without mutation authority | prune / dissolve: read-only successor/tombstone plan, reversibility, and risk disclosure |
| **Viṣṇu** (witness boundary) | propose constitutional interpretation without mutation authority | preserve / witness: source-and-tier review, amendment packet, and explicit return to the authorized L4 route |

A configured **agent team** is therefore an ensemble over the seven profiles:
L4 routes accountable writes, L1–L3 supply bounded candidates and audits, and
the Executive rows witness architecture, pruning, and constitutional context.
Every deployment also needs typed inputs/outputs, dispatch predicates,
handoff/loop/stop rules, permissions, tool/model/token budgets, and blinded
evaluation. A role prompt is a treatment to test, not proof of a competence.

## 3. Economics, niche, and politics are projections (not a genotype)

What a DAC **models as possible economics** (D5 content carried in D4 model
tokens), **who it competes with** (its ecological niche), and **how it governs**
(its regime) may be read as projections of the selected seven-vector; they are
not causally determined by it or independent genotype axes:

- A **Kṛṣṇa-heavy** agent → enabling / service economics (it exports capability).
- A **Brahmā-heavy** agent → creation / IP economics (it makes new structure).
- A **Kālī-heavy** agent → audit / assurance economics (it sells truth-cuts).
- A **Viṣṇu-heavy** agent → custody / preservation economics (it holds patterns).

The five "economic intensities" κ/λ/μ/ρ/ν are a *factor-economics read-out* of
this projection, useful as a lens but **not a second genotype**. They do not
follow causally from the selected profile. Under Rosetta Primacy, such a number
is a read-out to test, not a gene.

## 4. The constitution as dispatch and permission discipline

The invariants can be read as **rules for dispatch, refusal, and permission**.
This does not repeal the constitutional axiom namespace; it routes each
invariant back to the operator source before reuse:

- **η=0** is a necessary non-extraction screen in this private-DAV grammar,
  not a sufficient ethical verdict or a dispatch predicate by itself. No
  profile is dispatched merely because another actor has been labeled a defector.
- **[S] Accountable authorization** is the **Arjuna discipline** — the decision surface names who authorizes, who acts, who pays, who benefits, and how the act can be contested. Private-DAV K2 may instantiate this rule but does not define it.
- **[S] Executive boundary** — Brahmā/Śiva/Viṣṇu are **boundaries/phases**, not ordinary moves; an agent reads them, it does not "deploy" them as sovereign runtime modes.
- **[S] A7** — tier every claim; **K3** — Śiva prunes by archive rather than silent deletion; **K4** — no lock-in.

The historical private-DAV **5+1** may instantiate permission discipline for this
router, but it is product governance, not a worldview or agency primitive. No
invariant should be reused without naming scope, tier, authorization, and
archive/receipt implications.

> **Not "six refusals."** The constitution is **5 + 1**: five refusals (η=0, K2, K3, K4, A7) on the *refusal* axis **plus Ω** the directional `+1` (noospheric direction) on a categorically distinct *direction* axis — never a sixth refusal.
