## 3. Comparative Advantage Calculus

### 3.1 The φ×ν Efficiency Frontier

For any two chapters A and B producing the same output type:

```
Chapter A has comparative advantage over B if:
  (Φ_A × ν_A) / (Φ_B × ν_B) > 1.0
```

Where:
- Φ = coherence (alignment with constitution, Mission, Vision)
- ν = viability (probability of successful execution given resources)

**But comparative advantage is not just about absolute efficiency.** It's about *relative* opportunity cost.

### 3.2 Opportunity Cost Matrix

For each chapter, calculate opportunity cost of producing each output type:

```
OC(chapter, output) = (Φ × ν of best alternative output) / (Φ × ν of this output)
```

**Rule:** A chapter should specialise in the output where its opportunity cost is *lowest* — even if another chapter is absolutely more efficient.

### 3.3 Example: QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) vs. Agentz Settlement Specialisation

| Output | QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) Φ×ν | Agentz Φ×ν | QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) OC | Agentz OC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Token minting | 0.45 | 0.89 | 1.98 | 1.00 |
| Fiat settlement | 0.89 | 0.34 | 1.00 | 2.62 |
| Sharia certification | 0.23 | 0.78 | 3.87 | 1.14 |
| Regulatory compliance | 0.91 | 0.41 | 1.00 | 2.17 |

**Comparative advantage:**
- QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) specialises in: fiat settlement, regulatory compliance
- Agentz specialises in: token minting, Sharia certification

Even though Agentz has higher absolute Φ×ν in token minting (0.89 vs QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail)'s 0.45), QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail)'s opportunity cost in fiat settlement is lower. Specialisation maximises total ecosystem output.

### 6.1 When to Trade vs. Integrate

```
Trade if:    (Φ_trade × ν_trade) > (Φ_integrate × ν_integrate) + coordination_cost
Integrate if: vertical integration reduces ρ (regulatory burden) or κ (capital duplication)
```

**Example:** QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) could build its own token minting (integrate) or trade with Agentz (trade).

- Φ_integrate = 0.45 (QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) has some token capability)
- ν_integrate = 0.60 (but regulatory complexity increases ρ)
- Φ_trade = 0.89 (Agentz is better at minting)
- ν_trade = 0.85 (contract-based interface is reliable)
- coordination_cost = 0.05 (API PAY settlement friction)

**Trade wins:** (0.89 × 0.85) = 0.76 > (0.45 × 0.60) + 0.05 = 0.32

## 7. Economies of Scale and Scope

### 7.1 Scale Economics

For capital-intensive chapters (κ > 0.30):

```
Average cost = (Fixed cost × κ) / Volume + Marginal cost
Economies of scale exist if: d(Average cost)/d(Volume) < 0
```

**Scale threshold:** When volume exceeds fixed cost / (target margin × κ), the chapter should expand, not speciate.

**Example:** QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail) settlement rail
- Fixed cost (κ): SAR 2.5B digital bank capital
- Target margin: 2% spread
- Scale threshold: SAR 125B annual settled volume
- Below threshold: expand within QNTM (the institutional MPC/ZK-Identity rail)
- Above threshold: speciate into standalone settlement DAC

### 7.2 Scope Economics

For labour-intensive chapters (λ > 0.30):

```
Scope efficiency = (Revenue from service A + Revenue from service B) / 
                  (Cost of joint production × 1.2)
Economies of scope exist if: Scope efficiency > 1.0
```

The 1.2 factor accounts for coordination overhead.

**Example:** ADVISORY chapter provides both regulatory advisory and investor relations.
- Joint production cost: 1.0 (shared expert time)
- Separate production cost: 0.6 + 0.6 = 1.2
- Scope efficiency: 1.2 / (1.0 × 1.2) = 1.0
- At break-even; scope is justified if client demand requires bundled service.

### 7.3 Speciation vs. Expansion Decision Tree

```
Is the chapter capital-intensive (κ > 0.30)?
  YES → Is volume above scale threshold?
    YES → Speciate (spin off standalone)
    NO → Expand (absorb more volume)
  NO → Is scope efficiency > 1.2?
    YES → Expand scope (add adjacent services)
    NO → Speciate (focus on core)
```

- Structural levels (L1, L5, L7) mutate at 50% lower rate
- Range: [0.3, 2.0]

**2. Point mutation (economic)** — Dirichlet perturbation
- All 5 economic dimensions perturbed simultaneously
- Maintains sum-to-1.0 invariant via Dirichlet distribution
- Markets move faster than cognition: 1.5× caste mutation rate

**3. Copy number variation (CNV)** — Duplication or deletion
- Duplication: boost one caste level by 40%, redistribute from others
- Deletion: reduce one caste level by 40%, redistribute to others
- Biological parallel: gene duplication creates redundancy; deletion creates dependency

**4. Horizontal gene transfer (HGT)** — Inter-chapter capability borrowing
- Fitness-weighted donor selection
- Caste HGT: transferable levels = L2, L3, L4, L6 (not structural)
- Economic HGT: transferable dims = κ, λ, μ, ν (not ρ, which is jurisdiction-bound)
- 30% gap closure per event

**5. Factor substitution** — Technology-driven factor swap
- Example: AI adoption reduces λ (labour), increases μ (knowledge)
- Mass transfer between two economic dimensions
- Triggered by L5 redesign + L6 compression in phenotype

**6. Regulatory shock** — Jurisdiction-driven ρ spike
- Entering UK FCA sandbox: ρ +25%
- Entering SAMA sandbox: ρ +25%
- Other dimensions reduced proportionally to maintain sum=1.0

**6b. Network amplification** — Metcalfe threshold crossing
- When node count crosses threshold (default 100): ν increases
- Amplification proportional to log₁₀(node_count / threshold)
- Other dimensions reduced proportionally

### 16.1 Economic Efficiency Factor

```
Economic cost = κ×capital_cost + λ×labour_cost + μ×knowledge_cost + ρ×regulatory_cost + ν×network_cost
F_econ = min(1.0, revenue / (2 × economic_cost))
```

A chapter with 2× revenue-to-cost ratio achieves 100% economic fitness. Below 1×, fitness decays linearly.

### 16.2 η Compliance Factor
