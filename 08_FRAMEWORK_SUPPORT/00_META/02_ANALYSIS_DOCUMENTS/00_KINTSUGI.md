## BREAK 1: N=3 Is Not the Primitive Set
**What broke:** Proof 1 v1.0 claimed {0, 1, ∞} were three primitives. The reviewer showed: 1 ∈ {0, 1}, so 1 is trivially in the closure of any subset containing it. Emergence self-contradicts.
**The gold:** [S/I] {0, ∞} is the primitive pair. 1 emerges from their interaction via the Möbius involution. The repair reframes the Zero-Sum Resolution Equation as theorem-level within this paper's argument, not just as a sigil. The framework's own symbol was treated as the proof path that had not yet been seen.
**Why it's stronger:** [I] The framework now says what it always meant: the ground is duality (nothing and everything). The unit emerges between them. Emergence is real, not assumed.

## BREAK 2: 0 × ∞ Is Undefined
**What broke:** Multiple reviewers showed that 0 × ∞ is formally undefined on ℂP¹. Using it as an algebraic step in a formal proof is invalid.
**The gold:** The Möbius involution z ↦ 1/z exchanges 0 and ∞ with fixed point 1. The triad emerges as orbit + fixed point. This is mathematically clean AND more elegant — the involution is a symmetry, not an arithmetic hack.
**Why it's stronger:** The framework now rests on a symmetry principle (the involution), not on an indeterminate form. Symmetry is deeper than arithmetic.

## BREAK 3: B(ν) Is Not Strictly Concave
**What broke:** B''(ν) changes sign at ν = √3. The claim of global strict concavity was false.
**The gold:** B(ν) is strictly pseudo-concave (unimodal). In the θ-parameterization, B(θ) = sin(θ) IS strictly concave. The proof now works in BOTH parameterizations, each correctly characterized.
**Why it's stronger:** The dual-parameterization treatment is more rigorous than the original single-parameterization claim.

## BREAK 4: The Game Has Zero Strategic Interaction

**The gold:** ν = 1 is a STRICTLY DOMINANT STRATEGY, not merely a Nash equilibrium. Price of Anarchy = 1. This is the "holy grail" of mechanism design — a system where selfishness and altruism converge automatically.
**Why it's stronger:** A dominant strategy equilibrium is STRONGER than a Nash equilibrium. The reviewer's criticism upgraded the result.

## BREAK 5: "Geometric Exclusion" Is Wrong — The State Exists
