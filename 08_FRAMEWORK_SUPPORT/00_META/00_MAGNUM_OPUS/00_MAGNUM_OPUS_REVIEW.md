## Core Weaknesses / Risks

### 1. Axiom Dependency Remains The Deepest Philosophical Vulnerability

The review identifies one load-bearing premise:

> Evil is a negation, not a substance.

The rest of the architecture may be developed rigorously from that premise, but the premise itself is not independently forced on the reader.

This means the manuscript is strongest as:

- a coherent framework conditional on a central ontological wager

and weakest as:

- something that pretends the wager is already universal common ground

### 2. A-Brahmism Scope Risk Persists

The review confirms a major reception hazard:

- the umbrella term is structurally ambitious
- but its breadth risks being read as polemic or over-generalization

This aligns with the existing refinement requirement for a prominent structural-vs-theological disclaimer.

### 3. Empirical Gaps Still Matter

The review flags several claims as under-documented in the current presentation:

- total suffering maximized
- long-duration continuous operation claims
- fertility-collapse and related civilizational claims

This does not destroy the framework.
It does mean the manuscript should not let empirical confidence outrun its documentation layer.

### 4. The Self-Assessment Layer Still Needs External Pressure

The L6-style coherence scoring is valuable.
It is also self-assessed.

That makes it useful as an internal gauge, but not yet a substitute for outside scrutiny.

### 5. The "How Gap" Persists

The review agrees with earlier signals:

- the corpus explains what and why better than how
- recovery protocols exist
- but implementation specificity is still thinner than the diagnosis layer

That means the manuscript is stronger as analytical and cognitive technology than as a finished deployment manual.
