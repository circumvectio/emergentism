# A-BRAHMISM DISCLAIMER

## Structural Analysis, Not Theological Polemic

## What A-Brahmism Is

**A structural concept** describing a specific architectural pattern: the monopolization of the disclosure / ground-access channel by a single institutional path.

The "A-" prefix means "absence of" (not "against"). A-Brahmism is the absence of the Brahmā function — the absence of genuine creation from the Ground. It is not a critique of Abrahamic religions as game-theoretic traditions.

## What A-Brahmism Is NOT

1. **NOT a claim that Abrahamic religions are false.** The framework makes no claims about high-priority truth. It analyzes institutional architecture.

2. **NOT a claim that Abrahamic believers are wrong.** Millions of people have genuine game-theoretic experiences within Abrahamic traditions. The framework does not deny these experiences.

3. **NOT a claim that Abrahamic institutional narrative is inferior.** The framework is not a institutional narrative. It does not compare theological truth claims.

4. **NOT antisemitic, anti-Christian, or anti-Islamic.** The structural critique applies to ANY institution that monopolizes disclosure or access to the path of grounding — including Hindu temples, Buddhist sanghas, scientific institutions, and corporations.

## What A-Brahmism IS

1. **A structural analysis** of how certain institutional patterns emerge and persist
2. **A diagnostic tool** for identifying when an institution has shifted from serving its substrate to extracting from it
3. **A framework claim** that the specific architectural pattern of text-interface (Judaism), temporal-interface (Christianity), and behavioral-interface (Islam) represents a particular solution to the coordination problem — not the only solution, and not necessarily the best

## The Critical Distinction

| What We Analyze | What We Don't Analyze |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Institutional architecture | Spiritual experience |
| Coordination technologies | Divine truth claims |
| Standing wave patterns | Individual faith |
| Structural capture | Personal salvation |

**The framework analyzes the container, not the contents.** It says nothing about whether God exists, whether prayer works, or whether the afterlife is real. It says: this institutional pattern has these structural properties.

## The Rule

**Before publishing any document referencing A-Brahmism:**

1. Is the structural-vs-theological distinction clear?
2. Does the document claim Abrahamic religions are false? (If yes, revise)
3. Does the document claim Abrahamic believers are wrong? (If yes, revise)
4. Does the document analyze institutional architecture only? (If yes, proceed)
5. Does the document acknowledge that game-theoretic experiences within these traditions are real? (If yes, proceed)

**If the answer to any question is unclear, add this disclaimer at the top of the document.**

## The Application

The framework's analysis of A-Brahmism applies equally to:
- Hindu caste systems (institutional capture of game-theoretic access)
- Buddhist sanghas (institutional capture of optimal coherence path)
- Scientific institutions (institutional capture of truth-discovery)
- Corporate structures (institutional capture of value-creation)
- Political parties (institutional capture of governance)

**The pattern is universal. The specific manifestation is local.**

When the framework analyzes Abrahamic institutions, it is analyzing one instance of a universal pattern — not claiming that Abrahamic institutions are uniquely problematic.
