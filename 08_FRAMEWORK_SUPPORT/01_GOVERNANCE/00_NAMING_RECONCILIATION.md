## Part II: The Executive / God / Witness Resolution

### The Problem

Three incompatible operator ontologies have coexisted:

1. **MF-309 / Correction 1 (canonical):** 3 Titans (boundary conditions, NOT deployable) + 4 Gods (deployable operators) + 3 Witnesses
2. **Vol III Ch 13 (book):** 5 "Divine Algorithms" (Brahmā/Viṣṇu/Śiva/Kṛṣṇa/Kali) -- all treated as deployable
3. **AGNI Folder 2 / various:** "Stabilize (Viṣṇu)" listed as a surface move (contradicts Executive status)

### The Resolution: MF-309 Is Canonical

**Titans** are boundary conditions. They describe PHASES, not actions. They are the weather, not the steering.

| Executive | Function | What It Describes | NOT This |
|-------|----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Brahmā** | Creation boundary | The phase when new structure emerges | Not an operator you "deploy" |
| **Viṣṇu** | Preservation boundary | The phase when structure holds stable | Not an operator you "deploy" |
| **Śiva** | Transformation boundary | The phase when old structure dissolves | Not an operator you "deploy" |

**Gods** are deployable operators. They are the four orthogonal moves on the Phi-V plane.

| God | Direction | Function | Shorthand |
|-----|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Arjuna** | upward Phi | Integrate meaning, set boundaries, protect | The Warrior |
| **Kṛṣṇa** | upward V | Build capability, expand capacity | The Charioteer |
| **Kali** | downward Phi | Excise false meaning, surgical decay | The Ego-Slayer |
| **Kali (Demon)** | downward V | Extract capability, parasitic drain | The Extractor (CONSTRAINED: fires only at η > 0; excluded at η = 0) |

**Witnesses** are observational positions at L5-L7. They see the field but do not directly deploy operators.

### How This Resolves Each Contradiction

**Vol III Ch 13 ("Five Divine Algorithms"):**
The three Titans DESCRIBE the phases that the four Gods IMPLEMENT. When the book says "deploy Brahmā," it means: "the system is in a creation phase, which results from deploying Arjuna (upward Phi) and/or Kṛṣṇa (upward V)." The Titans are emergent descriptions of operator combinations, not independent operators. Future editions of Vol III Ch 13 should clarify this.

**"Stabilize (Viṣṇu)" in AGNI and other teaching documents:**
[S/I] "Stabilize" means "hold the current operator." It is the Isomeric Hold -- continue doing what is already working without shifting to a new mode. It is NOT deploying Viṣṇu as an operator. The Viṣṇu boundary condition is what RESULTS from holding your current operator steady. The name "Stabilize" is permitted as a pedagogical shorthand when accompanied by the clarification that it refers to maintaining the current operator, not deploying a fifth one.

**"Viṣṇu intervention" in operational documents (README, P_SCORE_ASSESSMENT, L4_STABILIZATION_STACK, CORPUS_COHERENCE_AUDIT):**
These documents use "Viṣṇu" as metaphorical shorthand for "we need to stabilize before creating more." This is acceptable in operational/informal contexts but must not be read as contradicting the formal operator core state. In formal contexts, say: "Hold the current operator (Isomeric Hold)" or "Stabilize before creating."
