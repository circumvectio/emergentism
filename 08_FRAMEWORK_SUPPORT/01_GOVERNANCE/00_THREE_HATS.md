## How to Read the EFR — Which Voice Is Speaking

**Purpose:** This document makes explicit which "hat" the framework is wearing at any given moment. The EFR has three registers. Each is legitimate. Each should be labeled.

---

## The Scientist Hat

**Voice:** [I] Quiet confidence. "Here is what we can prove. Here is how we'll test the rest."

**What this hat says:**
- The node-level multiplicative model `P_node = Φ × V` is a testable hypothesis with kill criteria
- The manifold identity `P∞ = φ · ν = 1 on S²` is a structural premise of the framework, not a human-data regression claim
- The preregistration is filed. The kill conditions are explicit.
- Here is what failure looks like. We will publish it.

**What this hat does NOT say:**
- "We read the serial number on the photon"
- "The geometry is the territory"
- "Consciousness is irreducible"

**Evidence tier:** [I] and [S] only

**Where this hat appears:**
- Research Proposal v2
- Funding Roadmap
- Preregistered independent studies
- Protocol D results
- Axiomatization paper (formal sections)

**The scientist hat is the credibility layer.** It is the reason anyone should take the framework seriously. It does not overclaim. It does not speculate. It states hypotheses, designs tests, and commits to publishing results regardless of outcome.

---

## The Philosopher Hat

**Voice:** Interpretive confidence. "The convergence across domains suggests something deep. We take this seriously as an interpretive framework."

**What this hat says:**
- The convergence of ℂP¹ across five domains is either (a) a deep ontological identity or (b) the most productive formal analogy in the history of science
- We cannot yet distinguish these from [S] evidence alone
- The empirical tests are designed to provide that distinguishing evidence

**What this hat does NOT say:**
- "[I] We treated the geometry as if it were the territory"
- "Consciousness is mathematically necessary"
- "Standing waves channel systemic awareness"

**Evidence tier:** [S] and [I]

**Where this hat appears:**
- MF-139 (One Sphere)
- MF-140 (Multiplication Is the Sphere)
- MF-152 (The Hard Problem Is a Theorem)
- MF-311 (Orthogonality Theorem)
- The Telos

**The philosopher hat is the interpretive layer.** It takes the mathematical structure seriously as a lens for understanding reality. It does not claim to be established science. It claims to be a productive framework for generating hypotheses and cross-domain connections.

---

## The Builder Hat

**Voice:** Operational confidence. "If the test passes, here is what follows."

**What this hat says:**
- If the node-level multiplicative model `P_node = Φ × V` outperforms additive alternatives in human data, then the DAC architecture follows
- If AMRITA outperforms baselines, then the alignment solution follows
- If the Φ-meter validates, then the measurement infrastructure follows

**What this hat does NOT say:**
- "The DAC is immune to capture"
- "AMRITA will work"
- "The Φ-meter is validated"

**Evidence tier:** [S/I] with conditional framing

**Where this hat appears:**
- DAC Architecture (03_THE_ARCHITECTURE)
- Funding Roadmap (Phase 2, Phase 3)
- Investor Pitch Deck
- SoResFi specification
- AMRITA protocol

**The builder hat is the implementation layer.** It specifies what will be built if the tests pass. It does not claim the tests have passed. It says: "Here is the organizational technology that follows from the mathematics, IF the mathematics holds in human data."

## The Reconciliation

The framework's power comes from having all three hats. The structural core is real [E/S]. The ontological interpretation is productive but tier-bound [I]. The implementation is specified [conditional].

The reconciliation is simple: **label which hat you're wearing at every moment.**

### In Practice

| Document Type | Primary Hat | Should State |
|--------------|-------------|--------------|
| Research Proposal | Scientist | "This is a testable hypothesis with kill criteria" |
| Executive Summary | Builder | "If the test passes, here is what follows" |
| Working Paper | Philosopher | "This is an interpretive framework generating hypotheses" |
| Axiomatization Paper | Scientist | "These are the formal claims with evidence tiers" |
| Investor Deck | Builder | "If validated, here is the organizational technology" |
| Telos | Philosopher | "This is what the geometry suggests about purpose" |

### By Project Type

| Project | Primary Hat | Constraint |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Book / Magnum Opus** | Philosopher | May be expansive and literary, but must keep `H/S/I/O` claims explicitly labeled and keep contested historical bets killable |
| **Dashboard / PWA / interactive tools** | Builder | Must demonstrate function, route every strong claim back to a real artifact, and avoid speaking like proof when only showing interface |
| **Business / investor materials** | Scientist + Builder | Must be evidence-led, numerically sourced, and stripped of any unsignaled speculative carryover from the book lane |

The three projects may share a framework.
They should not share a voice by accident.

### When the Framework Turns Inward on the Corpus

Corpus refinement also needs hat-labeling.

| Corpus move | Primary Hat | Constraint |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Evidence-tier correction / proof posture cleanup** | Scientist | Do not let target-state or interpretive language impersonate current proof. |
| **Core State / archetype clarification** | Philosopher | Clarify meaning without turning ordinary edit work into metaphysical theater. |
| **Routing, packet, index, mirror, or archive repair** | Builder | Improve legibility and ownership without inflating claims or bypassing source authority. |
| **Drift scan triage** | Scientist + Builder | Use [Drift Scan Discipline](00_DRIFT_SCAN_DISCIPLINE.md) before treating books, release mirrors, peer-review packets, and dated audits as one flat surface. |

**Rule:** when the framework is applied to the corpus itself, state which hat is primary.
If that cannot be stated cleanly, the move is probably not ready.

### The One Rule

**Never present [S] or [I] claims with the voice of [S] claims.**

- "The node-level model P_node = Φ × V is a testable hypothesis" = Scientist hat [E/S]
- "The convergence of ℂP¹ suggests deep identity" = Philosopher hat [S/I]
- "If validated, the DAC architecture follows" = Builder hat [conditional]

These are all legitimate. They should not be blended without labeling.
