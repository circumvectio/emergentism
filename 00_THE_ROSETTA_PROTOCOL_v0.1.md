# The Rosetta Protocol v0.1 — Be the Stone, Not the Decree

> **The one-line doctrine:** the most famous artifact of translation in human history is, in substance, a priesthood tax filing — and nobody remembers the filing. The stone became immortal because the same meaning was carved three times in three registers. **We were, for one message, about to become the decree. We should be the stone.**

## §0 · The joke history kept for 2,200 years

`[S]` — well-attested history, with one `[I]` gloss kept honest.

The Rosetta Stone's content is the **Memphis Decree of 196 BC** — a proclamation by the priesthood of Memphis honoring the young Ptolemy V Epiphanes, confirming among other things the temples' privileges, including tax remissions. That is the content: in substance, **a religious tax-exemption filing**. (`[I]` gloss: "a tax-exemption filing" is our cheeky compression; the decree is a broader honors-and-privileges instrument. The compression survives contact with the scholarship; the wit is marked as wit.)

Nobody remembers the decree. Nobody has *ever* cared about the decree. The stone became immortal for one reason only: **the same meaning was written three times, in three scripts** — hieroglyphic for the sacred register, demotic for the street, Greek for administration and commerce. The content died with its dynasty. The *parallelism* unlocked a civilization: Champollion cracked a dead sacred script because one column was written in a language the living still read.

The stone never claimed the three scripts were cosmically aligned. It never argued that hieroglyphic and Greek share a deep essence. It just put them **in parallel** — and that humble parallelism did what no proof could: it let anyone who knew one script *enter* the others.

## §3 · The Greek-column principle

A translation stone works only if **one column is already readable by everyone.** Without it, the stone is three mysteries stacked; with it, the known column becomes the key that decrypts the sacred ones.

Our columns, mapped:

| Script | Our register | Surface |
|---|---|---|
| **Hieroglyphic** (sacred) | The doctrine — the sphere, the Titans, the glyph | The canon, the Book, `/axioms/` |
| **Demotic** (vernacular) | The plain telling — the fable, the overview | `/fable/`, `/plainly/` |
| **Greek** (universally readable) | **Value** — working products, honest money, receipts, exits that open, fees only where value was created | The organs: Circle, RealityFutures, APU.BOT, Skyzai, Aureus |

The Greek column is the one register every living person already reads: **receipts verifiable with zero faith.** It must be carved first and deepest, because it is the column strangers can check without believing anything. This is THE_SPREAD §1 (users, not believers) restated in stone: **ship the Greek; the curious will decipher the hieroglyphs on their own** — the way Champollion did, drawn in by the column he could verify.

## §4 · The three-scripts rule (shipping doctrine)

**The rule:** every product, page, and institution carries the same truth in three registers, always parallel —

1. **The story** (fable-script): why this exists.
2. **The plain terms** (demotic): what it does, what it refuses, honestly tiered.
3. **The receipt** (Greek): the contract, the fee table, the exit clause, the ledger entry — verifiable with zero faith.

**One decree, three scripts, always parallel. Any artifact readable in only one register is a broken stone.** A product with a receipt but no story is inert; a story with no receipt is posture 2; plain terms with no receipt are marketing. The rule is a ship-gate: no organ artifact ships mono-script.

The site now carries the rule's first instance `[S]` on disk: `/fable/` (story, 2026-07-18) → `/plainly/` (plain terms) → `/record/` + `/axioms/` (receipts and graded claims) — the funnel as a three-script stone in public.
