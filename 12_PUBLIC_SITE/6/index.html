§1 · The Moves

What Is Actually Asserted Here

The scaffold is selected, not discovered [I]. Five positive rungs and five apertures — matter, bond, life, mind, choice — are a chosen taxonomy. D6 is the scaffold's chosen exit marker: our naming, our act.

D6~D0 means D6 ≈role D0 [I]: resemblance of function (limit-marking), with no implication of equality, ordering, geometry, homeomorphism, physical recurrence, fractal self-similarity, personal attainment, supermind, or proof that no further freedom exists. If a sixth positive freedom ever emerges, this page is renumbered, not defended.

"The state-of-all-states is self-similar to no-state" is not an asserted result. Without a typed state space, a meta-state, and named similarity invariants, it collapses unlike objects. The only survivor is the narrower role analogy already stated: two selected limit markers. The spiral is an image [I], not geometry or self-similarity.

Inherited, not ours: the incompleteness family (Gödel, Tarski) is used, not discovered — and used narrowly: it motivates humility about self-description; it does not lift to "every D_n problem needs D_{n+1} tools" (that claim is dead — Presburger arithmetic and real-closed fields are complete and decidable). Wittgenstein 6.1 is likewise inherited: a tautology is the frame and says nothing about content.

Ours: the refusal to close. The kernel commits no completeness claim; "we are tautological so we cannot be wrong" is a forbidden shield — it would kill the correction axiom that everything else stands on. No description closes or certifies itself; contact with living readers remains external evidence, not metaphysical closure.

§2 · The Dissolutions

What This Height Refuses to Claim

The Liar / self-reference family is not dissolved here. The Liar is not an infinite map-containing-map regress, and an external reader does not provide a formal closure theorem. The page instead adopts a limited practice [I]: the corpus refuses self-certification and leaves formal results to their declared systems. The reader judges whether the map earned its keep; that is governance and pedagogy, not a solution to the Liar.

The completeness temptation. The pull, at the top of any ladder, to declare the system total — "the framework explains the framework." Cure: the tautology is the frame, not the content (settled ruling, K2 2026-07-12). A tautological chart identity carries no contingent world-content; this does not imply that every necessary truth is empty. Contentful world-claims can still lose. Named open debts stay open: consciousness, Born weights, why this world, μ-saturation.

The ladder-worship failure. Mistaking the exit marker for a sixth prize; climbing D6 as attainment. Cure: the grave. D6 ≡ D0 died in audit and stays dead; "~" is fenced at [I]. A summit that were literally the ground would make the climb a circle and the climber a fool. The similarity is the honest residue: you return changed, carrying the record — the spiral, not the circle.

§3 · The Ology

An Epistemology of Exit

[I/D] Every prior rung answers "what can be known here?" with more structure. D6 answers with a direction: out. The last thing this map teaches is how to put it down — and that this was always the design. A worldview whose door is painted on is a cage; this one marks the exit on the inside.

Facing the gap between any formal system and the world, the framework distinguishes four postures [I] (the taxonomy is chosen; its ethics is a reading): deny the gap (dogma — makes subjects), weaponize it (propaganda — makes ammunition), confess it (this corpus's wager — restores agency), withdraw from it (the apophatic move — put the wager down when it is no longer needed, and keep your freedom). The fourth posture is D6 practiced. The grace-exit refusal is the fourth posture written into constitution: no entrapment, assets redeemable, the leaving blessed.

1 · The map is good exactly where it is checkable — tiers on, kills attached, graves visible; nothing here is to be obeyed, only checked. 2 · The map cannot certify itself — you may decide whether it earned its keep, but that act is not a formal closure the text lacks. 3 · When the map has done its work, put it down — if you can reach what it points at directly, the pointing is finished.

The ethic below this height is a vow, never a theorem. D5's universalization test is structural only inside named closure and regeneration premises; D6 adds nothing to the vow. It notices only that a finite agent or constitution chose the horizon, maximand, and enforcement. The door was inside the whole time. The ladder ends where the climber begins.

Foundations · limits of formal closure

What is axiom, theorem, and chosen exit

D6 has no inherited theorem that proves “return.” It uses results about formal limits to motivate humility, then makes a separate governance choice: no map may certify itself or trap its reader.

Formal systems · inherited [A]

The declared base of any proof

A formal system specifies a language, formation rules, axioms, and inference rules. A conclusion is a theorem only relative to that base. Different logics and axiom sets have different powers; there is no axiom-free view from nowhere.

Gödel + Tarski · inherited theorems [A]

Scoped limits, not universal mysticism

Gödel's incompleteness theorems apply to sufficiently expressive, effectively axiomatized formal systems under named consistency assumptions. Tarski's undefinability result constrains internal truth predicates in sufficiently expressive formal languages. These are theorems, not D6 axioms, and they do not show that every problem needs a higher dimension.

Popperian method · inherited norm

Falsifiability is a discipline

Requiring empirical claims to risk failure is a methodological norm, not a theorem that guarantees truth. Evidence tiers, kill criteria, survivor statements, and external replication operationalize that norm for this corpus.

Emergentism · selected [I]

The local exit rule

D6 ≈role D0 is a selected resemblance between two boundary roles. It is not equality, topology, recurrence, or a theorem of Gödel or Tarski. Grace exit and the refusal of self-certification are constitutional choices; if a sixth positive freedom appears, the scaffold is revised.

Attribution: formal axiomatization, incompleteness, undefinability, and falsifiability are inherited. Emergentism's contribution here is the explicit refusal to turn those limits into a proof of itself.
