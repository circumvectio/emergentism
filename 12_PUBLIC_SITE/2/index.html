§1 · The Moves

The Sphere, the Declared Slice, and the Iron Separation

The sphere, its charts, and the declared slice (inherited: Riemann/stereographic geometry — used, not discovered). Take the unit sphere S² and restrict the scalar pair to a chosen positive-real meridian. A point on that meridian at polar angle θ reads φ = cot(θ/2) and ν = tan(θ/2). The pair omits azimuth and therefore does not parameterize an arbitrary point of the full sphere. Two lamps, one position on the declared slice.

The seam [A]. Multiply the two readings: P∞ = φ · ν = cot(θ/2) · tan(θ/2) ≡ 1 — identically one on the declared open reciprocal slice. A coordinate identity, true by construction and empty of world: never a discovery, a conserved quantity, or a maximum. The two scalar readings encode one independent parameter on this slice; that fact says nothing about the full dimension of S² or about the rung ordinal.

The score [A] — derived by chord product (inherited: circle geometry + AM-GM — used, not discovered). Draw the two chords from the point to the two poles: lengths 2 sin(θ/2) and 2 cos(θ/2). Their product is 4·sin(θ/2)·cos(θ/2) = 2·sin θ. Normalize: B = sin θ = 2/(φ + ν) ≤ 1, with equality iff φ = ν = 1 (AM-GM). B is a genuine variable on the slice: it moves with θ and peaks where the selected meridian crosses the equator. The imbalance price γ = (φ+ν)/2 rises toward either pole along the slice.

The iron separation (keel-108, CC-CORE-1): the seam is never the score is never the node. P∞ ≡ 1 is a constant (chart identity); B ≤ 1 is a variable (max at the equator); P_node = Φ×V is a finite-node model whose product interior is [C] — only its AND-class boundary (lose either factor entirely, lose everything) is settled [S]. The seam stays 1 even as ν → 0; it therefore never witnesses the zero-factor catastrophe, and citing it as the ethics warrant is the convicted laundering move. Three objects, three tiers, no silent bridge.

One-and-Many, and Plato with the Debt Shown

One-and-many. The chart offers an [I] reframe: its constant seam and variable positions answer different mathematical questions, so conflating them is a register error inside this lens. That does not dissolve the world-level metaphysical problem. The tautological unity says nothing about empirical plurality; register separation is a teaching aid, not a proof.

Plato, reframed — with the debt shown. The proposed survivor reads forms as stable organization across constrained degrees of freedom, not as degrees of freedom themselves and not as perfect objects in a separate realm. A typed freedom specifies admissibility; it does not establish actuality. The plenitude/filter and μ aperture remain [C] causal wagers with named tests. This is an interpretive reframe [I], not a proof that two-world metaphysics is false.

What Is There? Typed Existence

[I/D] Ontology asks: what is there? This page answers with typed existence. A thing exists as something — mathematical, modelled, possible, actual, stable, or lived — and no type silently entails the next. φ is a coordinate; B a derived score; the sphere a mathematical lens. None of those facts establishes that the world instantiates the lens.

So the rung's ontology in one breath: being is position-under-constraint, read through declared charts, with the seam as frame and the surface as content. What is necessary here is thin — the chart arithmetic, [A], empty of world. What is actual is thick and stays conjectural — which freedoms are instantiated, which patterns persist, whether das All realizes the algebra at all (W0, the crown wager, never the proof).

The stranger takes one thing from this page: the same point has two readings whose product is always one and whose balance is at most one — and the whole discipline of the framework is refusing to confuse those two sentences.

Foundations · Euclid to the Riemann sphere

The geometric axiom stack

D2 uses more than one geometry. Euclidean circle geometry supports the chord calculation; complex-projective and manifold structure support the sphere. The selected reciprocal meridian is our lens, not Euclid's or Riemann's conclusion.

Euclid · inherited [A]

The five Euclidean postulates

  1. A straight line can be drawn from any point to any point.
  2. A finite straight line can be extended continuously in a straight line.
  3. A circle can be drawn with any centre and radius.
  4. All right angles are equal.
  5. The parallel postulate: in modern form, through a point outside a line there is exactly one parallel to that line.

Modern rigorous treatments usually replace or complete this list with Hilbert- or Tarski-style incidence, order, congruence, continuity, and parallel axioms.

Manifold geometry · inherited [A]

What makes the sphere a surface

A topological 2-manifold is Hausdorff, second-countable, and locally homeomorphic to ℝ². The sphere S² satisfies those conditions. The Riemann sphere adds a compatible complex chart structure and is equivalent to the complex projective line ℂP¹.

Geometry fence · inherited [A]

Euclidean chord calculation; spherical surface

The chord lengths and trigonometric identities on this page use ordinary Euclidean circle geometry. The sphere is locally planar only in the topological/chart sense, not locally flat as a metric surface; globally, its great-circle “lines” do not satisfy Euclid's parallel postulate. Stereographic projection is a standard conformal construction, not an Emergentist axiom.
