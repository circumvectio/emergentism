Every term is real. The metric is real. The stress-energy tensor is real. The cosmological constant is real. Gravity curves real spacetime along a real worldline. 2.2 Quantum Mechanics Is Imaginary

Quantum Mechanics is constructed on complex numbers. The Schrödinger equation:

iħ ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ

The i is right there in front. It is not removable. The wave function ψ is complex-valued. Probability amplitudes are complex. Interference patterns arise because complex numbers interfere — they have phases that add and cancel. Superposition is the coexistence of multiple complex amplitudes. Measurement collapses the complex wave function to a single real outcome.

[I] Attempts to reformulate QM without complex numbers have faced substantive no-go and experimental challenges. The Hilbert space of quantum states is a complex vector space. The Born rule |ψ|² projects complex amplitudes to real probabilities. The unitary evolution e^(−iĤt/ħ) rotates states through complex phase space.
