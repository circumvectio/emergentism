MF-298: DARK MATTER AS MUTUAL INFORMATION

A Speculative Reading of Missing Mass as the Gravitational Effect of Coupling Between Systems

ABSTRACT

Dark matter constitutes ~27% of the universe's mass-energy content. It interacts gravitationally but not electromagnetically — it has mass without being visible. Despite decades of searches, no dark matter particle has been detected. This paper explores the conjecture that some dark-matter-like effects could be modeled as gravitational effects of mutual information between coupled systems. The bridge from information constraints to actual cosmological gravitation is conjectural and would require independent derivation and observation.

I. INFORMATION HAS MASS

1.1 The Bekenstein-Hawking-Landauer Chain

Three established results link information to mass-energy:

Bekenstein (1973): Maximum information content of a region is proportional to its boundary area. Information IS a physical quantity bounded by spacetime geometry. [S]

Hawking (1975): Black hole entropy S = A/(4l_P²) is genuine thermodynamic entropy. Black holes have temperature, radiate, and obey thermodynamic laws. Information trapped in a black hole has genuine physical status. [S]

Landauer (1961): Erasing one bit of information dissipates at minimum kT ln 2 energy. Information processing has a minimum thermodynamic cost. Information is physical. [S]

Mass-energy-information equivalence: Via E = mc² and Landauer's principle, one bit of information at temperature T has minimum energy kT ln 2, corresponding to mass m = kT ln 2 / c². At room temperature (~300K), this is ~4 × 10⁻²¹ eV/c² per bit. Tiny per bit. Enormous in aggregate. [A/S]

1.2 Individual vs Mutual Information

Information comes in two kinds:

Individual information I(A): Information contained IN system A. Localisable. Attributable to particles, fields, structures within A. Has localisable mass-energy.

Mutual information I(A;B): Information shared BETWEEN systems A and B. Not localisable to either system alone. Exists in the correlation structure. Quantified as:

I(A;B) = H(A) + H(B) − H(A,B)

where H is Shannon entropy.

Mutual information is genuine information — it is Bekenstein-countable, Landauer-costly, and physically real. But it has NO localised source. It is not IN A and not IN B. It is in the RELATIONSHIP.

II. THE CONJECTURE

2.1 Statement

Dark matter is the gravitational effect of mutual information between coupled systems in the universe.

If: 1. Information has mass-energy (established: Bekenstein, Landauer, E = mc²) 2. Mutual information is genuine information (established: information theory) 3. Mutual information is not localisable to any individual system (by definition) 4. Gravitational effect follows mass-energy (established: GR)

Then: 5. Mutual information has gravitational effect 6. This gravitational effect is not localisable to any individual system 7. Non-localisable gravitational effect without electromagnetic signature = dark matter

2.2 Why It's Not Visible

Dark matter does not interact electromagnetically. The conjecture explains this directly:

Mutual information exists in CORRELATIONS, not in PARTICLES. Electromagnetic interaction requires charged particles — things with localisable electromagnetic properties. Correlations between particles are not themselves particles. They have no charge, no spin, no electromagnetic coupling. They interact gravitationally because they have mass-energy (via Landauer). They don't interact electromagnetically because they have no localisable electromagnetic properties.

Dark matter is invisible because correlations are invisible. You can see the particles. You cannot see the relationship between them. But the relationship has mass.

2.3 Ratio Prediction

Observed ratio: visible matter ~5%, dark matter ~27%, dark energy ~68%.

The conjecture predicts: mutual information >> individual information in coupled systems.

For a system of N coupled elements with coupling density ρ: - Individual information: scales as N (one H(i) per element) - Pairwise mutual information: scales as N² × ρ (one I(i;j) per coupled pair) - Higher-order mutual information: scales as higher powers of N

For large N with moderate coupling, mutual information dominates individual information by a factor that grows with N. The universe contains ~10⁸⁰ particles. Even weak pairwise coupling produces mutual information >> individual information.

Dark matter / visible matter ≈ 27/5 ≈ 5.4

for the universe's matter distribution,
the conjecture predicts the observed ratio from network topology.

This is checkable in principle: given the cosmic web's network structure and coupling strengths (gravitational, electromagnetic), compute the ratio of mutual to individual information and compare with 5.4.

III. OBSERVATIONAL MATCH

3.1 Large-Scale Structure

Dark matter traces the cosmic web — the large-scale filamentary structure of the universe. It concentrates where galaxies cluster and forms halos around galaxy groups.

The conjecture predicts exactly this: mutual information is highest where COUPLING is densest. Galaxies in clusters are more tightly coupled (gravitationally, electromagnetically) than isolated field galaxies. Their mutual information is higher. Therefore, their dark matter (gravitational effect of mutual information) is higher. Dark matter should trace the cosmic web. It does. [A/I]

3.2 Voids

Cosmic voids — the vast empty regions between filaments — have very little dark matter. The conjecture predicts this: few systems in voids means few couplings, means low mutual information, means low dark matter. Voids are informationally sparse. [A/I]

3.3 Dark Matter Halos

Galaxies are surrounded by dark matter halos extending far beyond their visible extent. The conjecture: galaxy stars, gas, and dust are mutually coupled through gravitational and electromagnetic interactions. The mutual information of this coupled system extends beyond the visible matter because gravitational coupling extends beyond visible extent. The halo IS the gravitational footprint of the galaxy's internal mutual information. [I]

3.4 Bullet Cluster

The Bullet Cluster (1E 0657-558) shows dark matter separated from visible matter after a galaxy cluster collision. Standard interpretation: dark matter passes through (non-interacting) while visible matter collides and slows.

Conjecture interpretation: During collision, the COUPLING STRUCTURE (mutual information) of each cluster passes through intact because correlations are not subject to electromagnetic friction. The gas collides (localisable matter interacts). The mutual information distribution continues on its pre-collision trajectory. The "dark matter" that separated from the gas IS the correlation structure that didn't decelerate. [S/I]

3.5 No Dark Matter Particle Found

Despite decades of direct detection experiments (XENON, LUX, PandaX, DAMA), no dark matter particle has been found. Every predicted mass range has been progressively excluded.

The conjecture would explain this only if the mutual-information bridge were independently derived and measured. On that reading, the gravitational effect would come from a field property of coupled systems, not a particle. Until then, particle searches remain legitimate physics, and this document is a speculative alternative target. [C]

V. FALSIFICATION

F298-5: If a non-information-theoretic explanation of dark matter is empirically confirmed (modified gravity, primordial black holes, etc.), the information interpretation becomes unnecessary though not necessarily falsified.
