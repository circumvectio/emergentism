ABSTRACT

A black hole captures everything that crosses its event horizon — matter, energy, information. From the framework's perspective, this resembles the Red Demon pattern (MF-115, C3): extraction without return and circuit-breaking at gravitational scale. Hawking (1974) showed that black holes are not permanent: quantum effects near the horizon cause them to emit thermal radiation, slowly losing mass until they evaporate completely. The information paradox — whether the captured information is truly destroyed or eventually returned — has been the central problem of quantum gravity for fifty years. This paper reads Hawking radiation as an A7 analogy: an extreme boundary where capture may still face return conditions. That analogy is suggestive; it is not a public proof that A7 is enforced by physics.

II. HAWKING RADIATION AS SELF-CORRECTION

2.1 Hawking's Discovery (1974)

Quantum field theory near the event horizon predicts particle-antiparticle pair production. One particle falls in, one escapes. The escaping particle carries energy away from the black hole. The black hole loses mass. Over cosmologically long timescales, the black hole evaporates completely. [S]

The temperature of Hawking radiation:

T_H = ℏc³ / (8πGMk_B)

Inversely proportional to mass: smaller black holes are hotter.
As mass decreases, temperature increases, radiation rate increases.
The evaporation ACCELERATES — the self-correction intensifies as the capture weakens.

2.2 The Information Return

If no: the information MUST be encoded in the Hawking radiation. The emitted photons carry the captured information back to the universe.

Current consensus (following Page curve calculations, island formula, ER=EPR): information IS returned. Unitarity is preserved. The black hole is a temporary information vault, not a permanent one. [A for Page curve; S for island formula interpretation]
