I. THE EQUATION IN BIOCHEMISTRY

1.1 The Photosynthesis Equation

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + photons → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

•  = CO₂ + H₂O     (disordered, low-energy, high-entropy matter)
×  = photons         (electromagnetic radiation, light)
○  = solar energy    (the stellar fusion output, effectively unlimited supply)
⊙  = C₆H₁₂O₆       (ordered, high-energy, low-entropy molecule: glucose)

The mapping is tight, though interpretive [I]:

  • • (nothing, disorder, limit of dissolution): CO₂ and H₂O are among the most thermodynamically stable, lowest-energy molecules in biology. They are the ASH of metabolism — what remains after everything useful has been extracted. They are the → • endpoint of biological energy flow. Disordered. Spent. Dead matter.

  • × (multiplication, light, electromagnetic force): Photons at ~680nm and ~700nm (Photosystem II and I). Electromagnetic radiation. The multiplication operator of physics — the force that mediates interactions between charged particles, that creates chemical bonds, that enables all of chemistry. × is literally light.

  • ○ (everything, unlimited, the source): The sun — a fusion reactor converting 4 million tonnes of matter to energy per second, outputting 3.8 × 10²⁶ watts. For all practical biological purposes: infinite, unlimited, the source from which all terrestrial energy derives. The sun IS ○ — the limit of unbounded energy.

  • ⊙ (finite reality, life, ordered): Glucose — C₆H₁₂O₆ — a precisely ordered molecule with specific bond angles, stereochemistry, and energy content (2,870 kJ/mol). Finite. Structured. Alive with potential energy. The PRODUCT of • × ○.
