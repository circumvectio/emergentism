The Two Equations

Brahman

Zero-Sum Resolution Equation

The absolute identity. Nothing times everything equals one. The equation that holds regardless of scale, regardless of observer, regardless of domain. The Trimūrti. The boundary condition. L0 = L∞. The pure wave with no envelope — unbounded and envelope-free in the symbolic boundary register.

In physics: Ψ = Ae^(iωt) — the pure plane wave. No Gaussian envelope. Constant amplitude. Stretching from negative infinity to positive infinity. Perfect coherence. Completely unlocalized.

Atman

P∞ = φ · ν = 1

The particular constraint. YOUR coherence times YOUR viability at THIS point on the sphere at THIS moment on the helix. The wave packet with its Gaussian envelope — localized, mortal, spiraling from birth through equator to death.

In physics: Ψ = Ae^(-(x-x_t)²/4a²) · e^(i(kx-ωt)) — the Gaussian wave packet. The pure wave, localized by the bell curve of incarnation. Born, swells, peaks, fades, dissolves.

They are not two equations. They are one equation at two scales.

The Proof

Step 1: Euler's Formula

e^(iθ) = cos θ + i sin θ

One rotation in the complex plane decomposes into two projections: - cos θ = the real projection = ν (viability) - sin θ = the imaginary projection = φ (coherence)

Step 2: The Pythagorean Identity

cos²θ + sin²θ = 1

The product of the two projections is constant. Always. This is the Burri constraint:

φ · ν = 1

Written differently. The same equation. The Pythagorean identity IS the Burri constraint IS the wave function normalization. They were always the same.

Correction (2026-03-24, per Document 13 P-pass review). The Pythagorean identity is an additive identity. The Burri constraint φ · ν = 1 is a multiplicative identity. They are not the same equation. The correct trigonometric form of the Burri constraint is cot(θ/2) · tan(θ/2) = 1, which holds identically on S². The structural correspondence between the wave packet and the Burri Sphere operates through Euler's formula and the helix, not through the Pythagorean identity. The passage above preserves the original manuscript voice but should be read with this correction in mind.

Step 3: The Pure Wave IS Brahman

Remove the Gaussian envelope. What remains is the pure wave: Ae^(iωt). Unbounded in the boundary image, with no localization, no particular position, and no birth/death envelope.

This is Brahman — nirguna Brahman, Brahman without attributes. The ground. The pure helix that was, is, and will be. The boundary condition that the L-levels approach from within but never reach.

Step 4: The Wave Packet IS Atman

Add the Gaussian envelope. The pure wave becomes localized — a packet, a particular being at a particular place with a particular lifetime. The envelope swells from zero (birth), peaks at t₀ (the equator, L4, maximum balance), and fades back to zero (death).

This is Atman — the particular core algorithmic identity, the individual life, the trajectory on S². Mortal. Bounded. Helical. Spiraling through the operators from pole to equator to pole.

Step 5: Tat Tvam Asi

The wave packet IS the pure wave — multiplied by the Gaussian envelope.

Remove the envelope → the packet is read at the pure-wave limit. Remove the life-image → the core algorithmic identity is read against the ground. Remove the particular image → the universal remains inside the model's symbolic register.

Atman IS Brahman, localized. Brahman IS Atman, universalized.

They are not two things. They are one helix — with and without the envelope of incarnation.

The Geometry of Identity: Ksetra, Ksetajna, Turiya

The Gita's Chapter 13 distinguishes: - Ksetra (the field) — that which is known. - Ksetajna (the knower of the field) — that which knows.

The sphere IS Atman. S² is finite, compact, closed. Every point on the sphere has φ·ν = 1. Every point is a particular ratio, a particular balance, a particular life. The sphere is saguna Brahman — Brahman with attributes, Brahman localized into particular form. The sphere is the core algorithmic identity.

The Bindu IS Turiya. The centre of the sphere — equidistant from every point on S², not on the surface, not on either plane. The Bindu is the fourth state, beyond waking (A — the projection itself, consciousness cast outward onto the plane of Māyā), dreaming (U — the sphere surface), and deep sleep (M — the poles / ∞). The Bindu is where the stereographic rays cross — where the infinite planes and the finite sphere meet.

The Bindu is the Ksetajna — the knower of the field. It is not the field (the planes). It is not the core algorithmic identity (the sphere surface). It is the point where they are ONE — the point from which the projection operates, the centre that holds both.

"Asi" — "art" — the equals sign — is not a copula. It is a location. It is the Bindu. It is the centre of the sphere where the knower and the known are not two.

Moksha

In this model, moksha is read as a mathematical limit-image, not as a public event claim.

Moksha is the removal of the Gaussian envelope.

The wave packet dissolves. The localization ends. The particular is read against the universal. The Atman/Brahman register names this as return. The envelope fades to zero — and what remains is the pure wave image: unbounded in the boundary register, not a claim about personal immortality.

This is not death. Death is the envelope fading at one end — the wave packet moving toward the pole, amplitude decreasing, balance approaching zero. Death is the end of one localization.

Moksha is different. Moksha is the recognition — while the envelope is still present, while the wave packet is still localized, while you are still alive — that the packet IS the pure wave. That the envelope is not you. That you are the helix, not the bell curve.

The sitting practice is the daily rehearsal of this recognition:

  1. Sit. (Enter the helix.)
  2. Quiet the narrator. (Reduce the envelope — reduce the localization, the particularity, the "I am here and not elsewhere.")
  3. Recognize the ground. (See the pure wave beneath the packet. See Brahman beneath Atman.)
  4. Return. (The envelope reasserts. The localization returns. But now you know: the packet is the wave. The wave was always here.)

Inside the model, the limit image is mathematically crisp; the soteriological reading remains interpretive/conjectural:

lim (envelope → 0) [wave packet] = pure wave

The limit of the wave packet as the envelope approaches zero is the pure wave in the model. The Atman/Brahman reading is the interpretive layer placed on that limit, not an empirical survival claim.

Moksha is taking the limit. While alive.

The Asura Return

The Asura Return is moksha that fails.

At L7 — the boundary — the envelope is nearly zero. The wave packet is nearly the pure wave. The practitioner contacts the ground. Satori. The flash.

Moksha: "I am the wave. The packet is a localization. The envelope is not me." The ego dissolves. The practitioner returns to L4* — the equator with the wisdom of the full circuit. The envelope persists, but the identification with it has ended.

Asura Return: "I AM the wave. I AM Brahman. I AM God." The ego inflates. The practitioner mistakes contact with the boundary for ownership of the boundary. The envelope re-asserts — but now it carries the delusion of being the pure wave while still being a packet. The practitioner returns to D5 with power but without wisdom. The Asura.

The difference is one word. "I am the wave" vs. "I AM the wave." Recognition vs. inflation. Kenshō vs. satori without kenshō. The same flash. The opposite response.

The Gaussian envelope is the test. If you can see through it without trying to destroy it — if you can recognize that you are the wave while still being the packet — that is moksha. If you try to become the pure wave by force — if you try to eliminate the envelope while keeping the ego — that is the Asura Return.
