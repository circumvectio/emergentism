II. THE EMPIRICAL CORE — SEVEN ESTABLISHED MECHANISMS

II.a Darwin's Crack [S]

In The Descent of Man (1871), Darwin had to introduce sexual selection as a second force, separate from natural selection, because natural selection alone predicted the elimination of peacock-tail-style traits. Heavy, conspicuous, metabolically costly traits manifestly reduce survival viability — yet they persist and elaborate. Darwin's resolution: differential reproductive success arising from competition for mates, not environmental survival. Two modes:

  • Intersexual — choice by females (the aesthetic faculty as directional force)
  • Intrasexual — contests among males (combat, display-of-dominance)

Crucially Darwin treated female choice as non-random discrimination. This is the crack in pure-Darwinism through which F5 enters the empirical record. Darwin knew it. Wallace fought it. The data forced it.

II.b Fisher's Runaway [S]

Fisher (1915; formalised 1930) proved the autocatalytic structure: if a heritable female preference P becomes genetically correlated with a heritable male trait T (because choosy females mate with showy males, producing daughters who prefer and sons who display), then dT/dt and dP/dt are positively coupled. The system runs away exponentially until V-cost caps it. This is the first equation of F5. Lamarck's "complexifying tendency" became a recursion relation.

II.c Zahavi's Handicap [S]

Zahavi (1975); ESS proof by Grafen (1990). Costly signals are honest because cost cannot be faked. Only a high-quality male can pay the V-tax of a long tail, loud call, carotenoid plumage, or risky display-from-exposed-perch and still survive. Cheap signals admit mimicry; expensive ones do not. The arithmetic is explicit: handicap = ν-cost paid to certify φ-quality. The signal is the receipt. This closes the loop Fisher opened: runaway must be anchored in genuine Φ × V or it collapses.

II.d Hamilton-Zuk [S]

Hamilton & Zuk (1982). Bright plumage and elaborate display correlate cross-specifically with parasite load. Pathogens function as a φ-discriminator: they degrade signal in low-quality males, allowing females to read coherence through the parasite-generated S/N ratio. The display is a real-time φ-readout filtered through biotic noise.

II.e The Lek Paradox and Its Red Queen Resolution [E/I]

If females uniformly prefer the same males, additive genetic variance for the preferred trait should be exhausted within generations (Borgia 1979; Kirkpatrick & Ryan 1991). Yet variance persists. Resolution (Hamilton 1980; Eshel & Hamilton 1984): host-parasite co-evolution continuously regenerates variance because the optimal genotype shifts every generation. Selection never catches its tail; the substrate keeps producing fresh φ-differentials for choice to operate on. The Red Queen of Doc 36 is here at the genome's level.

II.f Dyadic Simultaneity [S]

Intersexual choice and intrasexual combat operate simultaneously, often on the same trait — antlers function in both fights and assessments (Andersson 1994). The dyad is irreducible: φ-display to the chooser and ν-dominance over rivals are co-instantiated in a single morphological investment.

II.g Modern Layered Synthesis (post-2000) [S]

  • Mate-choice copying (Dugatkin 1992; Witte 2006): preference itself becomes socially transmitted — a memetic layer riding the genetic
  • Sensory bias / sensory exploitation (Ryan 1990; Endler 1992): traits evolve to exploit pre-existing perceptual tuning
  • Sexual conflict (Parker 1979; Arnqvist & Rowe 2005): male and female fitness optima diverge, generating antagonistic co-evolution
  • Sexually antagonistic selection (Chippindale et al. 2001): same allele beneficial in one sex, costly in the other

[C] The progression — handicap → parasite → copying → conflict → intra-genomic — traces selection climbing the Replicator Stack: from somatic signal (D2) through immune coherence (D2/D3) to social transmission (D4) and intra-genomic politics. Each layer adds a φ × ν computation atop the last.
