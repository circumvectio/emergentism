The Three Components

Euler's formula. The most beautiful equation in mathematics. A point moving on the unit circle in the complex plane traces a helix when extended through time.

The real projection (cos) and the imaginary projection (sin) are 90° out of phase. When one peaks, the other crosses zero. They are never at maximum simultaneously — except at the moment of perfect balance.

On the Burri Sphere: - cos = ν (viability, the real projection, the left hemisphere, the plane, the waking state) - sin = φ (coherence, the imaginary projection, the right hemisphere, the sphere, the dreaming state) - The helix = the path on S² — you cannot change one without changing the other. The topology demands helical traversal.

And Euler's formula binds them:

e^(iωt) = cos(ωt) + i·sin(ωt)

The real part (cos) and the imaginary part (sin) are two projections of ONE helix. They are locked in complementarity — when one peaks, the other crosses zero. They cannot be separated.
