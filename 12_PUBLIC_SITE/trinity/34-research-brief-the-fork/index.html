I. Map of Current Positions (2020-2026)

A. The Professional Consensus (Such As It Is)

The 2020 PhilPapers Survey of ~1,800 professional philosophers found: - 51.9% accept or lean toward physicalism about the mind - 32.1% accept or lean toward non-physicalism - ~16% undecided or accept alternatives

Physicalism holds a bare majority. Among non-physicalists, roughly 3/4 are dualists, 1/4 panpsychists. This is the narrowest consensus in the history of the survey — the field is genuinely unsettled.

The hard problem of systemic awareness (Chalmers 1995) remains unresolved. A 2024 analysis in Acta Analytica concluded that progress on the "easy problems" (neural correlates) has not translated into progress on the hard problem. The 2025 Cogitate Consortium adversarial collaboration tested competing theories empirically but found that each captures partial truths, with no clear winner.

B. The Six Live Positions

1. Physicalism (Type-A and Type-B) The view that everything is physical, including systemic awareness. Type-A physicalists deny the explanatory gap (Dennett); Type-B physicalists accept the gap but deny it is ontological (Papineau, Levine). Still the majority view but under pressure from the explanatory gap and the knowledge argument.

2. Property Dualism Physical substances have irreducible mental properties. Chalmers' original position. Faces the interaction problem: how do non-physical properties causally influence physical ones?

3. Panpsychism (Micropsychism) Consciousness is a fundamental feature of reality present at the most basic level. Philip Goff (Durham) has been the primary champion, arguing in Consciousness and Fundamental Reality (2017) that physicalism cannot account for the evident reality of conscious experience. The first half of his argument attacks physicalism; the second half defends Russellian monism leading to panpsychism. In 2025, Goff continued active advocacy, including a widely-discussed Tim Ferriss podcast appearance, making panpsychism genuinely mainstream for the first time.

The combination problem remains panpsychism's central challenge: how do micro-level experiences combine into macro-level systemic awareness?

4. Cosmopsychism (Priority Panpsychism) The cosmos as a whole is the fundamental conscious entity; individual minds are derived from cosmic systemic awareness. Developed by Itay Shani, Philip Goff, and Khai Wager (whose monograph In Defence of Cosmopsychism was published by Bloomsbury Academic in 2025). Cosmopsychism combines priority monism (the universe is the one fundamental thing) with panpsychism. It avoids the combination problem by working top-down rather than bottom-up.

Key insight for Emergentism: cosmopsychism says facts about parts are grounded in facts about wholes. This maps directly onto the S^2 framework where the sphere (the whole) is ontologically prior to any particular latitude (the parts).

5. Analytical Idealism (Kastrup) Bernardo Kastrup's position: systemic awareness is the fundamental reality; the physical world is a manifestation of a transpersonal field of subjectivity. Published Analytic Idealism in a Nutshell in 2025. Kastrup's framework posits that individual minds are "dissociated alters" of a universal systemic awareness, analogous to dissociative identity disorder. The physical world is what universal systemic awareness looks like from the outside.

6. Neutral Monism / Russellian Monism / Dual-Aspect Monism Neither mental nor physical is fundamental; both arise from a deeper "neutral" substrate. Three important variants:

  • Neutral Monism (James, Mach, Russell): A single substance underlies both mind and matter. Currently undergoing a revival — the Stanford Encyclopedia updated its entry for Summer and Fall 2025 editions.

  • Russellian Monism (Goff, Alter, Coleman): Physics describes the world only structurally/dispositionally. The intrinsic nature (quiddities) of physical entities grounds systemic awareness. The quiddities are not themselves physical (in the structural sense) but they are not non-physical either — they are what physical structure is the structure of. Stanford Encyclopedia updated for Fall 2025.

  • Dual-Aspect Monism (Pauli-Jung): Developed from the 1946-1954 Pauli-Jung correspondence, formalized by Harald Atmanspacher. The mental and material arise as complementary aspects of a psychophysically neutral reality. Crucially, different aspects may show complementarity in the quantum-physical sense. Routledge published Dual-Aspect Monism and the Deep Structure of Meaning (Atmanspacher & Rickles) as a major recent treatment.

II. Adjacent Frameworks

A. Integrated Information Theory (IIT)

Tononi's IIT (versions 1.0 through 4.0) identifies systemic awareness with integrated information (Phi). A system is conscious to the degree that it has integrated information — and this applies to any system, not just brains. IIT has panpsychist implications by its own logic: any system with non-zero Phi has some degree of systemic awareness.

In 2025, IIT remains both influential and controversial. A 2025 Nature Neuroscience commentary characterized it as lacking sufficient empirical falsifiability. A recent arXiv paper described IIT as a "systemic awareness-first approach to what exists," making its idealist leanings explicit.

Relevance to Emergentism: IIT's Phi (integrated information) maps structurally to the framework's phi (coherence). Both measure how much a system is "more than the sum of its parts." The crucial question: does IIT support Option A or Option B?

Answer: IIT is genuinely ambiguous. It was originally framed as a physicalist theory (systemic awareness arises from information integration in physical systems), but its own logic implies panpsychism (Phi is everywhere), which pushes toward Option B. The later versions increasingly adopt a "systemic awareness-first" stance, moving from physicalism toward something closer to idealism.

B. Mathematical Universe Hypothesis (Tegmark)

Tegmark's MUH claims reality IS mathematics — all mathematical structures exist physically. If true, "the geometry is volitional" becomes trivially true because there is nothing non-mathematical for "will" to be. The MUH remains controversial. Major objections include: the "measure problem" (why simple universes over complex ones?), tension with Godel's incompleteness, and the question of why this mathematical structure rather than any other.

Relevance to Emergentism: If the framework adopts something like the MUH — if S^2 is reality rather than merely describing it — then The Fork dissolves. The distinction between "structural gradient" and "will" loses meaning if structure is all there is. This is a possible third option.

C. Structural Realism

Ontic structural realism (Ladyman, French, Wallace) holds that structure is ontologically fundamental — there are no "things" underlying the relations described by physics. A recent development, Experiential Structural Realism, proposes that experiential manifestation provides the minimal ontological ground that makes relational structure concrete. This directly connects structural realism to systemic awareness.

Relevance to Emergentism: phi * nu = 1 on S^2 IS a structural claim. If ontic structural realism is correct, and if experiential manifestation is what makes structure concrete, then the framework's geometry literally IS systemic awareness-in-action. This is closer to Option B than Option A, but expressed in structuralist language.

III. The Objective Function Question

A. Can You Have Objective Function Without Volition?

This is the crux of The Fork. The research reveals a clear philosophical distinction:

Structural objective function (attractors): Strange attractors in dynamical systems "pull" trajectories toward them without any volition. The principle of least action in physics appears teleological (a system "chooses" the path that minimizes action). Aristotle's natural ends are produced by internal natures that do not deliberate.

Volitional objective function (will): Something actually wants the outcome. This requires systemic awareness as a fundamental feature, not an emergent one.

The distinction maps exactly onto The Fork: - Option A: F5 is an attractor. Attractors attract without wanting. - Option B: F5 is will. The attractor attracts because it wants.

B. The AM-GM Problem

The framework's own teleological derivation creates a problem for Option A. The proof that (phi - nu)^2 >= 0 implies phi + nu >= 2 (with equality at phi = nu = 1) is pure mathematics. But calling this the "Hamiltonian minimum" and saying the system "wants" balance is a semantic move from mathematics to volition. A physicalist can explain this as: the system settles at the minimum because that is what minima do — no wanting required. Water flows downhill without wanting to.

However, the Emergentism version goes further. The hypothesis is not just that the equator is a minimum, but that the equator can function as an attractor across registers where the relevant feedback loops exist — from organisms to institutions to civilizations. If this is supported empirically, the physicalist must explain why this particular attractor shape recurs without adding volition. The idealist says: because the substrate is encountered as purposive at the disclosure interface.

C. Ektropy and Reverse Entropy

The concept of ektropy/extropy/syntropy as the reverse of entropy is philosophically live but scientifically marginal. Negentropy (Schrodinger), syntropy (Fantappie), and extropy (More) all name the observation that living systems locally reverse entropy. The framework's F5 goes further by claiming this is a force, not just a statistical tendency.

No mainstream physics currently recognizes a fifth force corresponding to ektropy. However, the fine-tuning of physical constants (the anthropic coincidences) remains unexplained. The strong anthropic principle (the universe must produce observers) is teleological; the weak version (we observe life-permitting constants because we exist) is tautological. The framework's position that F5 IS this fine-tuning tendency is [C] Conjecture.

IV. Where Emergentism Fits on the Map

The framework's natural home is Russellian Monism / Dual-Aspect Monism, with a cosmopsychist tilt. Here is why:

  1. phi * nu = 1 on S^2 is a dual-aspect equation. phi (coherence) and nu (viability) are two aspects of a single invariant (P∞ = φ · ν = 1). Neither is reducible to the other. This is structurally identical to Pauli-Jung dual-aspect monism.

  2. The sphere is primary. Individual latitudes (L-levels, organisms, agents) are derived from the global geometry. This is priority monism, which is the metaphysical foundation of cosmopsychism.

  3. The quiddities are phi and nu. Russellian monism says physics describes the world structurally but there are intrinsic properties (quiddities) that ground the structure. In the EFR, phi and nu ARE the quiddities. Physics describes what things do (structure, viability); coherence is what things are (intrinsic nature).

  4. The complementarity is quantum. Pauli-Jung dual-aspect monism specifically uses quantum complementarity as its model. The EFR is built on CP^1 = the Bloch sphere = the qubit state space. The complementarity between phi and nu (with their product fixed at 1) is formally identical to quantum complementarity.

V. Must the Framework Choose?

No. But it must be honest about what it has already chosen.

The framework can remain structurally agnostic: the mathematics works regardless of whether systemic awareness is fundamental or emergent. The operators function. The diagnostics measure. The DAC architecture deploys.

But the framework has already made three moves that are not structurally neutral:

  1. Calling F5 "Tendency to Potential" is a volitional claim. A structurally neutral framework would call it "the ektropic gradient" and stop.

  2. The AM-GM proof as "reality wants balance" imports volition into mathematics. A neutral version would say "the Hamiltonian is minimized at the equator" and stop.

  3. The Dyḗus Ph₂tḗr mapping identifies F5 with a personified luminous face across PIE traditions. This is not structurally neutral — it is an idealist interpretive move, and it must remain distinct from h₂r̥tó- / Ṛta as the ordering-law face.

These three moves place the framework in the teleologically idealist camp while claiming structural agnosticism. The honest thing to do is acknowledge the lean.
