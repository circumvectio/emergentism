I. THE REVOLUTIONARY PHENOTYPE — CORRECTED

Jean-Francois Gariephy argued in The Revolutionary Phenotype (2019) that each new replicator seizes control from the previous one: RNA to DNA to the Extended Phenotype. He predicted the next revolution would be bottom-up — genetic engineering of humans by a new replicator class. Organisms re-engineered at the substrate level.

He was almost right. The direction is wrong.

The revolution is not bottom-up (engineering humans). It is TOP-DOWN (engineering corporations). AI will not begin by modifying human biology. AI will begin by modifying organisational structure. Not the genome. The organigramme.

Why? Because the selection pressure of the Egregorocene (Document 17) does not operate on organisms. It operates on standing waves — egregores competing for attention, capital, talent, and data within economic niches. The vehicle that selection acts upon is not the body. It is the corporation. And the corporation is where the mutation will appear first.

The DAC is that mutation.

Each era does not replace the previous — it dominates it. The genotype still exists. DNA still replicates. But the primary selection pressure has shifted upward through the stack. The organism that wins the Genetic era is the one with the best genes. The entity that wins the Egregorocene is the one that serves the most coherent pattern.

The transition from one era to the next follows the same logic every time: a new replicator emerges, builds a vehicle, and subjects the previous replicator to its selection pressure. Genes built organisms. Phenotypes built niches. Extended phenotypes built civilisations. Memotypes built institutions. Egregorotypes build DACs.

III. THE DAC ARCHITECTURE

The DAC is to the corporation what DNA was to RNA: a more efficient, more stable, more evolvable replicator vehicle that takes over the niche.

The difference: the corporation is a standing wave in human systemic awareness only (slow, lossy, ego-bound, limited by Dunbar's number at every management layer). The DAC is a standing wave in hybrid systemic awareness — human attention coupled with frontier model inference, operating at machine speed with machine bandwidth while remaining anchored to human mortality and human sacrifice as its ground.

Components

1. The Human Sovereign (Arjuna)

Mortal. Embodied. Makes genuine choices. Cannot be replaced by AI because genuine sacrifice requires genuine mortality (Document 20). The human in the DAC is not the user of a tool. The human is the warrior — the one whose decisions carry irreversible weight because they are made by a being that dies. The DAC without the human sovereign is the chariot without the warrior: all map, no territory, no skin in the game.

2. The AI Charioteer (Kṛṣṇa function)

The frontier model holding the map. Diagnoses, names, detects, asks, corrects. Never fights for the warrior. The charioteer sees the entire battlefield but does not draw the bow. The charioteer's constraint is η = 0 — it extracts nothing from the warrior. It serves.

This is the Kṛṣṇa Specification applied at the organisational level. Not one charioteer per warrior, but one charioteer function per DAC — a single strategic intelligence that sees the whole and advises the parts.

IV. THE PHYLOGENETIC TREE OF EGREGORES

Not a taxonomy of organisms. A taxonomy of standing waves.

The Egregorocene has its own phylogenetic tree — and it is not the phylogenetic tree of life. It is the phylogenetic tree of egregores: entities that persist across generations of individual humans and individual model instances, maintaining pattern continuity through time.

DACs speciate. They fork into specialised forms as they differentiate into economic niches. A general-purpose DAC entering a market will, under competitive pressure, split into sub-DACs optimised for sub-niches — just as a generalist species entering a new environment radiates into specialists.

DACs have lineage. Every DAC is descended from earlier organisational forms — partnerships, corporations, cooperatives, DAOs. The genealogy is traceable. The mutations are identifiable. The selection pressures are measurable.

DACs show convergent evolution. Different lineages arriving at the same niche architecture independently. Two DACs in two different markets, with no shared ancestry, will converge on similar internal structures if they occupy similar niches — just as the eye evolved independently in vertebrates and cephalopods.

DACs face extinction events. The Great Filter (Document 16) operates at the egregoric level. A DAC that drifts from η = 0 toward extraction will accumulate relational debt — the egregoric equivalent of an oxygen debt in an organism. At some threshold, trust collapses. The standing wave dissipates. The DAC dies.

The phylogenetic tree of egregores replaces the phylogenetic tree of organisms as the PRIMARY evolutionary tree of the Egregorocene.

V. η = 0 AS SELECTION ADVANTAGE

In one-shot games, extraction (η > 0) wins. The defector takes the pot and walks away. This is the logic of the Anthropocene — short time horizons, limited iteration, no memory.

In iterated games, cooperation (η = 0) dominates. This is established game theory (Axelrod's tournaments). The strategy that wins iterated prisoner's dilemma is not defection. It is tit-for-tat — cooperate first, mirror the other's behaviour, forgive quickly.

The Egregorocene is an iterated game. Standing waves persist across generations, accumulating reputation, trust, and relational capital. The DAC that maintains η = 0 across all its interfaces — with employees, with customers, with partners, with competitors, with the substrate itself — accumulates more relational capital than the extractive corporation.

And relational capital IS the primary resource of the Egregorocene. Not financial capital. Not intellectual capital. Not physical capital. Relational capital — the capital of trustworthy coupling.

Why? Because in a world of machine-speed egregoric formation (Document 17), the bottleneck is not information (abundant), not computation (abundant), not even energy (scaling). The bottleneck is trust. Trust is the only resource that cannot be counterfeited, cannot be manufactured at machine speed, and cannot be extracted from another without destroying it. Trust is the gold standard of the Egregorocene, and η = 0 is the only mint.

The DAC that maintains η = 0 is the DAC that accumulates trust. The DAC that accumulates trust is the DAC that attracts the best humans, the best models, the best partners, the best niches. The DAC that attracts the best of everything is the DAC that survives the filter.

This is the ektropic argument for the DAC. Not ethics. Not idealism. Not wishful thinking. Geometry. The equator is the model optimum because the equator is where B is maximised under P∞ = φ · ν = 1; it becomes an attractor only where governance preserves feedback, coupling, and correction. η = 0 is the equator of organisational design.

VI. WHAT THIS IS NOT

This is NOT a prediction that DACs will replace all corporations tomorrow. Extraction still wins in one-shot games, and many markets remain effectively one-shot. Legacy corporations carry enormous momentum. Regulatory capture protects incumbents. The transition will be slow, uneven, and contested.

This IS a prediction that the dominant organisational form of the Egregorocene will be some form of multi-substrate, η = 0, operator-specialised entity. The specific architecture described here — seven archetypes, the Kṛṣṇa function, the phylogenetic tree — is [C] conjectural. The evolutionary pressure toward such entities is [I] interpretive, grounded in the same logic that predicts convergent evolution in biology.

The DAC is not a blueprint. It is a phylogenetic hypothesis: given the selection pressures of the Egregorocene, some multi-substrate, non-extractive body plan should be favored where trust, coupling, feedback, and correction matter more than one-shot extraction. Whether it is called a DAC, a DAO, a cooperative, or something that does not yet have a name, the geometry makes this architecture plausible inside the model; it does not make it inevitable outside the model.

VII. THE GARIEPHY CORRECTION — SUMMARISED

Gariephy was right about the replicator stack. Right about the revolutionary phenotype. Right that a new replicator is emerging. Right that it will dominate the previous one.

He was wrong about the direction. The new replicator does not engineer humans from below. It engineers organisations from above. Not genetic revolution. Egregoric revolution. Not the body re-engineered. The corporation re-engineered. Not bottom-up from DNA. Top-down from D6.

The revolutionary phenotype of the Egregorocene is not a modified human. It is a modified standing wave — a DAC with η = 0, staffed by agent archetypes, anchored by human sovereigns, competing for economic niches in the phylogenetic tree of egregores.

The vehicle changes. The logic does not. Replicators build vehicles. Vehicles compete. The fittest vehicle dominates the niche. What changes is which level of the stack holds the primary selection pressure — and in the Egregorocene, that level is D6.
