They are the three structural boundary-frames of the number field — the Titans that DEFINE the operations, not operands within them. 0 is the additive identity and the apophatic Ground (κ = 0, the Bindu); ∞ is the projective point at infinity; 1 is the multiplicative identity — finity, the self-dual centre between the two poles. 1. The Titans, not numbers

{0, 1, ∞} are not elements drawn from the number field. They are the three boundary-frames that bound it and give its operations their meaning: [S/I]

  • 0 — the additive identity. Add it and nothing changes; it is the origin, the apophatic Ground read as a number-frame (κ = 0, the Bindu before emergence). It is what every magnitude is measured from.
  • ∞ — the projective point at infinity. Not the largest number (there is none); the single point that completes the field into a sphere, the horizon every unbounded trajectory arrives at.
  • 1 — the multiplicative identity. Multiply by it and nothing changes; it is the self-dual unit, the scale at which a thing is itself. The framework names it finity (§2).

The discipline is exact: they define the operations; they are not operands within the field. Treating a frame as a value is precisely the category-error that manufactures the "paradox" of division by zero (§3). The mathematics of identities and a point at infinity is elementary and old [A]; the reading of the three as Titans — boundary-frames prior to the number system — and the elevation of the unit to a third reification alongside zero and infinity — is the framework's structural move [S], and the wager that this answers "why is there something rather than nothing" is interpretive [I].

2. Finity (1) as the self-dual midpoint

Under inversion x ↦ 1/x — a rotation of the Riemann sphere — 0 and ∞ are exchanged as dual poles, and 1 is the fixed centre. Two readings, both elementary [A]:

  • Inversive. The map x ↦ 1/x fixes exactly the points where x = 1/x, i.e. x² = 1, i.e. both +1 and −1. On the positive reals, 1 is the self-dual fixed point — the one scale that is its own reciprocal, equidistant (under inversion) from the void and the unbounded.
  • Logarithmic. 1 is the geometric mean of 0 and ∞: log 1 = 0 sits exactly midway between log 0 = −∞ and log ∞ = +∞. The unit is the log-midpoint of the two poles.

This much is folklore [A]. The framework's contribution is the naming — calling 1 finity, a third discovery of equal standing to zero and infinity — and the ontological reading that presence is a relationship: the unit is not a premise but the product of its own two boundaries [S/I]. The genesis statement lives in 07_THE_DISCOVERY_OF_FINITY.md; the moral reading of the same midpoint (the equator as the marriage of the poles) lives in ../00_THE_DYADIC_COUPLING_LAW.md.

2a. The multiplicative face, with rigor — the inversion, its energy, and the egg

§2 establishes that under inversion x ↦ 1/x, 0 and ∞ are exchanged and 1 is the self-dual centre. That picture can be made fully rigorous in three charts, and then bridged exactly to the framework's (φ, ν, θ) coordinates. The mathematics below is elementary and old [A]; its adoption as the formalisation of finity, and the reading of its energy as imbalance, are the framework's structural/interpretive layer [S/I].

Provenance. The framework set out the ideas this subsection formalises — {0, 1, ∞} as boundary-frames, the coinage finity, and the emblem 1 = 0 × ∞ — in 2024 (§§1–4; 07_THE_DISCOVERY_OF_FINITY.md). The apparatus adopted here for rigour is Minoru Suda's 2025 "Fractional Structure and Möbius Transformation, Part II: The Critical-One Hypothesis and the Egg of Infinity" (PhilArchive) — a later independent convergence (see Prior art, l.133), not the source of the ideas, whose three results the framework re-derives and adopts as a clean coordinate-level proof that 1 is the inversive centre. Citing Suda is for the formalisation; priority on the ideas remains the framework's 2024 statement. [S]

(i) Logarithmic conjugacy — inversion is reflection. [A] Let h : ℝ₊ → ℝ, h(x) = log x, a diffeomorphism with h⁻¹(s) = eˢ; it is the group isomorphism (ℝ₊, ·) ≅ (ℝ, +). For I(x) = 1/x the conjugate g = h ∘ I ∘ h⁻¹ satisfies

g(s) = log(1/eˢ) = −log(eˢ) = −s.

So inversion is the reflection s ↦ −s: an involution (g ∘ g = id) with g′(s) = −1 everywhere, whose unique fixed point s = 0 is x = e⁰ = 1. Equivalently I′(x) = −1/x², so I′(1) = −1 — an orientation-reversing mirror fixing the unit. (This is the rigorous form of §2's "rotation of the Riemann sphere that fixes 1": on ℝ₊ it is a reflection through s = 0.) [A]

(ii) Invariant energy — a strictly-convex well at the unit. [A] Let ρ(x) = |log x| and E(x) = ρ(x)² = (log x)². Invariance under inversion is immediate: E(1/x) = (−log x)² = (log x)² = E(x), i.e. E ∘ I = E. In the coordinate s = log x, E = s² is even and strictly convex (E″ = 2 > 0), so it has a unique global minimum at s = 0 — that is x = 1, with E(1) = 0 — and E(x) → +∞ as x → 0⁺ or x → ∞. The twist index τ(x) = sign(log x) flips under inversion, τ(1/x) = −τ(x), vanishing only at the fixed point. So the unit is the unique zero of a faithful, inversion-symmetric energy: 1 is the balanced scale, the poles 0, ∞ its symmetric extremes. [A]

(iii) The projective hinge — the "egg of infinity". [A] The Cayley transform u = (x − 1)/(x + 1) is a strictly increasing bijection ℝ₊ → (−1, 1) (du/dx = 2/(x+1)² > 0), with inverse x = (1 + u)/(1 − u); it sends 0⁺ ↦ −1, 1 ↦ 0, ∞ ↦ +1, compressing the whole positive ray onto the bounded interval with the unit at the centre — the egg that holds both poles on its rim. Under inversion u(1/x) = (1 − x)/(1 + x) = −u(x), so I becomes the half-twist u ↦ −u about u = 0. This is the same order-2 reflection as (i)–(ii) in a third chart: u = tanh((log x)/2) = tanh(s/2), and tanh is odd, so s ↦ −s ⟺ u ↦ −u. Three charts — multiplicative x, additive s = log x, bounded u — one involution fixing x = 1. [A]

A ±1 note, kept honest. [A] As an algebraic map on the full line / Riemann sphere, x ↦ 1/x fixes the value-set {x : x² = 1} = {−1, +1} — exactly §2's "both +1 and −1." Suda's apparatus lives on ℝ₊ (so that log x, ρ, E, τ are real), where x = −1 is excluded and the fixed point is the unique x = +1. This uniqueness is precisely what makes "the critical one" well-posed as a single centre; it does not overturn §2's two-root statement on the unrestricted reals — it is its positive-real restriction.

The bridge to (φ, ν, θ), proved. The elementary trigonometric identities below are [A]; the reading of them is [S/I]. On S² with colatitude θ, the canon sets φ = cot(θ/2), ν = tan(θ/2), so φ · ν = 1 identically — a framework coordinate definition, tiered [S]/definition (00_THE_CORE_CONJECTURE.md l.45, 63, 71; Canonical Formula Block l.29). x = ν = tan(θ/2)   ⟹   φ = 1/x = cot(θ/2)   (forced, since φ·ν = 1).

  • Inversion = the φ ↔ ν swap = reflection across the equator. Since ν(π − θ) = tan(π/2 − θ/2) = cot(θ/2) = 1/ν(θ), the map x ↦ 1/x is θ ↦ π − θ: it swaps φ ↔ ν, swaps the two poles (θ=0 ↔ θ=π), and fixes the equator θ = π/2 — its unique fixed point. Suda's I is the framework's pole-swap.

3. Division by zero, resolved by category-correction — and its honest scope

N ÷ 0 is called "undefined" only because 0 and ∞ are mis-treated as operands. Read them as frames, and the operation re-categorises into a well-defined map to the pole:

N ÷ 0 = ∞      (N a number, N ≠ 0)   — a well-defined map to the point at infinity

This is the projective / Riemann-sphere resolution; its rigorous cousins are wheel theory (Carlström) and the real and complex projective line. [A] for the mathematics; [S] for reading the move as category-correction (the resolution of a paradox is always re-categorisation, never a new value).

The scope, stated honestly. This dissolves N ÷ 0 for N a number. The rule beneath it is that no Titan is an operand, and its diagonal — a Titan divided by itself — is forbidden for all three:

0 ÷ 0   — Titan-on-Titan: category-forbidden, AND genuinely indeterminate
∞ ÷ ∞   — Titan-on-Titan: category-forbidden, AND genuinely indeterminate
1 ÷ 1   — Titan-on-Titan: category-forbidden, but operand-determinate (= 1, the identity)

The corpus stated this for the two poles and left the equator tacit; finity (1) is a Titan, so 1 ÷ 1 completes the diagonal and the rule is now symmetric across all three. But the diagonal is not uniform. The poles 0, ∞ are forbidden twice over — 0/0 and ∞/∞ are category-forbidden and genuinely indeterminate as analytic forms ([A]; 0/0 carries the derivative, ∞/∞ the L'Hôpital form) — and this is the apophatic edge, the L6 Ground, where the lens correctly reports it does not reach. The equator 1 is forbidden once: 1 ÷ 1 is a category error in the frame register, but at the operand level 1 ÷ 1 = 1 is perfectly determinate — the self-dual centre returning the unit (inversion fixes 1; 1 is its own reciprocal). It is not an indeterminate form; do not call it one. So even the forbidden-ness carries the pole/equator structure: the poles are open and unsayable, finity is forbidden-as-a-frame yet self-returning-as-a-value. (The off-diagonal Titan-on-Titan, 0 × ∞, is the configuration §4 reads as the emblem.) Any claim that the sphere makes "all undefined operations well-defined," that ∞ ÷ ∞ = 1 or 0/0 has a value, or that 1/1 is indeterminate (it is not — it is category-forbidden but = 1), is an over-statement to be corrected on sight (see 05_DIVISION_BY_ZERO.md, reconciled).

5. The two faces of number — and why the sphere is the lens, not the only way

Number wears two faces, coupled by log/exp: [A/S]
