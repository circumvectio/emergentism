0. Why this is the cleanest available test (not "the one clean test")

Most empirical surfaces in this corpus (GFS, Protocol D, Agent V×Φ) are either survey-proxy-dependent or framework-designed and therefore circular (per 00_WHAT_ACTUALLY_TESTS_THE_THEORY.md). This prediction is less circular than those on three counts — though it carries its own residual circularity risk, disclosed and mitigated in §3:

  1. The data already exists and was built by people who never heard of this framework. Piaget, Kohlberg, Loevinger, Kegan, Maslow, Erikson, and Commons each independently constructed an ordered ladder of human stages. The framework did not design these instruments, so the test is not circular in the instrument the way Protocol D is.
  2. The prediction is sharp and non-obvious. It says the integration/balance optimum sits at the middle of a maturity ladder, not the top — the opposite of the default reading of almost every stage theory.
  3. It has a single numeric kill (§6). Either the coded balance-maximum lands in the central tertile or it does not. There is no large grey zone to retreat into.

The residual circularity (do not hide it): the rubric is framework-adjacent even though the ladders are not. A balance rubric that rewards "two capacities held at once" can mechanically peak in the middle of any ladder whose extremes are described one-sidedly. §3 pre-registers an adversarial null rubric to break exactly this confound. P-EQ is therefore "cleanest available", not "clean".

1. The exact prediction and the framework's mechanism

1.1 The prediction (tier [C])

P-EQ. When an independent, ordinally-staged developmental / maturity / cognitive scale is mapped onto the unit interval [0,1] (lowest stage → 0, highest stage → 1), and a construct of balance / integration / equilibration is operationalized stage-by-stage, the balance score B̂(s) is single-peaked (unimodal) with its argmax strictly interior — in the central tertile of the ordered stages — and not at the top stage.

Operational kill (made precise in §6): P-EQ is falsified if the per-stage balance score is not unimodal-with-interior-peak — specifically if (a) the argmax falls in the top tertile of the ordered stages, or (b) a monotone-increasing fit beats the single-peaked fit by AIC on the pooled blinded data.

1.2 The mechanism (tier [S], resting on [A])

The framework's geometry (S10, S11 in The Honest Position; the Helix; The Dyadic Coupling Law) gives a specific shape:

  • A developmental position is a colatitude θ on the Burrisphere S². The lowest rung sits near one pole, the highest rung near the other; the middle rung is the equator (θ = 90°, the L4 Kṣatriya equator, 00_SEVENFOLD_FOUNDATION_ROOT.md §2.1). [S]
  • The two coordinates are coherence φ = cot(θ/2) and viability ν = tan(θ/2), constrained by φ · ν = 1. [S]
  • Balance B = sin θ. This function is non-monotonic: B → 0 at both poles and B = 1 (maximum) at the equator. The trig is [A]; reading sin θ as "balance" is [S].
  • The maximum is forced by AM-GM (S11): (φ − ν)² ≥ 0 ⟹ φ + ν ≥ 2, with H minimized — equivalently B maximized — uniquely at φ = ν = 1, i.e. the equator. The inequality is [A]; reading it as the balance optimum is [S].
  • The poles are runaway, not climax (The Dyadic Coupling Law §1): losing a pole is not a mere lack of its complement, it is a runaway of the complement (φ → ∞ as ν → 0, and vice-versa). "The poles are the two shadows; the latitudes are life; the equator is the marriage." So the top of a ladder, read as a pole of pure coherence/integration, is predicted to be less balanced, not more.

The bridge claim under test (this is the part that is [C]): that an independently-constructed maturity ladder's lowest→highest ordering is a monotone reparametrization of colatitude θ from one pole toward the other, so that its middle rung sits near the equator where B is maximal. If that bridge holds, the balance construct must peak in the middle. If maturity ladders instead place their integration optimum at the top, the bridge fails for those ladders and P-EQ is falsified (§6).

Honesty note on a genuine ambiguity — and an admission the framework owes itself. The framework's own L-ladder is not a clean monotone pole→pole map. Per 00_SEVENFOLD_FOUNDATION_ROOT.md §2.3 ("the loop is not monotone"), L1 and L7 are both unmediated contact, and L7 (Ṛṣi / the Bodhisattva return, L4; the Helix) is a return toward the equator, not a pole of pure coherence. So the framework's own seven-rung ladder would FAIL P-EQ's literal middle-band test — its balance would rise to L4, dip, and rise again toward L7. We state this plainly. It has two consequences we accept rather than hide: 1. P-EQ is a claim about independent ladders that are assumed monotone, not about the framework's own L-ladder, which is explicitly non-monotone. We are testing the bridge, not the L-ladder. 2. A defender could try to rescue any top-stage peak by re-reading it as "secretly the equator" (the L4 return). That escape hatch is pre-registered SHUT. For this test, a top-tertile argmax counts as falsification, full stop. We do not permit a post-hoc re-reading of a top-stage peak as "the return." This is the price of making the claim falsifiable rather than unfalsifiable, and it is the single most load-bearing commitment in this document.

3. Operationalizing "balance / integration maximum" — and breaking the circularity

The dependent variable is a per-stage balance score B̂(s) ∈ [0,10] assigned by blind raters to each stage s of each scale, from a de-labelled, paraphrased published stage description with the stage's rank-position concealed (see §4).

3.1 The balance rubric (given to raters; hypothesis-neutral)

Raters score each stage description on how strongly it expresses simultaneous, mutually-constraining possession of two complementary capacities — named in plain language so coders cannot reverse-engineer φ/ν or the hypothesis:

  • Holding-together vs. holding-open — does the stage describe a person who keeps structure/principle and openness/responsiveness at the same time, neither swallowing the other?
  • Self-direction vs. other-attunement — agency and relatedness held jointly, not one at the expense of the other.
  • A stage scores high only when both poles are described as co-present and constraining each other; it scores low when the description is dominated by one pole (pure structure, or pure flux; pure agency, or pure merger).

3.2 The adversarial NULL rubric (the circularity-breaker — pre-registered)

A balance rubric that rewards "two things at once" will mechanically peak in the middle of any ladder whose extremes are written one-sidedly. To prove the mid-peak is a property of the data and not of the rubric, a second, independent rubric is scored on the same blinded stages by different raters:

  • NULL rubric (competence / maturity): "How developmentally advanced, capable, and mature is the person described?" This rubric is predicted by the null hypothesis to be monotone increasing, peaking at the top stage.

Decision rule (pre-registered): P-EQ is supported only if, on the same blinded stages, the balance rubric peaks in the central tertile AND the competence rubric peaks in the top tertile and is monotone-increasing. If both rubrics peak in the middle, the mid-peak is a rubric artifact and P-EQ is not supported (the design failed, not the framework — reported as such). If the balance rubric peaks at the top, P-EQ is falsified (§6).

3.3 Rubric pre-validation

Before freeze, a third party (blind to P-EQ) confirms that the de-labelled extreme-stage descriptions are not artifactually one-sided beyond what the source theory states — i.e., we are not smuggling the result into the stimulus. Any stage whose paraphrase cannot be made faithful without naming its rank is dropped and the drop is logged pre-freeze.

4. Blinding protocol (two levels; both required)

Concealing rank-position alone is insufficient, because many stage descriptions name their own order ("formal operational", "self-transforming", "Integrated", "metasystematic"). Two leak-paths must be closed:

Level 1 — Stimulus blinding (preparer is blind to P-EQ). A preparer who has never been exposed to the Burrisphere/φ·ν framework takes each source ladder and produces, per stage, a de-labelled, paraphrased description: the stage name, its ordinal label, and any ordinal cue ("highest", "final", "mature") are removed or neutralized; content is preserved. Each stage gets an opaque ID. The preparer records the true rank order in a sealed mapping not shown to raters.

Level 2 — Rater blinding (raters blind to framework AND hypothesis direction). Raters are recruited without exposure to this framework, are not told the hypothesis, receive the stages of each ladder in randomized order, and score the §3.1 balance rubric (a separate rater pool scores the §3.2 NULL rubric). Raters never see the rank order, never see two ladders' stages adjacently labelled, and are debriefed only after all scores are locked.

Analyst separation. A pre-committed analyst, separate from the raters and from the preparer, joins blinded stage-IDs back to rank order after all scores are frozen, and runs the §5 analysis exactly as pre-specified. No analyst degrees of freedom: the unimodal-vs-monotone model comparison, the tertile definition, and the inclusion list are fixed at freeze.

Reliability floor. ≥3 raters per rubric per stage; inter-rater reliability (ICC) ≥ 0.70 required for a ladder's data to count. Ladders below the floor are reported as uninformative, not as support or falsification.

5. Analysis (pre-specified; no degrees of freedom after freeze)

For each ladder, with stages mapped to [0,1] by rank:

  1. Compute mean balance B̂(s) per stage (mean across raters).
  2. Fit and compare by AIC: (M1) monotone-increasing (isotonic), (M2) single-peaked unimodal (interior max), (M3) flat/null.
  3. Locate the argmax of the unimodal fit; record which tertile (bottom / central / top) it falls in.
  4. Repeat 1–3 for the NULL/competence rubric.
  5. Pooled test across the 7 ladders: count of ladders whose balance argmax is in the central tertile with M2 ≻ M1 by AIC; pre-registered support threshold = ≥ 5 of 7 ladders (and the same ladders must show the NULL rubric peaking in the top tertile).

All thresholds, tertile boundaries, AIC rule, and the 5-of-7 bar are frozen at §8.

6. The kill criterion (single, numeric, pre-registered)

P-EQ is FALSIFIED if any of the following holds on the frozen, blinded, reliability-passing data: - (K1) Fewer than 5 of 7 ladders show a balance argmax in the central tertile with the unimodal fit (M2) beating the monotone fit (M1) by AIC; or - (K2) On a majority of ladders, the balance argmax falls in the top tertile (the literal "integration peaks at the top" reading — the L4/return re-reading is not allowed to rescue this, per §1.2); or - (K3) The balance rubric and the NULL/competence rubric peak in the same tertile* on a majority of ladders (mid-peak is a rubric artifact, not a data property — design failure, reported as such).

What dies if killed: the bridge claim [C] that maturity ladders are monotone colatitude reparametrizations. What survives: the [A] AM-GM inequality, the [S] geometry, and B = sin θ as an internal structural identity. P-EQ dying tells us the lens does not project usefully onto developmental ladders; it tells us nothing about the algebra or about whether the equator is the balance optimum inside the model.

What would upgrade it: P-EQ confirmed here is [C]→[S] (a structural consequence borne out on independent ordinal data). Independent replication by a team with no authorship overlap, pre-registered, would be required to approach [A]. A single positive run does not make the geometry "true" (L6 guard).
