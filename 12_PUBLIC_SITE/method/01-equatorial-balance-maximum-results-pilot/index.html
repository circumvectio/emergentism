Paper V — RESULTS (Companion to Pre-Registration) — Tier [I] Pilot

1. What was pre-registered

The pre-registered prediction (Paper V): across independent developmental constructs, the INTEGRATION/BALANCE maximum sits in the MIDDLE tertile of each stage ladder, not the top. The pre-registration also fixed the kill criterion: a high-agreement top-dominant pattern is a genuine falsification and must be reported as such.

2. What was actually run (the honest scope)

This is the [I] pilot only: two coders blind to the prediction scored published developmental stages on the INTEGRATION rubric (balance of two complementary poles vs. one-pole domination). Both confirmed blindness and confirmed they did not read any framework / "Finity" / "equator" / "L4" material. Both coded from recalled canonical stage descriptions, not from the original scoring manuals. This is a dry run to check the machinery and the direction of effect — it is not the confirmatory test.

4. Verdict (tier [I], stated honestly)

PRELIMINARY FALSIFICATION. The integration/balance maximum did not sit in the middle tertile. For both blind coders, on every one of the five jointly-coded constructs, the peak fell in the top tertile, with 100% inter-coder agreement. This is precisely the top-dominant, high-agreement outcome the pre-registration designated as the kill criterion. The framework's own falsification condition fired. The Paper V prediction, as operationalized in this pilot, is preliminarily disconfirmed, and that is reported here without reframing it as support.

5. Why this is still only preliminary (and what the real test must do)

  • Recall, not instruments. Both coders scored from remembered canonical descriptions, not the published scoring manuals — a confound between true stage content and LLM training-data priors, including a likely "higher stage = more integrated" valence already present in the source literature.
  • "Top by construction" risk. A monotone-increasing rubric will mechanically peak at the top of any ladder. The [S]¹ design must control for this — e.g., by including instruments where the literature locates an explicit mid-ladder integrative optimum, and by scoring blind from the actual manuals.
  • Thin sample / coarse bins. n = 5 constructs, 2 coders, raw agreement only (no kappa, no CIs), coarse tertiles on 6-stage ladders, incomplete Coder A Maslow row.
  • MHC/Piaget unavailable on this rubric without fabrication, so the design's hierarchical-complexity arm is untested here.

6. Standing requirement

The definitive test — the [S]¹ blind-HUMAN study on the published instruments — is still required and is not satisfied by this pilot. This [I] result is recorded as a preliminary falsification and is carried forward to constrain the [S]¹ design; it does not by itself overturn or confirm the broader program, but taken at face value it weighs against the middle-tertile prediction.
