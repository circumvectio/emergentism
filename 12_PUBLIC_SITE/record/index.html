GFS·ANDCLASS

A test — retracted 2026-07-13

Does flourishing follow the product law in the ordinary range of survey data?

What ran — the product against additive, min (Liebig), geometric mean and CES on flourishing-survey data (128,868 respondents, 23 countries). In the mid-range it read roughly additive. Then we retracted it — not because of the numbers, but because it tested the wrong register.

Retractedtested the wrong register

an interior reading the framework never load-bears on → the zero-factor claim is untouched

Why it was retracted: the real claim is the zero-factor catastrophe — the knockout, where one factor hits zero and the whole product collapses. Everyone has a plan until they get punched in the face. A survey can't reach that: nobody at zero fills out a questionnaire — the dead don't, the knocked-out don't. It can only measure how well Tyson's opponents were doing before the punch landed. The claim lives at the boundary; this measured the middle. Kept here rather than hidden — a record you can prune is not a record.
