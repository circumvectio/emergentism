01What this is

Emergentism is a framework that asks whether structure can be derived from the principle of non-extraction — the claim that sustainable systems cannot systematically gain at the expense of their own sustaining field. [I] It is not a religion, a political program, or a finished physics. It is a navigational instrument. You test it by whether it orients you toward flourishing without demanding belief. The 5+1 constitutional fences (η=0, K2, K3, K4, A7, Ω) are the operational reading of that principle.

The framework names its own poisons, publishes its failures, and refuses to pretend it is finished. [S] That refusal is structural, not rhetorical — the constitution mandates self-correction (A7), archive-first repair (K3), and one mortal signature for every irreversible act (K2).

02The needle

One question orients everything:

Does this raise both the thing and the ground that sustains it, without extraction?

If yes, hold your course. If no, you've drifted — something is gaining at another's expense. The needle does not tell you what to do. It tells you whether what you are doing is pointed toward flourishing or away from it. [I]

02½Where you live in this

Before the claims, the part that's actually about you — choice, foresight, mortality. Three plain sentences, each wearing its tier:

Choice = foresight × means. A child beside a jet has the means but not the foresight; a grounded pilot, the reverse. Neither is free with respect to flying. You are free exactly where you hold both — and that region can be widened. [I]

“Free or determined?” is a malformed question. Randomness is not freedom — a coin flip is not an act of will. The framework re-reads the old binary as two views of one thing, and marks that re-reading as interpretation, not proof. It competes with compatibilism; it does not refute it. [I]

Mortality is load-bearing. A being that can be copied without being destroyed has nothing at stake; sacrifice, signature, and will belong to beings that can actually end. That is why a mortal person signs every irreversible act here — and software never does. [I] [C]

Deeper: the agency register — the option cone, foresight × means · the free-will reframe · and if you want the whole ladder, walk every degree of freedom, D0→D6 — a loop, not a summit, and entirely optional.

03The claims

Every claim carries an evidence tier and a falsifier. If you find a claim without both, the compass is broken. [S]

[A]

AM-GM inequality → extremum at equality (within scope)

For any two positive real numbers, the arithmetic mean is always greater than or equal to the geometric mean, with equality only when the numbers are equal. This is a proven theorem, not a hypothesis. The framework uses this as a structural law: for complementary couplings (σ ≤ 1, Leontief/min, Cobb-Douglas), systems that balance necessary quantities at equality are at an extremum. Scope: the "σ ≤ 1" condition is the AND/OR gate. For substitutes (σ > 1: k-of-n redundancy, failover) a factor at its pole can survive — the σ check must run before the AM-GM claim. Register discipline: "extremum" is the chart identity. It does NOT license "equator = optimum" as a universal claim; the disconfirming pass (receipt 121) found SSB places order at an extreme order parameter, not at balance. The AM-GM extremum is a [A] chart fact; the framework's reading of it is [S] scoped to complementary couplings only.

Find a counterexample to AM-GM within positive reals (none in 300+ years). Or: find a complementary coupling where equality is not an extremum (which would scope the law further). Or: show that σ > 1 substitutes can hold a necessary factor at its pole and sustain the system — that would refactor G1's law, not AM-GM.

[A]

ℂP¹ frame product — an identity, stated at true strength

The complex projective line (Riemann sphere) admits a natural frame product structure. Under the mass-shell identity this reduces to φ·ν = 1 — an identity that holds for all θ by construction. Numerical checks confirm the algebra but add no evidence: a definitional identity cannot be "verified," only stated at its true strength.

Find a mathematical error in the derivation stack (Papers A–H). 8/9 audit agents found none — convergence across agents, not independent review.

[D]

Conservation-coupled systems tend toward balance

A model output: in systems where conservation laws couple complementary observables, the AM-GM optimum acts as an attractor. This is derived, not observed. It applies only to systems that are conservation-coupled, complementary, and symmetrically priced.

Measure a conservation-coupled system that does not tend toward balance. No such measurement exists yet.
