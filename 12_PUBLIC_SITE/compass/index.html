The lens

Three distinctions prevent most category errors.

Map ≠ territory

A model can be coherent and still fail to be instantiated. Mathematical necessity never silently becomes empirical evidence.

Possible ≠ actual

D5 contains modeled alternatives. D4 contains performed interactions, outcomes, and records. An actual model token may represent possible content without making it actual.

Commitment ≠ consequence

An agent can authorize and attempt an action. The world separately returns an outcome. The selector does not manufacture its own receipt.

Physical cone ≠ option cone

The physical light cone stays bounded by spacetime and c. An option cone is the subset of admissible histories an agent can model, rank, coordinate, and reach.

The Soul Loop · Rosetta stone

Models act through bodies and return as receipts.

territory → fallible model → option field → authorization + D4 means → commitment receipt → world interaction → outcome receipt → model and selector update

This separates the cognitive gap (territory versus model), execution gap (intention versus performed action), and outcome gap (expected versus receipted consequence). Anticipated futures matter causally because actual agents represent them now.

The practical needle

Ask before acting.

  1. What is actual, what is possible, and what am I merely assuming?
  2. What future am I representing, and what evidence could change that representation?
  3. What means are truly available, and what constraint or other agent can veto the action?
  4. Who authorized it, who acts, who pays, who benefits, and who bears irreversible consequence?
  5. Can the affected person contest, repair, revoke, or exit?
