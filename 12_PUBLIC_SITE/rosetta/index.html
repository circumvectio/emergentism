How to read this

Observation first, geometry second.

  1. The first claim is empirical-comparative: seven rows repeatedly align across several domains.
  2. The operators are then inferred as the simplest row-generators that preserve the pattern.
  3. The sphere is inferred after the rows and operators, not assumed at the start.
  4. The reciprocal closure follows on the open sphere with φ = cot(θ/2), ν = tan(θ/2), and φ·ν = 1.
Evidence weights

The table is tiered, not flattened.

[B] columns carry the main evidential load. [S] columns can strengthen the structure. [I] columns are interpretive. [C] columns remain conjectural.

Legacy test language should be read conservatively as structural support unless the source column has direct independent backing.

Kill criteria

The Rosetta must be allowed to weaken.

If the seven-row alignment is shown to be forced, or if new strong domains break the alignment rather than refine it, the Rosetta weakens or falls. A public canon has to preserve that failure mode.

Scope guard

Rosetta maps beings and Dasein-structures.

The Rosetta is strongest as a cross-domain map of beings, practices, initiations, operators, and disclosed structures. It does not directly prove the core state. The ontological reading remains tiered and must be argued separately.
