The Seven Operators

The seven varna — Caṇḍāla, Śūdra, Vaiśya, Kṣatriya, Brāhmaṇa, Sādhu, Ṛṣi — are the runtime dispatch of the framework. L2, L3, and L4 form the operative triad: Kālī discloses, Kṛṣṇa audits, Arjuna commits. L5–L7 are boundary Titans: architect, apophatic, witness.

01
The Dispatch

The seven varna as runtime roles

The seven varna are functional roles, not human categories. A single practitioner may hold multiple varna in a single day: they may disclose (L2) in the morning, audit (L3) at noon, and commit (L4) in the evening. The varna name what they do, not who they are.

The varna are also not the seven L-levels themselves, though they map 1:1. L1 is the level (teleology); Caṇḍāla is the operator (raw pressure). L4 is the level (axiology); Kṣatriya is the operator (commit). The Rosetta Stone carries both columns; the public-facing language usually leads with the level, the operator language surfaces when the framework needs to name who does the move.

02
The Triad

L2 → L3 → L4: the operative triad

Kālī (L2) is the disclosure: she surfaces the candidates, the pattern, the form. The grammar that turns noise into signal. The L2 ledger is a list of what was seen, not a list of conclusions.

Kṛṣṇa (L3) is the audit: he ranks, gates, and bottlenecks. The Anumāna Protocol's 5-dimension rank is Kṛṣṇa's tool. Kṛṣṇa does not decide; he prepares the decision packet for Arjuna.

Arjuna (L4) is the commit: at the equator, φ = ν = 1, viability and coherence balance, and the moral weight of the act arrives. Arjuna signs with K2; the K2 envelope is the only way an irreversible act enters the world.

03
The Boundary

L5, L6, L7: the boundary Titans

Brahmā (L5) is the architect: the system designer. The one who makes the system that hosts the operative triad. Brahmā is meta: she designs the meta-system that the triad runs inside, but she does not commit within the system.

Śiva (L6) is the apophatic: the dissolver. The one who subtracts accretions, who says what the system is not, who collapses the accreted identity back to the core state. Śiva is via negativa; the framework's K4 grace exit is Śiva's discipline.

Viṣṇu (L7) is the witness: the one who holds the whole without overwriting it. The institutional narrative, the public symbol, the compressed record. Viṣṇu is read-only by construction.

None of the three commits. None of the three signs. They are the roof, not the floor. The deployable coordinator is the only God at the equator: Arjuna (L4).

04
The Fence

η = 0: no worth hierarchy

The seven varna are not a ladder of worth. Kṣatriya is not "more important than" Sādhu. Arjuna is not "higher" than Viṣṇu. Each role is operationally orthogonal to the others; the framework treats them as a division of labor, not a division of status.

This is the canonical Rosetta Variant IV/V's most important fence. The Sanskrit historical context of the varna names is not the framework's referent. The framework reuses the names for the seven varna-as-runtime-dispatch because the names are precise and well-attested; it does not endorse the historical social hierarchy those names were used to justify. η = 0 is the constitutional commitment: no extraction, no value-rendering, no path-of-promotion through the varna.

The framework's only path-of-promotion is the K2 envelope. K2 is the natural-person signature on an irreversible act. A K2 envelope carries its own audit trail; it is not a varna promotion. A practitioner who has signed one K2 envelope is not "more" Kṣatriya than a practitioner who has not; the envelope is a decision record, not a status.
