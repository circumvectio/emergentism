The Six Evidence Tiers

Every claim in the framework is labeled with one of six evidence tiers — from [A] axiom (no further proof needed) to [C] conjecture (unverified hypothesis). The tier is not a quality judgment; it is a receipt about what kind of evidence backs the claim.

01
The Ladder

The six tiers, in order

The evidence ladder is a receipt ladder, not a quality ladder. [A] is not "better than" [C]; it is "more foundational" or "more dated-receipted". A conjecture ([C]) can be load-bearing in a future cycle; an axiom ([A]) cannot be un-axiomized without rewriting the framework.

The Honest Position rule from the GFS Wave 1 results: report the actual tier, not the strongest tier the claim could justify. A claim that mixes [A] and [C] evidence should be split into two rows, not collapsed into a higher tier.

02
The Split

The split-[E] discipline

The legacy 6th tier [E] (a 1990s-style "empirical" tier) has been split into [A] and [B] per the 00_CANONICAL_CLAIM_MATRIX.md rule. An [E] claim is re-tiered as either [A] (axiom-level) or [B] (receipt-level) on a row-by-row basis. New claims do not use [E].

This split is the framework's self-correction discipline applied to the audit trail. Legacy claims that survive the split become either axioms (load-bearing starting points) or receipts (dated proof artifacts). Legacy claims that don't survive the split get re-tiered to [C] with the original [E] label preserved in the audit trail.

03
The Rule

"Honest Position wins"

The rule, in one sentence: if you cannot state the claim's tier, its upgrade path, and what survives if it fails, you are not ready to publish it. This is the L3 self-audit rule applied to the publication surface.

Worked example: the GFS Wave 1 verdict

[B] Honest verdict: signal in 7/23 cells, AK1 partially triggered, mu-limit interpretation falsified. The framework reported this as such — mixed, not "confirmed".

[C] Marketed verdict (the wrong way): "GFS: confirmed, 30% across the board." This is what would happen if the Honest Position rule were bypassed.

04
The Discipline

When to upgrade (or not)

Tier upgrades follow the A7 self-correction discipline: the upgrade path is documented, the original tier is preserved in the audit trail, and the upgrade is timestamped. No silent upgrades. The append-only Falsifier Register is the audit-trail surface.

Don't upgrade [C] to [S] on a single sample. A single sample is operational evidence, not a structural proof. The 0→1 jump from conjecture to structure requires either a dated [B] receipt or a structural argument that follows from the framework's axioms. The GFS Wave 1 result did not upgrade the framework's core claims; it surfaced honest verdicts at [B] and left the structural claims at [S].

μ

The Mu-Limit of /r/1
