The Burrisphere Anchor

Why the Burrisphere, why the equator

(φ − ν)² ≥ 0
The distance function on the Burrisphere. A square cannot be negative. Therefore this distance has a hard floor at zero. The equator — where φ = ν = 1 — is the unique global minimum. The equator is not a moral recommendation; it is the boundary of what's structurally possible.

The Burrisphere is S², the Riemann sphere. On it, the seven positions are spaced by latitude, with L4 at the equator. The equator is the only place where viability (ν) and coherence (φ) balance: ν = tan(θ/2), φ = cot(θ/2), and φ · ν = 1. The load-bearing arithmetic constraint is that (φ − ν)² ≥ 0: as a distance function, it cannot go below zero, so the equator is the closest possible point to balance. Every step away from the equator costs more to sustain.

The Burrisphere is the public anchor of the framework. Phase 1 /5 is the dimension that explains it; this page is the Rosetta that catalogs it. The two pages are complementary: /5 shows the geometry, this page shows the cross-domain pattern.
