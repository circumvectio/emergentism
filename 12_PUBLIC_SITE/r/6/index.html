The Distinction

What the Rosetta is, and what it is not

The Rosetta is a catalog. It maps the sevenfold pattern across independent traditions, surfaces the load-bearing arithmetic constraint ((φ − ν)² ≥ 0), and names the L1–L7 questions + varna + operators. The Rosetta does not commit — it reads.

The dimensions spine (Phase 1) is the framework. It explains the seven dimensions themselves — what each dimension is, what each one shows, why the sequence goes from /0 (Titans) to /6 (Convergence). The dimensions commit: they are the things the framework is.

The two pages are complementary, not redundant. The Rosetta is about; the dimensions are.

Returning to /0

After Phase 2, the reader has the Rosetta table. The next natural move is to return to the gateway (/0 — the Titans of Numbers) and re-read the dimensions spine with the Rosetta in hand. The Whole

The cycle as a whole
