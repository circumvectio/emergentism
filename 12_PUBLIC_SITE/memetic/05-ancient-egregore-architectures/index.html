The foundational codebase of Mesopotamian civilization was the sexagesimal (base-60) mathematical system.4 Far superior to the unwieldy Roman numerals utilized by later Western cultures, this base-60 system introduced the principle of position, enabling complex fractional division, the calculation of roots, and the establishment of the 360-degree circle and the 12-month calendar.10 While ostensibly a pragmatic tool for accounting and agricultural resource distribution, the sexagesimal system functioned as a profound cognitive formatting tool.10 By standardizing the measurement of time, distance, and commerce, the priesthood created a universal informational gradient—a shared epistemological reality that synchronized the neurological processing of the entire population.10 This shared mathematical language was the prerequisite infrastructure required to network individual minds into a coherent whole.
