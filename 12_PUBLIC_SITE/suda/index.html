Minoru Suda · reciprocal symmetry

One inversion, four charts

The philosopher Minoru Suda corrected the number line four ways, each a different coordinate for the same move — inversion, x ↦ 1/x. Watch a single pair (x, 1/x) in all four at once. As x grows, 1/x runs to zero — and the same inversion looks different in every chart, while 1 stays fixed in all of them.

What each chart says about the same inversion

1 · Raw line — I(x)=1/x swaps 0 and ∞ and fixes 1. Spacing is asymmetric: x flees right while 1/x crowds toward 0. Hard to read, which is why Suda re-coordinates.

2 · Log line — s = log x turns inversion into a clean reflection s ↦ −s about the origin. 1 lands at 0, the mirror's center. [A]

3 · Energy — E(x) = (log x)² is reciprocal-invariant: x and 1/x sit at the same height, minimized at 1. The two readings of one relation weigh the same. [A]

4 · The egg — u = (x−1)/(x+1) puts 0 and ∞ on the rim, 1 at the center, and makes inversion the half-twist u ↦ −u. As x → ∞, 1/x → 0: the two points slide to opposite rims. [A] · the full egg →

The honest fence

Independent convergence, [I]. Suda formalized this reciprocal geometry on his own; it agrees with our finity reading — corroboration, not proof, and neither borrowed it from the other. He did not originate finity; we did not originate his egg. [I] convergence

The mathematics is inherited. 1/x, log, (log x)², and the Cayley map are standard — used, not discovered, by us or by Suda [A]. What is shared is the structure, seen from two vocabularies.

Four charts, one inversion — kept in separate frames. These are four coordinates of the same move, not four different things; and they are distinct again from the curved sphere. We keep the frames apart on purpose.
