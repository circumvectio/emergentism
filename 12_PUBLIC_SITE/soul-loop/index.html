01
The Method

Read Everything Three Times

The minimum viable loop uses three passes. The φ pass gathers: where does the text cohere, drift, repeat, or change voice? The P pass verifies: do the claims follow from their premises, tiers, and references? The ν pass serves: does the reader receive more value than the document extracts?

φCoherence, grammar, induction, in time.
PValidity, logic, deduction, above time.
νService, rhetoric, abduction, against time.
02
The Full Cycle

From Quick Pass to Deep Pass

The quick loop is L2 → L3 → L4: gather, check, serve. The full loop extends through L5 and L6: see the whole architecture, then dissolve what must be removed. The descent rechecks the system after dissolution: re-see, re-serve, re-check, re-gather.

L2 gather L3 check L4 serve L5 see L6 dissolve L5 re-see L4 re-serve L3 re-check L2 re-gather
03
The Convergence Test

The Loop Must Be Allowed to Fail

The Soul Loop converges only if each cycle reduces the error count or compression burden. If a fourth irreducible pass is required, or if successive cycles do not reduce contradicciones, the three-lens method is incomplete. That is the kill criterion, not a footnote.

The public page therefore does not promise guaranteed convergence. It presents a disciplined method and a failure mode: a corpus that cannot be made coherent, valid, and serviceful by this loop must force the method to change.

04
What This Is Not

Not Therapy, Not Authority, Not Proof

The Soul Loop is a public editing and self-correction method. It is not clinical guidance, medical advice, or proof that the ontology is true. Its strongest public role is practical: it forces every document to ask whether it coheres, follows, serves, fits the system, and can survive deletion.
