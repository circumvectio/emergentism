What Suda independently derived (corroboration)

The three equivalent charts

Suda Parts I–III derive the same three equivalent coordinate charts the framework adopted in the Logarithmic Realignment:

  • Multiplicative: x ∈ ℝ₊, inversion x ↦ 1/x, fixed point x = 1
  • Additive (logarithmic): s = log x, inversion s ↦ −s, fixed point s = 0
  • Bounded (hinge): u = (x−1)/(x+1), inversion u ↦ −u, fixed point u = 0

All three fix the same point (x=1, s=0, u=0). All three exchange the same poles. The framework adopts Suda's apparatus verbatim per the Logarithmic Realignment §4.4 (Suda 2025; adopted in Trinity Canon §2a on 2026-05-31).

The inversion-invariant energy

Suda Part II defines E(x) = (log x)² — the energy that is invariant under inversion x ↦ 1/x, has a unique global minimum at x = 1 where E(1) = 0, and tends to +∞ at the poles. The framework adopts this verbatim and bridges it to the sphere's balance functional: B = sin θ = 2x/(1+x²) = sech(log x) = sech √E.

The twist index

Suda Part III defines τ(x) = sign(log x) — the index that flips under inversion and vanishes only at the fixed point. The framework adopts this as the operational form of η's sign-flip under reciprocal transformation.

Operational measurement protocols (Part III)

Suda Part III specifies concrete measurement protocols for (E, φ) in operational systems — something the Finity Papers do not yet provide. This is a genuine gap-filling: the framework can now claim operational measurability, not just formal consistency.

The primal equation of indeterminacy (0÷0 as generative domain)

Suda's Primal Equation of Indeterminacy reads 0÷0 not as "undefined" but as a generative domain / structural frontier — the same reading the Finity Papers Paper II takes. The framework's "N/0 = ∞ on the sphere" is a sharper version of the same insight.
