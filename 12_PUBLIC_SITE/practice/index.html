The path · stage six — the practice

What a person
actually does.

A worldview without a practice is a book. Here are three practices — transmissible in an afternoon, valuable with zero belief. No membership. Nothing to join. The only signature is your own, on your own verdicts.

[I] interpretive — the practices, not the metaphysics, are what this page asks of you [S] the trial record they imitate is public and dated

Practice one

01

The sitting Pratyakṣa — the first-person anchor

Every map on this site points at one place it cannot go: the crossing where a bounded view and an embodied hand meet. That crossing is not ahead of you. It is the reader — it always was. The sitting is the direct check: stop reading the map and look at where you're standing.

How — ten minutes

Sit. Eyes open or closed. Let one thing be seen — a sound, a breath, the room — and notice the one to whom it appears. Don't name it, don't improve it. When a thought offers to explain the framework to you, decline politely. The framework dissolves here on purpose.

02

The personal trial record №017 — the practice that is ours alone

Keep a ledger of your own claims — the ones you actually load weight onto. For each, write four things: its tier (how you know), what would change your mind (the kill criterion), what would upgrade it, and what survives if it falls. When one dies, hold the funeral: strike it through, date it, leave it legible. Sign the verdict.

This is A7 as a life discipline — the same discipline our public trial record runs at worldview scale, installed in one person in an afternoon. It works even if every metaphysical claim we make is wrong. That is deliberate.

How — one page, forever

One document. Newest entries on top. Never delete — strike through. Once a year, count the funerals. A ledger that stops growing means the thing is dead and doesn't know it.

03

The dyadic check The ethic as a habit, not a theory

Before an act that matters, ask one question with two ends: does this move raise me — and raise what sustains me? If it raises you by draining your ground, it subtracts from the very product you live inside. If it feeds the ground by hollowing you, the same. Both directions, or neither.

How — one question, two ends

i = you, at the scale being tested. H = the whole that sustains you, at that scale. The move is clean only if both sides are preserved or raised — under η = 0: nothing taken without return.

Why these three

No belief required.

The sitting teaches you to check maps against ground. The trial record teaches you to grade claims and bury the dead ones. The dyadic check teaches you to spot extraction before you commit it. None of them asks you to believe the framework — they install the posture, and the posture is the part every audit has left standing.

If the framework is right, the practices are its shortest path into a life. If the framework is wrong, the practices still leave you sharper than they found you. That asymmetry is the honest offer of this page — and it is the same offer the whole site makes, at every stage of the path: verify first, believe never, keep what works.
