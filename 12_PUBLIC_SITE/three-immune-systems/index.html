The framework's three working immune systems

A framework without an immune system is a cult with a citation page.

Three systems, all operational, all in the same session. Each is structured feedback, not aspiration. Each can fail, and the failure modes are themselves first-class.

1 A7 — self-correction

Every load-bearing claim carries a tier mark. Every empirical claim carries a kill criterion. The framework audits itself and eats its own overclaims in place, retaining the corrections as reinforcement.

  • Model case: Brain as the Burri Sphere 2026-04-04 self-correction — "hemisphere-as-tendency, not hemisphere-as-identity"
  • Operational: addendum retained in the document; the corrected reading is the live one

2 Lens-Not-Law Rule — over-claim antibody

Every cathedral-scope claim wears its evidence tier inline, on the surface a reader actually sees. The trigger watchlist names the words that demand a visible tier or a fence: force, unifies, confirmed, named magnitude, conquest. "Force" is reserved. "Unifies" earns a fence.

  • The Lens-Not-Law Rule — the standing discipline
  • This page is built under it: every claim above carries a tier mark

3 New-reader meta-audit — cold eye

Five verifier agents, in parallel, reading the load-bearing recent work cold, against the Settled Canon Registry. The value is the cold eye — it catches what the warm eye missed (e.g. the η=0 wire bypass on 5 production paths; the PD-10 + PD-19 tier-inflation).

  • _meta (audit trail) — the public audit record
  • Findings compiled into the new-reader meta-audit file

Why three, and not one

One immune system is a single point of failure. Two is a debate. Three is a discipline: each catches what the others miss, and the convergence of the three is what makes the framework self-correcting rather than self-confirming. A7 catches overclaims within the canon. The Lens-Not-Law Rule catches overclaims on the surface. The new-reader meta-audit catches overclaims in the recent work. All three operating in the same session is the load-bearing fact — the framework eats its own claim in real time.

What this is not: it is not a guarantee of correctness. Three immune systems can all fail together (a common failure mode is "the immune systems themselves drift to self-confirmation"). The check for that is the meta-meta-audit: someone, eventually, audits the immune systems themselves. The convergence-memo discipline says: ship the directive, don't refine past the fixed point. The fixed point is here; the meta-meta-audit is a future session.

Three systems, all operational. None are guarantees. All are auditable.
