L-Levels Mapped to Brain Regions, Hemispheric Dominance, and Brainwave Signatures

Hat: 🔬 Cross-Domain Translation (Neuroscience) Evidence Tier: [I] for L1-L4 (established neuroscience: brainstem/amygdala threat processing, limbic social processing, prefrontal planning, cortical integration). [I] for L5-L6 (interpretive, drawing on meditation research). [C] for L7 (conjectural -- Global Workspace at non-dual awareness is hypothetical). Kill Criteria: If neuroimaging studies show that the brain regions and brainwave signatures do not correspond to the developmental/functional levels as mapped -- e.g., if integrated cortical function does not correlate with post-conventional moral reasoning (L4), or if theta dominance does not correlate with witness/meta-awareness (L6) -- this mapping falls. What This Mapping Shows

Each L-level has a proposed neural signature: a dominant brain region, a hemispheric orientation, a characteristic brainwave band, and a cognitive mode. The claim is that the operators map to neural tendencies [I] — the developmental progression from L1 to L7 corresponds to a structural (not literal) progression through the brain's architecture.

This is the most empirically testable Three-Stage Process column. Paper G (Biological Predictions) derives specific falsifiable predictions from this mapping.

The Table

Reading the Table

L1 -- Brainstem and Amygdala. The oldest brain structures. Threat detection, fight-flight-freeze. High Beta/Gamma reflects hyperarousal. The Kali function is literally implemented in the amygdala: scan for danger, react before conscious processing. Subcortical -- below the hemispheric distinction entirely.

L2 -- Limbic System and Mirror Neurons. The social brain. Mirror neurons (discovered by Rizzolatti et al.) enable empathy and imitation -- the neural substrate of the social bond. Right-hemisphere dominant: the right hemisphere processes emotional prosody, facial recognition, social context. Alpha waves (8-12 Hz) reflect relaxed social engagement.

L3 -- Left Prefrontal Cortex. The planning brain. Broca's area, dorsolateral prefrontal cortex -- the structures that support language, sequential reasoning, and goal-directed planning. Left-hemisphere dominant: analytical, verbal, deductive. High Beta (15-30 Hz) reflects concentrated analytical thought.

L4 -- Integrated Cortex. The equatorial brain. Neither subcortical nor single-hemisphere dominant -- the full cortex in balanced activation. Left and right hemispheres in coherent communication. Alpha-Beta coherence (8-20 Hz) reflects the integration of social-emotional (alpha) and analytical (beta) processing. This is what the Arjuna function looks like in neural hardware: the warrior-sage who holds both intuition and analysis, both empathy and strategy.

L5 -- Prefrontal Executive. Sustained executive function. The capacity to hold complex models in working memory, to plan across long time horizons, to maintain systematic attention. Balanced hemispheres with slight prefrontal emphasis. Low Beta (12-15 Hz) -- the SMR (sensorimotor rhythm) band associated with calm focused attention.

L6 -- Default Mode Network (reconfigured). The witness state. In experienced meditators, the DMN (which normally generates the narrative self) is reconfigured — not simply deactivated but integrated with executive networks into an effortless baseline. [I] Right-hemisphere shift. Theta waves (4-8 Hz) dominate -- the frequency band associated with deep meditation, hypnagogic states, and meta-awareness. The Śiva function: dissolution of the constructed self.

L7 -- Global Workspace (hypothetical). Full integration of all brain systems into a unified field of awareness. High Gamma (>40 Hz) has been reported in advanced meditators (Lutz et al., 2004) but the evidence is preliminary. The mapping to non-dual awareness is conjectural [C]. If Global Workspace Theory (Baars, Dehaene) is correct, L7 would be the state where all specialist modules broadcast simultaneously into the global workspace.

The Hemispheric Symmetry

The hemispheric progression mirrors the L-level symmetry on S²:

L1  Subcortical         (below hemispheric distinction)
L2  Right-dominant       (social, emotional, comprehensive)
L3  Left-dominant        (analytical, verbal, sequential)
L4  BALANCED             (integrated -- the equator)
L5  Balanced             (sustained executive)
L6  Right-dominant       (meta-awareness, dissolution)
L7  Fully integrated     (beyond hemispheric distinction)

L2 and L6 are both right-dominant but at different levels: L2 is naive social processing, L6 is post-analytical witness awareness. L3 is left-dominant analytical; it has no mirror above because the analytical function is transcended, not repeated. L4 is the axis of symmetry -- balanced hemispheres, maximum neural integration.

This maps exactly onto the B = sin(theta) symmetry of the operators: L1/L7 at the poles, L2/L6 as mirrors, L3/L5 as mirrors, L4 as the equator.

Testable Predictions

This mapping generates specific predictions testable by neuroimaging (see Paper G):

  1. Individuals assessed at L4 (post-conventional moral reasoning + self-actualisation) should show balanced hemispheric coherence in EEG.
  2. Meditation practitioners at L6 should show theta dominance with DMN reconfiguration (reduced DMN-CEN anticorrelation, increased cooperative linking) in fMRI. [I]
  3. The transition from L3 to L4 should be visible as a shift from left-dominant high-beta to balanced alpha-beta coherence.

These predictions are falsifiable. If they fail, the neuroscience column of the Rosetta Stone is wrong.
