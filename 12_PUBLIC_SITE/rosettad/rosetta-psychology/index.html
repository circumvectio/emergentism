What This Mapping Shows

Three Western developmental scales -- Piaget (cognitive), Kohlberg (moral), Maslow (motivational) -- each describe a progression that falls into seven rows when extended to include pre-social (L1) and post-conventional (L5-L7) territory. Note: Piaget and Kohlberg share intellectual lineage (Kohlberg built directly on Piaget's stage theory), so these represent two independent traditions (Piaget-Kohlberg and Maslow), not three. The framework adds two further columns: a Sanskrit virtue naming the positive attractor at each level, and a Shadow naming the pathological attractor when that level becomes stuck.

The claim is not that these researchers intended to describe the same thing. The claim is that each discovered the same structural positions on S², projected onto cognitive, moral, and motivational substrates respectively. (Piaget and Kohlberg share lineage; Maslow is genuinely independent.)

Reading the Table

Each row is a position on S². The operator at that position (Kali, Kali, Kṛṣṇa, Arjuna, Brahmā, Śiva, Viṣṇu) determines what cognitive, moral, and motivational capacities are available.

The columns are projections:

  • Stage names the developmental territory in plain language.
  • Piaget gives the cognitive structure. Sensorimotor is body-only cognition (L1). Formal operational is abstract-hypothetical reasoning (L4). Post-formal is the capacity to hold contradictions (L6).
  • Kohlberg gives the moral structure. Pre-conventional is punishment-avoidance (L1). Post-conventional is principled moral reasoning beyond social conformity (L4). Universal principles are L6.
  • Maslow gives the motivational structure. Physiological needs dominate at L1. Self-actualisation peaks at L4. Self-transcendence (added by Maslow late in life) maps to L6-L7.
  • Virtue names the positive attractor in Sanskrit: the quality that stabilises each level when functioning well.
  • Shadow names the pathological attractor: what happens when a person is stuck at a level without the capacity to move.

L4 is the apex of balance (B = sin theta = 1). Not the highest level -- the most balanced. The integrated person who has formal reasoning, post-conventional morality, and self-actualisation is not above the contemplative (L5) or the transcendent (L7). They are at the equator, holding maximum balance between coherence (phi) and viability (nu).

The shadows are not moral judgements. Terror at L1 is not evil -- it is the pathology of survival when survival becomes permanent. Dogmatism at L5 is not evil -- it is the pathology of knowledge when knowledge becomes identity. Each shadow is A2 violation: the operator stuck where it should flow.

Evidence Notes

  • Piaget's stages are among the most replicated findings in developmental psychology [S].
  • Kohlberg's moral stages have been validated cross-culturally, though with debate about universality of post-conventional stages [S]. Note: Kohlberg built directly on Piaget -- these are NOT independent discoveries.
  • Maslow's hierarchy: universal needs confirmed [S], but the sequential ordering (hierarchy) is NOT supported by large-scale empirical data (Tay & Diener 2011, n=60,865 across 123 countries: needs are simultaneous, not sequential) [S].
  • L5-L7 extend all three theories beyond what the originators established empirically [I]. Cross-cultural validity degrades at higher levels (WEIRD bias).
  • The seven-level alignment is the framework's structural contribution [S]. The number 7 is a design choice, not a discovered constant -- the source theories range from 4 to 15 stages.
  • Sanskrit virtue and Shadow columns are framework-internal [I].
  • See ROSETTA_PSYCHOLOGY_RESEARCH_BRIEF.md for full meta-analysis with 20 references.
