0. Column Ceiling

Music is independent of the framework, but the specific seven-row mapping is not. The audit therefore separates:

  • [S] acoustic and music-theory facts
  • [S] structural features such as tonal centers, harmonic tension, silence/noise boundaries, and 4-3 pedagogy
  • [I] row placement of musical forms

The strongest claim is not "all music is sevenfold." The strongest claim is: tonal systems repeatedly organize around a center, tension, release, and boundary states that can be read through L-level geometry.

2. Mirror Score

Pair Score Reason
L3/L5 strong Harmony and counterpoint are source-domain distinct as vertical and horizontal musical ordering.
L2/L6 partial Both simplify or suspend functional harmony, but L2 is participatory/pre-harmonic while L6 is post-tonal/axiomatic.
L1/L7 strong Noise and silence are clean auditory boundary states when silence is framed as listening.
L4 strong / tradition-bound Tonal center is domain-native in tonal traditions; not universal across all music.

3. Failed / Partial Appendix

  • Universal-seven claim fails. Pentatonic, chromatic, maqam, gamelan, and microtonal systems prevent any simple "music is seven" claim.
  • L6 is mixed. Drone can be a center, not only dissolution. Atonality, drone, ambient, and modal jazz may need separate sub-cells.
  • L4 is strong but tradition-bound. Tonal center appears widely, but non-tonal traditions must be audited on their own terms.
  • 4-3 pedagogy needs sourcing. Grade-system claims should be treated as institutional examples, not universal music law.

Public Claim Ceiling

Music provides interpretive translation with structural support. The strongest cells are L4 tonal center, L3/L5 harmony-counterpoint, and L1/L7 noise-silence. The column should not be used to claim universal sevenfold music.
