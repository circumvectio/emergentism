0. Axiomatic guard

The Indo-European/Mediterranean V-bis convergence (Mithraic, Sufi nafs, Carmelite, Sefirot, Rosicrucian) is striking. The framework's stronger claim is that this 7-stage pattern is universal. That stronger claim is what this packet tests.

Three things can happen: 1. The traditions cleanly map to 7 stages. Universal-claim strengthens. 2. The traditions have different stage counts but degrade gracefully. Framework survives but narrows to "7-stage cleanly-graded traditions converge"; other traditions express the same operator geometry through non-graded structures. 3. The traditions break the operator framework outright. Framework adjusts (some rows merge, or operators are not exhaustive). Per packet's stated kill discipline.

Honest reporting requires distinguishing these outcomes. No forcing.

1. Daoist 內丹 (Internal Alchemy)

1.1 The tradition

內丹 (nèidān) is the Daoist alchemical art of cultivating the body-mind toward immortality and union with Dao. Three Treasures (三寶 sānbǎo): jīng 精 (essence), qì 氣 (vital breath), shén 神 (spirit). Cultivation transmutes coarser into finer.

1.2 Stage counts in the source tradition

There is no single canonical stage count. Multiple lineages, multiple counts:

Lineage / source Stage count Stages
Classical 3-stage (most-cited in scholarly literature) 3 Refining essence into qi (煉精化氣) → refining qi into spirit (煉氣化神) → refining spirit and returning to void (煉神還虛)
5-stage (Quanzhen/Complete Reality school, common) 5 Building foundation (築基) → 3 classical stages → refining void and merging with Dao (煉虛合道)
7-stage (some later Quanzhen + syncretic lineages) 7 Body-purification → essence-conservation → essence→qi → qi→spirit → spirit→void → emptying-of-emptiness → union-with-Dao
9-stage (Lü Dongbin / Northern lineage variants) 9 Sub-stages within the spirit-refinement phase

1.3 Honest assessment

The 內丹 tradition does NOT have a single canonical 7-stage structure. The 7-stage versions exist but are interpretive elaborations of the canonical 3- or 5-stage core. To assert that "Daoism has a 7-stage initiatory ascent" is to over-claim.

However: the 內丹 operator structure maps cleanly onto the framework regardless of stage count: - The Three Treasures (jīng, qì, shén) are themselves a Three-Stage Process — viability (jīng = bodily essence), balance (qì = animating breath), coherence (shén = pure spirit). 3-fold ascent on the same axis as L2→L3→L4. - "Returning to void" (還虛) and "merging with Dao" (合道) are the axiomatic ascent (L5→L6→L7). - The cultivation path is helical, not linear — same as the Soul Loop. Lineages emphasize this.

1.4 Verdict for the Rosetta

  • Stage-count claim: Daoism does NOT have a clean 7-stage structure. Universal-7 claim FAILS for 內丹.
  • Operator-axis claim: Daoism cleanly expresses jīng→qì→shén→xū→Dao as a coherence-viability cultivation gradient. Operator-grammar claim HOLDS.
  • Outcome: the framework survives by narrowing the universal claim. The 7-stage convergence is Indo-European/Mediterranean; the operator-grammar convergence is broader. Alignment degrades gracefully — some "rows" merge in 內丹, but the underlying coherence-viability structure remains visible.

2. Buddhist 10 Bhūmis (Bodhisattva Stages)

2.1 The tradition

The Daśabhūmika Sūtra (Sanskrit: "The Sutra of Ten Stages") describes the bodhisattva path in 10 progressive stages. Canonical in Mahāyāna Buddhism. Most authoritative source for the staged path.

2.2 The ten bhūmis

# Sanskrit Meaning Traditional emphasis
1 Pramuditā Joyful First realization; emergence of bodhicitta
2 Vimalā Stainless Moral perfection (śīla)
3 Prabhākarī Luminous Meditative absorption (dhyāna)
4 Arciṣmatī Radiant Burning away defilements; vigor
5 Sudurjayā Hard to Conquer Penetrating Four Noble Truths
6 Abhimukhī Manifesting Direct vision of dependent arising
7 Dūraṅgamā Far-Going Skillful means; the canonical pivot
8 Acalā Immovable Non-regression; effort becomes spontaneous
9 Sādhumatī Good Wisdom Fourfold analytical knowledge
10 Dharma-meghā Cloud of Dharma Buddha-knowledge cloud; verge of buddhahood

Key structural feature: bhūmis 1–7 are with effort (sa-prayoga); bhūmi 8 onward is spontaneous (anābhoga). The transition at bhūmi 7 is the canonical pivot point.

2.3 The 10 → 7 collapse — multiple plausible mappings

The framework's kill-criterion test: does the 10-stage structure collapse cleanly to 7?

Proposal A — Equator at bhūmi 4:

Bhūmi L-level Rationale
1 Pramuditā L1 First emergence (Caṇḍāla → first individuation)
2 Vimalā L2 Moral discipline (Śūdra → labour-of-purification)
3 Prabhākarī L3 Meditative absorption (Vaiśya → productive cultivation)
4 Arciṣmatī L4 Radiant — the burning equator; vigor at peak balance
5 Sudurjayā L5 Penetration of truths (Brāhmaṇa → systematic insight)
6 Abhimukhī L5 Direct vision (collapsed with 5 — same level, different aspect)
7 Dūraṅgamā L6 Far-Going — Sādhu → renunciant transition
8 Acalā L7 Immovable — first transcendent pole
9 Sādhumatī L7 Wisdom — same level (collapsed)
10 Dharma-meghā L7 Cloud — same level (collapsed)

Proposal B — Equator at bhūmi 7 (the canonical pivot):

Bhūmi L-level Rationale
1 Pramuditā L1 Emergence
2 Vimalā L2 Moral discipline
3 Prabhākarī L3 Absorption
4–5 Arciṣmatī, Sudurjayā L4 Vigor + truth-penetration as twin equatorial work
6 Abhimukhī L5 Manifesting wisdom
7 Dūraṅgamā L4 transition Far-Going = the effort/spontaneity pivot, structurally an L4-equivalent transition
8 Acalā L5 Immovable (post-transition)
9 Sādhumatī L6 Wisdom
10 Dharma-meghā L7 Cloud-of-dharma

2.4 Honest assessment

Both proposals are plausible. Proposal A respects the numerical center (bhūmi 4 of 7, when 10→7 collapsed); Proposal B respects the canonical pivot at bhūmi 7. The Buddhist tradition itself emphasizes the bhūmi-7 pivot (effort→spontaneity).

This is genuinely interpretive. The framework cannot pick one without authoritative scholarly Buddhist input. The 10→7 collapse is not unique.

2.5 Verdict for the Rosetta

  • Stage-count claim: Buddhism canonically has 10 stages, not 7. Universal-7 claim FAILS for bhūmis.
  • Pivot-point claim: The bhūmi-7 pivot (effort→spontaneous) is structurally analogous to L4 (the equator where dual modes meet) — but its numerical position is 7, not 4. Either: the framework's "L4 = equator" naming is parochial (Indo-European pivot at numerical 4 of 7), or the bhūmi numbering is parochial (the pivot is at the 7th of 10 by historical accident). Both are possible.
  • Operator-axis claim: The bhūmis express progressively refined coherence (śīla, dhyāna, prajñā) in a clearly graded ascent. Operator-grammar claim HOLDS.
  • Outcome: alignment degrades gracefully. The 10-stage structure collapses to 7 with interpretive ambiguity at the equator's location. The collapse is not catastrophic but is genuinely non-unique. Honest framework reading: "the bhūmis express the same operator gradient, but with an indeterminate equator placement when collapsed to 7."

3. Sub-Saharan African Initiatory Grades

3.1 What is documented

Sub-Saharan African religious-initiatory traditions are vast, varied, and often poorly documented in English-language scholarship. Caution required: the temptation to find 7-stage structures is high precisely because documentation gaps allow projection.

Surveyed lineages (best available scholarship):

Tradition Documented structure 7-grade match?
Akan (Ghana) chieftaincy initiation Multi-stage including ritual death/rebirth, name-giving, regalia investiture No clean 7-grade structure documented; varies by stool/lineage
Yoruba (Nigeria) Ifá divination 16 odu (basic), 256 odu meji (compound), then odu-kindreds Not 7 — 16/256 base structure is fundamental
Dogon (Mali) Awa society ~60 initiatory grades documented in Marcel Griaule's work; later scholars (van Beek) dispute Griaule's reconstruction Disputed; if Griaule is right, structure is far richer than 7-grade
Bantu system architecture (Kagame) 4-fold classification: muntu/kintu/hantu/kuntu (person/thing/place-time/modality) Not 7 — 4-fold base
!Kung / San healing dance Trance-state grades (n/om holders); not a 7-grade ascent Not 7 — functional, not graded

3.2 Honest assessment

Sub-Saharan African traditions do NOT present a consistent 7-grade initiatory structure. Where graded structures exist, they are often: - Based on different counts (4, 16, 60+, varies) - Functional rather than developmental (n/om holders, not stages of attainment) - Disputed in their own scholarly traditions (Griaule's Dogon vs van Beek's critique)

3.3 Verdict for the Rosetta

  • Stage-count claim: Sub-Saharan African traditions FAIL the universal-7 claim. They have other counts (4, 16, 60+) or non-graded structures.
  • Operator-grammar claim: Some lineages show coherence-viability gradients (e.g., Bantu's progression from kuntu/modality to muntu/personhood) but these are NOT 7-grade ascents.
  • Outcome: This is a scope-narrowing result for the framework. The V-bis 5-column convergence is genuinely Indo-European/Mediterranean. Honest framework reading: "the 7-stage initiatory archetype is a feature of Indo-European and Semitic-Mediterranean traditions; non-7-stage traditions express coherence-viability through other geometries."

4. Polynesian Initiatory Grades

4.1 What is documented

Tradition Documented structure 7-grade match?
Hawaiian kahuna lineages Functional categories (kahuna pule/prayer, lā'au lapa'au/herbal medicine, kuhikuhi pu'uone/architecture, etc.) Not 7 — functional, ~8–12 categories depending on island
Maori tohunga grades Historical 3-tier system (tohunga ahurewa, matakite, makutu, with specializations); whare wānanga schools had grades Not 7 — 3-tier base, with specialization branches
Tongan/Samoan ariki/matai Chiefly succession ranks (tu'i, matai, etc.); genealogical-political Not 7 — political grading, not game-theoretic ascent
Tahitian arioi society 7 grades reportedly documented in some early ethnographic accounts (e.g., Ellis 1829), but disputed Possible 7-grade structure here, but evidence is contested

4.2 Honest assessment

Polynesian traditions do NOT present a clean 7-grade game-theoretic initiatory structure. The Tahitian arioi society's reported 7 grades is an interesting possible exception but the historical evidence is contested (Ellis was a missionary observer; later scholars vary).

4.3 Verdict for the Rosetta

  • Stage-count claim: Polynesian traditions FAIL the universal-7 claim with the possible exception of Tahitian arioi.
  • Outcome: Same as Sub-Saharan — scope-narrowing. The framework should not claim Polynesian validation for the universal-7 stage structure.

5.3 What this is NOT

This is NOT a falsification of the framework. The kill-criterion section in 04_DEEP_RUMINATION_2026_04_25.md names three failure modes:

  1. A domain requires a fundamentally different number of levels — partially observed (Buddhism: 10; Confucian: 8; Daoism: 3-5; Sub-Saharan: 4 or 16+; Polynesian: variable). The framework is now obligated to explain why 7 specifically converges in Indo-European/Mediterranean traditions and not elsewhere.
  2. The operator grammar proves non-exhaustive — NOT observed. No fifth operator type encountered.
  3. The equator is not a global minimum in the geometry — NOT observed. φ·ν=1 on S² remains intact.

The framework therefore survives at the operator-grammar level but narrows at the stage-count level.

5.4 The honest revised claim

The Rosetta Stone's strongest defensible claim post-sweep:

The operator grammar (4 mixed-sign Gods + 3 same-sign Executive boundary frames on a surface where φ·ν=1) is structurally exhaustive and universally expressed. The specific 7-stage articulation of this grammar is empirically Indo-European/Mediterranean; other traditions express the same operator-axis through different stage counts (3, 5, 8, 10, 16, varying) or non-graded functional structures.

This is a stronger claim than "all human traditions fit 7 stages" because it is honest about the data. And it is more defensible because it survives every counter-example surveyed.
