0. Why L4 First

L4 is the strongest and riskiest Rosetta claim.

Strongest, because the geometry names it cleanly: phi = nu = 1, B = 1, the equator.

Riskiest, because many domains do not have a native center. If the framework projects "center" onto domains that are linear, acyclic, or merely fourth-in-a-list, the Rosetta becomes pattern-matching.

This packet therefore audits L4 cells first. A strong L4 cell must show at least one of:

  • domain-native center
  • balance or mediation function
  • irreversible commitment / action function
  • bridge between lower building and upper holding
  • mirror stability under L3/L5 inversion

3. First Conclusions

The L4 row is not uniform.

Group Cells Claim ceiling
Strong native-center cells Geometry, Anahata, Tiferet Structural support / interpretive convergence
Useful pressure cells Timocracy, Pareto, Mechanism Design Interpretive translation / derived diagnostic
Weak center cells Computation / Gradient Descent Downgrade candidate

This is good news, not bad news. The audit is doing its job: L4 is not allowed to win by being beautiful.
