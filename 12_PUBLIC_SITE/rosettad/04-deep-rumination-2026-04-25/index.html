The Kill Criterion

If the seven-row alignment is shown to be forced — if the domains genuinely require different numbers of levels, or if the alignment breaks when tested with new domains — the Rosetta Stone falls.

The framework survives the kill criterion if: - New domains can be added and the seven rows still hold - The alignment degrades gracefully (some rows merge) rather than breaking completely - The operator column remains exhaustive (four moves + three boundaries)

The framework does not survive if: - A domain requires a fundamentally different number of levels - The operator grammar proves non-exhaustive (a fifth move exists) - The equator is not a global minimum in the geometry
