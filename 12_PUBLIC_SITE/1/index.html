01
Two Centers

Zero Is the Center of Addition; One Is the Center of Multiplication

The ordinary number line places zero at the center because it is built around addition. In additive symmetry the map is x ↦ −x, the fixed point is zero, and the natural metric is |x|.

Multiplicative reality has a different center. Under reciprocal symmetry I(x) = 1/x, the fixed point is one — the only positive real satisfying x = 1/x. The natural metric is not |x| but |log x|: the distance from finity measured in logarithmic units.

Both symmetries are involutions (self-inverse). Both exchange boundary regions. But they fix different points because they answer different questions: addition asks "how far from nothing?" while multiplication asks "how far from unity?"
