01
The Geometry

Burrisphere as S² / ℂP¹ Public Geometry

The Burrisphere is the Riemann sphere S² reinterpreted as a constraint surface for coupled variables. Where the ordinary Riemann sphere maps complex numbers to points on a sphere, the Burrisphere maps coherence (φ) and viability (ν) to reciprocal coordinates that multiply to one everywhere on the open surface. In the D5-limit reading, this becomes the D5 Landauer Barrier: Maxwell-demon-like worldline selection is an asymptote because perfect branch knowledge would require physical memory, computation, measurement, and control inside entropy accounting.

The equation φ · ν = 1 is not decorative. It is a binding constraint on the open surface. Away from the poles, the lowercase coordinate readings are locked in an inverse relationship. Raise one, and the other must fall. This is a sphere-coordinate statement, not a claim that finite actors can freely trade one uppercase action factor for another. The only point where both coordinates can be equal is the equator: φ = ν = 1.

This is the geometric origin of the Power-Max constraint: the equator is the unique point where sight and means can both remain usable. Not the north pole (coherence-coordinate runaway with no usable means). Not the south pole (viability-coordinate runaway with no usable foresight). The equator, where both are unity and balance makes action possible.

Read for an agent, the two coordinates name the two faces of acting: coherence (φ) can become D5 worldline-foresight — the capacity to envision, rank, and predict the reflexive effects of a move on the option cone of reachable futures — and viability (ν) can supply D4 means-to-act — body, tools, energy, access, timing, control, and execution capacity at the action boundary. Agency needs both, so it lives only at the equator: the φ-pole is all foresight with no means; the ν-pole is all means with no foresight to rank or aim them. [I/S] the agency reading — the option cone is the D5 cone of selectable futures, not the c-bounded physical light cone, lowercase ν remains the sphere coordinate, finite-node action uses uppercase V in P_node = Φ × V, and the gloss is not upgraded by the mass-shell.

The simple test is concrete. A child beside a jet has enormous apparent means but not the trained foresight to use them; a trained pilot without usable bodily or tool access has foresight without the relevant means. A hand, a shovel, and an industrial digger are all D4 means-to-act at different scales, but D5 asks whether the actor can envision the worldline those means open and use them without collapsing the larger option cone. Potential is maximized only when sight and means stay coupled.

At the limit, D5 becomes a Maxwell-demon-like selector: complete discrimination among the reachable worldlines of all relevant actors, with enough foresight to route action toward the branch that lowers disorder locally. The fence matters: this is not free reversal of the second law. Perfect branch knowledge needs memory and control; memory and control need matter and energy; matter and energy stay inside the entropy bill. Like a massive body approaching light speed, D5 can approach the ideal only by paying a cost that diverges at the limit. A finite selector can lower entropy locally only by paying or exporting entropy elsewhere, and reflexive action changes the branches being predicted. [S/I]

The Projections

Dual Stereographic Projections

The Burrisphere carries two stereographic projections, one from each pole — one cast downward from the north pole (∞), reading the sphere in the coherence coordinate φ = cot(θ/2); one cast upward from the south pole (0), reading it in the viability coordinate ν = tan(θ/2). Both rays pass through the same point of the sphere — they meet on the surface — and land on the equatorial plane at reciprocal radii. That is the equation: φ · ν = 1.

In the live model, the θ slider holds latitude while the azimuth keeps rotating. Sliding toward the φ pole gives foresight without usable means; sliding toward the ν pole gives means without foresight to aim them. The equator is where the two projections agree and finite-node action can be scored as P_node = Φ × V.

This duality is the geometric engine of the framework. Every decision, every system, every organism can be read as a point on the sphere — a particular calibration of lowercase φ and ν, with an agency gloss only when the point is scored as finite action. The north-pole projection asks: "given this coherence-coordinate reading, what viability-coordinate reading is possible?" The south-pole projection asks: "given this viability-coordinate reading, what coherence-coordinate reading is possible?"

The two projections meet at the equator, where both questions have the same answer: φ = ν = 1. The equator is the only point where the two projections agree — where the coordinate readings coincide and the action factors can both be scored without zeroing the move.

04
Game Space

Why Balance Is a Conditional Equilibrium

In game-space language, the Burrisphere is not just a manifold — it is a game board. The two stereographic projections trade viability (ν) and coherence (φ). Every agent on the sphere is playing the same game: widen the cone of futures you can reach — subject to keeping foresight and means in balance. Balance is not the whole goal; it is the condition under which potential can be used. Push means to a pole with no coherence and the product φ·ν drives the other factor to infinity — the system collapses into its shadow. Hoard foresight without usable means and the mirror fails in the opposite direction. So the equator is the feasibility surface the pursuit of potential must stay near, not a decorative midpoint. [S] — the constitution's five refusals are this constraint set; the directional Ω is the objective (this corrected an earlier "maximise balance" reading after the R6 test).

The extraction coefficient η measures asymmetry of exchange — how much a system takes relative to what it gives. η = 0 names balanced exchange: neither side gains at the other's expense. But balanced exchange is not an unconditional Nash equilibrium. Left to private incentives a player can still do better by defecting; and no mechanism can be strategyproof, efficient, and budget-balanced at once (Green–Laffont, 1977) [A]. So η = 0 is a conditional equilibrium — it holds only when enforcement (reputation, reciprocity, a licensed boundary) makes defection unprofitable [S/I]. The equator is where the game can rest, not where it rests on its own.

The poles are the imbalanced extremes: structure with no capacity to act at one, capacity with no coherence at the other (B → 0 at both). Neither pole is "extraction" — extraction is a direction of transfer (taking from a cooperator, η > 0), not a place on the sphere [S]. And an extractive move is self-defeating: to take from another you must first become a taker. The sustainable position is the equator — reached and held only under the constitution's refusals, not because the geometry commands it [S/I].

The Mirror

The Equator as the Playable Mirror

The equator is not merely a mathematical curiosity. It is the playable mirror — the position from which a system can act without being consumed by its own structure or dissolved by its own freedom. At the equator, the organism has enough coherence to see a reachable worldline and enough viability to actualize one.

The seven positions of the Rosetta are arranged around this sphere. Each position is a different latitude in the φ/ν coordinate pair — and, in the agency gloss, a different coupling of foresight and means. The poles themselves are L0 and L∞ (B → 0); L1 and L7 sit nearest them. L4 sits at the equator (B = 1). The three Gods (L2–L4) rise through the southern hemisphere to the equator; the three Executive Boundaries (L5–L7) stand in the northern hemisphere above it.

The framework's entire taxonomy — castes, operators, disciplines, questions — is derived from this geometry. Change the geometry, and the framework changes. Deny the geometry, and the framework becomes arbitrary. The Burrisphere is not an illustration of the doctrine. It is the doctrine.
