The two stakes — what every move moves

Φ (foresight/coherence factor) = D5 worldline-foresight — the capacity to envision reachable futures and predict a move's reflexive effects on the option cone (recursion depth, theory-of-mind, legibility, trust). V (means/viability factor) = D4 means-to-act — body, tools, energy, access, execution capacity: hands, shovel, industrial digger, runway, machine. A player needs both: foresight without means is the seer who cannot do; means without foresight is the machine that cannot rank or aim. A child beside a jet and an old pilot without the needed bodily/tool access are opposite zero-factor failures. The playable position is the equator, where there is enough of each. I/S · the option cone here is the D5 cone of selectable futures, not the c-bounded physical light cone; lowercase φ and ν belong to the sphere shorthand, while finite-node action uses uppercase Φ and V.

The sharpest caseWar, where nobody is sentimental

Military doctrine carries the D5 thesis in its least romantic form: institutions choose which game is being played.

Lanchester — the law is selected by the information regime

Combat power is multiplicative, never additive — you cannot trade zero soldiers for any amount of quality. But the exponents depend on what you can see. In aimed fire (you can target individuals) strength is q·N² — the Square Law, asymmetric, and its lesson is concentrate force. In sanctuary / area fire (you fire blind into a zone) strength is q·N — the Linear Law, symmetric, the one-for-one trade. Fourth-generation warfare is mechanism design at war: the weaker player does not fight the stronger force — it redesigns the information structure of the conflict to drag it from Square to Linear and erase the numerical advantage. [A] the attrition laws · [I] the D5 reading.

Honest scope: this witnesses multiplicative, not additive robustly. It does not make the symmetric balance-sphere universal — the Square Law is a different exponent structure with a corner optimum (see the Dyadic Coupling Law §5c). That the governing law flips with the information regime is the framework's register-dependence, observed in doctrine.
