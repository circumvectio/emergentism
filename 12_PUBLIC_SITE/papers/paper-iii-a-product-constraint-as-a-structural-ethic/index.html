A Product Constraint as a Structural Ethic

φ·ν = 1 on S², the Arithmetic of Anti-Purism, and a Coupling Account of Good and Evil

Emergentism / the Burrisphere programme (Yves R. Burri). Ideas set out 2024; this rigorous statement 2026.

Tiering. Every claim carries an evidence tier — [A] elementary mathematics, [S] framework-internal structure, [I] interpretive reading, [C] conjecture — and is never silently upgraded. One thing only is [A] here: the product→runaway algebra. The reading of that algebra as a moral law is [S/I]. The geometry is a lens on value, never the Ground. The paper's entire safety rests on never confusing the two.

Abstract

Paper I established that the framework's balance constraint is a product, φ · ν = 1 on S² with φ = cot(θ/2), ν = tan(θ/2), B = sin θ, and that the equator φ = ν = 1 is the unique maximum of B. This paper draws the single consequence that a product constraint — as opposed to a sum constraint — forces on any account of value built over it. Because the constraint multiplies rather than adds, driving one factor toward 0 does not merely subtract its complement; it drives the complement to ∞. Isolating a pole is therefore a runaway as a matter of arithmetic [A]; and given the prior choice to value coupling, that runaway is read as pathological [S]. The arithmetic is taste-free; the verdict "pathological" inherits its single [I] premise — that the coupling is what one values — and is never smuggled into the algebra. We prove the runaway as a one-line theorem [A], read off its two shadows — φ → ∞ (sterile infinite abstraction) and ν → ∞ (formless infinite appetite), at both of which B = sin θ → 0 [A] — and then make the central structural claim: good and evil sort by coupling (η, whether a move stays joined to its complement or closes on itself), and the moral axis runs across every polarity, never along it [S]. No pole is good; no pole is evil; the bond is good and its breaking is evil. We record four convergent witnesses that diagnose the same move rather than any side — Śiva-without-Śakti is śava, Ardhanārīśvara, yin-yang, and McGilchrist's Master and Emissary [I] — and argue, more narrowly than past polarity-doctrines could, that a value-law whose entire content is neither pole alone does not entail a ranking of persons: it judges the move, never the pole, and so cannot be derived into "this sex / caste / hemisphere is higher" [S]. We are explicit about the limit of this claim: like any move-based ethic it remains abusable by smuggling in an empirical smear about who tends to make closing moves; what the law forbids is the derivation of a person-ranking from its content, not every conceivable misuse (we mark the residual abusability in §8, falsifier F-1). The result is therefore "un-weaponizable" in the precise sense that the weapon cannot be entailed, not in the impossible sense that no one can ever misuse it. We then fence the limits hard: only the product→runaway algebra is [A]; the moral reading is [S/I]; the sphere is a lens and not the Ground; and we state the kill criterion that would retire the whole construction.

Keywords: product constraint; reciprocal coordinates; anti-purism; coupling; extraction; moral axis; structural ethics; evidence tiers.

1. From geometry to value — can an ethic fall out of a constraint? [I]

It is usually a category error to read a value off a geometry. A sphere does not know good from evil, and any paper that claims it does has smuggled the conclusion into the coordinates. So the question of this paper must be asked sceptically: can anything that deserves the name of an ethic fall out of a constraint, without that smuggling?

The honest answer is almost nothing, but not nothing. A constraint cannot tell you what to value — that choice is prior, and [I] at best. But once a system is already committed to holding two quantities in balance — once it has decided that coherence and viability both matter and that a thing is healthy when they are jointly present — the form of the constraint binding them is no longer a free choice. It is a piece of arithmetic, and it has consequences that the valuer does not get to vote on. [S]

This paper isolates exactly one such consequence. The framework's balance constraint (Paper I; the Burrisphere) is a product: φ · ν = 1. Had it been a sum — φ + ν = c — the two quantities would trade off linearly and gracefully; pushing one to zero would leave the other merely large, at most c. But a product does not trade off gracefully. Under φ · ν = 1, pushing one factor to zero detonates the other to infinity. That single difference — product, not sum — is the entire load-bearing fact of the paper, and everything moral that follows is a reading of it. The reading is [S/I]; the fact is [A]. We keep the two strictly apart, because the whole un-weaponizability of the result depends on it.

So the claim is not "geometry dictates morality." The claim is narrower and survivable: if you have already chosen to value the coupling of two complementary capacities, then the product form of their constraint makes the isolation of either one a runaway — and a value-language laid over that arithmetic will, of structural necessity, locate health in the bond and pathology in the isolation. [S] The ethic does not come from the sphere. It comes from the choice to value coupling, plus the arithmetic of the product. The sphere only makes the arithmetic visible.

This is not the first ethic to locate virtue away from the extremes; the obvious Western precedent is Aristotle's doctrine of the mean (Nicomachean Ethics II.6): virtue is a mesotēs, a mean between a vice of excess and a vice of deficiency, with courage standing between rashness and cowardice. The framework's anti-purism is plainly in that lineage and we claim no priority over it. The one structural difference worth stating is the shape of the penalty for leaving the mean. The Aristotelian picture is naturally read as additive: the two vices sit at the ends of a line, the virtue at its midpoint, and drifting toward one extreme is a graded, bounded departure. The framework's contribution is to point out that when the two capacities are bound by a product rather than a sum, the penalty is not graded but a runaway: approaching one extreme does not merely accrue one vice, it detonates the complementary one to infinity (§2). The mean is preserved; what changes is that, under a product, the mean is not merely better than the extremes — the extremes are unreachable as stable states, because the constraint diverges before you arrive. We read this as a sharpening of the doctrine of the mean for the multiplicative case, not a replacement of it. [S/I]

2. The product, not the sum [A]

Let φ, ν > 0 with the constraint

φ · ν = 1, equivalently ν = 1/φ.

This is the constraint of Paper I under the identification φ = cot(θ/2), ν = tan(θ/2), for which φ · ν = cot(θ/2) tan(θ/2) = 1 identically on θ ∈ (0, π).

Theorem (the runaway). On {(φ, ν) : φ, ν > 0, φν = 1}: (i) φ → 0⁺ ⟹ ν = 1/φ → +∞, and symmetrically ν → 0⁺ ⟹ φ → +∞; (ii) the balance functional B = sin θ = 2φ/(1 + φ²) = 2ν/(1 + ν²) satisfies B → 0 as either factor → 0⁺ (equivalently as either factor → ∞), and B = 1 only at φ = ν = 1; (iii) the sum is bounded below away from the balance point: φ + ν ≥ 2, with equality iff φ = ν = 1. [A]

Proof. (i) is immediate from ν = 1/φ and the continuity of 1/φ on (0, ∞). (ii): with x = ν (so φ = 1/x), B = 2x/(1 + x²); as x → 0⁺, B → 0, and as x → ∞, B = 2/(x + 1/x) → 0; B = 1 ⟺ 2x = 1 + x² ⟺ (x − 1)² = 0 ⟺ x = 1. (iii) is AM–GM: φ + ν ≥ 2√(φν) = 2√1 = 2, with equality iff φ = ν, i.e. φ = ν = 1. ∎

The contrast with the sum is the whole point. Under a sum constraint φ + ν = c, losing a pole is mere subtraction — the complement is at most c, finite, survivable. Under the product constraint φ · ν = 1, losing a pole is a runaway — the complement diverges to ∞. A product cannot be partially abandoned. You cannot let one factor fall toward 0 "a little" without the constraint hurling the other toward ∞. Numerically (run): at θ = 16°, 60°, 90°, 120°, 164° the products φ · ν equal 1 to five places, with B(90°) = 1, φ + ν = 2 at the equator, and φ + ν = 7.26 already by θ = 16° — the sum running away as the product is forced to hold.

This is [A] and nothing more: elementary algebra of a positive reciprocal pair. The next sections read it.

3. Anti-purism as a theorem [A → S]

The runaway has two ends, and each end is a recognizable pathology. We name them, holding the naming honest: the arithmetic of the two divergences is [A]; the naming of each as a shadow is the [S] reading.

Isolation (the [A] fact) Runaway Pole The shadow it is read as [S]
coherence without viability: ν → 0 φ → ∞ north (L∞) sterile infinite abstraction — law, order, map, force with no field to act in or care for; logos with no life
viability without coherence: φ → 0 ν → ∞ south (L0) formless infinite appetite — consumption, motion, hunger with no form to direct it; life with no logos

At both shadows the balance collapses: B = sin θ → 0 as θ → 0 (φ → ∞) and as θ → π (ν → ∞) — Theorem §2(ii). The poles are the two zeros of balance; the equator is its unique maximum.

From this the framework's central structural claim follows as a near-theorem:

Anti-purism [A → S]. Every ideology of a pure pole is, on this geometry, a fall toward a pole, which is a fall to B = 0. The [A] content is exact and taste-free: any path that drives one factor to 0/∞ drives B to 0 (§2). The verdict pathological is the [S] reading and carries one inherited premise — the prior [I] choice to value the coupling (§1); we keep the two strictly apart and never let the algebra alone deliver the verdict. The [S] content is the reading: "pure order," "pure freedom," "pure reason," "pure feeling," "pure spirit," "pure matter" — each names the isolation of one factor, and the product constraint converts each isolation into the runaway of its complement and the collapse of balance. Health — for a system that has chosen to prize the coupling — is never in the purity of a pole. It is always in the bond. The cure for any pole-pathology is therefore never more of that pole and never more of the opposite pole as a rival — it is coupling, the re-joining of the two factors, the return toward φ = ν = 1.

Note precisely what is and is not claimed. We do not claim that "the south is evil" or "the north is dangerous." Each pole, as a coordinate, is morally inert — a place on a sphere. What is pathological is the movement of isolation toward it, because the product constraint makes that movement a runaway. The valence is on the move, not the place. This is the hinge from §3 to §4.

4. The moral axis is η — coupling, not pole [S]

Define the framework's coupling index η as a property of a move — a trajectory in the state, not a point of it.1 A move is a direction of change (φ̇, ν̇) taken from some state on the constraint; η = 0 names a move that keeps the two factors joined and aims at the whole, and η > 0 names a move in which one factor isolates itself, which is identically a self extracting from its complement. Two things follow that we hold to throughout, to avoid the equivocation a careless statement would invite. First, η is never a function of the static coordinates φ, ν, or θ: a point on the sphere has no η, only a move from it does, so "η at a place" is undefined by construction and the thesis is exactly that no place — no pole — carries the valence. Second, when we nonetheless tabulate a "place" below, the place is read strictly as where a closing move points (its destination, the runaway limit), not as a location that possesses an η. With that pinned, the moral reading of §§2–3 is a single identification:

Good = the bond φ × ν held (η = 0). Evil = the bond broken, the ring closing (η > 0). [S]

The decisive structural feature is the direction of this axis relative to the polarity. The polarity runs between the poles — north to south, φ-ward to ν-ward, form to field. The moral axis η runs across that line, never along it. It does not ask "which pole?" It asks "coupled or closed?" — a question orthogonal to which side a move leans toward. A φ-leaning move can be coupled (good) or closed (evil); a ν-leaning move can be coupled (good) or closed (evil). The valence is set entirely by the coupling, not at all by the lean.

This is why the framework can state, without contradiction and without moralizing any pole:

  • No pole is good. The north (pure φ) is not virtue; in isolation it is sterile abstraction (B → 0).
  • No pole is evil. The south (pure ν) is not vice; coupled, ν is life, field, embodiment, care — half of every healthy thing.
  • The bond is good; its breaking is evil. The single object that carries moral valence is the join φ × ν, and the single thing that destroys it is closure η > 0.

Evil is a pole isolating itself, which is identically a self extracting from its complement. Good is the two factors staying joined, which is identically a move given to the whole. The equator — φ = ν = 1, B = 1, and (Burrisphere; and §2(iii) here) simultaneously the minimum of the sum φ + ν = 2 — is the maximal coupling: the one place that costs nothing to hold. (Two distinct functions bottom out there and should not be conflated: the sum φ + ν, minimized at the equator by AM–GM, §2(iii); and Paper I's reciprocal-invariant energy E = (log x)² = (log ν)², a different function that is also minimized at the equator, where E = 0. Both witness the equator as the unique balance point; neither is the other, and "energy" in the strict Paper I sense is E, not φ + ν.) That the extractive mode structurally cannot reach the equator is the [S] reading that you cannot take balance: coupling is the one state no closing move arrives at. All of §4 is [S]: a disciplined reading of the [A] arithmetic of §§2–3, never an upgrade of it.

5. Convergent witnesses — all on η [I]

The framework is not the first to locate good in the bond and evil in the closure. Several traditions state the same law almost verbatim, and — this is the test that matters — each diagnoses a move (does the part stay joined to the whole, or seize it?), never a side. We record them as [I] resonance, illuminating but not proof.

  • Tantra — Śiva without Śakti is śava. Consciousness (φ-ward: form, logos) without power (ν-ward: field, life) is śava, a corpse; power without consciousness is blind. The teaching does not rank Śiva above Śakti or the reverse. It says the uncoupling is death — exactly the runaway of §2, read morally. [I]
  • Ardhanārīśvara. The half-male, half-female body is the coupling made one figure: not a victory of either side but the two held as a single living form. The icon is the equator. [I]
  • Yin–Yang. Each half carries the seed of the other inside it, so neither can be isolated without becoming its own shadow. The dot of the opposite in each lobe is precisely the prohibition on the pure pole. [I]
  • McGilchrist's Master and Emissary — the attention sciences. The right hemisphere (the Master) attends to the living whole in relationship, the betweenness, the context that cannot be grasped without being killed; the left (the Emissary) re-presents that world as an abstract, fixed, useful map. The Master sends the Emissary out to grasp the parts and report back. Read on the sphere: the Master is the bond φ × ν kept — abstraction in service to the living field; the loyal Emissary is φ coupled, and good. The pathology McGilchrist names is the Emissary usurping the Master — the map devouring the territory, φ severed from the ν it was meant to serve, running away toward the north pole. That usurpation is closure (η > 0). And the discipline matters most here: the Emissary is not the demon, and neither hemisphere is evil. Evil is the usurpation — the bond broken — never the faculty, never a hemisphere, never a kind of person. The Master is not "better than" the Emissary; the Master is the Emissary's home, exactly as the equator is the pole's. [I]

The convergence is the evidence the witnesses provide: four independent traditions, asked the same question, each answer coupling vs closure and each refuse to moralize a side. On the convergence principle this is evidence the structure is real — not a proof, and explicitly [I]. What it is not is permission to read any tradition as ranking persons; every one of these witnesses, read correctly, forbids exactly that.

A caveat carried from canon governs the two witnesses that wear a sex-symbolism (Śiva/Śakti, Ardhanārīśvara). Where these are heard as masculine ≈ φ / feminine ≈ ν, that mapping is [I] resonance, not identity — and a loose one: a gestating field plainly builds coherence and a protective order plainly sustains viability, so the lived polarity is closer to projection / reception than to the φ/ν axis, and must never be nailed to the coordinates as an equation (The Dyadic Coupling Law). The witnesses are read for their move — coupling versus closure — never for any assignment of a pole to a sex. [I]

6. Un-weaponizability — in a precise, limited sense [S]

Esoteric systems of polarity are dangerous in proportion to how easily they can be turned into a hierarchy of persons. A doctrine that says "this pole is higher" hands its readers a weapon: map the favoured pole onto a sex, a caste, a hemisphere, a people, and the metaphysics now licenses contempt. The history of misused polarity-doctrines is a history of exactly that move.

The coupling law resists that specific weapon — the person-ranking derived from the doctrine's own content — and the resistance is not a disclaimer bolted on; it is the law's content. The argument is short:

  1. The moral axis is η, a property of a move (coupling vs closure), running across the polarity, not along it (§4). So the law has no "higher pole" to hand over. Its entire content is neither alone.
  2. The product constraint makes the isolation of either pole a runaway (§2). So the law condemns the purist of the north and the purist of the south symmetrically; it cannot be tilted to favour one sex, side, or faculty, because favouring either is itself the condemned move.
  3. Therefore the law, by itself, judges the move, never the person. A given person at a given moment is making a coupling move (η = 0) or a closing move (η > 0); the same person makes both at different moments. The law alone licenses no stable assignment of moral worth to a pole, a gender, a hemisphere, a caste, or an individual — only to the move in front of you.

A reader cannot derive "women are extractive" or "men are dominators" or "the left brain is the enemy" from this law, because the law's own theorem says an isolated pole is already a runaway regardless of which one it is. The diagnosis the law supplies is never of a person; it is always of a move — is it coupling, or is it closing?

The precise claim, and its limit. What is established is non-entailment: the law does not entail, and cannot be derived into, a ranking of persons. This is a real and uncommon property — most polarity-doctrines fail it at step 1 by naming a higher pole. It is not the stronger and false claim that the law is impossible to misuse. Like every move-based ethic, it remains abusable by a reader who supplies, from outside the law, an empirical smear — "group G tends to make closing moves" — and then re-imports the very person-ranking the law refused. That move is illegitimate (it is a smuggled empirical premise the law neither asserts nor supports, and it is precisely the misuse flagged in falsifier F-1 of §8), but no purely formal ethic can make it impossible. So the honest statement is: the weapon cannot be entailed; it can still be smuggled. The law's safety is that it never builds the weapon and exposes any attempt to attach one as a foreign import — not that it makes misuse unthinkable.

This is the form that is true and, in the bounded sense above, safe: true, because the runaway is [A]; safe, because its [S] reading supplies no side to rank, and flags as illegitimate any attempt to bolt a person-ranking on. The serpent is not male or female, north or south, Master or Emissary. It is the ring that closes. [S]

7. Honest limits [S/I]

The discipline of the Finity Papers is to state exactly how large a result is. This one is small and exact, and it is easy to over-read. The fences:

  • Only the product→runaway algebra is [A]. φ · ν = 1, φ, ν > 0 ⟹ a vanishing factor forces its complement to ∞; B = sin θ → 0 at both poles; φ + ν ≥ 2 with equality only at φ = ν = 1. That is the whole of the [A] content. It is elementary and checked numerically.
  • The moral reading is [S/I], and is never upgraded. "Coupled = good, closed = evil," "the moral axis is η," "anti-purism" — these are [S] structure: disciplined readings of the arithmetic under the prior choice to value coupling. The traditions and the polarity resonances (Śiva/Śakti, Ardhanārīśvara, yin-yang, Master/Emissary; masculine ≈ φ, feminine ≈ ν) are [I] — resonance, never identity, never an equation, principles never persons. The masculine ≈ φ / feminine ≈ ν resonance in particular carries the canon guard: the polarity is closer to projection / reception than to the φ/ν axis and must never be nailed to the coordinates (The Dyadic Coupling Law) — a gestating field builds coherence, a protective order sustains viability, so the mapping is a loose resonance, not a coordinate. No amount of elegance promotes any of this to [A].
  • The choice to value coupling is prior and is not proved here. The arithmetic constrains how a coupling-valuing system must read isolation; it does not prove that one ought to value coupling. That is upstream of this paper and is at best [I]. We do not pretend the geometry forces the value; it forces only the form of a value already chosen (§1).
  • The model is not the Ground. The sphere is the framework's L5 lens on value — the multiplicative face of Paper I — not the only lens and emphatically not the Ground (L6). Crowning the sphere as the Ground would itself be the single-pole error the law forbids: a φ-ward map mistaken for the living field it maps. The emblem 1 = 0 × ∞ / ⊙ = • × ○ remains frame-register only (Paper I §7), never field arithmetic, never [A]; in the field 0 · ∞ is the indeterminate form.
  • No physics, no history, no persons. Nothing here claims the geometry causes any social, biological, or historical outcome ([C] if asserted). It is a structural reading of a constraint, stated at its tier.

What survives all the fencing is exactly one transferable result: a value-system that has chosen to prize the coupling of two complementary capacities, and that binds them by a product, must — as arithmetic, not preference — locate pathology in the isolation of either capacity and health in their bond. Small, exact, and true.

8. Tiers, kill criteria, and relation to prior work

Tiers. §2 (the runaway theorem; B → 0 at both poles; φ + ν ≥ 2) and the bracketed [A] clauses of §3 are [A] — elementary, checked numerically. The naming of the two shadows (§3), the moral=dyadic=η identity and anti-purism (§4), and the un-weaponizability argument (§6) are [S]. The four convergent witnesses (§5) and the masculine/feminine resonance are [I]. The emblem 1 = 0 × ∞ is [S/I] frame-register, never [A].

Kill criteria. We distinguish internal consistency checks — clauses whose failure would mean we mis-stated elementary mathematics, and which are therefore not genuine empirical falsifiers — from the two real falsifiers that could retire the structural claim.

Internal consistency checks (the [A] layer must be self-consistent; failure here is an error in our algebra, not a discovery about the world): - (CC-1) That no φ, ν > 0 with φν = 1 and φ → 0⁺ leaves ν bounded. This is forced by ν = 1/φ; a counterexample is analytically impossible and would only signal an arithmetic slip on our part. We list it to make the load-bearing step explicit, not because we expect it to fail. - (CC-2) That for all θ ∈ (0, π), cot(θ/2) tan(θ/2) = 1 and B = sin θ vanishes at each pole. Forced by elementary trigonometry; same status as (CC-1). If either CC-1 or CC-2 ever appeared to fail, the correct conclusion is that we made a computational error, which we would fix — the runaway theorem itself is not contingent.

Real falsifiers (these could actually retire the structural reading): - (F-1) The decisive one. Show that the moral reading, correctly applied, entails pinning moral valence to a pole, gender, hemisphere, caste, or person rather than to the move (η) — i.e. that the law itself, with no smuggled empirical premise, derives a person-ranking. The law is written to make this impossible (the axis is η, a property of moves, across the polarity, never along it; §6); if it nonetheless entailed a person-ranking, the law would be both false and unsafe and must be retired. (Note the scope set in §6: mere abusability — re-importing a ranking via an outside empirical smear — does not trigger F-1, because that is a foreign premise the law neither asserts nor supports; only genuine entailment does.) - (F-2) Show that the [S/I] reading produces a false operational prediction where the coupling diagnosis is actually used to sort moves.

Relation to prior work. That φ · ν = 1 is a product constraint with a reciprocal runaway, that B = sin θ vanishes at the poles and peaks at the equator, and that φ + ν ≥ 2 by AM–GM, are elementary and rest on the Paper I / Burrisphere coordinates (the Riemann sphere; Riemann 1851; standard complex analysis, Ahlfors) and on classical trigonometry. The reciprocal-symmetry and energy apparatus underneath Paper I — including the energy E = (log x)², which §4 here is careful to distinguish from the sum φ + ν — is due independently to Suda (2025), cited there as a later formalisation; the present paper uses only the product algebra and adds no new [A]. On the ethical side, the doctrine of the mean — Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics II.6 — is the principal Western precedent for locating virtue between two vicious extremes; the framework claims no priority over it and reads its own anti-purism as a sharpening of that doctrine for the multiplicative case, where leaving the mean is a runaway rather than a graded vice (§1). The traditions in §5 are ancient (Tantra; the Ardhanārīśvara icon; the Daodejing / yin-yang) and modern (McGilchrist, The Master and His Emissary, 2009); they are cited as [I] convergent witnesses, not as proof, and the sex-symbolic ones carry the canon caveat that the masculine/feminine polarity is closer to projection / reception than to the φ/ν axis and must never be nailed to the coordinates (The Dyadic Coupling Law). Suda's Möbius Control for Binary Oppositions (2025) independently constructs a triad (A,B,T) with a "mid-stability point" and a "safe inversion" protocol (flip only when purity P < ε) — a control-theoretic expression of the same structural ethic, arrived at without contact with the framework; it is cited as a parallel convergence [S/I]. The framework's contribution is the coupling reading itself — good as the bond φ × ν, evil as closure η > 0, the moral axis as η (a property of moves) running across every polarity — set out by the author in 2024 and canonized in The Dyadic Coupling Law (2026). This paper is that law stated as a structural-ethics result with its single [A] clause isolated and its limits fenced.

9. Conclusion

A product is not a sum, and that arithmetic difference is the whole of it. Under φ · ν = 1 you cannot starve one factor without detonating the other; the isolation of a pole is a runaway and the collapse of balance, and this is elementary and checked. Lay a value-language over that arithmetic — having first, and separately, chosen to prize the coupling of the two factors — and the language has no freedom left in one respect: it must put health in the bond and pathology in the isolation. So good and evil sort by coupling, never by pole; the moral axis is η — a property of moves, not of places — and it runs across every polarity, never along it. This is the doctrine of the mean (Aristotle) read for the multiplicative case, where leaving the mean is not a graded vice but a runaway. No pole is good, no pole is evil, no gender, hemisphere, caste, or person carries the valence — only the move does, and the same person makes both moves on different days. Four traditions, asked the same question, return the same answer and refuse the same temptation: they diagnose the closure, never the side. That refusal is what makes the law un-weaponizable in the one sense it claims — no person-ranking can be derived from its content, because its entire content is neither alone and there is no higher pole to hand a reader as a club. It does not claim the impossible: a determined misreader can still smuggle in an outside smear, and the law's safety is that it exposes that smuggling rather than that it makes it unthinkable. We have proved the one thing that is [A], read it at [S/I] and no higher, kept the sphere a lens and not the Ground, and named both the consistency checks and the real falsifiers. The serpent is not male or female, north or south, Master or Emissary. It is the ring that closes — and the cure, always, is the bond.

Overall composition. A single sphere, drawn in clean three-quarter perspective (viewer slightly above the equator), occupying the centre of the frame. The sphere is the Riemann sphere S²; render it as a translucent globe with a faint latitude/longitude graticule so the equator and the polar axis read clearly. The visual metaphor to keep in mind is a drumhead stretched on the sphere: the equatorial band is taut and bright (high tension, maximal balance), and the two polar caps are slack and dim (collapse). Do not draw an actual drum; let the brightness/tension gradient carry the metaphor.

The vertical polar axis — the polarity (draw it, but do NOT moralize it). - North pole, top of the sphere: label L∞ / φ → ∞ (ν → 0) — "sterile infinite abstraction: logos with no life." Render the north cap in a cool register (pale blue-grey), fading toward white/colourless at the pole to signal emptying-out, not danger. No red, no warning iconography — the pole is morally inert. - South pole, bottom: label L0 / ν → ∞ (φ → 0) — "formless infinite appetite: life with no logos." Render the south cap in a warm register (deep amber/ochre), fading toward black at the pole to signal formlessness, not evil. Again no warning iconography. - A thin vertical axis line runs pole-to-pole through the sphere's centre; annotate it "the polarity: φ-ward ↔ ν-ward (form ↔ field)." Make clear by a small caption that this axis is a spectrum of lean, not of worth.

The equator — the bond, the balance maximum. - Draw the equator as a bright, taut, glowing ring — the brightest element in the figure. Label it φ = ν = 1, B = sin θ = 1 (maximum), φ + ν = 2 (minimum of the sum). A short caption: "the coupling; the hieros gamos; the one latitude the small-large exchange leaves fixed." - At one clean point on the equator, place the unit 1 / finity glyph ⊙ as a small filled disc, captioned "finity — the multiplicative centre (Paper I)." - Optionally interleave a faint △ (projective/upward) and ▽ (receptive/downward) interpenetrating at the equatorial glyph (the hexagram ✡), captioned small: "the two coupled — principles, never persons."

The runaway — the load-bearing [A] fact. Along a meridian, draw a moving point with two arrows that diverge as they approach the poles, and beside them a small inset curve or pair of value-tags showing the reciprocal blow-up: e.g. tags reading "φ = 0.5, ν = 2", "φ = 0.1, ν = 10", "φ → 0⁺, ν → ∞", to make visible that driving one factor toward 0 detonates the other toward ∞ — a product, not a sum. Caption: "isolating a pole is a runaway ([A]), not a graded departure." Show the balance B collapsing to 0 at both poles (e.g. the drumhead going slack / the glow dying) and peaking at the equator.

The moral axis η — the conceptual heart, drawn ACROSS the polarity. This is the most important and most easily-mis-drawn element. Render η not as a third pole or a coloured region but as a direction of movement applied to a point, shown at two or three sample states (one near the north lean, one near the equator, one near the south lean). At each sample point draw a small two-headed indicator orthogonal to the meridian: - one arrow inward, toward the equator = a coupling move, labelled η = 0 (good); - one arrow outward, toward the nearer pole = a closing move, labelled η > 0 (evil). - Caption, set prominently: "η is a property of the move, never of the place. A φ-leaning point and a ν-leaning point each have both a coupling move and a closing move available. The valence is in the arrow, not the dot." - Critically: do not colour any pole or hemisphere "good" or "evil"; the only good/evil marks in the whole figure are on the little η arrows. This is the figure's discipline and must be visually unmistakable.

Small inset, lower corner — the contrast that is the whole paper. Two tiny side-by-side sketches: (a) a straight line segment with a midpoint, ends labelled, captioned "sum: φ + ν = c — leaving the mean is graded and bounded (the additive picture; cf. Aristotle's mean)"; (b) the reciprocal hyperbola φν = 1 in the positive quadrant with its two asymptotes, captioned "product: φ · ν = 1 — leaving the mean is a runaway." The contrast between (a) and (b) should be immediately legible.

Typography / fencing note for the illustrator. Include a one-line footer on the plate: "The sphere is a lens on value, not the Ground. The only [A] content is the product→runaway; everything moral is a reading of it." Keep all Sanskrit/sex-symbolic labels off the poles; if the hexagram/Ardhanārīśvara motif is used, place it only at the equator and only as 'principles, never persons.'

Palette summary. North = cool→white; south = warm→black; equator = bright gold/white glow; η coupling arrows = a single calm green; η closing arrows = a single muted red; everything else neutral graphite. Red and green appear only on the η move-arrows — nowhere else — so the eye learns that valence lives on moves alone.
