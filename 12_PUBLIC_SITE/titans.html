The floor · D0 — where the map begins

The three that are
not numbers.

Before arithmetic there are three frames: the dot •, the unit ⊙, the ring ○. Everyone learns their names in school — 0, 1, ∞ — and almost nobody learns the one thing that matters: they are not operands. They are the frames the operations live in. This page proves it, breaks it, and then puts it back together.

[A] the limit and the field laws [S] the three boundary-frames [I] the emblem — sung, not summed

Scene one — the three transformations

One glyph, three readings.

The framework's whole floor fits in one line — but the line is read three ways, and only two of them are arithmetic:

⊙ = • × ○

descent 1 · 0 = 1 / ∞ — computable [A]

descent 2 · ∞ = 1 / 0 — computable as a limit, never a value [A]

ascent · 1 = 0 × ∞ — the indeterminate. The emblem. Sung, not summed. [I]

Watch the difference the whole page hangs on: the descents can be computed — they are limits, honest and one-sided. The ascent cannot — 0 × ∞ is one of arithmetic's forbidden forms. The framework's most famous line is the one line its own floor refuses to calculate. Hold that thought; it becomes the punchline.

A precision the schoolroom skips: limx→0⁺ N/x = +∞ is true — a well-defined limit. N/0 as a value is undefined — no number at all. The limit is fine. The value is the casualty. The next scene shows why.

Scene two — the blow-up, both arms

Approach zero. From both sides.

From the right, 1/x climbs without bound. From the left, it falls without bound. The two arms fly to opposite infinities — so no single value can be assigned. That is the real proof that division by zero is undefined.

Scene three — the catastrophe

Force a value. Lose the field.

What happens if you ignore the fence and divide by zero anyway? The whole of arithmetic pays. Watch the most famous wrong proof in the world — one hidden division, and 2 becomes 1:

let  a = b
a² = ab
a² − b² = ab − b²
(a − b)(a + b) = b(a − b)
a + b = b   ←  divided both sides by (a − b) = 0
b + b = b
2 = 1

Every line looks legal. One line is a crime scene: the moment both sides were divided by (a − b) — which is zero, because a = b. Assign division-by-zero a value, and any equality becomes provable. The field's price for one forced value is the whole field.

Scene four — turn the table

So these three were never numbers.

Look at what just happened. Zero broke addition's inverse. Infinity absorbed every product. The unit sat untouched at the center of both. These three do not behave like the others — because they are not in the game. They are the boundary-frames that define it:

•  0 — the additive identity the floor of counting [S]

⊙  1 — the multiplicative identity the self-dual center [S]

○  ∞ — the projective point at infinity the ring's own edge [S]

Three boundary-frames of the number field. The Titans. They define the operations; they are not operands. Every number you will ever use lives between them — on the charts they frame.

Not claimed here: anything about countability. Our ○ is Riemann's one added point that compactifies the line into a ring — not Cantor's ℵ₀, and the dot is not 𝔠. Two infinities worth of poetry live in that mistake, and it is a mistake; the frames above are the whole definition.

Scene five — the rule of forbidden forms

Where a Titan may not sit.

The rule is not "never touch a Titan." It is sharper: a frame may not sit in an operand slot where it collapses the operation. Frame-role and operand-role are contextual, not exclusive — 1/1 = 1 and 0 + 5 = 5 are perfectly ordinary. The forbidden set is exactly the forms that break:

formverdictwhy
N / 0undefinedthe arms part — no value exists (scene two)
0 / 0indeterminateany value fits — no unique answer
∞ / ∞indeterminaterace of two frames — undecided by form
0 × ∞indeterminatethe ascent itself — the emblem, [I]
∞ − ∞indeterminatethe gap of two infinities — undecided by form
1 / 1 = 1lawfulthe identity being itself — an ordinary operand
0 + 5 = 5lawfulthe additive identity at work — ordinary

And there it is — the punchline the floor was saving. 0 × ∞ sits in the forbidden column, and it is the framework's own glyph. The descents compute; the ascent is the one indeterminate — which is precisely why the ascent is the sung line, the wager, the emblem [I], and never [A] arithmetic. The forbidden-forms table and the glyph's own typing are the same fact.

Scene six — the numbers return

Everything else lives between them.

Once the three frames stand, the rest of mathematics walks back in — as the content that plays between the poles, around the center, on the charts the Titans define. One beautiful chart of exactly that shape is already on this site: Suda's Egg of Infinity, u = (x−1)/(x+1) — the whole number line folded into a finite egg, the poles as its tips. An independent convergence, kept in its own chart.
