1. The Cone

1.1 Feasible Region

$$ \ПҶ\ОҪ = 1. $$

$$ \frac{\ПҶ + \ОҪ}{2} \geq \sqrt{\ПҶ\ОҪ} = 1 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \ПҶ + \ОҪ \geq 2. $$

$$ \mathcal{R} = { (\ПҶ, \ОҪ) \in \mathbb{R}^{+2} : \ПҶ\ОҪ = 1,\; \ПҶ + \ОҪ \geq 2 }. $$

Because $\ПҶ\ОҪ = 1$ already implies $\ПҶ + \ОҪ \geq 2$, the inequality does not further restrict the hyperbola branch; rather, it certifies that the hyperbola lies entirely outside the open unit square. The boundary of $\mathcal{R}$ is the single point

$$ \partial\mathcal{R} = { (1,1) }, $$

called the equator. In the framework's spherical parameterization,

$$ \ПҶ = \cot\frac{\Оё}{2}, \qquad \ОҪ = \tan\frac{\Оё}{2}, \qquad \Оё \in (0,\pi), $$

the equator is $\Оё = \pi/2$, the north pole is $\Оё \to 0$ ($\ПҶ \to \infty$, $\ОҪ \to 0$), and the south pole is $\Оё \to \pi$ ($\ПҶ \to 0$, $\ОҪ \to \infty$).

Consider the unconstrained plane. The inequality $\ПҶ + \ОҪ \geq 2$ with $\ПҶ, \ОҪ > 0$ defines a convex cone whose apex is $(1,1)$. 3. The Gradient Flow

3.1 Projected Gradient on the Constraint Manifold

Define the potential

$$ V(\ПҶ, \ОҪ) = (\ПҶ - \ОҪ)^2. $$

The unconstrained gradient is

$$ \nabla V = \bigl( 2(\ПҶ - \ОҪ),\; -2(\ПҶ - \ОҪ) \bigr). $$

The constraint $g(\ПҶ, \ОҪ) = \ПҶ\ОҪ - 1 = 0$ has normal

$$ \nabla g = (\ОҪ, \ПҶ). $$

The projected gradient flow is

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}\ПҶ}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\partial_\ПҶ V + \lambda\ОҪ, \qquad \frac{\mathrm{d}\ОҪ}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\partial_\ОҪ V + \lambda\ПҶ, $$

with $\lambda$ chosen so that the flow remains on the manifold:

$$ 0 = \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}(\ПҶ\ОҪ) = \ОҪ\frac{\mathrm{d}\ПҶ}{\mathrm{d}t} + \ПҶ\frac{\mathrm{d}\ОҪ}{\mathrm{d}t} = 2(\ПҶ - \ОҪ)^2 + \lambda(\ПҶ^2 + \ОҪ^2). $$

Solving for $\lambda$:

$$ \lambda = -\frac{2(\ПҶ - \ОҪ)^2}{\ПҶ^2 + \ОҪ^2}. $$

Substituting back gives the projected flow equations:

$$ \boxed{ \frac{\mathrm{d}\ПҶ}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\frac{2\ПҶ(\ПҶ - \ОҪ)(\ПҶ + \ОҪ)}{\ПҶ^2 + \ОҪ^2}, \qquad \frac{\mathrm{d}\ОҪ}{\mathrm{d}t} = \frac{2\ОҪ(\ПҶ - \ОҪ)(\ПҶ + \ОҪ)}{\ПҶ^2 + \ОҪ^2} } \tag{3.1} $$

3.2 Solution in Framework Coordinates

Using $\ОҪ = 1/\ПҶ$ on the manifold, (3.1) collapses to a single ODE:

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}\ПҶ}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\frac{2\ПҶ(\ПҶ^4 - 1)}{\ПҶ^4 + 1}. \tag{3.2} $$

Introduce the log-coordinate $s = \ln\ПҶ$ (so $\ПҶ = e^s$, $\ОҪ = e^{-s}$). Then

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}s}{\mathrm{d}t} = \frac{1}{\ПҶ}\frac{\mathrm{d}\ПҶ}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\frac{2(e^{4s} - 1)}{e^{4s} + 1} = -2\tanh(2s). \tag{3.3} $$

Equation (3.3) is the central dynamical law of Path D. It is separable:

$$ \int \frac{\mathrm{d}s}{\tanh(2s)} = -2\int\mathrm{d}t. $$

Integrating yields

$$ \ln\bigl|\sinh(2s)\bigr| = -4t + C_0, $$

or equivalently

$$ \sinh(2s) = C_1 e^{-4t}. \tag{3.4} $$

As $t \to \infty$, $s \to 0$ (i.e. $\ПҶ \to 1$, $\ОҪ \to 1$) for any finite initial condition. The equator is a global attractor. [S]
