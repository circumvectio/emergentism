1. The Paradox

Classical local realism says that physical systems carry definite local properties before measurement, and that influences propagate locally through spacetime. Bell's theorem and Bell-test experiments put that conjunction under pressure: local hidden-variable models constrained by Bell inequalities do not reproduce the observed quantum correlations.

The public shorthand, especially after the 2022 Nobel Prize, is that "the universe is not locally real." That phrase is useful only if unpacked carefully. The safer statement is:

Experiments violate Bell inequalities and agree with quantum mechanics, so naive local pre-written-property models fail under the test assumptions.

This is enough for the framework. No stronger physics claim is needed.

3. What This Dissolves

The paradox only arises if D4-style actuality is treated as exhaustive:

  • every relevant property must already be definite;
  • every relation must be mediated by local spacetime influence;
  • measurement merely reveals a pre-existing value.

Quantum foundations do not permit that simple picture. The framework translation is therefore:

Classical assumption Experimental pressure Emergentism translation
Properties are pre-written and local Bell-inequality violations D4-only inventory metaphysics is insufficient.
A photon must be only particle or only wave Single-photon anticorrelation plus interference The particle/wave split is a projection problem, not a substance split.
The path must be a determinate past fact before measurement Delayed-choice results Which receipt exists depends on the measurement context; no naive path-history is forced.

This is an interpretive dissolution, not a new physics derivation.
