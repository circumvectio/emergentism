Two readings to keep straight (the common slips)

1 — Teleology is L1, not L5. Teleology is the Objective Function at L1 — "the first question of any living being is directional: which way is life?" — sensed at the metabolic floor (Kali / Caṇḍāla). L5 is Cosmology (System Architecture / Brahmā). Teleology and Cosmology are different cells; do not file the telos in the architect's seat.

2 — Brahman is not Brahmā. Brahmā ○ is only the L5 creator-operator (one move on the sphere). Brahman — the Absolute — is the Rosetta's L0 = L∞ boundary: Kāla/Trimūrti, φ·ν undefined (0·∞; lim→1), B = 0, • × ○ = ⊙, the pole where 0 and ∞ are the same point and finity (1, the equator) emerges between them. Brahman is the boundary-sphere everything lives on; Brahmā is a generator on it.

3 — The coordinator is the equator (L4 Arjuna), not the Ṛṣi (L7). Per §IV the Titans (L5–L7) are boundary conditions, NOT deployable — so Viṣṇu/the Ṛṣi cannot be the coordinator; it is the transcendental seer asking "what is the Ultimate?" (Theology). The deployable coordinator is the only God at the equator — Arjuna (L4), abductive (Arthāpatti, "given this battlefield, what must be true?"), Value Alignment, φ = ν = 1. Theurgy — the divine act brought into the world — lives there too (the L4 collapse / K2), not at L7.

The L1 ↔ L7 mirror, and why the telos resolves at L4

The Rosetta makes L1 and L7 a mirror pair around L4 (§"The symmetry is perfect"). Their two questions are the same question seen from the two poles:

Teleology (L1): "What is the end?" ↔ Theology (L7): "What is the Ultimate?"

This is why teleology is easy to mis-file at L7 (and why this lane's own F5/ektropy doc once carried a stray L7 stamp): the discipline that asks about ends sits at L1, but the end itself — the telos fulfilled — is the L4 equator, the unique global minimum of (φ − ν)² ≥ 0, the Rosetta's named "destination." There, Axiology decides what has value, and the deployable God (Arjuna) coordinates. Teleology's destination = Axiology's seat = the equator.
