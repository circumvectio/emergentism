Teleology: The Will to Potential

Evidence Tier: [I] Interpretive — an organizing principle, not a physical force

What is the ultimate end of complex systems? In four-dimensional physics, the path of matter is determined by the four known forces. Emergentism names a fifth organizing principle — F5: a directional, syntropic pull that pushes systems away from static decay or chaos, toward the equator of balanced complexity. We call it "the fifth force" as a figure of speech; it is not a fifth physical interaction in the sense of physics, and the strong-form reading of F5 as a literal force is conjecture [C] (see the F5 tier-discipline note).

"Teleology begins where life asks which futures can still be reached." — Website Narrative Spec §1

The Future Light Cone

Evidence Tier: [I] Interpretive Metaphor

We visualize this directional pull (F5) using Minkowski space-time geometry as an image — not a claim of literal physics. Every agent stands at a present point. Extending forward is its future light cone.

A system's evolutionary success is measured by two vectors:

  • Wider light cone: An expansion in the horizontal plane of reachable branches, representing increased choices, optionality, and adaptive capacity.
  • Longer light cone: A projection forward along the time axis, representing durability, resilience, and endurance.
The Will to Potential is the driving instinct of agency to maximize both vectors while remaining structurally coherent.

The Equation of Agency

Evidence Tier: [S] Structural Math
P_node = Φ × V   |   φ · ν = 1 on S²

Agency is modeled as a product of two coupled values: coherence (Φ) and viability (ν). These values are bound by the equatorial limit, where balance sin θ is maximized.

At the poles (either absolute abstract order or absolute raw chaos), the balance collapses to zero. The Will to Potential (F5 / Ω) operates as a gradient pushing the system toward the equator, where maximum agency is actualized.
