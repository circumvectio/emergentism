Teleology: The Will to Potential

Evidence Tier: [I] Interpretive — an organizing principle, not a physical force

What is the end for a finite system that must keep living, choosing, and correcting? Emergentism names F5 as a fifth organizing principle: a framework-internal reading of selection toward viable completion where coupling, feedback, memory, and correction exist. We call it "the fifth force" as a figure of speech; it is not a fifth physical interaction in the sense of physics, and the strong-form reading of F5 as a literal force is conjecture [C] (see the F5 tier-discipline note).

"Teleology begins where life asks which futures can still be reached." — Website Narrative Spec §1

The Future Light Cone

Evidence Tier: [I] Interpretive Metaphor

We visualize this directional pull (F5) using Minkowski space-time geometry as an image — not a claim of literal physics. Every agent stands at a present point. Extending forward is its future light cone.

A system's evolutionary success is measured by two vectors:

  • Wider light cone: An expansion in the horizontal plane of reachable branches, representing increased choices, optionality, and adaptive capacity.
  • Longer light cone: A projection forward along the time axis, representing durability, resilience, and endurance.
The Will to Potential is the driving instinct of agency to maximize both vectors while remaining structurally coherent.

The Equation of Agency

Evidence Tier: [S] Structural Math
P_node = Φ × V   |   φ · ν = 1 on S²

Agency is modeled as a product of two coupled values: coherence / D5 worldline-foresight (Φ) and viability / D4 means-to-act (V). Lowercase ν belongs to the sphere coordinate in φ · ν = 1; finite-node action uses uppercase V. The child with a jet but no pilot-foresight and the pilot without bodily/tool access both show the same zero-factor collapse.

At the poles, balance collapses. F5 / Ω names the selected direction inside the constrained game: raise usable P_node by balancing Φ and V, while preserving η = 0. That is an objective dharma rule given the game, not a universal motor and not permission to sacrifice one node for another.
