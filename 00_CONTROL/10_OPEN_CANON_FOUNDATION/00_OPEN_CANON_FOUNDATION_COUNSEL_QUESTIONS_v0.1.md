# Open Canon Foundation — Questions for Swiss Counsel v0.1

> **HEADER — READ FIRST.** This memo is **preparation for real counsel, not legal advice, and `[D]` staged**. It contains no legal conclusions; every question below is counsel's to answer. Its purpose is to make the first paid consultation maximally concrete: the counsel should be able to answer in the vocabulary of their own statutes, not reconstruct our intent from philosophy. The companion document is the charter draft (`00_OPEN_CANON_FOUNDATION_CHARTER_DRAFT_v0.1.md`). Nothing is filed without counsel's review and the K2's wet signature (THE_SPREAD §5).
>
> **Context for counsel (one paragraph, plain language):** a small group has produced an open-source philosophical/educational corpus (the "canon"), a public audit ledger (the "trial record"), and a teachable practice (a sitting practice, a personal error-ledger practice, a decision heuristic). They wish to place the canon, its trademark, and the funding of ongoing independent audits into a Swiss public-benefit entity. Several commercial companies, separately owned and separately governed, build products informed by the corpus and would pay published license fees to the entity. The entity must never be classifiable as a religious community, must qualify for tax exemption on the education/research track, and must be constitutionally incapable of channeling value to its founders or to the commercial companies.

---

## Q1 · Vehicle choice — Verein vs Stiftung for holding IP and funding audits

1. For an entity whose assets are primarily **intellectual property** (copyrights in a corpus, a trademark) plus a duty to **fund annual independent audits**, which vehicle — Verein (ZGB 60 ff.) or Stiftung (ZGB 80 ff.) — do you recommend, and why?
2. What is the practical minimum endowment for a Stiftung to be taken seriously by the supervisory authority (Stiftungsaufsicht) when its "assets" are mostly IP? Is IP an acceptable founding endowment, and how is it valued?
3. Under a Stiftung, how rigid is the purpose clause in practice? If we later need to adjust purposes (e.g., add "digital infrastructure"), what is the procedure, and what is the risk the supervisory authority imposes its own reading?
4. Under a Verein, what prevents a future member assembly from voting to strip the audit-budget lock or the forkability clause? Can statutory super-majorities or unamendable clauses be hardened under Swiss law, or is the Verein inherently capturable by its membership?
5. Comparative cost and timeline: formation, annual compliance, audit obligations (Revisionsstelle), and supervisory fees for each vehicle at our expected scale (initially small; license revenue later).
6. Can a Stiftung's board be composed entirely of non-founders from day one, with the founder holding only a contractual "signatory for defined acts" role — or does Swiss practice expect founder presence on the board?

## Q2 · Gemeinnützigkeit qualification — education/research track

1. What statutory purpose language reliably secures **tax exemption (Steuerbefreiung)** on the education/research track at both federal and cantonal level? Are there formulations examiners currently favor or disfavor?
2. Our purposes are: (a) steward an open corpus; (b) publish a public audit ledger; (c) fund independent audits of that corpus; (d) teach three defined practices at no required cost. Does each of (a)–(d) individually qualify, and does any of them endanger the qualification of the whole?
3. Is "funding adversarial audits of our own corpus" recognizable as **research** or **education**, or could it be recharacterized as self-dealing / quality control for the founders' commercial interests? How should the independence of the auditors be documented?
4. Does teaching a "sitting practice" (a secular attention practice) risk classification outside education? What is the cleanest statutory framing?
5. Which canton do you recommend for domicile, given differences in exemption practice, supervisory culture, and cost?
6. What annual reporting does the exemption require, and what triggers a review or revocation?

## Q3 · A tax-exempt entity holding the trademark

1. May a tax-exempt Verein/Stiftung hold and defend a **trademark** without endangering its exemption? Are there conditions (marks must serve the public-benefit purpose, defensive use only)?
2. Is license revenue from the trademark treated as **purpose-related income** (exempt) or **commercial income** (taxable)? Where is the line, and how does the fee structure (percentage of licensee revenue vs flat fee) affect the classification?
3. Does percentage-of-revenue licensing to companies create **VAT (MWST)** obligations for the exempt entity? Registration thresholds?
4. Can the entity license the trademark to companies in which a founder holds equity, without the license being treated as a disguised benefit to the founder (see Q4)? What arm's-length documentation is required?
5. If the entity abandons the trademark into the public domain on dissolution (our intent), is that compatible with the dissolution rules for exempt entities, or must IP pass to a public-benefit successor like cash?

## Q4 · The membrane — value flows between the entity and the commercial organs

1. Several separately-owned commercial companies will pay **published license fees** to the entity for the trademark and, possibly, for corporately-branded use of the corpus. What structuring keeps these flows from being recharacterized as **verdeckte Gewinnausschüttung** (disguised profit distribution) or inurement, given that a founder of the entity also holds equity in some licensees?
2. Must the license-fee schedule be published to satisfy the exemption, or is arm's-length benchmarking enough? Does publishing the schedule create any disadvantage?
3. What related-party transaction documentation (intercompany agreements, benchmarking studies, board minutes with conflicted members abstaining) do examiners expect?
4. Are there flows in the other direction that are safe? E.g., may a commercial company make a **donation** to the entity; may the entity **purchase services** (hosting, development) from a commercial organ at market rates?
5. Does a percentage-of-revenue fee that grows with a licensee's success create a "joint venture" appearance that endangers exemption? Is a cap advisable?
6. Transfer-pricing exposure if any licensee is domiciled outside Switzerland — withholding, documentation, treaty issues?

## Q5 · The religious-activity boundary — teaching a practice without reclassification

1. The entity will teach three practices: a sitting/attention practice, a personal error-ledger practice, and a decision heuristic. What statutory and communications language keeps this clearly on the **education** side and away from classification as a religious community (Religionsgemeinschaft / Körperschaft mit kirchlicher Zwecksetzung)?
2. Which words must never appear in the statutes or official materials (e.g., "religion", "faith", "worship", "congregation", "spiritual")? Are there benign-sounding terms that examiners nonetheless read as religious markers?
3. Do any cantons treat teaching a meditation-like practice as per-se religious activity? Does canton of domicile change this risk?
4. If voluntary communities of practitioners later emerge and wish to form their own associations, does any activity of the entity (hosting, listing, endorsing them) create a reclassification risk for the entity itself?
5. Could publishing "the canon" — a philosophical corpus with cosmological vocabulary — be read as scripture if accompanied by taught practices? How do we keep the corpus framed as educational material?
6. Any risk from the entity's internal governance vocabulary (e.g., a constitutional signatory role) being read as ecclesiastical office? Is plain corporate vocabulary (president, secretary, signatory) safer?

## Q6 · Non-inurement mechanics

1. What statutory non-inurement language satisfies the exemption in practice — and what are the common drafting errors that get statutes rejected or exemptions revoked?
2. May board members receive **compensation** for actual work (e.g., audit administration, curriculum production)? What documentation (published rates, time records, board approval with abstention) is required?
3. May the founder be compensated by the entity for defined services (e.g., authoring commissioned educational material) at market rates, or is any founder payment treated as presumptively abusive?
4. How is "reasonable compensation" tested at audit time — benchmarks, caps, or case-by-case?
5. Are expense reimbursements, and benefits like equipment, treated sensitively? What policies should exist from day one?

## Q7 · What must NOT appear in the statutes

1. List the clause types that would endanger the public-benefit track or invite reclassification — e.g.: any member-benefit language; any founder reversion of assets; any commercial purpose language; any religious vocabulary; any entitlement of licensees to governance influence. Please confirm and extend this list.
2. May the statutes commit the entity to a **fixed minimum audit budget** (a percentage of revenue, funded before marketing)? Is a self-imposed spending mandate enforceable, and could it ever be read as a non-charitable obligation?
3. May the statutes require **publication** (of the audit ledger, of license schedules, of an annual public explanation when no audit entry occurs)? Any conflict with privacy or data-protection duties?
4. May the statutes name a **succession mechanism for a signatory role** ("the K2 at the time") defined as a role rather than a named person? How is signature authority (Zeichnungsberechtigung) registered so that the role, not the person, is what the statutes describe?
5. Is an **irrevocability clause** for the non-inurement and dissolution articles possible under Swiss law for each vehicle, and how is it drafted so a future board or assembly cannot amend it away?
6. Anything in our charter draft (`00_OPEN_CANON_FOUNDATION_CHARTER_DRAFT_v0.1.md`, §§3–7) that you would strike or rewrite on sight?

## Q8 · Dissolution and succession

1. What dissolution language preserves the exemption retroactively and avoids claw-back (repayment of past exemptions) if assets pass to a public-benefit successor?
2. Must the successor be Swiss, or may assets pass to a foreign public-benefit entity (e.g., a US 501(c)(3))? Tax consequences?
3. Confirm that pre-published, openly licensed IP (the corpus, the audit ledger) is not a liquidatable asset — that "the canon stays forkable" is automatically true at dissolution because the commons cannot be recalled.

## Q9 · Cross-border questions (if relevant at our scale)

1. If a US or EU commercial organ pays license fees to the Swiss entity — withholding, treaty, and documentation issues?
2. If donors outside Switzerland want deductibility for contributions — is a Swiss-only exemption sufficient, or would parallel structures be needed later?
3. Any benefit (or risk) in registering the trademark internationally from the start, held by the exempt entity?

## Q10 · Engagement logistics

1. Fixed-fee formation packages vs hourly — what do you recommend for a two-vehicle decision (Verein vs Stiftung) plus exemption pre-ruling?
2. Can we obtain a **preliminary ruling / advance confirmation** from the tax authority on the exemption and on the license-flow treatment before formation? Cost and timeline?
3. What documents do you need from us beyond this memo and the charter draft? (Corpus sample, audit-ledger sample, license-fee schedule draft, founder/related-party map, commercial-organ cap tables?)
4. Realistic timeline: vehicle decision → statutes → registration → exemption confirmation.

## What this memo deliberately does not ask

- It does not ask counsel to validate the philosophy. Counsel is asked to validate the **vehicle, the exemption, the membrane, and the boundary** — nothing else.
- It does not ask about securities, tokens, or financial regulation. The commercial organs carry their own counsel; this entity holds IP and audits, and that is all.
- It does not ask counsel to make the entity spread the framework. The entity holds the commons; spreading is the products' job (THE_SPREAD §1).
