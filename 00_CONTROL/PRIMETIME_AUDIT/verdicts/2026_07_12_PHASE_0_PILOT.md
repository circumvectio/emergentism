# Phase 0 Pilot — Three Verdicts, Format Validation

## Why this file

The Phase 0 pilot executes the re-audit method on three foundational
papers to validate the verdict format before scaling. The pilot
produces (a) three verdicts, (b) a methodology reflection, (c) a
recommended pace for Phases 1-13. If the format holds, Phase 1
proceeds with the same schema.

## Methodology validation

The verdict format held across three very different papers
(master scaffold, canonical block, audit registry). Specific
validations:

1. **The verdict schema captures what we need.** η-sign +
   god-moves + demon-moves + bounded action + K2/K3 status +
   Registry compliance. Each section earned its place: I never
   reached for an absent field. *No format gap.*
2. **The three primetime tests are sufficient.** Tiered? Yes,
   visible in every paper. Headline ≤ components? Yes, the
   demoted reading surfaces in two of three. Right surface?
   Yes, all three are internal doctrine docs.
3. **The Registry compliance check is the load-bearing primitive.**
   All three verdicts reduced to "compliant" with minor
   caveats. *The Registry works as an audit lens.*
4. **The bounded-action section is doing real work.** Two of
   three verdicts have "in place, none" — the action is *not*
   "rewrite" or "archive"; it's "leave alone, this is correct."
   The bounded-action field can hold a no-op, which is the
   right schema for primetime-ready papers.

### Format gap (minor)
- The current format does not have a field for **"downstream
  papers that depend on this one"**. Verdicts 1 and 2 both
  *cite* dependents (the seven layers' downstream papers; the
  formula block's downstream consumers). Adding a `dependents:`
  field would help Phase 1+ plan which other papers' verdicts
  this verdict influences.
- Recommended addition: `dependents: [list of papers that cite
  this one]`. *Optional, not required for Phase 0.*

## Recommended pace

Per the pilot, the three papers together took ~15 minutes of
read + write. Adjusted for the *type* of paper:

| Paper type | Time per paper | Tier 1-13 phases |
|---|---|---|
| **Master scaffold** (e.g. Seven-Layer Ontology) | 10-15 min | Phase 1 (Cosmology) |
| **Canonical block / formula** | 5-10 min | Phase 1 (one of them) |
| **Audit / meta** (Registry) | 10-15 min | Phase 10 (Meta) |
| **Sub-00_* paper** (typical) | 3-7 min | Most of Phases 1-13 |
| **Auxiliary** (README, AGENTS, _PLANS) | skip | out of scope |

At ~5 min/paper average × 880 papers = **~73 hours of audit
time**. This is the same estimate the plan proposed. The pilot
validates it.

### Phasing recommendation
- **Phase 0 (this pilot):** 3 verdicts, 15 min, complete.
- **Phase 1 (Cosmology 00_*):** ~25 papers, ~3-5 hours. Heavy
  load — the Burrisphere papers are the master geometry.
- **Phase 2-9 (ologies + support + uplink):** ~70 papers, ~6-10
  hours total.
- **Phase 10 (Meta, excluding Registry):** ~7 papers, ~1 hour.
- **Phase 11 (Tier C, sub-directory papers):** ~300 papers,
  ~25-30 hours. Largest single phase.
- **Phase 12 (Tools/Seed):** ~50 papers, ~3-5 hours.
- **Phase 13 (Public-site, conditional on K2):** ~200 papers,
  ~15-20 hours.

**Total estimate, including pilot:** ~75-80 hours of audit
work, phased over multiple sessions.
