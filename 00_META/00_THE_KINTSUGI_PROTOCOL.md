# The Kintsugi Protocol

> 金継ぎ — "golden joinery." The broken vessel, repaired with gold, is worth more
> than before it broke — because the repair is visible, the history is honest,
> and the seams are the strongest lines in the body.

## 1. Why (what the trial taught)

The 2026-07-10 formal trial of all load-bearing claims returned: **zero claims
won outright, six overclaimed, one refuted — and the refuted one was the
co-author's own compression of the central ethical gate** ("widest measurable
cooperator" — a paraphrase that silently rewrote ΣΔP into an extraction license).
Two lessons, both structural:

1. **Compression is where doctrine rots.** Every core fence held; every live
   smuggle was found in a summary, one-pager, or agent memory. The canon does not
   drift — its *shadows* drift.
2. **Breaks are information.** Each conviction produced something the corpus did
   not have before: a sharper fence, a named discriminator, a machine-greppable
   antibody. The attack *added* value. That gain must become mechanical, not
   incidental.

## 2. The inversion (the Kintsugi move)

The corpus already refuses to hide its history (K3 archive-first, banners,
receipts). Kintsugi completes the refusal by **inverting the shame polarity**:

**A repaired claim outranks an untested one.** A seam is not an apology — it is
a *verification mark*: this line was attacked by a named adversary, broke in a
specific way, and the repair survived re-trial. Pristine surfaces carry no such
evidence. Therefore: **seams are prestige, erasure is the only disgrace.**
Corollary for readers: trust the seamed passage *more*.

## 3. The Golden Seam standard (the visible repair)

Every repair to a load-bearing claim MUST leave a seam — a compact, greppable
block at the repair site:

> **[金] SEAM** · broke: *date + trial/receipt* · crack: *what failed, exactly*
> · gold: *what the repair adds that did not exist before (sharper fence,
> discriminator, antibody)* · credit: *who broke it* · receipt: *link*

Rules: the `[金]` token is mandatory and unique to seams (machine-findable);
the *crack* names the real failure, never a euphemism; the *gold* must be
non-empty — **a repair that adds nothing is not permitted to close a break**;
the *credit* names the breaker, adversary or friend. Silent fixes of
load-bearing text are a protocol violation: repair without a seam = erasure.

## 4. The four gain-mechanisms (what makes it antifragile, not just honest)

1. **The immune registry (antibodies).** Every confirmed break adds a row to the
   Settled Canon Registry's flag columns: the *exact drifted phrase or move*
   that failed, so it can never pass unflagged again. One infection → permanent
   immunity. (Founding antibodies: "widest measurable cooperator", "superior in
   BOTH under ×", "[A]-fact quoted as synthetic work across registers".)
2. **The discriminator queue (optionality).** Every open wager exposed by a
   trial must leave behind its *cheapest settling experiment*, filed in a
   standing queue. Small probes, asymmetric payoff: a failed probe costs little,
   a decisive one settles canon. The queue is the corpus's option book on truth.
3. **The hormesis cycle (scheduled stressors).** Trials are not emergencies —
   they are metabolism. A standing cadence: (a) any NEW load-bearing claim faces
   the vegetable method *before* its K2 countersign (trial-before-sign);
   (b) the full claims-trial re-runs on a fixed rhythm or after any major canon
   event; (c) a compression-audit (below) runs with it. A corpus that is never
   stressed is not robust — it is untested.
4. **Compression conformance (the C5 cure).** Every compression surface
   (blueprints, one-pagers, agent memories, constitutions-in-summary) that
   carries a load-bearing gate MUST cite its canon anchor, and a machine check
   greps all compression surfaces against the antibody list. The co-author's own
   drift becomes the corpus's permanent immune coverage.

## 5. The breakage bounty (inviting the hammer)

The protocol *invites* attack. A standing offer, η=0-clean on both sides: bring
a break that survives adversarial verification, and the corpus pays **in gold**
— your name in the seam, permanently, in canon. The attacker gives real value
(a found crack); the corpus pays in the only currency that appreciates
(credited, visible, load-bearing repair). Enemies become unpaid quality
engineers; skeptics become co-authors of the fences. There is no version of a
successful attack that damages the corpus — that is the definition of the
property we are building.

## 6. The antifragility criterion (the protocol's own kill-test)

After every trial or confirmed break, the following must **strictly increase**:

    verified-tier mass  (claims re-tiered on evidence, either direction)
  + antibody count      (new registry flags)
  + queued discriminators (new cheapest-tests filed)

If a stressor leaves all three unchanged, **the protocol has failed for that
stressor** — either the trial did not bite, or the repair was cosmetic. A break
that produces only a patch (no gold) is robust-not-antifragile and must be
re-opened. This criterion applies to the protocol itself: if Kintsugi ever
becomes ceremony — seams without gold — retire it by its own test.

## 7. Fences (what this is NOT)

- **Not an operator.** The registry's seven-operator scheme is closed (no fifth
  mixed-sign slot). Kintsugi is a *repair discipline* — protocol layer, like the
  BTJ gates — not an eighth operator. The `[I]` resonance is lawful and only
  that: Kālī cuts counterfeit coherence; Kintsugi gilds the truth-scar the cut
  leaves. The pair reads as one metabolic cycle — cut, then gild — but only the
  cut is an operator.
- **Not a license to break things carelessly.** The bounty pays for *confirmed*
  breaks (adversarially verified); noise is not a crack.
- **Not retroactive erasure.** Pre-protocol banners and errata stand as they
  are; they may be *upgraded* to seams (crack/gold/credit made explicit), never
  rewritten away.
- **Subject to itself.** This document is load-bearing; it faces the vegetable
  method like everything else, and wears its future seams.

whose deepest law is that the representation must not usurp the thing.
