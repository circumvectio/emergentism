## Why This Document Exists

If the framework is going to unify the sciences, it must do so without making every science into physics, and without making every higher-level phenomenon dissolve into poetry.

This document states the scientific translation rule:

> **Each D0-D5 level has a nearest public study surface. D6 is the axiomatic limit of public study, not one more positive science.**

That does not mean each science belongs to only one level.
It means each science has a **dominant aperture** through which it studies reality.

The framework's job is not to abolish those sciences.
It is to show how they stack, where they meet, and where they stop explaining one another.

---

## The Core Rule

**D-level studies are sciences of dominant access, not exclusive ownership.**

So:

- a science may span several D-levels
- a D-level may host several sciences
- the framework assigns the level by the science's dominant object, method, and explanatory horizon

`D6` is the exception:

- it hosts no final positive science
- it names the axiomatic boundary where the sciences cease to be exhaustive

The practical goal is:

- cleaner translation across sciences
- less category confusion
- less premature reduction
- a public register for cross-level synthesis

## The Important Clarification

This document does **not** change the core state.

The core state remains:

- D0-D4 structural in derivation
- D5 idealist in lived disclosure, D6 axiomatic in closure

What this document changes is the **public scientific translation**.

In that public register:

- D4 is where the sciences of embodied causal cognition dominate
- D5 is where the sciences of strategic selection, institution, and civilizational sediment dominate
- D6 is not a public science but the axiomatic limit of those sciences

So when the framework says:

> **D5 is selection**

the nearest established sciences are not mystical.
They are:

- game theory
- behavioral economics
- mechanism design
- institutional economics

That is the public bridge.

## Why D5 Matters So Much

D5 is the first level where the framework's moral language becomes formally public.

At D5, the framework can be translated into:

- strategic interaction
- principal-agent problems
- bounded rationality
- repeated games
- public goods
- trust and defection
- mechanism design
- exit, voice, and capture

This is why D5 is the strongest near-term scientific unification surface for:

- ethics
- politics
- economics
- institutional design
- AI alignment

It should also be read asymmetrically:

- from below, D5 is **strongly emergent in genesis**
- from above, D5 becomes **weakly emergent in governance**

So:

- strategic order cannot usually be shortcut from isolated lower-level states
- but once stabilized, strategic order can often be seen and modeled compactly as a higher-order constraint

In other words:

- D4 tells us how a bounded agent operates
- D5 tells us what happens when bounded agents meet, coordinate, defect, institutionalize, sediment into history, and live the present
- D6 is not another positive or experiential layer above this; it is the terminal boundary where the claim of final description is released

## Cross-Level Sciences

Many sciences are inherently cross-level.

Examples:

- **physics** spans D1-D2 and partly D4 when observation enters
- **biology** spans D3-D4 and sometimes D5 when social coordination matters
- **neuroscience** spans D3-D4 and approaches D5 at action selection
- **economics** spans D4-D5 depending on whether it studies incentives, institutions, or civilizational order
- **comparative religion** sits at the upper edge of D5 and may point apophatically beyond it without becoming a positive D6 science

So the framework should never say:

> "This science is only Dn."

It should say:

> "This science is primarily Dn-facing, while borrowing constraints from below and consequences from above."

## The Translation Rule

When translating the framework into public science:

1. name the nearest established science first
2. state the D-level as the framework's integrative register
3. mark any stronger unification claim as theory

For example:

- do not say "D5 proves morality"
- say "D5 is the strategic-relational level where ethical structure becomes legible through game theory, behavioral economics, and mechanism design"

Likewise:

- do not say "biology already proves Teleological Force"
- say "biology studies several D3-D4 appearances that the framework proposes to unify"

## What Scientific Unification Means Here

The framework does not unify the sciences by replacing them with one vocabulary.

It unifies them by giving:

- a layered dependency order
- a bridge discipline between levels
- a non-reductive translation surface
- a way to state when one science cannot exhaust another

This means:

- mathematics is not abolished by physics
- physics is not abolished by biology
- biology is not abolished by neuroscience
- neuroscience is not abolished by game theory
- game theory is not abolished by phenomenology

Each level retains its own irreducible object.
The framework supplies the bridge.
