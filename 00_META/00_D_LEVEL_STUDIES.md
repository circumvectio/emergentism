## Why This Document Exists

If the framework is going to translate across the sciences, it must do so
without making every science into physics and without making every higher-level
phenomenon dissolve into poetry. The D-level stack is a selected lens for that
translation, not a demonstrated unique decomposition of nature.

This document states the scientific translation rule:

> **Each D0-D5 level has a nearest public study surface. D6 is the axiomatic limit of public study, not one more positive science.**

That does not mean each science belongs to only one level.
It means each science has a **dominant aperture** through which it studies reality.

The framework's job is not to abolish those sciences. It proposes a way to read
how their objects stack, where their explanations meet, and where a reduction is
still missing. A missing reduction is recorded as `currently_unreduced`; it is
not evidence that reduction is impossible.

---

## The Core Rule

**D-level studies are sciences of dominant access, not exclusive ownership.**

So:

- a science may span several D-levels
- a D-level may host several sciences
- the framework assigns the level by the science's dominant object, method, and explanatory horizon

`D6` is the exception:

- it hosts no final positive science
- it names the axiomatic boundary where the sciences cease to be exhaustive

The practical goal is:

- cleaner translation across sciences
- less category confusion
- less premature reduction
- a public register for cross-level synthesis

## The Important Clarification

This document does **not** change the core state.

The modality is fixed across the corpus:

- D3 is probability-bearing quantum-state content/model that generates
  probabilities only relative to a measurement context; the actual preparation
  or assignment act belongs to D4, and the state is not one classical distribution
- D4 is causal actuality: embodied tokens, means, performed actions, interactions,
  institutions, historical events, and their records
- D5 is counterfactual possibility: modeled futures, options, and rankings; an
  actual model token is D4 while the possible content it represents is D5
- D6 is an interpretive axiomatic boundary, not another positive freedom

What this document changes is the **public scientific translation**.

In that public register:

- D4 is where sciences observe embodied cognition, interaction, institutions,
  history, and records as actual carriers
- D5 is the selected register for the counterfactual contents, option fields,
  and rankings reconstructed from or tested through those D4 carriers
- D6 is not a public science but the axiomatic limit of those sciences

So when the framework says:

> **D5 is the counterfactual option-and-ranking register**

the nearest established sciences are not mystical.
They are:

- game theory
- behavioral economics
- mechanism design
- institutional economics

That is the public bridge.

## Why D5 Matters So Much

D5 is the register in which alternatives relevant to the framework's declared
moral predicates can be compared. The comparison does not derive the predicate,
and its actual representation, enactment, and receipt remain D4.

At D5, the framework can be translated into:

- strategic interaction
- principal-agent problems
- bounded rationality
- repeated games
- public goods
- trust and defection
- mechanism design
- exit, voice, and capture

This is why D5 is a useful selected cross-disciplinary lens for:

- ethics
- politics
- economics
- institutional design
- AI alignment

The D4-to-D5 crossing is a candidate emergence crossing with reduction status
`currently_unreduced`. Present methods may fail to reconstruct a finite agent's
full counterfactual option field from lower-register variables, while a compact
option model may still improve prediction or control. Neither observation proves
irreducibility. A successful lower-level reduction would reclassify the crossing
as weak emergence without destroying the scaffold.

In other words:

- D4 records how bounded agents operate, meet, coordinate, defect,
  institutionalize, and sediment into history
- D5 names the possible moves, modeled futures, and rankings that those actual
  D4 carriers can represent and use
- D6 is not another positive or experiential layer above this; it is the terminal boundary where the claim of final description is released

## Cross-Level Sciences

Many sciences are inherently cross-level.

Examples:

- **physics** spans D1-D4: mathematical distinctions and configurations at
  D1-D2, quantum-state assignments at D3, and actual preparations, interactions,
  outcomes, and records at D4
- **biology** studies actual D4 bodies and histories through D1-D3 mathematical
  and physical models; it uses D5 when it models alternative policies or
  reachable futures
- **neuroscience** studies actual D4 bodies, signals, and records through D1-D3
  models and approaches D5 when it reconstructs represented options or rankings;
  the neural representation token itself remains D4
- **economics** observes D4 choices, institutions, and records while using D5
  models for incentives, counterfactual policies, and expected outcomes
- **comparative religion** studies D4 texts, practices, institutions, and histories;
  their represented possibilities may be read through D5 without becoming D6

So the framework should never say:

> "This science is only Dn."

It should say:

> "This science is primarily Dn-facing, while borrowing constraints from below and consequences from above."

## The Translation Rule

When translating the framework into public science:

1. name the nearest established science first
2. state the D-level as the framework's integrative register
3. mark any stronger cross-domain identification as interpretation or conjecture

For example:

- do not say "D5 proves morality"
- say "D5 is the possibility-and-ranking register through which strategic
  sciences compare alternatives; actual choices and receipts remain D4"

Likewise:

- do not say "biology is D3" or "biology proves Teleological Force"
- say "biology studies D4 bodies and histories using lower-register models;
  Emergentism's cross-level reading remains a selected translation lens"

## What Cross-Science Compression Means Here

The framework does not claim to have unified the sciences or to replace them
with one vocabulary.

It offers a selected compression with:

- a layered dependency order
- a bridge discipline between levels
- a non-reductive translation surface
- a way to mark where one science has not yet reduced another

This means:

- mathematics is not abolished by physics
- physics is not abolished by biology
- biology is not abolished by neuroscience
- neuroscience is not abolished by game theory
- game theory is not abolished by phenomenology

Each level is assigned a distinct object inside the scaffold. Where no reduction
law is known, the honest status is `currently_unreduced`, never "irreducible by
proof." The framework supplies a bridge hypothesis and a test discipline.
