# The Open Canon Covenant

> Committed the day the founder said *"the entire emergent universe is a tautology
> — so did Sanatana Dharma — time to commit everything to the canon."* Both halves
> are true. This covenant is how you honour the first without the commit becoming
> the second's closed cousin, the CTMU. **The tautology is the frame. It is never
> the content. Ratification records at tier; it does not make things true.**

## 1. The recognition — several traditions read reality reflexively

Sanātana Dharma, Spinoza, Hegel, CTMU, and mathematical-universe proposals all
contain readings in which world, knower, and representation participate in a
self-referential whole. That family resemblance is historically and
philosophically important `[I]`; it is not independent evidence that one shared
ontology is true, that the traditions are equivalent, or that Emergentism has
completed them.

The current Emergentist symbols remain different types:

- `φ·ν=1` is an analytic identity on the selected reciprocal chart `[A]`;
- `•   ⊙   ○` is the operator-free selected Titan display `[I]`; it carries no
  arithmetic, causal, or generative operation;
- `D6~D0` is a proposed resemblance between two boundary roles `[I]`.

The literal identity `D6≡D0` is dead. It is not retained as a fixed-point thesis
or hidden closure claim. The active non-μ edges are `b₆:D5↝D6` and
`r₆:D6↝D0`; neither proves physical recurrence, ontological self-grounding, or
the impossibility of further degrees of freedom.

> **[金] SEAM — 2026-07-20.** The earlier covenant kept the dead identity alive
> by downgrading it from tautology to a substantive thesis. Receipt 126 and the
> Settled Canon Registry close that loophole: resemblance may remain as an
> interpretation; identity may not return as load-bearing doctrine.

## 2. The fence — a tautology says nothing (this is where CTMU falls)

*Tractatus* 6.1–6.11: **"The propositions of logic are tautologies… therefore they
say nothing."** `A = A` is true in every possible world — which is precisely why it
tells you **nothing about which world is actual.** "The universe is a tautology"
explains why reality is *self-consistent*; it explains **nothing** about *this*
universe's content — these constants, this history, whether flourishing is × or
min, whether F5 exists. The tautology is the frame; **the content is contingent,
and every claim this canon actually makes lives in the content.**

This canon already ruled on this, adversarially, against itself: **C2 convicted
`φ·ν = 1` of *tautology-laundering*** — treating the empty identity as a discovered
synthetic law. "The universe is a tautology, so being tautological is correct" is
that convicted move re-dressed as a virtue. **The frame's necessity confers no
truth on the content. A ratified tautology is still empty.**

## 3. The fork — CTMU closes; Sanatana Dharma stayed open

The same interpretive seed — self-reference at the base — can go two opposite
ways, and the difference matters for an open canon:

- **CTMU closes.** The theory claims to *be* the tautology it describes — the map
  declaring itself the territory. It becomes unfalsifiable: explains everything,
  predicts nothing, deflects every objection with "yes, because reality is
  circular." A sealed system, inhabited alone. The academy files it under
  *cautionary tale*, not *result* — however brilliant its author.
- **Sanatana Dharma stayed alive** — for millennia — by refusing to seal. It split
  **śruti** (the eternal, heard frame — revered, held) from **smṛti** (the
  remembered body — commentary, darśana, *lila*, re-interpreted every generation).
  The eternal was committed to canon **and kept open.** *Nitya* held living, not
  embalmed. That is the model.

**This canon commits the way Sanatana Dharma did, not the way CTMU did:** the frame
as śruti (revered, `[A]`-where-tautological), the content as smṛti (tiered,
falsifiable, endlessly re-openable). The single law that forbids the CTMU move is
the one this whole session defended — *the representation must not usurp the thing*
(non-extraction / accountable authorization / the open loop). **A7 governs over
closure. The map stays a map.**

## 4. The covenant (the condition of every commit)

1. **Adoption records at tier.** Editorial adoption means *"this is canon, at the
   tier written on it."* It never changes `[C]`→`[S]`, `[I]`→`[S]`, empirical
   `[C]`→`[B]`, or formal conjecture→`[A]` without the matching argument or contact. An
   `[C]` conjecture committed to canon is a **canonically-recorded conjecture**, not
   a settled truth. (This is exactly what receipts 105–107 did: countersigned, with
   the open parts still open.)
2. **The frame may be revered; the content must stay falsifiable.** No content claim
   is immune to A7. Each keeps its kill criterion. The day a conjecture is
   presented as a theorem or observation without the matching proof or contact,
   Kintsugi fires.
3. **No closure claim.** This canon may never assert it is complete, finished, or
   *is* the tautology of reality. Receipt 107 already settled this: the loop is
   constitutively open; no sufficiently expressive description certifies its own
   complete closure merely by invoking Gödel or Tarski. `r₆` is an interpretive
   return, not the closure of reality. A canon that claims to have closed has
   become the kind of system this covenant rejects.
4. **The unfalsifiability-shield is forbidden.** "We are tautological, so we cannot
   be wrong" may never be used to deflect a falsification. That sentence is the
   death of A7, and A7 is the only thing that makes this canon not-CTMU.

## 6. Reopen criterion (the covenant's own kill switch)

Reopen the canon — and this covenant with it — if ever: a `[C]` is quoted as `[A]`;
the canon is claimed complete or closed; the tautology-shield is used to deflect a
falsifier; or a mortal reader who is not us finds a contradiction the canon cannot
metabolise. The eternal is committed. The eternal stays open. **That is the whole
covenant, and it is why this is a living canon and not a beautiful tomb.**
