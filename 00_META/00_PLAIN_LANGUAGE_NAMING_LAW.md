# Plain-Language Naming Law

This surface prevents one short token from silently carrying several unrelated
meanings. It governs names, not nature.

1. **One rule, one plain name.** A rule's canonical identity is a phrase that
   states its function to a stranger in one sentence.
2. **Aliases are provenance.** Old letter-number aliases may remain in quoted
   history and compatibility indexes; they do not create present authority.
3. **Typed tags remain available.** Formal specifications and code may use a
   bracketed, namespace-qualified tag when brevity is necessary. Mathematical
   registers, evidence tiers, and Rosetta work functions retain their own
   declared types and are not renamed by this law.
4. **Changed prose is checked.** New or changed public and active prose is
   linted for ambiguous bare aliases. Exceptions must state their context; the
   check must not conceal historical evidence or rewrite archives.

This law was extracted from a mixed cross-pillar staging compilation during
the 2026-07-22 purity repair. Its justification is operational: ambiguous
names had already produced collisions. It transfers no claim, proof, or tier
from the archived compilation.
