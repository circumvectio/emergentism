## 0 · The Demon caught first — and it was this task

Protocol 54 §4 names the corpus's single pathology: **mirror-first patching** —
editing a summary so it *reads* healthy while the source stays broken. Its
re-dating test:

> *"Would 'fixing' this mean changing a date or a checkmark rather than a
> source? If so, stop — re-dating a summary over a hollow source **is** the
> Demon move."*

**The ledger is a mirror.** Its sources are the rule definitions scattered
across six namespaces. To "refine the ledger" — sharpen its prose, add lens
vocabulary, re-rank its tables — while `03A` still carries a retracted slogan
and two canonical rulings still contradict, is **precisely** the named
pathology, committed in the organ built to detect it.

So this pass does not refine the ledger. It locates false-greens, names the
error in the framework's own grammar, and hands the lawful moves upward.

## 3 · What the lens actually says about the collision

### 3.1 · CC-CORE-1 names the error type exactly `[S]`

The corpus's convicted cardinal sin is **register-collapse**: citing the *seam*
(a chart identity, true by construction, empty of world) as the *node* (a
binding law about the world).

The K-namespace commits that exact error in the naming layer:

| symbol | register | what it is |
|---|---|---|

A slot index and a refusal are not the same kind of thing, and **one letter
forces the reader to perform the laundering automatically.** This is not an
analogy to CC-CORE-1. It is the same error, one layer down.

### 3.2 · The notation defeats tier discipline `[S]`

Same letter, four tiers: a slot index is `[S]` definitional · a refusal is `[S]`

**Liebig ranks K3 above K2.** The ledger's ordering optimised for magnitude

is a correction to this writer's work, not to the corpus's.

`L(x) = x/(1−x)`. As senses-per-symbol approach the symbol's carrying capacity,
