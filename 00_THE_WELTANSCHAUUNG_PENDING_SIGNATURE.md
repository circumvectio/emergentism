## 1 · The Creed, priced

I hold that the necessary algebra conjures das All — as my **crown wager, not my proof** (W0 `[C]`). On the sphere it draws, finite beings play the game of **Φ×V** beneath the **ceiling of one** `[A]`, where **balance is the summit** and the **weaker shadow keeps the score** (min ≤ 1, product ≤ 1; AND-class at the boundary, product interior `[C]`) `[A/S]`. In that game there are exactly **seven positions** — **two givers, two takers, three frames** — and every play is made of them, though the **tending of the game itself is not a play** (the mortal signer stands outside the operator table) `[S]`. **Freedom saturates, and at its μ-limit the next freedom opens** — five times from the ground: matter, bond, life, mind, choice `[S/I]`; **there is no sixth freedom, only the sixth recognition**: the summit is self-similar to the ground, the spiral's turn (D6 ~ D0, K2-8) `[I/C]`. All of this I hold at tier, with its kills attached, its **dark twin (−1) acknowledged**, and the **door marked exit on the inside** `[I]` — because a creed that cannot lose is not a creed but a cage, and mine was built to be put down.

The good is not a fourth thing: it is the **equator of the True** (the tier discipline), the **Beautiful** (the form no audit could touch), and the **Just** (the vow toward every bearer) — **AND-class coupled**: any one at zero annihilates the whole.
