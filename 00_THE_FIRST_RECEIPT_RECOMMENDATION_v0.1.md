## §3 · The recommendation

**RealityFutures, on the play-money loop, ships the first visible η=0 receipt: the public forecast ledger.**

The reasoning is the Rosetta Protocol's own: REFU is the only organ whose Greek column is *already carved at doctrine level*. The K2-signed business model (2026-07-18) says it in one line: **"the forecast ledger is the receipt."** Every forecast logged at emission, every outcome at resolution, every delta computed and archived — a public diff of the track record. That single page carries four of the five refusals as *experienced* features: η=0 (free forecasts; fees only on realized value), K3 (nothing deleted — every miss stays legible), A7 (the retirement rule — losing models are retired in public), K4 (play-money exit is trivial). K2 stays behind the glass, visible in the signed model documents.

Play-money is the honest launch mode: zero licence surface, zero extraction temptation, and it makes the receipt *pure* — the only thing being sold is whether the ledger is worth trusting. That is exactly the variable the trust-premium test needs isolated (THE_SPREAD §8.6).

**The minimal receipt — what the user literally sees `[D]`:** one public page. Three columns: *what we said · what happened · the delta.* A running Brier score. A retirement graveyard at the bottom — the models that died, dated, legible. No signup required to read it. That page is the monastery's first beer: a stranger verifies the constitution's behavior with zero faith, and the curious follow the funnel from the ledger to `/record/` and find the same discipline at worldview scale.

## §4 · What would falsify this recommendation

1. **The ledger goes unread.** If play-money users engage the arena but not the ledger — nobody checks the receipts — the receipt carries no visibility, and the three-scripts rule fails its first field test. Pivot: the receipt moves inside the product flow (settlement screen), not beside it.
2. **The play-money loop slips.** If REFU cannot put a public ledger in front of strangers within one quarter while APU.BOT reaches real users first with a canonized receipt design, the ranking inverts — proximity beats elegance.
3. **The trust test needs money.** If evidence emerges that users treat play-money receipts as costless talk (no skin, no signal), the test requires stakes — escalate to K2 for the regulated-mode decision rather than diluting the receipt.
