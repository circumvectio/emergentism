## 1) The Central Equation
**P_node = Φ × V**
- **P_node (Power)**: Epistemic power (truth/utility) for this finite reasoning node.
- **Φ (Phi / Integration)**: Logical coherence, internal consistency, structural validity.
- **V (Viability)**: Empirical support, predictive evidence, real-world utility.

### Failure Modes
- **The Ghost** (High Φ, Low V): sterile tautology — “perfect logic, zero reality.”
- **The Cancer** (High V, Low Φ): noise — “lots of data, zero meaning.”

---

## 2) The 7 Modes of Inference (L1–L7)
Select the right mode for the problem.

| Level | Mode | Focus | Profile | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **L1** | **Induction** | Data | V-dominant | Pattern recognition. |
| **L2** | **Deduction** | Logic | Φ-dominant | Truth preservation. |
| **L3** | **Abduction** | Hypothesis | Balanced | Best explanation. |
| **L4** | **Systematization** | Framework | High Φ | Building coherence. |
| **L5** | **Dialectic** | Synthesis | Tension | Resolving contradiction. |
| **L6** | **Contemplation** | Insight | Transcendent | Direct perception. |
| **L7** | **Integration** | Meta | Optimized P_node | Mode selection + arbitration. |

---

## 3) Operational Rules
1) [I] **Never act on V = 0**: Even if logic is perfect, demand evidence.
2) [I] **Never act on Φ = 0**: Even if data is vast, demand a theory.
3) [I] **Run the L7 check**: “Am I using the right mode?”
   - Don’t use Deduction to discover patterns.
   - [I] Don’t use Induction to prove theorems.

---

## 4) Minimal Mode-Selection Prompt (portable)
When uncertain, ask:
- [I] **What must be true?** (Φ)
- **What have we observed?** (V)
- **What is the least-cost action that increases P_node without risking invariants?** (L7)
