## 0. The Core Tension

K4 says: **Grace Exit means total export. The organism retains nothing.**

But a Business Account is:
- A governed entity with counterparties
- A source of receipts that prove economic reality
- A node in the NEXUS graph with uplinks, downlinks, and connections
- A participant in disputes, obligations, and credit relationships

If K4 is applied naively to a Business Account, the result is:
- Counterparties lose the ability to verify past transactions
- The graph loses historical topology, making connectivity metrics unreliable
- Disputes become unresolvable because evidence was erased
- Credit history evaporates, harming the broader network

This document resolves that tension. K4 remains absolute for **private data**. But **receipted truth** and **graph topology** are not "retained by the organism" in the extractive sense. They are **public consequences of voluntary actions** that belong to the network, not to the departing entity.

## 1. The Two Domains: Private vs. Public

### Domain A: Private (K4 Applies Fully)

| Asset | Fate on Grace Exit | Rationale |
|-------|--------------------|-----------|
| Private keys | Exported to user-controlled vault | K2 sovereignty |
| Biometric templates | Exported, then organism copies destroyed | D1–D3 privacy |
| Unpublished strategies | Exported to user | Intellectual property of the entity |
| Internal deliberations | Exported to user | DAC council privacy |
| Treasury signing rights | Revoked from organism infrastructure | No custodial continuation |
| Agent configurations | Exported to user | Personal/custom organizational tooling |
| Unsent drafts, private memos | Exported to user | Pre-publication privacy |

**Rule:** Everything that is genuinely private to the entity is exported and organism copies are destroyed.

### Domain B: Public Consequence (K4 Does Not Erase)

| Record | Fate on Grace Exit | Rationale |
|--------|--------------------|-----------|
| OFN receipts | **Remain in the DAG / Arweave** | Receipts are proof of voluntary transactions involving counterparties. They are not the organism's property to destroy. |
| Settlement transactions on Layer 1 | **Immutable on-chain** | L1 truth is independent of any DAC. The organism cannot and does not control L1. |
| NEXUS graph topology (historical) | **Retained as historical graph** | Links were voluntarily created by mutual consent. Erasing them would violate the consent of the other party. |
| Published Vision / Mission / Objectives | **Remain in public Cortex** | These were published as public commitments. They are not private data. |
| AXIOM resolutions involving the DAC | **Immutable** | Dispute resolutions are settled truth. |
| P-score and connectivity history | **Retained as historical record** | `c(DAC)` was computed from public graph data. It is a network property, not a private file. |

**Rule:** Grace Exit does not rewrite history. It removes the entity's *ongoing participation* and *private data*. It does not remove the *public consequences* of its past actions.

## 2. The Receipt Exception: Why K4 Cannot Erase Counterparty Truth

K4 is often misread as "the organism deletes everything." But that reading creates a moral hazard:

- A fraudulent DAC could Grace Exit to destroy evidence of fraud
- A DAC in dispute could erase the receipts needed to resolve it
- Counterparties who relied on published statements would be left with no verification path

**The correct reading:**
> K4 guarantees that the organism does not **hold** your private data against your will. It does not guarantee that the organism will **forge history** on your behalf.

Receipts are not "your data" in the sense of private property. They are **mutual attestation events** between you and a counterparty. The counterparty has an independent right to the proof. The DAG/Arweave has an independent right to immutability. K4 respects both.

---

## 3. The Graph Topology Exception: Why Links Persist

When DAC-A connects to DAC-B, the link is a **bilateral Nostr event** signed by both parties. It exists on relays independent of either DAC's infrastructure.

Grace Exit by DAC-A cannot legitimately destroy DAC-B's copy of the connection event, nor can it erase the relay's record. To do so would require:
- Violating DAC-B's sovereignty
- Censoring relays outside Skyzai's control
- Rewriting a graph that is maintained by distributed consensus

**Therefore:**
- The graph retains **historical topology** (DAC-A was connected to DAC-B from T1 to T2)
- `c(DAC-A)` drops to zero post-exit because there are no *active* links
- But the **historical connectivity record** remains for dispute resolution, credit retrospectives, and network learning

This is not the organism "retaining data." It is the **network retaining its own structure**.

## 4. What Happens to Active Obligations?

Grace Exit is not bankruptcy. It is not liquidation. It is **withdrawal**.

### Obligations at the moment of exit:

| Obligation Type | Treatment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| **Pending payment to counterparties** | Must be settled before Grace Exit is finalized. The exit process includes a 7-day settlement window where open invoices are paid or held under Algorithmic TRO. |
| **Pending payment from counterparties** | The departing DAC's treasury rights are exported. Future incoming payments route to the user's exported wallet, not to the organism. |
| **Disputes in progress** | Grace Exit does not abort disputes. AXIOM resolutions continue using immutable receipts. If the DAC is found liable, the resolution is enforced against exported treasury assets via pre-committed bond. |
| **Credit positions (borrowed SKY)** | Must be repaid or collateralized before exit. Grace Exit is blocked if x > 0 and no repayment plan is bonded. |
| **Service subscriptions / SLAs** | Active SLAs are terminated. Refunds or proration are handled per the Fee Doctrine (`32_FEE_DOCTRINE.md`). |
| **Employment / contractor relationships** | These are internal to the DAC and are exported as part of the entity's governance records. |

### The Settlement Window

Grace Exit for a Business Account is not instant. It requires:

1. **Export request** (K2-signed)
2. **Obligation scan** (automated reconciliation of pending payments, disputes, credit)
3. **Settlement window** (7 days minimum for counterparties to submit claims)
4. **Bond posting** (if unresolved disputes or credit remain, a bond is locked)
5. **Final export** (private data released; organism copies destroyed)
6. **Exit receipt** (a public OFN receipt certifying that the exit process completed lawfully)

The exit receipt is itself a public document. It proves that the DAC did not evaporate mid-obligation.

## 6. Re-Entry: From Exit to New Entity

A person who Grace Exits a Business Account may later choose to form a new one.

**What transfers:**
- NEXUS identity continuity
- Personal graph relationships (can be re-linked)
- Exported private data (re-imported under new entity)

**What does not transfer:**
- Historical `c(DAC)` score (new entity starts fresh)
- Historical credit history (new entity must earn its own structural proof)
- Dispute resolution consequences (if a bond was posted, it resolves against the old entity)

This preserves both **continuity of the person** and **accountability of the entity**.

## 7. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Damage | Defense |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Instant Grace Exit for Business Accounts** | Counterparties left unpaid; disputes unresolvable | Mandate 7-day settlement window + obligation scan |
| **Grace Exit erases receipts** | Destroys counterparty proof; enables fraud | Receipts are immutable by architecture (DAG/Arweave/L1) |
| **Grace Exit severs graph history** | Makes network learning impossible; violates counterparty consent | Graph retains historical topology; only active links sever |
| **Organism retains private data "for compliance"** | Violates K4; creates extraction risk | Private data is cryptographically destroyed, not archived |
| **Re-entry inherits old credit score** | Wipes accountability; encourages shell gaming | New entity starts with zero `c(DAC)` |
