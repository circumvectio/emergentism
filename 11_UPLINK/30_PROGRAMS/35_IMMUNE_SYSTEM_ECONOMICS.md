## 0. Purpose

The contagion flywheel punishes bad graph hygiene. But punishment is a dangerous power. If the organism profits from penalties, it gains an incentive to manufacture enemies. This doctrine removes that incentive structurally.

---

## 1. The One-Sentence Law

**All graph penalties must be destroyed, redistributed to victims, or reinvested into immune infrastructure. No penalty may be captured as revenue.**

---

## 2. The Three Penalty Destinations

| Destination | Mechanism | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Burn** | Penalty is sent to an unspendable null address or contract | Slashed SKY is burned, reducing supply |
| **Redistribute** | Penalty is split among graph neighbors who were directly harmed | Fraudulent DAC's locked collateral distributed to counterparties with failed receipts |
| **Reinvest** | Penalty funds public goods: immune-system probes, audit bounties, DAG attestation subsidies | Slashed stake funds cross-report variance probing or triangulation testing |

### What is NOT a valid destination

- **Treasury capture** — penalty enriches the DAC that issued the judgment
- **Validator reward** — penalty pays those who detected the fraud (this creates bounty-hunting distortions)
- **Protocol revenue** — penalty treated as operating income

These are η > 0 by definition.

---

## 3. The Penalty Design Rules

### Rule 1: Proportionality
Penalty must not exceed the demonstrated harm.

```
penalty ≤ verified_harm + system_maintenance_cost
```

### Rule 2: Transparency
Every penalty must be receipted and explainable:
- What rule was broken
- What evidence proved the breakage
- Which neighbors were harmed
- Where the penalty went (burn/redistribute/reinvest)

### Rule 3: Gradation
Penalties escalate with repetition, not severity of first offense:
- First detected lie: warning + small c(DAC) reduction
- Second lie: temporary tool suspension
- Third lie: larger penalty + quarantine
- Sustained malicious pattern: full penalty + long recovery window

This preserves syntropy (room for correction) while protecting the graph.

### Rule 4: No Central Judge
Wherever possible, penalties are triggered by:
- Automated triangulation (triangle inequality, reciprocal inconsistency)
- Multi-party attestation (not one validator's word)
- Pre-published invariants (the rule was known before the act)

Human or AI judgment may *flag*; structural proof must *trigger*.

**Harm:** Routing inefficiency, possible eclipse attack

**Destination:** Reinvest into probe infrastructure

**Harm:** Direct financial harm to counterparties, trust erosion  
**Penalty:** Larger financial penalty from locked collateral  
**Destination:** Redistribute to harmed counterparties first; burn remainder  
**Receipt:** Audit trail showing fabricated vs. real events

### Case: Repeated K2 bypass attempt
**Harm:** Sovereignty violation, systemic trust erosion  
**Penalty:** Severe c(DAC) reduction + tool suspension + mandatory governance review  
**Destination:** Burn (no victim to redistribute to; harm is systemic)  
**Receipt:** Signed evidence of unauthorized execution attempt

Before any penalty mechanism is approved:

```
1. Does the protocol/operator profit from the penalty? [NO]
2. Can the penalty be gamed to enrich a clique? [NO]
3. Is the destination transparent and pre-committed? [YES]
4. Is the penalty triggered by structural proof, not opinion? [YES]
5. Does the penalty leave room for honest recovery? [YES]
```

Any NO means the mechanism is rejected.
