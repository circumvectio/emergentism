## Summary

Verdict: **cut all strong theoretical promotion from this packet.** The empirical result supports only a narrow claim: in one small ultimatum-game dataset, a fairness/coherence proxy improves prediction over material payoff alone, while the product-only multiplicative proxy does not.

The packet does **not** establish the full Extraction Law, self-termination at scale, justice as a universal gate, biological generality, the Brahmā limit, or any titan/composition metaphysics.

## Claims To Cut

- "Extraction self-terminates at scale" as an empirical conclusion. The dataset tests acceptance behavior in `n = 93` annotated games, not long-run substrate collapse.
- "The gate is justice" as proven. The data supports a fairness predictor, not justice as a general evolutionary or metaphysical gate.
- "Nature installs eta = 0" or "ungated lineages died." Not tested by the packet.
- Squid/cannibalism claims as empirical support for the verdict. The packet does not test biology.
- Product-only or `phi * nu` style behavioral prediction. The packet directly rejects the lone multiplicative proxy.
- "Sigma-delta-P across the widest boundary" as empirically validated. The packet has local behavioral proxies, not widest-boundary power accounting.
- "The pair enters the Brahmā frame" as empirically supported. Mutualism convergence is untested.
- Titan Composition Law relevance to this empirical packet. Its algebra may stand internally, but it has no evidentiary support from the ultimatum-game result.
- Any canon-level status. All three theory docs are `[D]` / K2-staged; the verdict is `[B]` local result plus `[D]` interpretation.

## Claims That Survive

- Payoff-only reduction fails in this dataset.
- A fairness/coherence variable adds predictive value over material payoff alone by log loss.
- The best log-loss model is `payoff_plus_fairness`.
- `fairness_only` is also strong and very close, so fairness appears load-bearing.
- Multiplicative single-score compression is not supported here.
- "Extraction is always forbidden" remains unsupported; the packet does not test moral absolutism.
