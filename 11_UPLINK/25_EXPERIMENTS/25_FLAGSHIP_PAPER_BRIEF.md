# FLAGSHIP PAPER BRIEF

**Evidence tier:** [I]  
*Organism document. Interpretive operational content. Bounded by current system state.*

**Rosetta boundary:** [I/D] This brief defines a candidate public-paper path. It does not [B] prove the multiplicative-collapse claim, establish a preregistered study, or replace future replication evidence.

## Collapse Is Multiplicative, Not Additive

**Canonical path:** `25_EXPERIMENTS/25_FLAGSHIP_PAPER_BRIEF.md`

### Working subtitle
A testable structural claim about failure, fragility, and weak-link dependence in human and institutional systems

---

## Purpose

This brief defines the first serious public paper path for the framework.
It does not attempt to defend the whole worldview.
It isolates one bounded empirical claim that can be tested, preregistered, replicated, and publicly argued without requiring full assent to the core state.

---

## Core Thesis

> Systems often fail not because the total sum becomes too small, but because one essential relation collapses.

In bounded complex systems, multiplicative or weak-link-sensitive models should outperform additive score models in identifying fragility and predicting breakdown.

---

## Why this paper, not a larger one

The framework does not need a bigger corpus first. It needs one clean public fact.

This paper is designed to be:
- narrow,
- falsifiable,
- replicable,
- low-metaphysics,
- and meaningful outside the framework’s own vocabulary.

---

## The Claim

### Primary hypothesis
Multiplicative or weak-link-sensitive models outperform additive models in explaining or predicting collapse-prone outcomes in complex coordinated systems.

### Secondary hypothesis
Additive models systematically miss fragile-success cases: systems that appear healthy in aggregate but are actually near failure because one critical dimension is collapsing.

---

## Model Comparison

Compare at least:

1. additive baseline
2. weighted additive baseline
3. multiplicative / geometric model
4. one nonlinear competitor (for example power-law or sigmoidal fit)

The framework should not win by strawman. It must win against plausible alternatives.

---

## Candidate Domains

Preferred early domains are those with hard outcomes rather than self-report dependence.

Strong candidates:
- institutional failure cases
- governance/protocol collapse cases
- multi-agent or alignment benchmarks
- military or maneuver/intelligence analysis if clean public data and variable definitions can be made honest

The first selected domain should maximize:
- public legibility,
- hard outcomes,
- replication feasibility,
- and low dependence on worldview buy-in.

---

## Minimal Paper Structure

### 1. Problem
Why additive scoring often hides fragility.

### 2. Formal distinction
Additive vs multiplicative / weak-link-sensitive models.

### 3. Bounded theoretical motivation
Why coupled systems may be balance-sensitive rather than compensatory.

### 4. Empirical design
Datasets, variables, model classes, evaluation criteria.

### 5. Kill criteria
Pre-registered falsification and narrowing conditions.

### 6. Replication packet
A stranger can rerun the test.

### 7. Interpretation boundaries
What the result means, and what it does not mean.

---

## Kill Criteria

### K1
If additive or other non-multiplicative models explain the relevant outcomes equally well or better, the strong multiplicative claim is not supported in that domain.

### KC2
If multiplicative advantage appears only under arbitrary preprocessing or hand-tuned variable choice, the result is not robust.

### K3
If a second independent dataset or case family fails to replicate the result, the broader public claim remains provisional.

---

## Replication Standard

The paper should ship with a replication packet containing:
- dataset source
- variable mapping
- preprocessing rules
- exact models compared
- evaluation metrics
- kill criteria
- code or notebook

The standard is simple:

> A stranger should be able to rerun the test without reading the whole corpus.

---

## Tone Discipline

The paper must not sound like a grand-system manifesto.
It should sound like one strong empirical proposal with explicit boundaries.

Avoid:
- total-system rhetoric
- civilizational triumph language,
- core state inflation from one positive result.

Prefer:
- exact claim,
- exact test,
- exact kill criteria,
- exact replication path.

---

## What a Positive Result Would Mean

A positive result would justify saying:

> In this tested domain, weak-link-sensitive or multiplicative structure tracks reality better than additive accumulation alone.

[I] It would not by itself prove the full core state.
It would establish one serious public bridge.

---

## Final Formulation

# Flagship brief rule

> One paper. One claim. One test. One replication path. No grand tour.

That is the strongest route to legitimacy.
