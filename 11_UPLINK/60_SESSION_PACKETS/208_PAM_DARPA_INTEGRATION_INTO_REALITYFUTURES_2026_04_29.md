## 2. Historical Provenance

### 2.1 What PAM Was (DARPA/IAO, 2001–2003)

The **Policy Analysis Market** (PAM, also internally called FutureMAP) was a DARPA program under the **Information Awareness Office (IAO)**, run by **Admiral John Poindexter** (former Reagan-era National Security Advisor). The IAO was the same office that ran Total Information Awareness (TIA) and other post-9/11 surveillance research programs.

PAM's design was straightforward and academically defensible:
- Users buy/sell contracts paying out on future Middle-East political events
- Prices express implied probabilities
- Aggregating dispersed information through markets predicts events better than expert analysis (Hayek's "knowledge problem" + Robin Hanson's "Wisdom of Crowds" / market-scoring-rule mechanics)
- Categories included: regime change, GDP, military activity, *and* "specific terrorist attacks" and "assassinations"

The academic partner was **Net Exchange**, with **Robin Hanson** as a key designer of the market-scoring-rule mechanics PAM used.

### 2.2 What Killed It (July 2003)

Senators **Ron Wyden (D-OR)** and **Byron Dorgan (D-ND)** held a press conference on **2003-07-28** condemning PAM. Dorgan called it a program that *"combines the worst of all of our instincts"* and *"a tragic waste of taxpayers' money."* The objection was structural: a U.S. government program where traders profited by correctly predicting (or *causing*) violence against civilians and foreign leaders. PAM was scrapped within 24 hours of the press conference; **Poindexter resigned from DARPA shortly after**.

### 2.3 The Lineage After PAM

After PAM was killed publicly, the prediction-market design pattern moved to private/academic surfaces:

- **Iowa Electronic Markets** (academic, presidential elections) — preceded PAM, continues
- **InTrade** (Dublin-based, 2001–2013) — political and economic event contracts; collapsed after CFTC enforcement
- **Hollywood Stock Exchange** (1996–) — film performance contracts; movie-industry use
- **Augur** (Ethereum, 2014–) — first decentralized prediction market on a smart-contract chain
- **Gnosis** (Ethereum, 2015–) — competing decentralized infrastructure
- **Kalshi** (CFTC-regulated US, 2018–) — first US-regulated prediction-market exchange
- **Polymarket** (Polygon, 2020–) — current dominant decentralized prediction market

### 2.4 Why The Lineage Matters

The Corbett Report (2025) documents that Polymarket is materially funded by **Peter Thiel's Founders Fund** (lead in a $200M round) and **Donald Trump Jr.'s 1789 Capital**, with Trump Jr. as adviser. The institutional thesis Corbett advances: PAM's design pattern was correct as information theory but failed politically; private capital + decentralized rails + regulatory ambiguity together resurrect the same design pattern *without* the public accountability that killed PAM.

That is the lineage RealityFutures inherits from. It must therefore inherit deliberately — naming what it takes from PAM's mechanics and rejecting what killed PAM and what is killing Polymarket's public legitimacy now.

## 3. The Four Failure Modes

The Corbett Report names four structural failure modes in Polymarket. These are not Polymarket-specific; they apply to *any* prediction market that does not constitutionally guard against them. RealityFutures must specify its immunity to each.

### 3.1 The Ick Factor (Betting on Violence)

**The pattern:** Markets price the probability of assassinations, regime change, terrorist attacks, deaths, war outbreaks, hostage events. Each contract is morally repugnant on its face — humans intuitively reject *profiting from someone's murder.*

**The PAM precedent:** PAM was killed in 24 hours specifically because senators read the contract list aloud. *"You can bet on whether King Abdullah of Jordan will be assassinated"* is the sentence that ended the program.

**The Polymarket continuation:** Polymarket trades on geopolitical violence under the cover of "information aggregation" — the same defense Hanson + Poindexter offered for PAM.

**RealityFutures structural rule:**

> **No contract may be listed where the underlying event is the violent death, kidnapping, severe injury, or non-consensual harm of any identifiable human or group.**

This is a constitutional rule, enforced at contract-creation time by the Evolutionary.Network (SHOULD NOT) organ before any market opens. Forbidden categories include but are not limited to:

- Assassinations, deaths, kidnappings, hostage events of named individuals
- Terrorist attacks (specific event or actor)
- War outbreak with specific named country, dated
- Regime change of specific named leader
- Coup outcomes
- Disaster fatality counts (forbidden as bet *outcome*; permitted as research register only)
- Election outcomes where the bet itself meaningfully shapes turnout via signal effects

The "ick factor" is treated **not as squeamishness but as a structural signal**: if the outcome is morally repugnant to bet on, the market is misaligned with the framework's η = 0 boundary — someone profits from harm at someone else's irreversible cost.

### 3.2 Insider Trading

**The pattern:** Participants with material non-public information bet on outcomes their insider position lets them know or cause. Corbett's 2025 example: U.S. Master Sergeant Ganon Ken Van Dyke allegedly netted $400,000 by betting $33,000 on the Maduro raid hours before public announcement, using classified intelligence. Israeli Air Force reservists similarly exploited the June 2025 Iran conflict.

**Hanson's argument** (in his defense of PAM and prediction markets generally): insiders *should* trade because their information makes prices more accurate.

**RealityFutures rejects Hanson's argument on this point.** Hanson's position requires accepting that the market is solely an information-aggregation device. Under the framework's η = 0 invariant, however, profit accrued by extraction of non-public information is **unjust enrichment** — value is captured from positional advantage, not created through analysis. Hanson's design optimizes for accuracy at the cost of extraction; the framework optimizes for accuracy *and* zero extraction simultaneously.

**RealityFutures structural rule:**

- **Identity at signature.** All non-trivial bets are tied to K2-verified identity (private DAC mode) or PRISM-holon registration (public DAC mode). No anonymous accounts beyond a low-stake threshold.
- **Conflict-of-interest disclosure.** Any participant with material non-public information related to a market they bet on must disclose at trade time. Failure to disclose → bet voided + receipts retain the disclosure violation.
- **Classified-information recusal.** Government employees with classified clearance in domains relevant to a market are categorically barred from betting on those markets.
- **Insider-information detection.** Pattern detection in F2 pipeline flags trades clustered around private-information-asymmetric moments (Maduro-raid pattern, Charles-de-Gaulle-temperature pattern). Detection results in market settlement freeze pending K2/PRISM review.

### 3.3 Outcome Manipulation

**The pattern:** Participants don't predict outcomes — they *cause* them. Corbett's 2025 example: Polymarket's Charles de Gaulle airport temperature contracts had **suspicious sensor anomalies** correlating with $1.4 million in concentrated bets, suggesting bettors physically tampered with airport sensors.

**The PAM precedent:** Wyden's 2003 letter explicitly named this problem: a market on terrorist attacks creates an incentive to *commit* terrorist attacks to win the bet. PAM's defenders had no answer to this.

**RealityFutures structural rule:**

- **Outcome influence test.** Before any contract is listed, an automated screen assesses whether the bet outcome is physically influenceable by any plausible bettor population. If yes, contract is forbidden.
- **Oracle independence.** The settlement oracle must be structurally independent of the bettor population. Sensor data must be tamper-evident (cryptographic attestation, multi-source verification). Schelling-point oracles (UMA-style "voters paid to match majority") are explicitly rejected — they reward consensus, not truth, and become capturable by capital.
- **Categorical exclusion of physically-controllable outcomes.** Weather at a specific sensor (controllable by sensor tampering), election turnout (controllable by suppression / mobilization signaling), commodity-supply outcomes (controllable by hoarding) → forbidden unless the oracle is provably tamper-resistant against the bettor universe.

### 3.4 Predictive Programming as Weapon

**The pattern:** Once populations understand prediction markets signal insider knowledge (the "9/11 put options" pattern), orchestrators can flood markets with *false* signals to misdirect public scrutiny. Corbett: *"It's QAnon on steroids."* Conspiracy researchers trained to spot real insider signals become disinformation vectors.

**The structural concern:** The market becomes a coordination signal for false-flag operations. A real attack can be foreshadowed by manipulated market positions to create plausible deniability ("the market predicted it"); a fake attack can be foreshadowed by manipulated positions to weaponize subsequent media cycles.

**RealityFutures structural rule:**

- **K0 (No Receipt No Reality) makes every bet auditable.** Every position emits a cryptographic receipt with timestamp, identity (K2-tied or PRISM-holon-tied), counterparty, stake, contract terms. False-flag coordination becomes structurally traceable.
- **Transparency Score ≥ 90 gate.** Same gate PRISM uses to gate Faucet capital flow, applied to RealityFutures market depth. If transparency degrades below 90, market new-position emission pauses (existing positions can still settle).
- **Three-Stage Process SHOULD NOT layer.** Evolutionary.Network can refuse a market that pattern-matches to predictive-programming weaponization. Sunlight + Lightning slashing apply: detected coordinated-false-signaling positions are slashed; receipts retain the immune response for future training.
- **K2 / PRISM sovereignty boundary.** No prediction market opens without K2 sign-off (private DAC mode) or PRISM-collective ratification (public DAC mode). The sovereignty boundary explicitly does not delegate this to AI agents — the "AI does not sign K2" rule applies (per `01_EMERGENTISM/11_UPLINK/00_CORE/06_AGENTS.md`).
