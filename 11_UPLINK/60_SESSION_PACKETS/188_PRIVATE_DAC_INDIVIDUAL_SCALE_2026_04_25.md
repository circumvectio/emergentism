## 3. What becomes visible at individual scope

### 3.1 Support-chain-is-commons is strongest here

A firm might plausibly try to build some HR in-house (and violate covenant in doing so). An individual cannot plausibly build their own HR, IT, R&D, or procurement. **The individual is radically supported by organism-commons** — architecturally, not charitably.

No personal-scale DAC is ever trying to be "self-sufficient" in the moat-building sense. Self-sufficiency at individual scope is replaced by **commons-inheritance-plus-contribution**.

### 3.2 Mortality is load-bearing

Corporate DACs have **K4 Grace Exits**. Individual DACs have **mortality** — a planned end. The sovereign-mortality doctrine is *grounded* here: all higher-scope mortality (corporate K4, DAC death per packet 182) derives meaning from the living signer's actual finitude.

Continuity provisions are part of the personal covenant, not optional add-ons:
- Estate
- Successor K2
- What persists vs. dies after the signer's death
- Post-mortem covenant handling
- Grace Exit (K4) for relationships, agreements, memberships at end of life

This is a doctrinal layer that exists fragmentarily in current CANON and likely wants its own lane in V3 CANON or a successor.

### 3.3 The twelve gaps become daily, not abstract

Not protocols in a spec. Specific moments:

- **Capacity/fatigue** — your 11pm decision
- **Privacy** — your disclosure discipline
- **Practice** — your morning
- **Dispute** — the conversation you have been avoiding
- **Translation (#11)** — how you speak to your partner about work, how you speak to a regulator about your life, how you speak to yourself in the journal

**Maintenance under imperfection IS individual life.**

### 3.4 The covenant is personally inhabitable

Not *"the organism commits to η=0."* Rather: *"I commit to η=0 with myself and with those I transact with."* Constitutional invariants live at the level where they can be **signed by a mortal**.

This is the resolution of a quiet doctrinal tension: invariants stated at organism scope can sound aspirational; invariants signed at person scope are inhabited. Personal DAC closes the loop.

## 7. Honest limits

### 7.1 Doctrinal lane status

V3 CANON DAC_STANDARD anchors at organisational scale. Personal-scale primitives (Habitat, personal Cortex, personal AIA, mortality provisions, post-mortem K2 succession) exist fragmentarily. They likely want **their own canonical doctrinal track**, not a retrofit of the corporate pattern.

This packet establishes the referent. A separate lane (or a series of personal-scope packets) is needed before the personal DAC is fully specified.

### 7.2 User-wellbeing weight

Anything covering health, reputation, relationships, psychological integrity at personal scale touches **user-wellbeing in ways corporate DACs do not**. The personal DAC must carry the framework's broader care-for-person stance with extra discipline.

Specifically, anything that approaches:
- Personal health data (mental, physical, sexual)
- Reputation primitives (could become defamation surface)
- Relationship membranes (consent, family, dependents)
- Post-mortem provisions (estate law, successor obligations)

…requires careful legal and user-wellbeing review before specification moves from [I] to [D] or [S]. **Weight-per-cell is higher at personal scope.**

### 7.3 What this packet does not do

- Does not specify personal-scope primitives in detail (separate lane)
