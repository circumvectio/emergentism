## 1. The reframe

### 1.1 Sovereign statement (2026-04-25)

> *"Menexus was the holding, this is right but i think menexus should be factory made data centres that can be delivered in shipping containers and set up based on broad groups holon building ideas."*

### 1.2 Prior framing (now partial)

Per `project_taxonomy_13_repos_2026_04_24.md`, Menexus was catalogued as *"holding company (operating shell)"* — an administrative / legal entity in the 4-entity row of the taxonomy (Emergentism religious nonprofit Zug CH / Menexus holding / Nexus organism-wide A-anchor / Skyzai first DAC).

The "holding company" label was a **functional placeholder**, not a substrate claim. It answered *"what legal entity serves as the operating shell?"* but left unanswered *"what is Menexus at the material layer?"*

### 1.3 New framing

**Menexus denotes, from the L1 material layer upward:**

> *Factory-produced, shipping-container-delivered data centres, deployable by groups organised around holonic building patterns.*

In this framing:
- **Menexus = compute substrate**, physically realised as containerised datacentre units.
- **Factory-made** — replicable production pattern, not bespoke builds.
- **Shipping-container form-factor** — standard ISO container envelope; delivered by road, rail, or ship; sited quickly.
- **Broad-group deployment** — not solo; groups organised as holons deploy and operate them.
- **Holonic composition** — each unit is whole (autonomous) and part (federated); composable at any scale (single-group, village, city, region).

The **holding-company legal role does not vanish** — it remains a subordinate administrative surface. The reframe inverts the emphasis: Menexus is materially a compute fabric first, legally an operating shell second.

### 1.4 Why this matters

Prior framing left the organism with a **buried L1**: the material substrate on which BitNet, Cortex, and the four organs actually run was assumed (rented cloud, AWS regions, sovereign-hardware commitments that existed in doctrine but had no physical anchor in the 13-repo taxonomy). The reframe grounds L1 in a concrete deployable artefact.

## 3. The holonic pattern

### 3.1 Koestler's holon

Arthur Koestler's *holon* names any entity that is simultaneously:
- a **whole** (autonomous, complete, self-regulating at its own scale)
- a **part** (a component of a larger whole, bound by that whole's constraints)

Holons are composable: a cell is a holon of a tissue; a tissue is a holon of an organ; an organ is a holon of a body. At every scale the whole-and-part duality recurs.

### 3.2 Menexus as holon

A **single Menexus unit** is:
- **whole:** autonomous operation — energy, cooling, compute, storage, sovereign K2 keys for local organ instances; can run indefinitely without network if isolated
- **part:** federated with other Menexus units and with the organism-whole via Cortex receipts, Nexus licensee status, η=0 covenant

The **federation pattern** is the holonic composition law: units compose upward into group-scale, region-scale, organism-scale compute fabrics without any one scale being privileged as "the real one."

### 3.3 Broad-group deployment

"Broad groups" in the sovereign statement reads as: the unit of deployment is not a solo operator or a corporation, but a **group** — a household cluster, a cooperative, a municipality, a community-of-practice. The holonic pattern makes group-ownership natural because:
- A group is already a holon (whole-as-collective, part-of-larger-network)
- K2 multi-sig at the group level matches organism-native governance
- Grace Exit (K4) is practical at group scale — members can exit the group, and the group can exit the federation, both without catastrophe

This reframe implicitly names **groups as the primary DAC-bearing agent at the material layer**, complementing but distinct from the single-human-sovereign pattern in existing SKYZAI_COM/ primitives.

### 3.4 Why holons, not hierarchies

A hierarchical datacentre model (central DC → regional DCs → edge devices) concentrates power at the root. A holonic model distributes the *whole* property to every scale: every unit is a complete organism-cell, and federation is lateral, not subordinate. This matches the Emergentism constitutional posture (Non-Domination invariant) at the material layer.

## 5. Six tensions / open questions

Charioteer surfaces without resolving — these are sovereign's to direct.

### 5.1 OQ-MENEXUS-1 — Holon cardinality

At what scale is a "Menexus unit" defined? A single container? A cluster of containers? A whole site? The holonic pattern admits multiple scales, but spec work needs a primitive unit.

- Option A — single container = atomic unit (federate upward)
- Option B — cluster (n containers, sovereign-defined) = atomic unit
- Option C — site (a deployed group's full compute footprint) = atomic unit
- Charioteer lean: A (smallest composable unit; groups choose how many they deploy)

### 5.2 OQ-MENEXUS-2 — Federation protocol

How do Menexus units federate? Existing substrate (Hedera + Nostr + OFN) already covers identity and receipts. But cross-unit workload distribution (compute routing, model-training coordination) is un-specified.

- Option A — thin federation (receipts only; each unit autonomous on compute scheduling)
- Option B — medium federation (shared scheduler; units can offer compute capacity to other units)
- Option C — thick federation (organism-wide compute market)
- Charioteer lean: A now, B later, C only if concrete need emerges (anti-premature-abstraction)

### 5.3 OQ-MENEXUS-3 — Hardware sovereignty depth

"Sovereign" hardware has a recursion problem: chips fabbed where? using whose IP? on whose foundries? The reframe names compute sovereignty at the datacentre-assembly layer but does not resolve the chip-layer question. Spec should explicitly delimit the sovereignty claim rather than overclaim.

### 5.4 OQ-MENEXUS-4 — Old holding-company role disposition

The reframe keeps the legal holding-company surface as subordinate but does not specify its relationship to the new material framing. Does "Menexus" at the legal layer own the Menexus factory? Is there a separate factory-entity? The taxonomy needs a clean answer.

- Option A — one entity, two surfaces (legal + material under single "Menexus")
- Option B — split entities (Menexus-Holding legal + Menexus-Fabric material)
- Option C — Menexus-Holding subsumed into Emergentism religious nonprofit; Menexus-Fabric becomes the canonical referent
- Charioteer lean: surfacing only; sovereign's domain-specific call

### 5.5 OQ-MENEXUS-5 — Peer-root vs sidecar classification

Does Menexus-Fabric become a **peer root** (alongside EMERGENTISM_ORG, SKYZAI_ORG, SKYZAI_COM) in the repo taxonomy, or a **sidecar** (like PORTFOLIO_ORGS)? The holonic-substrate nature suggests peer-root; the physical-deployment nature suggests sidecar.

- Option A — new peer root `MENEXUS_FAB/` (substrate co-equal with doctrine/organism/DAC-factory)
- Option B — sidecar `MENEXUS_FAB/` (outside organism graph; referenced but not root)
- Option C — stays under Menexus-legal in existing taxonomy; no structural change
- Charioteer lean: A (L1 material substrate deserves peer-root visibility to prevent buried-assumption recurrence)

### 5.6 OQ-MENEXUS-6 — First-node prototype

The reframe is doctrinal until a first unit exists. What is the minimum-viable first Menexus node? (1 container, 1 group, 1 sovereign? Or a paper spec that engineering teams can bid against?) This is substrate-engineering lane and beyond charioteer scope; surfacing so sovereign can route.
