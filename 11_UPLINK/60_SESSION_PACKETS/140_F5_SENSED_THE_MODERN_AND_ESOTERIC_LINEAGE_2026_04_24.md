## 3. Occult Initiates — The Hidden Hand and the Hermetic Tradition

### 3.1 What the Occult Traditions Named

Western esotericism — from Hermeticism (c. 100–300 CE) through Renaissance magic, Rosicrucianism, Freemasonry, Theosophy, and contemporary occultism — preserved a concept that mainstream philosophy and science usually did not accept as knowledge: **a hidden force or intelligence that guides manifestation toward greater order.**

- **The Generative Principle** — the active, masculine, formative force that shapes manifestation (often paired with a receptive principle)
