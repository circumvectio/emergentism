## 1. Door 2 — ICP Decision

### 1.1 Candidates stress-tested

| Candidate | Fit | Why rejected / kept |
|---|---|---|
| (a) Macro analyst at a fund | **PRIMARY** | $99/mo within discretionary budget; cross-sector coverage aligns with F.I.R.E.; lead-time + receipts directly serve their LP/PM work; IS-only posture fits research (not trading). |
| (b) Crypto-native trader | Rejected | Wants COULD/SHOULD. Circle's corruption_check will frustrate, not serve. Wrong cognitive mode. |
| (c) Policy researcher (think tank) | **SECONDARY** | Fit but slow-cycle; $99 is a budget line-item fight at most shops. Retain as conversion-adjacent, not primary. |
| (d) Journalist (tech/finance) | Rejected | Independent: below $99 sustained willingness-to-pay. Outlets: too cheap for corporate research budgets (wrong layer). |
| (e) Sovereign individual | Rejected for v1.0 ICP | Would self-build; too-niche to hit 30-unit Allee; keeps as Phase 2 Steward buyer. |

### 1.2 Primary ICP — The Cross-Sector Analyst

**Persona:** Independent or boutique research principal, hedge-fund analyst, family-office single-decision-maker, macro researcher. Covers ≥ 3 of the F.I.R.E. sectors. Spends ≥ 2h/day reading feeds and scanning for lead signal. $99/mo is real but below their approval threshold. Values **lead time** and **receipts** above all — they need to show their LP or PM they saw it first, with evidence.

**Pain solved:**
- Current stack: Bloomberg ($2k+/mo), niche Substacks ($10–50 × 20), Twitter/X (free but noisy), Telegram alpha groups (free, gamey). Everything siloed; no cross-sector lineage; zero receipts; zero diversity attestation.
- Circle alternative: one feed, four sectors, corruption-gated, provider-diverse, publicly verified by Watchman + G-score. Plus: the analyst can *direct* Circle via 3 objectives.

**Job-to-be-done:** "Help me see what's happening across sectors before my peers do, and give me evidence I can cite."

### 1.3 Secondary ICP — The Independent Researcher

Substack operator at 1k–10k paid subs, boutique research-service principal, solo advisor. $99 real budget. Wants **attribution** (can they say "as first reported by Circle"?) and **objective sponsorship** (can they tune Circle to serve their niche?). Converts at Contributor tier in Phase 2.

### 1.4 Tertiary ICP — Corporate Strategy (Enterprise, post-Allee)

Mid-cap competitive-intel, M&A scouting, board-prep functions. Enterprise tier with negotiated objectives, white-label feed, API quota. Not the v1.0 target; the Allee push surfaces this channel.

### 1.5 Anti-ICPs (explicit)

- Retail traders (wrong cognitive mode — they want COULD)
- Day-traders / quant shops (wrong latency — Circle is observation, not execution)
- B2C consumer (wrong price point)
- Academic-only users without industry adjacency (wrong willingness-to-pay)

### 1.6 Implications downstream

- All copy on `circle.news` addresses the analyst, not the trader.
- The F.I.R.E. sectors are cross-weighted; no sector dominates.
- Lead-time + receipt-count are the headline accuracy metrics (not "won/lost" framing).
- The trust surface (Door 5) exists *because* analysts need citeable evidence.
- Pricing stays at $99/mo v1.0; Enterprise negotiated; no retail $9 tier.

### 2.6 The invariant across Day 1–180

The user's daily loop is: **observe → contextualize → cite or act outside Circle**. Circle is never the terminal destination. It feeds their real work. Every surface optimises for *exit into the real work*, not for session-length. Brand guide §03 literalised: inhabitable, not addictive.

### 3.3 Corruption-check UX

The `_corruption_check` in `is_agent.py` already enforces IS-domain purity on emission. At objective creation time, we extend it upstream — the `question` field is regex-gated before acceptance. Rejected submissions don't fail silently — they surface a correction:

| User typed | Response |
|---|---|
| "Will BTC break 100k this quarter?" | "Circle observes, doesn't forecast. Try: *What signals precede price regime shifts in BTC?*" |
| "Should I buy Saudi tokens?" | "Circle doesn't recommend. Try: *What regulatory signals indicate Saudi tokenization adoption?*" |
| "Recommend biotech stocks to watch" | "Circle doesn't recommend. Try: *What biomarker-provider FDA filings indicate approval-stage activity?*" |

This is **Kālī 💀 made visible** — the user encounters the constitutional guard as a product feature, not a denial. The correction is the teaching.

### 3.4 Objective detail page

After creation and first signals arrive (within one cadence window):

- **Live signal stream** filtered to this objective (SSE).
- **Scorecard strip** (top of page, mono numerics):
  ```

  ```
- **Provider basin pie chart** — which providers contributed.
- **Routing-weight history** (line chart) — how scoring has reweighted providers over time. This is the visible compounding.
- **Receipt feed** — each verified outcome, with provenance (Watchman? APU? RF market resolved? user flag?).
- **Settings dropdown** — re-tune budget/threshold/cadence/providers inline.

### 3.5 Objective-life lifecycle states

| State | Trigger | User surface |
|---|---|---|
| `active` | Just created | Live in feed |
| `paused` | User-triggered | Dimmed in dashboard; no new signals consume budget |
| `budget-exhausted` | Monthly budget hit | Banner + upgrade-tier CTA |
| `drifting` | Aim-drift check flagged COULD/SHOULD leakage in accumulated signals | Review prompt; sovereign-assist offered |

### 3.6 The retention hook

**The user watches the scoring loop converge.** Week 1 hit_rate is noise-dominated (~0.30). By week 4, routing has reweighted providers to favour those correlated with verified outcomes on this objective. Hit_rate climbs toward the confidence_threshold. The user sees their own research niche *learning itself*.

This is the compounding mechanism from packet 121 §2 made visible. "More receipts → better routing & trust calibration" → the user's dashboard shows the slope.

### 4.3 Per-signal trust disclosure

Every `SignalCard.tsx` on the main feed exposes basin on hover (small pill showing unique provider count). Every signal detail page has a **"How we got this"** section with:

- Full ProviderBasin (which agent used which provider)
- Corruption-check pass receipt
- Evidence tier breakdown per fact
- Nostr event ID (verifiable on public relays)

### 4.4 The "Signals we got wrong" discipline

This is the hardest part of the product. Every verified-incorrect signal is published, not hidden. Brand guide permission: "inhabitable product, not concept illustration" = tell the truth about what didn't work. This is A7 (self-correction) made visible.

The operational risk is R4 (receipt gaming, packet 122 §7) — users flagging signals wrong to punish objectives they don't like. Mitigation: Watchman receipts weight 10×, user flags 1×; outlier detection on flag patterns. But the *public exposure* of wrong signals is non-negotiable — it's the moat.

### 4.5 Why competitors can't copy this

- DeerFlow / Goose / Hermes ship multi-agent harnesses — no constitutional constraint, no failure-disclosure norm.
- Bloomberg / Refinitiv don't publish their failure rate.
- Substack analysts don't publish their basin (they're single-provider by construction).
- To match Circle's trust surface, a competitor would need: (a) polygenetic routing, (b) corruption-gated emission, (c) commitment to publish failures. All three are constitutional, not technical. Copying requires crossing the η = 0 + K2 + Three-Stage Process covenant.

## 5. Door 6 — The Economic Ladder

### 5.1 Five tiers, one ladder

| Tier | $/mo | Objective cap | Budget cap | Write authority | What they earn |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | 0 | $0 | Read-only (see sovereign-seeded) | Trust in feed; 3 watchlist entities; personal bookmarks; public trust surface |
| **Premium** | $99 | 3 | $50/mo total | Private objectives | 3 dedicated research niches; exportable briefs; provider-routing tuning; morning digest |
| **Contributor** *(Phase 2)* | $199 | 10 | $200/mo | Public objectives; attribution badge | Signal attribution ("first reported by"); reputation; LP-100 φ-split on receipts |
| **Steward** *(Phase 2)* | $499 | 50 | $1,000/mo | Lane A governance | Governance vote; steward dashboard; affiliate-level LP-100 share |
| **Enterprise** | Negotiated | ∞ | Negotiated | Dedicated objectives; SLA | White-label feed; API quota; custom cascade composition |

### 5.2 Upgrade moments (natural, not gated)

- **Free → Premium:** Click "Create objective" → paywall → convert. Secondary trigger: click "Export brief (markdown)" → paywall.
- **Premium → Contributor** (Phase 2): First objective hits `receipt_count ≥ 10` → prompt: "Turn this objective public? Earn LP-100 φ-split for 30 days."
- **Contributor → Steward** (Phase 2): Reputation score crosses governance threshold → "Propose sovereign-wide objective."
- **Individual → Enterprise:** Usage pattern (3+ accounts from same email domain, or API consumption pattern) triggers sales outreach.

### 5.3 Downgrade and Grace Exit (K4)

- One click in `/account/billing`.
- Exports: all signals bookmarked, all objectives + their history, all receipts, all reputation data — as markdown + JSON.
- No reduced-functionality mode. No read-only lockout. No "pay by X to retain access" nags.
- Paid tier expires → account reverts to Free tier with full historical data intact.
- **The Grace Exit button is above the fold in `/account`.** Not buried. That visibility is the K4 proof.

### 5.4 η = 0 audit per tier

Every tier must pay for **capacity to direct or amplify sensing**, not access to existing content. Free gets the feed because the feed is the constitutional product (η = 0). Premium pays for sponsored objectives (capacity). Contributor pays for public attribution (amplification). Steward pays for governance voice. Enterprise pays for white-label + dedicated cascade.

Rejected patterns: ❌ "Free preview, $99 for the article." ❌ "Unlimited signals at Premium." ❌ "Extra accuracy at Enterprise." All would cross η = 0.

### 5.5 The Allee economics

Packet 119 set Allee threshold: 30 paying Premium subscribers sustained for 14 days. At $99/mo × 30 = $2,970/mo gross recurring. That's the **v1.0 success bar**, not the shipping bar. The shipping bar is "breathing" (packet 122 §0).

Month-by-month budget envelope from 30 subscribers:
- Gross: $2,970 (stablecoin-equivalent via Skyzai API PAY; no card-network rent)
- On-chain settlement fees (Hedera, negligible): ~$5
- Infrastructure (Hetzner CX22 + Postgres + Redis): ~$30
- Provider API spend (house keys + sovereign-seeded): ~$1,500 cap (packet 121 §10 R1 mitigation)
- Remaining for sovereign discretion: ~$1,350/mo

At 100 subscribers (Phase 2 gate): $9,900 gross → ~$5,000 provider spend → ~$4,500 discretionary → LP-100 activation economics are live.

### 5.6 No retail tier (explicit)

No $9 or $19 tier. Brand guide's terminal aesthetic + P = Φ × V cover positioning + analyst ICP all reject the retail layer. Free is the retail layer. Anyone who wants less than Premium gets the full Free experience; anyone who wants more pays $99 or leaves.

### 6.3 Implication: Circle UX can't over-optimize for the user

Every design choice must satisfy both the product layer *and* the network layer.

- **Nostr relay sovereign-held** (for APU/RF/Connect) → user can't self-host Circle; BYOK is optional not required.
- **corruption_check architectural** (for organism integrity) → objectives gate same rules; can't bypass even for Premium.
- **G-score publicly visible** (for organism trust surface) → user UX includes `/trust` by default.
- **Cortex memory writes mandatory** (for long-term organism coherence) → user can export but not suppress.

### 6.4 Circle as reference implementation, not only product

The pipeline (`ingestion/` → IS agent → `corruption_check` → `publishing/`) is organ-generic. The product (circle.news, F.I.R.E. sectors, objectives, trust surface) is Skyzai-specific.

Future: **Circle-as-substrate** could power a sensing organ for a different body — a medical research org, a policy foundation, a supply-chain observer. The Skyzai instance is the reference implementation. The pipeline open-sources under OFN (Organism Foundation Network). The product stays proprietary to Skyzai.

This is a **Phase 3+ option**, not a v1.0 concern. But it shapes architectural decisions *now*: keep the pipeline boundary clean against the product boundary. `01_DATA_ROOM`, `02_PRODUCT`, `03_BUILD`, `04_FRONTIER_FRAME` directory split in the existing backend tree already serves this. Preserve it.

## 7. Door 4 — The Habitat Question

### 7.1 The unknown

The brand guide §03 writes: *"Through Skyzai, Habitat, and the Trinity of TheCircle · RealityFutures · Skyzai.BOT, we close the epistemic loop."*

Habitat is named as a peer to the Trinity. No other repo surface I've scanned defines it. This packet raises the question and proposes a resolution for sovereign ratification.

### 7.2 Four candidate definitions

| Candidate | What it is | Fit to organism |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Identity/Reputation membrane** | Habitat = Skyzai Connect + D5 reputation layer. The wrapper between a natural person and the Trinity. | Strong — explains why reputation is cross-organ. |
| **B. Living-environment sensing** | Habitat = Helios-mediated physical/IoT sensors feeding Circle ingestion. Material-sensor layer complementing LLM-sensors. | Partial — fits a sensing role but conflates with Circle. |
| **C. Developer substrate** | Habitat = OFN SDKs + MCP surface + plugin marketplace. Where external builders attach. | Possible — but better named "OFN" already. |
| **D. Household/community layer** | Habitat = multi-user surface (families, teams, communities). Above-individual, below-organization tier. | Strong — fits the brand-guide "human-centric wisdom" language. |

### 7.3 Proposed resolution: A ∪ D

**Habitat = the user-side membrane.** Identity, reputation, household/community co-sharing, personal data sovereignty, Grace Exit as a function. The Trinity is *what the organism does*; Habitat is *how a natural person lives inside it*.

If this is right:

| Surface | Owner | Lives in Habitat |
|---|---|---|
| User identity (npub, D5 reputation) | Connect sub-surface of Habitat | ✓ |
| Objective ownership | Habitat — user's portfolio | ✓ |
| Brief archive (bookmarked signals) | Habitat — user's library | ✓ |
