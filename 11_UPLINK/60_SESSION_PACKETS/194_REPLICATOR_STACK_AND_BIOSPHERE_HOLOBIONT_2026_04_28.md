### 1.3 — F5 Force = Gradient-Selection-With-Staging-Zones

The F5 force currently lives at `01_TELEOLOGY/01_F5_FORCE/` with the Serpent identification. This packet proposes a refinement: **F5 is the directional pressure that gradient-selection-with-staging-zones produces on a substrate with reproductive surplus**.

The MEGA-plate experiment (Roy Kishony lab, *Science* 2016, "Spatiotemporal microbial evolution on antibiotic landscapes") is the cleanest laboratory-scale demonstration. E. coli on a 2x4 foot petri dish with stripes of antibiotic at 1x, 10x, 100x, 1000x lethal concentration. They colonize each zone only after the previous is mastered. Within 11 days they evolve resistance to 1000x lethal. The dynamic:

- **Graded environment** — pressure increases continuously, not in catastrophic steps
- **Safe staging zones** — previously-adapted region remains habitable while next is mastered
- **Lethal forward boundaries** — next zone kills the unprepared, so adaptation has real consequence
- **Reproductive surplus** — substrate produces enough variation that rare adapted mutation can occur
- **Selection-with-survival** — failure modes don't take everyone, only the unprepared

Run this pattern at scale and you get *churning* rather than specialization-into-narrow-niches.
