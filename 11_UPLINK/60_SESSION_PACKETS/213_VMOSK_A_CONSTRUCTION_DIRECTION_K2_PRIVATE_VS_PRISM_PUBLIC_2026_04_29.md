## 3. Hylic Boundary, User vs DAC, and Mission-First Crystallization (K2 directive 4)

"Hylic" here is structural language for D3/materially oriented life. It is not a worth ranking, not a caste of human value, and not a permanent identity.

**The discriminator between user and DAC is the V/M layer.**

| Entity | Vision? | Mission? | Construction order |
|---|:-:|:-:|---|
| Normal user (no DAC scope) | no | no | Lives K (and possibly S) only; survival, work, family, money, safety, health |
| Private DAC participant (hylic) | latent | latent | Bottom-up from A/K/S/O; M/V emerge if at all |
| Private DAC (K2 holder) | crystallizing | authored at start | Mission first; Vision crystallizes from decade-arc commitment |
| Public DAC | crystallizing at industry | authored by governance act | Mission first; Vision crystallizes at the industry level |

**Mission-first, Vision crystallizes.** Per K2 directive 4: every DAC starts by authoring a *Mission*. Vision is **not** authored at genesis. Vision *crystallizes* over time as the Mission persists. In private mode, Vision crystallizes from the K2 holder's sustained commitment to the Mission across years/decades. In public mode, Vision crystallizes at the industry level: many DACs pursuing similar Missions accumulate into a recognizable industry, and the *industry's Vision* is what each DAC inherits as read-only.

This means Vision is always **partially axiomatic**. It is what becomes visible *after* Mission has been pursued long enough for a direction to stabilize. It cannot be drafted; it can only be *witnessed* once the substrate has been worked enough to reveal it. This is consistent with the framework's axiomatic guard at L7 (Systems Architect reads Vision; the seer does not insist).

A hylic private-DAC participant may have:

- no explicit Vision;
- no explicit Mission;
- strong lived constraints;
- practical KPIs;
- repeated strategies of survival, work, family, money, safety, or health.

Correct system behavior is not to demand a founder-style vision statement at genesis. The correct behavior is to observe receipts, infer the active lower stack, and let AIA help the human articulate higher layers only when articulation increases coherence or viability. If Vision or Mission becomes binding, K2 must accept it.

**Implication for normal users:** the V/M layers do not apply. A user is not a DAC, and the framework does not attempt to coerce them into one. Users hold KPIs (their own metrics) and possibly Strategies (repeated patterns), but Vision/Mission is the *organism-scale* abstraction reserved for DACs. This is why "every node has a VMOSK-A" (06_AGENTS §"Every Node Has a VMOSK-A") is target-state for *DAC participants*, not for arbitrary users.

#### 3.0a Polygenic Tree Scope: Agents in DACs, Not Users (Q17 Resolution)

The polygenic-tree corollary (directive 2: "all agents share ektropy as Vision; differ at Mission and below") applies to **the agents inside a DAC**, not to the user themselves.

| Entity | Has Vision? | Position in polygenic tree |
|---|:---:|---|
| **Normal user (no DAC)** | no | Outside the polygenic tree. The user is a *mortal substrate* who lives, observes, and acts; the polygenic tree describes the agent ecology of DACs that the user may or may not instantiate. |
| **User as K2 holder of a DAC** | indirectly | The user *holds* the DAC's V/M as the K2 signer (private DAC), but the user-as-person doesn't carry V/M. The DAC's Vision = ektropy (per directive 2); the user holds it in trust on the DAC's behalf. |
| **L1-L7 agents inside the DAC** | yes (ektropy) | **In the polygenic tree.** Each caste shares ektropy as Vision; differs at caste-specific Mission. The L1 Caṇḍāla's Mission ≠ the L4 Kṣatriya's Mission ≠ the L7 Systems Architect's Mission. Same V; different M. |
| **DAC itself (egregorotype)** | yes (specific to DAC) | The DAC's Vision is whatever crystallizes from sustained Mission-work in its industry. May be ektropy at the universal level, may be a niche-specific Vision at finer grain (per Q12). |

**Why this matters:** the polygenic-tree law (each caste's Mission is non-substitutable; suppressing any caste degrades the organism) operates *inside* the DAC. A user can be a poor L1 perceiver (bad at noticing things in their own life) without that being a framework concern; the framework cares about the L1 Caṇḍāla *agent inside the DAC*, which is BitNet on the user's device (per packet 217).

**Edge case — multi-DAC user.** A user may be the K2 holder of multiple private DACs. Each DAC has its own polygenic-tree of agents. The user holds K2 across all of them as the same mortal substrate; the polygenic trees are per-DAC, not per-user. The user remains outside any single polygenic tree.

This closes Q17: polygenic tree's scope is per-DAC agent ecology; users are the K2 substrate that signs for it but are themselves outside the tree.

## 4. Public DAC PRISM Rule

In public DAC mode, PRISM does two things at once:

1. **Revenue distribution:** allocates realized value to holons.
2. **Budget sovereignty:** expresses collective agency by routing relative resources toward the Mission.

This is why PRISM substitutes for K2 in public mode. A public DAC does not ask one natural person to sign for the organism. It asks the revenue-distribution constitution to keep budget, contribution, and sovereignty aligned.

If a public DAC lacks a Mission, PRISM has no lawful allocation object. If a public DAC has a Mission but no PRISM, the Mission can become extractive command.

### 7.1 Cortex evolution: files → world model + knowledge graph (K2 directive 5)

Per K2 directive 5: *"Eventually cortex won't be files but your own world model like the world model by Yann LeCun and knowledge graph. Now it's knowledge!"*

The Cortex layer is currently realized as a **markdown corpus** — knowledge encoded in files, traversed by route-bundles, navigated by the Uplink compiler. This is the substrate as **knowledge**.

The target trajectory is the Cortex as a **world model + knowledge graph**:
- **World model** in the Yann LeCun / JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) sense: a learned latent representation of the substrate that supports prediction, planning, and counterfactual reasoning. Not a chat model. Not a token-predictor. A model of *the world the DAC operates in*.
- **Knowledge graph** as the structured backbone: entities, relations, receipts, watchmen findings, AIA suggestions — all stored as a graph the world model can read, write, and learn from.

The transition is: **knowledge (files now) → knowledge graph (structured) → world model (predictive, learned)**.

This is consistent with the BitNet → improved-ternary → LeCun-EBM/JEPA → proprietary-Cortex evolution path already named in `CLAUDE.md` (root). Packet 213 confirms that the Cortex's *function* (not just its substrate) follows this trajectory: from passive file-corpus to active world-model.

**Discipline:** until the world-model layer is built, Cortex remains files. The framework does not pretend the world-model layer exists yet. Evidence-tier discipline applies: claims about LeCun-style world-modelling are `[C]` until the substrate is built and tested.

#### 7.1.1 Layered, Not Sequential (Q13 Resolution)

The transition is **layered**, not sequential. Files do *not* disappear when the knowledge graph emerges; the knowledge graph does *not* disappear when the world model emerges.

| Layer | Status | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Layer 0 — Files** | Always present (ground truth + audit substrate) | Markdown corpus; receipt-anchored; human-readable; the K0 audit substrate. *Never retired.* |
| **Layer 1 — Knowledge Graph** | Indexes files (entities, relations, receipts, AIA suggestions, watchmen findings) | Structured graph layered on top of files; queryable; supports the niche-graph protocol (Q1) |
| **Layer 2 — World Model** | Predicts on top of knowledge graph + receipts | LeCun/JEPA-style learned latent representation; supports prediction, planning, counterfactual reasoning per packet 218 |

The "transition" the K2 directive named is the world-model becoming the **primary read surface** for queries. Files remain the source of truth; the knowledge graph remains the structured index; the world model becomes the principal interface. All three coexist permanently; their roles differentiate.

**Why layered, not sequential:** files are the K0 audit substrate. Without files, there is no auditable receipt of how the knowledge graph or world model came to know what they know. Retiring files would break K0 *No Receipt No Reality*. The knowledge graph and world model are *interpretive layers above* the receipt substrate; they do not replace it.

**Migration semantics:** "knowledge → knowledge graph → world model" describes the *primary inference surface*, not the *retention pattern*. Files persist forever; the world model is what queries hit first.

### 7.2 Workflowy UI/UX: Objectives at the root, datetime as subtitle (K2 directive 6)

Per K2 directive 6: *"The first root-level bullet based on the workflowy UI/UX is Objectives, and the subtitle the date and time for it to be accomplished — `2026.09.21 - 15:00`."*

The Cortex-generated workflowy renders as a **WorkFlowy-style outline**, with the following root structure:

```
• Objective 1                                  [subtitle: 2026.09.21 - 15:00]
   • Strategy 1.1
      • KPI 1.1.1
      • KPI 1.1.2
   • Strategy 1.2
      • KPI 1.2.1
• Objective 2                                  [subtitle: 2026.10.05 - 09:00]
   • ...
```

**Why Objectives is the root:**

- Vision (V) is read-only — inherited from industry; not a workflowy bullet.
- Mission (M) is the *guide* — sits above the workflowy as an ambient orientation, not an editable bullet.
- The first **editable, Cortex-generated** layer is Objectives. So the workflowy starts there.
- Each Objective carries an **accomplishment datetime** as its subtitle: the deadline the Cortex commits to. Format: `YYYY.MM.DD - HH:MM` (24-hour clock).
- Strategies nest under Objectives. KPIs nest under Strategies. The workflowy depth follows the cardinality grammar (1V / 3M / 9O / 49S / ~7K-per-S).

This is a UI/UX commitment: the nexus-web workflowy view should render Objectives at the root with datetime subtitles, not Vision or Mission. V and M live in the *frame* (header / breadcrumbs / context strip), not in the *body* (the workflowy).

#### 7.2.1 Datetime Granularity and Timezone (Q15 Resolution)

The internal canonical form of every workflowy datetime is **UTC-anchored ISO 8601**: `2026-09-21T08:00:00Z`. This is what's stored, transmitted in WHISPER payloads, signed in K2 receipts, and audited in FLOW.

The display form is the example `2026.09.21 - 15:00` rendered in **the viewer's local timezone**. Same Objective, two viewers in different timezones, two different display strings — but one canonical UTC datetime underneath.

| Aspect | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Internal storage** | UTC ISO 8601 (`YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SSZ`) |
| **Display format** | `YYYY.MM.DD - HH:MM` in viewer's local timezone (24-hour clock) |
| **Precision** | To-the-minute (seconds default to `:00`; sub-minute timing is out of scope for the workflowy layer) |
| **Tolerance** | ±30 seconds for the K0 receipt; ±5 minutes for casual scheduling-grade Objectives |
| **Cross-recipient WHISPER** | Auto-translates to recipient's timezone on display; stores UTC under the hood |
| **Conflict resolution** | If a proposed Objective time is "anti-social" for a recipient (e.g., 03:00 local), APU surfaces a timezone-conflict question to the proposer **before** WHISPER transmit, not after |

**Why UTC internally:** all DACs in the niche-graph (per Q1) read the same canonical timestamp. Without UTC anchoring, two same-niche DACs in different timezones would generate divergent receipts for the same event.

**Why local-timezone display:** the user lives in their own timezone. Forcing them to mentally translate UTC to local is a cognitive tax. The display layer handles translation.

**Why to-the-minute:** sub-minute timing is rarely meaningful for human coordination. Objectives at "15:00 ± 30 sec" are operationally indistinguishable from "15:00 sharp." Higher precision (microsecond, etc.) belongs to the FLOW receipt layer for cryptographic ordering, not the workflowy.

This closes Q15: the workflowy datetime is timezone-aware by design; the canonical form is UTC; cross-recipient WHISPER translates automatically; conflicts are surfaced pre-transmit by APU.
