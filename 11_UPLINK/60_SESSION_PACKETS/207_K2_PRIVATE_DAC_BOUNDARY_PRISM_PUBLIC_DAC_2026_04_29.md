## What This Resolves

Earlier canon spoke of K2 (the "Mortal Substrate" invariant — *the human signs consequential acts; agency cannot become blind power*) as if it applied uniformly across the DAC layer. The implicit reading was: *every DAC has a K2 signer somewhere*.

Packet 207 collapses that ambiguity. K2 is now scoped:

| DAC type | K2 status | Sovereignty mode | Revenue distribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Private DAC** (1 natural-person owner) | K2 active. The single owner is the K2 holder; their signature is required for consequential acts. | Single-signer sovereignty. | At owner's discretion, within K6 (η = 0) and K4 (Grace Exit) constraints. |
| **Public DAC** (no single owner) | **K2 absent.** The DAC is fully sovereign as a whole. There is no single human who signs for it. | Collective sovereignty by holon vote / PRISM-mediated coordination. | **All revenue distributes to holons via PRISM** (the Faucet mechanism). |

The "fully sovereign as a whole" framing is constitutional, not metaphorical: a public DAC has no human substrate it must defer to, *because no one person owns it*. The K-invariant K2 ("Mortal Substrate") is satisfied vacuously when there is no single mortal substrate to point to — sovereignty is held by the collective directly.

## Why This Matters

### 1. Resolves a latent ambiguity in K-invariant inheritance

The DAC-Constitutional invariants K0–K9 (per `01_EMERGENTISM/08_FRAMEWORK_SUPPORT/05_SYNTHESIS/00_K_INVARIANT_NAMESPACE_INDEX.md`) are carried by every Child DAC at ratification. The Index says of K2: *"The human signs consequential acts; agency cannot become blind power."* That language presupposes a singular human. Packet 207 clarifies that for public DACs, "the human" is replaced by **the PRISM-mediated collective**, and the K2 invariant survives via the collective's voting and revenue-distribution mechanics — not via a single signer.

### 2. Fixes the YieldFront / Foundation public-DAC distinction

YieldFront Charter v2.0 (ratified 2026-04-28) is an example of a Child DAC that may, depending on ownership structure, be **either** private (with a K2 holder) **or** public (PRISM-sovereign with no K2). The Charter must be readable in both modes; the K2 references in §4–§5 of the Charter are conditioned on private-DAC mode.

Foundation, Menexus, Aureus, Helios under `03_VENTURES/` are **legal/entity surfaces** rather than DAC ownership models — their K2 status follows the DAC mode, not the legal entity. A public DAC remains public even if a Foundation entity wraps it.

### 3. Aligns with η = 0 (K6) and Grace Exit (K4)

[S] For private DACs: the K2 holder signs; revenue can flow as the owner directs (within K6 zero-extraction). For public DACs: η = 0 means no one party should extract; **PRISM** is the structural mechanism intended to distribute revenue to all holons proportionally. K4 (Grace Exit) still applies in both modes — a holon can exit with their share regardless of DAC mode.

### 4. PRISM is now constitutional, not optional, for public DACs

Public DACs that lack PRISM revenue distribution would violate K2 (no defined sovereignty mode), K6 (revenue could pool extractively without a distribution rule), or K4 (no clean exit accounting). For a public DAC, PRISM is **the substitute for K2's mortal-signer role** — the structural mechanism that prevents agency from becoming blind power.

## Definitions Reaffirmed

- **PRIVATE DAC:** A DAC owned by exactly one natural person. The owner is the K2 holder. They sign consequential acts. The DAC's revenue and operations operate at their direction, bounded by K-invariants.

- **PUBLIC DAC:** A DAC with no single natural-person owner. Sovereignty is collective. **K2 = absent.** Revenue distributes to all holons via PRISM. Coordination happens through holon voting and PRISM-mediated mechanisms.

- **HOLON:** A participant in a public DAC who receives PRISM-distributed revenue proportional to their stake/contribution. (Term inherits from holarchy doctrine: a holon is simultaneously a whole and a part.)

- **PRISM:** The Faucet mechanism that distributes revenue to LP-100 holders / holons. Defined in canonical PRISM economics under `03_VENTURES/OPEN_FINANCE_NETWORK/GOVERNANCE/FOUNDATION/CANON/DAC_STANDARD/PRISM_ECONOMICS/`. For public DACs, PRISM is the constitutional revenue-distribution rail — without it, K2/K4/K6 cannot be satisfied collectively.
