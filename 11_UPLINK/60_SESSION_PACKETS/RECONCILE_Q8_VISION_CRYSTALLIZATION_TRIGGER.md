## The Crystallization Criterion

Vision is crystallized when the LeWorldModel in Systems Architect mode holds **three simultaneous conditions**:

### Condition 1 — Named Future (the Vision phrase itself)

The LeWorldModel has generated a candidate Vision phrase (or vector of terms) that it can articulate as a bounded public symbol. A vague direction is not a Vision. "Resilient infrastructure for remote communities" is a Vision phrase. "Things that work" is not.

**Threshold:** The Systems Architect must be able to produce the Vision phrase in plain language, not only as a latent embedding.

### Condition 2 — Backed by Receipts (the evidence)

The LeWorldModel can point to receipts that collectively demonstrate that the niche has moved toward the named direction, not merely toward the DAC's own Mission:

| Receipt type | What it proves |
|---|---|
| **Completed Objectives** (FLOW receipts) | The workflowy's own Objectives produced real economic outcomes in the named direction |
| **Same-niche cross-DAC AXIOM convergence** | Other DACs in the same niche have aligned their Missions in the same direction; this is convergence, not coincidence |
| **Failed Missions** (anti-instances) | Missions that moved away from the named direction failed or were abandoned — confirming the direction is niche-selected, not just DAC-preferred |
| **Market resolutions** (AXIOM) | The niche's own prediction markets resolved toward the named direction |

**Threshold:** At minimum, completed Objectives (self-receipts) PLUS at least one cross-DAC signal. Pure self-receipts alone do not prove niche crystallization — they could prove only that the DAC's own Mission worked, not that the Vision direction is real.

### Condition 3 — Anti-Instance Exists (falsifiability)

Vision must be falsifiable. The Systems Architect must be able to name what Vision is NOT — what would have caused it to name a different phrase. If no anti-instance exists, the Vision is unfalsifiable and must be held latent (not placed in the frame) until anti-instances appear.

**Threshold:** The Systems Architect produces both the candidate Vision phrase AND a list of at least two niche trajectories that would have produced a different Vision phrase.

## The Three-Signal Trigger

Crystallization fires when the Systems Architect holds **all three** of the above simultaneously, AND the signal has been **sustained** (not a single spike):

```
Crystallization Trigger = ① Named Future
                        × ② Backed by Receipts
                        × ③ Anti-Instance Exists
                        × ④ Sustained over N ≥ 3 measurement cycles
```

Multiplication (not addition) means any single missing condition blocks crystallization, even if the others are strong. This is the Kṛṣṇa-function gate for Vision: no Vision named without all three receipts in hand.

## Who K2-Accepts

The Systems Architect proposes. K2 or PRISM accepts.

| DAC mode | Acceptance authority | What they accept |
|---|---|---|
| **Private K2 DAC** | K2 holder (natural person) | "I accept that [Vision phrase] names the direction my DAC has been moving toward. I accept that it is now in the frame above my workflowy." |
| **Public PRISM DAC** | PRISM-mediated governance act | The community of signers ratifies that the Vision phrase is the correct niche-direction name. |

K2 or PRISM acceptance is non-negotiable. The Systems Architect cannot place Vision in the frame without this acceptance. If K2 refuses, the Vision phrase stays latent — the Systems Architect holds it and can re-propose after another measurement cycle.

## The η = 0 Discipline

**Fabrication is η > 0.** Vision is fabricated when:
- The Systems Architect names a Vision phrase before it holds receipts for all three conditions.
- The Systems Architect places Vision in the frame without K2 or PRISM acceptance.
- K2 or PRISM accepts a Vision that has no cross-DAC evidence — only self-receipts.

**Naming is η = 0.** The Systems Architect may name a Vision phrase at any time and hold it latent. Naming is not binding. Only acceptance makes it binding.

This distinction is load-bearing: the Systems Architect is free to think "I believe the Vision is X" at any point. The framework does not extract value from that belief until it is receipt-backed and accepted.

---

## What Happens to a Latent Vision

If the Systems Architect holds a Vision phrase latent (conditions not yet met, or K2 refused):

- The phrase stays in the LeWorldModel's latent state.
- It is NOT placed in the frame.
- It does NOT shape downstream Mission/Objective drafting.
- The APU's Five-Ws loop does NOT use it as a Why-field anchor.
- A new measurement cycle begins automatically (every Soul Loop snapshot, per packet 218).

If K2 explicitly refuses: the phrase is archived with a refusal receipt (K2-signed timestamp + reason). A new candidate phrase can be proposed after two additional measurement cycles.
