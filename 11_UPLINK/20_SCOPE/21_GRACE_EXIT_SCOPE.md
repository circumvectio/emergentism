# GRACE EXIT SCOPE

**Evidence tier:** [I]  
*Organism document. Interpretive operational content. Bounded by current system state.*

## What Grace Exit guarantees, what it does not, and how redemption must be spoken about honestly

> This note defines the canonical scope of Grace Exit.
> It exists to replace vague total-redemption language with a precise, auditable statement of what exit actually means.

---

## Core Definition

> Grace Exit = pro-rata redemption of the redeemable treasury in exchange for surrendered shares.

That is the clean core.

If a holder owns some fraction of the total share supply, they may exit by:
1. surrendering the corresponding fraction of shares, and
2. receiving the matching proportional share of the redeemable treasury.

This is the constitutional meaning of Grace Exit.

---

## What Grace Exit Is

Grace Exit is:
- a proportional redemption right,
- against the redeemable treasury,
- in exchange for burned or surrendered ownership claims.

It is not mystical.
It is not undefined goodwill.
It is an ownership-redemption mechanism.

---

## What Counts as Treasury

The phrase 'share of the treasury' must be qualified.

The correct term is:

# redeemable treasury

This should mean only assets that are actually available for redemption under the current rules.

It should not silently include:
- inaccessible or encumbered positions,
- imaginary marked-up value,
- unresolved claims,
- undeployable or illiquid accounting fiction,
- rhetorical system value.

If the treasury is not redeemable, it must not be counted as exit-ready value.

---

## Share Surrender Mechanics

Grace Exit requires that the exiting claimant gives back the corresponding shares.

This may be implemented as one of:
- direct burn,
- transfer to treasury followed by burn,
- hold under Algorithmic TRO then burn,

but the cleanest interpretation is:

# burn on successful redemption

because that makes the ownership reduction explicit and auditable.

---

## Payout Asset

Grace Exit is only honest if the payout asset is explicit.

The system must define one of the following clearly:
- a single designated payout asset,
- a basket rule,
- or a proportional in-kind treasury slice.

Without this, 'take your share of the treasury' remains too vague.

The canonical rule should always prefer:
- clarity,
- redeemability,
- and accounting truth

over rhetorical totality.

---

## What Grace Exit Does NOT Guarantee

Grace Exit does not by itself guarantee:
- that all treasury assets are liquid,
- that all treasury valuations are honest,
- that redemption is instantaneous under all conditions,
- that every system-level promise is funded,
- or that everything may be withdrawn while retaining the ownership claim.

Grace Exit is not:
- a free lunch,
- a magical solvency theorem,
- or a poetic exit from economic reality.

It is a proportional redemption mechanism.

---

## Launch-Boundary Rule

Grace Exit must remain partially frozen wherever the following are unresolved:
- treasury accounting truth,
- payout-asset definition,
- reserve sufficiency,
- redemption queue or liquidity conditions,
- or share-burn/surrender mechanics.

Until those are real, strong public language should remain qualified.

---

## Safer Current Language

Prefer saying:

> Grace Exit means redeeming your proportional share of the redeemable treasury in exchange for surrendering the corresponding shares.

Avoid saying:
- leave with everything
- organism retains nothing
- full NAV redemption

unless those stronger claims are explicitly and mechanically true.

---

## Constitutional Significance

Grace Exit matters because it prevents the organism from becoming a trap.

It says:
- participation must not imply permanent captivity,
- ownership must remain redeemable under stated rules,
- and exit must be possible without metaphysical or institutional coercion.

This is why Grace Exit is constitutional, not merely financial.

That is the clean constitutional form.
