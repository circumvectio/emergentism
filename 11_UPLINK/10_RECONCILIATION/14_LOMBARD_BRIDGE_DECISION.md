# LOMBARD BRIDGE DECISION — 2026-04-14

**Evidence tier:** [I]  
*Organism document. Interpretive operational content. Bounded by current system state.*

## Canonical Decision on Credit-Layer Identity

> This document resolves the semantic and product-identity fracture around “Lombard Bridge.”
> One name must no longer refer to multiple incompatible financial instruments.

---

## Decision

**Canonical decision (ratified 2026-04-14):**

> **“Lombard Bridge” is retired as a canonical term.**
> **Vault.sol (ZAI-collateralized SKY minting) is the sole live L2 credit primitive.**

The name Lombard Bridge referred to three materially incompatible products (P2P lending, ARB bonds, vault minting) across docs, specs, and code. The only product with deployed contracts is `Vault.sol`. Therefore:
- Lombard Bridge is retired from active canon,
- Vault.sol is promoted to canonical L2 credit primitive,
- The P2P lending and ARB-bond descriptions are moved to aspirational research (not launch claims).

---

## Why This Decision Is Necessary

The same name has been used to describe at least three incompatible financial realities:

1. a peer-to-peer SKY lending market,
2. an ARB-bond minter / bridge-like issuance mechanism,
3. a vault-style minting path against collateral.

These are not cosmetic variants of one product. They are different credit instruments.

One name cannot safely carry all three.

---

## What Lombard Bridge Is NOT (for now)

Until reconciliation is complete, “Lombard Bridge” must NOT be used as if it already means any one of the following implemented realities:

- a settled canonical lending market,
- a settled minting mechanism,
- a settled bond issuance layer,
- a launch-ready credit bridge,
- a coherent L2 financial product.
