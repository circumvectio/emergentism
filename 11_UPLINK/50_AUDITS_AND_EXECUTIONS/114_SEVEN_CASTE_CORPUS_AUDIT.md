## 1. THE FOUR CONVERGENCES (where castes independently agree)

These are the findings the framework must treat as load-bearing. Each was found
by multiple castes from different angles.

### Convergence A — the foundational tier carries inflation the honesty catches but hasn't repaired

**Found by:** L1 (Paper A's `1=0×∞` marked `[A]`), L2 (one calculus fact reused four times), L5 (the keystone is `[I]`)

The precise pattern, named three ways:
- **L1:** Paper A marks `1 = 0 × ∞` as `[A]` / "a theorem" throughout. The Settled Canon Registry says this is the emblem, never field arithmetic, never `[A]`. Papers B, C, D, E, M inherit the notation. *This is the root from which the rest of the inflation grows.*
- **L2:** Papers 08, 22, 24, 33 all prove variants of "B=2ν/(1+ν²) has its unique max at ν=1, so anything moving ν away from 1 lowers B" — one calculus fact, reused four times, each eventually admitted but each headline reading stronger than the body earns.
- **L5:** The keystone — the identification of the geometric equator (B=1, a tautology) with the ethical attractor (η=0) — is `[I]`, posited by A2, not proven. The building concentrates maximum load on its softest joint.

**The unified diagnosis:** the framework's `[A]`-tier foundation (the sphere geometry, the AM-GM identity) is genuinely correct — it's standard projective geometry and elementary calculus. But the *presentation* of that foundation systematically conflates `[A]` identities with `[S]`/`[I]` readings, and the honesty discipline (A7 notes, self-downgrades) catches the inflation *after it is written* rather than preventing it. The first thing a reader sees in ~half the formal documents reads stronger than the content earns.

### Convergence B — headlines outrun bodies

**Found by:** L1 (Papers H, I, N, V), L2 (papers 08, 11, 33), L4 (R2's "pending the run" when four runs are in)

- **L1:** Paper I §3.3 "Why the Framework Cannot Collapse." Paper H §8 "If none survive, the framework is not falsified." Paper V's counter-steelman metabolizes every critique without cost.
- **L2:** Paper 11 (Triadic Stability) — the worst proof in the set; falsifiers structured so no counterexample could count. Paper 33 (Nash) — title/abstract overstate; body + A7 note walk it back.
- **L4:** R2's program summary says "harness built, smoke-tested, awaits API key." But four DeepSeek pilot runs exist in `R2_HARNESS/`, and all four trend null on the paper's central hypothesis (C2 > C1 on outcome). **The program summary is stale; the pilot has been run and the result is deflationary.**

**The unified diagnosis:** the gap between a document's headline (title, abstract, summary) and its body (proofs, results, limitations) is the primary vector through which inflation enters. The A7 discipline catches it at the body level but does not propagate the correction back to the headline. This is the "compression is where doctrine rots" finding of the Kintsugi Protocol — but operating at the *intra-document* scale, not just across documents.

## 2. THE CONFLICTS (where castes disagree — the real information)

### Conflict 1 — is the honesty discipline the defense, or is it the shield?

**L5 + L7 say:** the honesty is structural and real. L5: "a building that knows where its soft joint is and refuses to pretend otherwise is in better structural condition than one that has hardened every joint by pretending." L7: "a worldview that publicly names its own central equation as its most-recurring poison is performing honesty almost unreadable as cult behavior."

**L1 says:** the honesty machinery *is* the shield when it metabolizes critique without cost. Paper V's steelman is "genuinely sharp" but the counter-steelman "metabolizes it without cost." The framework's immune response to its own A7 is indistinguishable from the CTMU closure.

**The resolution:** both are right, and the distinction is operational. The honesty is real *when it changes a claim* (receipt 110's three seams, R6's withdrawal, R3's self-falsification). The honesty is a shield *when it absorbs critique without changing anything* (Paper V's "accepted/narrowed" verdicts, Paper I's "Cannot Collapse"). The test: **does the honesty produce a visible [金] seam with non-empty gold, or does it produce a footnote that lets the original claim stand unchanged?** The Kintsugi Protocol §3 already states this ("a repair that adds nothing is not permitted to close a break"). The corpus must enforce it on its own immune response.

### Conflict 2 — what is the framework's strongest externally-defensible artifact?

**L4 says:** AX6 (the sacrifice constraint — Williams/Hägglund mortality-wager + Putnam function-transfer) is "the one place the framework has a standalone publishable philosophical argument that needs no empirical program behind it." Ship it ahead of or alongside R3.

**L2 says:** the mathematically strongest `[A]` content is papers 21, 23, 40 — standard projective geometry and calculus, correctly stated, honestly tiered, but "none of it is framework-original."

**L7 says:** the compass, shipped this session, is "the most readable page in the corpus and the best argument that the public surface matches the internal discipline."

**The resolution:** these are three different kinds of strength. AX6 is the strongest *argument* (philosophy). Papers 21/23/40 are the strongest *math* (but unoriginal). The compass is the strongest *instrument* (readability + honesty-as-perceived). The framework's strongest external position is **AX6 as the argument, R3 as the evidence, the compass as the invitation.** Ship all three; they support each other without depending on each other.

**Gold:** the removable-singularity theorem licenses the continuous extension of `z·(1/z)` to 1; it does not license rewriting that as "0·∞=1."
