## 3. The Plato correction, corrected

### 3.1 What Plato may have seen

The partial truth is not that a separate realm of Forms exists. It is that intelligibility is not exhausted by isolated particulars. Instances are recognized through repeatable structure: invariance, relation, symmetry, constraint, and stable organization.

### 3.2 Why “Forms are degrees of freedom” is still wrong

A degree of freedom specifies how a system may vary. A form specifies what remains organized or invariant across variation. They are complementary, not identical:

- **DoF:** axes/capacities of possible variation;
- **constraints:** what combinations are forbidden or coupled;
- **symmetries:** transformations preserving relevant structure;
- **forms:** robust invariants, equivalence classes, macro-patterns, or stable regimes across those variations.

Therefore the strongest replacement is:

> **Not transcendent Forms, and not bare DoF as new substances: constraint-structured freedoms whose lawful organization can produce stable invariants and emergent forms.**

### 3.3 “Everything exists” must be typed

The council rejects the unqualified phrase. The lawful cascade is:

`thinkable → logically admissible → mathematically satisfiable → model-admissible → physically possible → dynamically reachable → actual → stable → emergent → livedly available`

Every arrow requires additional warrant. None is reversible by rhetoric.

The corrected sentence is:

> Every configuration admitted by a specified possibility space exists **in that space as a possibility**. Fewer are physically lawful, fewer reachable, fewer actual, fewer stable, fewer emergent, and fewer available to a finite life.

This is not unrestricted modal realism.

If S²-derived consequences are used to prove S², the system is circular. The ontology remains `[I/C]` until discriminating external tests outperform alternative geometries.

Strictly separate:

- `φ,ν` chart coordinates;
- `Φ,V` finite-node capacities;
- mathematical DoF;
- physical DoF;
- model possibility coordinates;
- lived capacities.

### Is→ought

A possibility space says what may occur, not what should occur. Non-extraction is chosen and empirically investigated, not mathematically entailed.

### Emergence

Coherence and stability do not themselves establish emergent causal standing. Macro-level claims require a map, intervention or perturbation, lower-law closure, costs, baselines, and kill criteria.

### Modal collapse

The typed-existence cascade blocks movement from description to actuality without intermediate evidence.

### Hidden S² dependence

The clean DoF proposal survives even if S² is replaced. That independence is a virtue: S² becomes one candidate model rather than the Ground.

A7 and exit remain constitutive. If the kernel is made unkillable, compulsory, or self-certifying, it contradicts itself.
