### MT-1: AM-GM proves the minimum — it does not prove the tendency.

The Steel Thread (Link 7) states: the equator is the Hamiltonian minimum. Link 3 (T3) states: systems evolve toward the minimum.

**The gap:** AM-GM establishes that φ + ν ≥ 2. It does not establish that physical systems *move toward* 2. A minimum is a stable equilibrium — it describes where systems settle if they are subject to dynamics. But the dynamics themselves are not derived from the axioms.

T3 (Doc 26) states: *"Systems nearer the minimum (smaller (φ − ν)²) are more stable, persist longer, reproduce more, and outcompete systems further from it. Therefore (φ − ν)² → 0 over evolutionary time."*

The "therefore" is doing work that is not structurally present. The argument is:
1. Systems at the minimum are more stable
2. Stable systems outcompete unstable ones
3. Therefore the population converges to the minimum

This is a standard evolutionary argument. But it requires:
- A selection mechanism (competition, reproduction, differential survival)
- An inheritance mechanism
- A mutation mechanism

These are not provided by the axioms. They are provided by the Replicator Stack (Doc 18), but the Replicator Stack is not formally derived from A0-C0.

**Status:** Gap. The teleological tendency is the framework's core claim and is not fully derived from the axioms. It is plausible and consistent with evolutionary theory, but the derivation from A0 to "F₅ exists" requires the Replicator Stack as an additional premise.
