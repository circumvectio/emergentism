## 1. What This Wave Was

This wave was a **hardening phase**, not an expansion phase.

Its job was to make the reconciliation layer:

- easier to attack formally
- harder to overclaim publicly
- easier to reproduce from outside the corpus
- cleaner to route for agents, operators, and first-contact readers

The phase did **not** try to prove the whole worldview at once. It tried to make the strongest bounded claims legible, attackable, and non-inflationary.

The corpus now speaks more cleanly about the formula hierarchy:

- canonical operational law remains primary
- canonical source-foundation mapping remains explicit

This is a real correction. Earlier drift risked treating the explanatory companion as if it had already become doctrine.

### B. Operator overread

The operator audit now makes the key correction explicit:

`2 cosh(u) = 2/sin(theta)` is an exact **potential identity** across charts, but not a full flat-Hamiltonian equivalence.

This prevented a serious category mistake from becoming a stable public claim.

### C. Boilerplate execution drift

Stale execution language has been removed from:

- Academy front-door surfaces

Those surfaces now behave like:

The corpus now routes more honestly:
