The geometry is fine as written everywhere: `B = 2/(φ+ν)` maxes at `φ=ν` is unconditional AM-GM on the sphere. What is *not* fine is the **silent transfer** of that equator-optimum onto real finite systems (civilizations, brains, markets, meshes, agents, ecosystems). 53 passages assert "the equator is where real systems last / are optimal / must be" with no membership test — and aggregate markets (Munnell panel, `d² > 0` trough) are a live falsification of the *unscoped* claim. **The `[A]` geometric vs `[S]` empirical-transfer split is SOUND and NOT a dodge.** It correctly localizes the conditionality in the `P∞ → P_node` transfer step; it survives the circularity charge (the conditions are independently checkable system properties — binding budget? factors substitute? surplus storable? — which is exactly why Munnell can falsify it); it has a live kill-criterion. It is **incomplete, not empty.**

**REQUIRED CORRECTION (must ride into the canonical home before this packet is closed).** The three stated conditions — **(i) conservation-coupled, (ii) complementary, (iii) costly-in-excess** — are **necessary but not sufficient**. They under-determine the payoff/cost *functional*. On the same sphere, under the same `φ·ν=1` and complementarity:
- `output = φ·ν` → **flat** (no optimum anywhere — the "equal dignity" reading),
- `output = min(φ,ν)` → **Leontief plateau/kink** at balance, **no decline past it**,
- `output = φ+ν` → **corner**, rewards imbalance (this is γ, the cost — what a specialization system maximizes).

A perfectly complementary Leontief system with wasted surplus satisfies **all three** stated conditions (budget-bound, needs both jointly, excess is costly) yet yields only a plateau — "a master carpenter with too many tools does not produce *less*." The three-part test would therefore **wrongly certify** such a system as equator-optimal when it has only a kink. The note collapses "declines past balance" (the hump — needs the evenness/γ metric specifically) back into "costly excess" (satisfied by mere conjunction/plateau), silently dropping the framework's own hard-won concession (Dyadic Law §5b; VERDICT_BALANCE.md caveat 2): *conjunction ✓; symmetric balance-peak ✗, even conceptually.*

**The correction:** add a **fourth, independent condition on functional shape**. Split (iii) into:
- **(iii-a)** excess is costly — *rules out the flat/storable plateau*;
- **(iii-b)** the cost is the **symmetric, ratio-metric evenness price** — cost `= γ = (φ+ν)/2` (equivalently payoff tracks `B = 2/(φ+ν)`), penalizing over-Φ **and** over-ν **one-for-one** — *rules out the min-plateau and the additive-corner*.

Only **(iii-b)** actually produces the downslope the "hump" claims. **Files to update for the correction:** `00_THE_BALANCE_OPTIMUM_IS_CONDITIONAL.md` §3 "the one line" (the (i)/(ii)/(iii) list) and the §4b transfer table. This is a canon edit — **staged, not applied** — for Yves.
