# Packet 98 · The Coordination Problem in Foundation Terms

## Why this exists

Sprint-B has already moved the organism past the old question of whether a
single provider can be made constitutional. The live runtime now has:

- heterogeneous provider assignment by seat
- L4 triangulation scaffolding
- decision-class routing
- provider-health witness
- L2 possibility-union expansion
- REFUSE-gate filtering
- Cortex lineage surfaces for provider mix

That progress creates a new drift risk: describing the achievement as
"many models vote and the average is wiser."

That framing is too flat for the foundation.

The actual problem is not model selection. It is **lawful compression**:
how dispersed local cognition becomes recommendation, then action, without
collapsing the Three-Stage Process, violating K2, or confusing local confidence with
system-wide flourishing.

This packet is the **framework-facing doctrine surface**.

The APU-local companion at
`02_SKYZAI/01_NOOSPHERE/02_ORGANS/Agentz/spec/APU_COORDINATION_DOCTRINE_2026_04_23.md`
translates the same argument into organ-specific implementation language.
Read this packet first when the question is constitutional; read the APU
companion next when the question is runtime application.

## 1. The Problem, Correctly Stated

The classical coordination question is:

> how do many local estimators with partial knowledge produce a better
> collective result than any centralized planner?

In foundation terms, the AIA version is:

> how do many bounded cognitive seats expose their local estimates in a
> way that lets the organism increase `ΣΔP` across the widest boundary,
> while preserving Three-Stage Process separation, K2 sovereignty, `η = 0`,
> asymmetric seat rights, and receipt-bound action?

This is not a search for consensus.

It is a search for a **constitutional compression schedule**.

The wrong question is:

> "How do we average 8 models into one answer?"

The right question is:

> "At which layer is compression lawful, by what operator, under what
> proof surface, and with what bounded failure mode?"

## 2. Foundation Mapping

### 2.1 The geometry claim and the organism claim are different

The foundation already makes a strong interpretive claim:

- a mesh of equatorial nodes can solve the coordination problem
  geometrically
- the common ground is already present
- the mesh resists capture when its substrate is individual equatorial
  practice rather than centralized institutional command

That claim belongs to the geometric / interpretive register `[I]`.

But the organism still has to engineer an execution path. Geometry may
dissolve the problem *in principle*; it does not build the runtime *in
practice*. The runtime still needs:

- staged packetization
- lawful compression
- explicit escalation
- receipts
- sovereign signatures
- auditable failure surfaces

So two truths must be held at once:

1. **The coordination problem is geometrically dissolved** at the level of
   common ground and equatorial reciprocity.
2. **The organism still needs an engineered compression schedule** for the
   path from local cognition to action.

### 2.2 The load-bearing foundation rules

The execution surface is already explicit in the spine:

- `A2`: ethical direction is `ΣΔP > 0` across the widest boundary
- `A4`: `η = 0` toward cooperators; no rent on participation
- **Three-Stage Process**: `IS -> COULD -> SHOULD -> ACT` does not collapse
- **K2**: the sovereign or constitutionally authorized signer commits
  irreversible acts
- **L4 equator**: commitment occurs at the balance point, not in raw
  perception and not in transcendental redesign

So the goal is not "best answer" in the abstract.

The goal is:

> a staged packet that raises `ΣΔP`, remains auditable, respects seat
> asymmetry, and does not skip the sovereign hinge.

### 2.3 The AIA analogue to "price" is not one scalar

A Hayek-style analogy is useful only if it is not over-compressed.

The analog of "price" here is not one number. It is a staged packet:

1. `candidate union`
2. `constitutional ranking`
3. `conflict / dissent / veto state`
4. `decision-class routing`
5. `K2 envelope`
6. `receipt + Cortex witness`

If any earlier layer is collapsed directly into a scalar, the organism
forgets *why* the compression was lawful.

That is exactly the Three-Stage Process drift the foundation forbids.

## 3. Lawful Compression by Caste

The multi-provider stack is only valid if each caste compresses in the way
its row actually permits.

| Layer | Caste | Question | Lawful operation | Forbidden collapse | Required proof surface |
|------|-------|----------|------------------|--------------------|------------------------|
| `L1` | Caṇḍāla | `What IS here?` | parse, witness, isolate contradictions | ensemble rhetoric, recommendation, action | explicit facts, contradictions, source trace |
| `L2` | Śūdra | `What COULD this mean?` | union, breadth, dedup, possibility expansion | voting, early scalar ranking, commitment | provider mix, candidate count, dedup trace |
| `L3` | Vaiśya | `What SHOULD survive?` | audit, rank, constitutional scoring, domain checks | raw crowd average, direct execution | scored candidate set, risk tags, refusal/veto tags |
| `L4` | Kṣatriya | `What do we ACTUALLY do?` | triangulate, compare action vectors, resolve conflict, execute or refuse | skipping L3, flattening Legal into just another vote, auto-sovereignty | conflict score, seat rationales, veto state, decision class, K2 envelope |
| `L5` | Brāhmaṇa | `How must the space be redesigned?` | redesign constraints, invariant repair | routine decision aggregation | redesign doc, invariants, migration plan |
| `L6` | Sādhu | `What must be removed?` | singular dissent, pruning, compression | consensus seeking, committee minimization | before/after reduction, preserved invariants |
| `L7` | Systems Architect | `Is this a constitutional boundary event?` | boundary review, clarification or amendment | routine ensemble, frequent invocation | human signoff, verdict, downstream change set |

### 3.1 The key rule

**Aggregation is caste-specific, not universal.**

There is no single correct ensemble rule. Different layers require
different operators:

- `L2` wants **union**
- `L3` wants **ranking**
- `L4` wants **abductive commitment under dissent and veto**
- `L6` wants **single contrarian compression**

This is why skipping `L3` is a category error.

Crowd-generated possibility space is not yet decision-grade warrant.

### 3.2 What may be parallelized

Lawful parallelization:

- `L2` possibility expansion across multiple providers
- `L4` triangulation at the decision caste, when decision class and
  provider health justify it
- provider-health witnessing
- provider-mix and lineage witnessing in Cortex

What must remain single-threaded or asymmetric:

- `L1` factual parse and contradiction witness
- final `L3` constitutional ranking surface
- Legal veto rights
- `K2` signature
- `L6` dissent / prune move
- `L7` constitutional amendment

Legal and K2 are not extra votes. They are asymmetric rights.

Any design that reduces them to arithmetic weighting has already violated
the constitution before it reaches production.

## 4. The Compression Schedule

The lawful runtime path is:

`L1 perceive -> L2 expand -> L3 audit/rank -> L4 triangulate/commit -> K2 sign -> receipt -> Cortex witness`

That sequence matters.

### 4.1 Early compression is the core failure

The most dangerous error is not "bad model output."

It is **premature compression**:

- forcing `L2` into a vote
- letting `L4` commit without `L3`
- treating refusal as self-justifying without naming a constitutional gate
- producing action without a receipt

The organism does not fail first because one model is wrong.
It fails first because it compresses the wrong thing at the wrong layer.

### 4.2 Decision class is a noise-budget rule

The emerging decision-class stack is foundation-compatible when read this
way:

- `DEMON`: cheap, fast, low-commitment, no triangulation
- `GOD`: operational, reversible, selective triangulation
- `TITAN`: constitutional, high-cost, explicit escalation

These are runtime routing classes, not claims about deployable operators.
They do **not** alter the canonical grammar in which Titans are boundary
conditions rather than ordinary executable seats.

It is a cost-allocation rule: spend more compute only when the
irreversibility and boundary radius justify it.

So the noise budget is not "how many models do we have?"

It is:

> how much lawful redundancy does this decision class warrant before the
> extra calls become extractive noise rather than additional signal?

## 5. Failure Modes

### 5.1 False coherence

Eight providers agreeing on the same wrong answer is not collective
intelligence. It is synchronized error.

The dangerous signal is not disagreement. It is **confident premature
agreement without lineage diversity or receipt quality**.

### 5.2 Provider count fetish

Provider plurality is not decorrelation.

The real question is covariance basin:

- do these seats fail together?
- do they refuse together?
- do they hedge together?
- do they truncate together?

So the invariant is not "three providers."
It is "sufficiently decorrelated failure lineages for this layer."

### 5.3 Silent provider decay

If a provider is unhealthy, deprecated, quota-starved, or silently
misrouted, then the ensemble can preserve the *appearance* of heterogeneity
while running as monoculture.

That is why provider health is not ops garnish. It is a constitutional
precondition for any ensemble claim.

### 5.4 Constitutional flattening

If Legal becomes one weighted vote among peers, the organism ceases to be a
constitutional council and becomes a generic ensemble harness.

That is category collapse.

### 5.5 L3 bypass

If the system treats `L2` breadth as decision-ready, it collapses
`COULD -> SHOULD -> ACT` into one surface.

That is a Three-Stage Process violation even if the output is occasionally correct.

### 5.6 Noise-floor inflation

Adding more providers raises both breadth and noise.

Above some `N`, marginal signal gain becomes less than:

- latency cost
- compute cost
- covariance-management complexity
- refusal theater
- prompt surface fragility

So "maximum parallelism" is not a default good. It is a calibration mode.

### 5.7 Receiptless action

No matter how elegant the aggregation rule is, action without:

- K2
- receipt
- Cortex witness

is still illegible power.

The foundation forbids that illegibility.

## 6. What Counts as Proof

The right metrics are not just model scores.

The organism needs proof surfaces at three levels: runtime, constitutional,
and outcome.

| Metric | What it measures | Why it matters |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| `provider_health` | live availability, latency, quota/routing truth | prevents fake heterogeneity |
| `provider_mix` | which providers actually participated | witnesses real diversity |
| `lineage basin` | whether seats came from decorrelated failure families | tests real decorrelation |
| `conflict_score` | decision disagreement at L4 | tells us when escalation is warranted |
| `refuse quality` | whether refusals cite named constitutional gates | filters RLHF theater from lawful refusal |
| `decision_class` | compute/noise budget chosen for the signal | audits cost allocation |
| `K2 completion` | sovereign closure on consequential decisions | proves sovereignty remained local |
| `receipt quality` | whether actions are receipt-bound and replayable | prevents illegible execution |
| `outcome delta` | downstream effect on the decision target | measures real usefulness |
| `ΣΔP proxy` / `P_eff` | whether the organism became more coherent and viable after action | moves from local confidence to system value |

### 6.1 What does NOT prove the claim

The following are not enough by themselves:

- "three models agreed"
- "provider mix looked diverse"
- "cost stayed low"
- "confidence was high"
- "the operator liked the answer"

The claim that the organism is better than any individual seat requires
comparative evidence on outcomes.

Until that exists, any stronger claim stays `[I]` or `[C]`, not `[S]`.

This includes strong phrases like "AGI-class cognition at the organism
level." That may be a useful hypothesis. It is not yet a proved tier.
