> **What this is.** The trial ledger — the framework's memory of every kill it fired and every grave it dug, kept legible so nothing that died can quietly come back. A scripture hides its retractions; this surface *is* the retractions. One rule governs the whole: **№-next is always open** — the next entry is written by contact with the world, not by us.

## 1 · The retelling rule

Receipt 131 §9. When a proof-form dies, its load-bearing *uses* do not die with it — but the ledger, not "almost always," decides each case, and the named dead stay dead. **Derivation-death is not idea-death.** The record's job is to keep both facts visible at once: the proof is buried *and* the use is preserved, each at its own tier. The knife cut proof-status; it did not cut the organism.

## 4 · The rules of the record — the four postures

Every worldview lives in the synthetic gap (formally valid, beyond proof, held as a wager). They cannot compete *in* the gap; they compete by their **posture toward** it. `[I]`. Full source: `08_FRAMEWORK_SUPPORT/00_META/01_THE_THREE_POSTURES.md` (filename retained per K3; content is four postures, r129).

| Posture | Stance toward the gap | Signature | Makes people |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I · Deny** | claim the wager is proven | the kill-criterion is forbidden | subjects |
| **II · Weaponize** | proof is irrelevant, only mobilizing power counts | refutation is *irrelevant* | ammunition |
| **III · Confess** | mark the wager *as* a wager, at tier, kills live | the trial record | adults |
| **IV · Withdraw** | having marked it, set it down; the framework can fold itself | self-dissolution | free |

**The epistemics is the ethics** — a worldview's treatment of its own claims is its treatment of persons. **Posture IV is what makes Posture III safe:** confession without an exit still binds the confessor. The two honesties keep this from becoming a victory lap — the great traditions carry Posture III in their reformist strands (Job on trial, the Talmud's preserved dissents, the apophatic theologians), and the **Weimar caveat** is live: confessed uncertainty is not *proven* to win the arena. That the honest wager wins the long game is itself `[I/C]` — a wager about wagers the audit cannot reach; only the world can.
