# Receipt 155 — Grand Puzzle Assembly and Application Boundary

## Disposition

The dimension-first canon now has one non-authoritative assembly ledger joining
its typed owners, exact results, interpretations, wagers, rivals,
discriminators, and kill criteria. The work **assembled the research map**; it
did not complete the territory, prove a theory of everything, or externally
calibrate a μ-crossing, force correspondence, ontology, or ethic.

The eleven open contacts `GP-01…GP-11` are now **packet-complete and
evidence-open**. Each packet states what contact could mean, its strongest
rival, a discriminator, a kill, and what survives if it fails. Empty evidence
is never represented as successful emergence.

## Exact seam and open seam

Three saturation/contrast results are now separated from the universal
aperture wager:

1. On the reciprocal chart, `φν=1` and movement toward either extreme sends
   the balance score toward zero. This is analytic chart structure only.
2. For `P_node=ΦV` under the independently declared budget `Φ+V≤1`, either
   pole has product zero and the symmetric point maximizes the product. Without
   that budget, `Φ=V=1` is a counterexample to pole exhaustion.
3. In an Abelian gauge patch, a pure gauge `A=dλ` has local curvature
   `F=dA=0`; global topology can still carry observable holonomy.

The shared proposition — that exhaustive symmetry or undifferentiated
potential universally opens a new effective freedom — remains
`SC-CANDIDATE [C]`. It receives no theorem status by analogy. Every proposed
instance must name its state space, contrast, saturation observable, new
freedom, lower-law recovery, rival, prediction, and kill.

## External neighbors, not validators

The external calibration map now distinguishes three useful neighbors:

- Anderson's hierarchy argument motivates studying autonomy across scales but
  does not establish any Emergentist rung or crossing.
- Page–Wootters relational time is a mechanism neighbor for the D3/D4 problem;
  it neither proves the dimension census nor identifies the μ seam.
- Gauge curvature supplies a rigorous local saturation/contrast example while
  the Aharonov–Bohm/global-topology caveat prevents overgeneralization.

The Peters–Büchel intertemporal-choice result remains a component contact for
model-mediated future influence, not evidence of physical retrocausality.
