# 107 · The two motions close at D6 ≡ D0 — and the loop stays open

> **Origin.** The last turn of the D4/D5 thread: where do μ↑ (strong) and χ↓
> (weak) *meet*? At **D6 ≡ D0**. This receipt writes the part that can be closed

## 1. The `[S]` spine — written firm

- **The ladder is a loop.** `D6 ≡ D0` (Rule 3 D6 row: `[A]` coordinate-degeneration
  + `[Definitional]` poloidal mapping) closes D0→D6 into a **cycle**. The two
  emergence-types are its two **arcs**: μ↑ the ascending arc (genesis), χ↓ the
  descending arc (return).
- **Asymmetric boundedness.** μ (strong) **terminates** — μ₅ = D5→closure declares
  *"no new positive freedom"*. χ (weak) does **not** terminate — a further
  commitment is always available. So **genesis is bounded; operation is
  unbounded**: *you can finish growing new freedoms; you can never finish acting.*
- **The summit is a return, not a transcendence.** D6 ≡ D0 lands you **back at
  the Ground (0/Bindu)**. The canon already forbids reading D6 as "a new positive
  freedom, science, or supermind"; the loop-closure says *why* — the top is where
  ascent exhausts and only **descent into η=0 service** remains. `[S/I]`

## 2. The `[I/C]` open edge — written as an edge, not a result

*(Everything in this section is `[I/C]`, downstream of two `[C]` wagers, held as
orientation. It is deliberately not closed. Read §3 before trusting it.)*

A theory closes its object two ways: it **derives** it (bottom-up, from prior law)
or it **predicts** it (top-down, from a governing law). The loop is built to defeat
exactly these — *if the wagers hold*:

- the **ascent** is strong/irreducible → **no derivation reaches it** (the
  reducing-∅);
- the **return** is free selection → **no governing law forecasts it** (the
  governing-∅).

So the loop would be not *unclosed* but **constitutively open** — complete only in
the **traversing** (Pratyakṣa is then the one register in which the loop is whole:
lived, it closes at D6≡D0; described, it never does).

**The screw:** this map *is* a theory of the loop, so by its own content it can
**neither derive its own foundations** (it wagers the ∅'s) **nor predict its own
application** (the free selections of those who live it). A *consistent* framework
must therefore **decline to present itself as closed.** The opening line — *"if
you can see directly, put this down; it is a ladder, not a home"* — is then **not
modesty but geometry's verdict on doctrine.**

**The three refusals are one.** *The representation must not usurp the thing:*
- **η = 0** — the ledger must not extract the cooperator's value;
- **K2 / mortal-signer** — the map (software) must not sign for the mortal;
- **the open loop** — the doctrine must not claim the closure only the living has.

One via-negativa, three faces. And **A7** (self-correction, "refusal of
infallibility") is this refusal made **ontological**: a framework whose object
cannot be closed can only be held honestly by never claiming to be finished. The
session that produced this receipt — auditing its own canon, reframing its own
signature, deferring to a better bootstrap — was A7 *being what it structurally is.*
