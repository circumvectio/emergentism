# 110 · Formal-logic audit applied — three golden seams

> the tautology-of-the-frame move, (b) a formal-logic classification of the
> framework's load-bearing symbols, and (c) a compass/map audit. The audit found
> that **most of what it surfaced was already settled in canon** (the `φ·ν=1`

## 0. What the audit found, and what it didn't

The formal-logic audit classified each load-bearing symbol of the framework.
Cross-checked against the Settled Canon Registry and the Open Canon Covenant, the
result was:

- **Already settled (no edit needed):** the `φ·ν=1` double category error
  (convicted by C2, registered, laundered into the Rule-6 tautology ruling); the
  frame/content distinction (the whole point of the Open Canon Covenant); the
  CTMU fork (Covenant §3); the `⊙ = • × ○` product-typing; the Varṇa dispatch as
  typed sum-type; the "categorical completeness" overclaim (already downgraded to
  `[I]` in doc 09 v2.0).
- **Three real gaps (this receipt):** the framework invokes Gödel II and Tarski
  without stating whether it meets their applicability conditions; the Covenant
  §1 parenthetical lumps epistemically heterogeneous symbols under one
  "self-reference at the base" header; the Covenant §4.3 cites "(Gödel)"
  unconditionally as the reason descriptions don't close.

**The pattern of all three:** the canon is honest about *what* it claims, but
invokes heavy machinery (incompleteness theorems, tautology verdicts) without
declaring the *conditions* under which that machinery applies to itself. The
repair is the same in each case: **state the antecedent; the consequents are then
earned.** This is A7 (self-correction) applied to A7's own formal backing.
