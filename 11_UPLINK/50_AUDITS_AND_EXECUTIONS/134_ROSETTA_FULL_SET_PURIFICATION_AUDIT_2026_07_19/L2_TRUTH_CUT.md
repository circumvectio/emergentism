> "Plato was partially right about forms — but it is not forms that exist, it is degrees of freedom; and in those, everything that can exist — logically coherent and consistent and emergent — exists, till the limits."

1. **"Exists" does two jobs.** (a) Ontic: *degrees of freedom exist* (world-furniture). (b) Logical: *everything coherent exists in them* (admissibility). Read (b) as possibility → tautology: all possibles are possible. Read (b) as actuality → plenitude. The sentence runs on the equivocation.
2. **Plenitude in new clothes — and it self-destructs.** "Everything that can exist exists" is Lovejoy's principle / modal realism without the worlds. Its own negation ("some coherent possibles are never actualized") is also coherent; if all coherent things exist, thesis and negation both exist — contradiction. Kant's cut lands: existence is not a predicate; coherence cannot be defined into actuality (the Anselm structure).
