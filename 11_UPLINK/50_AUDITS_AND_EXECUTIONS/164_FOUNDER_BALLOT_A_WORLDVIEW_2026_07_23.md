# Founder Ballot A — the worldview gates

## Why this exists, and why it is split

A 16-agent council decided all twelve founder gates into one ruling each and
staged them for your countersignature (`00_THE_TWELVE_RULINGS_2026_07_22.md`).
That single sheet was then archived on the pure-worldview boundary, because it
mixed **worldview** gates with **Skyzai product** gates and so could not be a
standalone authority. An independent 26-agent verify→adversary council
(2026-07-23) confirmed **all thirteen items are genuinely still open** — none
had been quietly resolved — and its adversary found specific framing defects in
the staged rulings, folded into each entry below as **"Before you sign."**

This ballot carries only the gates whose home is the worldview. The product
gates are on **Ballot B** (receipt 165). Two gates straddle: their worldview
half is here, their product half is on B, cross-referenced.

**Nothing here is decided.** The prior council's ruling is shown as a
*recommendation*, never a verdict — receipt 141A froze a writer for treating
informal assent as a signature, and this ballot exists precisely so that does
not recur. Each gate ends in a blank line only you can fill.

## FG-2 · Who must sign a consequential act? *(the membrane)*

**The question.** For an irreversible act, who is the required final authorizer?

**Options.**
- **Universal mortal signature** — a living human authorizes every consequential act, unrestricted.
- **Two-mortal fail-closed quorum** — public acts need a bound receipt from **≥2 distinct enrolled natural persons**, failing closed until a second is enrolled; private acts carry the owner's own signature.
- **Private-DAC-only gate** — only single-owner private entities need a mortal; public DACs substitute PRISM.
- **`founder_signature_required=false`** in its narrow sense — the two-mortal quorum *is* the signature.
- **Option C — founder alone**, the ≥2 rule suspended (its own text calls this "invalid at the packet layer").

**Prior council recommendation (R-FG2), not a decision.** Two-mortal fail-closed quorum; the present one-councilor state re-cast as a **dated emergency with a signed 90-day expiry**, the fail-closed bar in force throughout; the flag renamed `founder_signature_required=false` so it can never read as "no human signs."

**Dissent preserved (Operator).** An emergency mode with no *enforceable* deadline **is** the constitution — the sunset binds only the person it constrains. If the second councilor is never enrolled, this ruling has dressed the permanent one-signer state in better paperwork.

**Before you sign.** The Operator's dissent is the crux: the ruling's safety depends on a second enrolled person actually existing. Confirm whether one does, or the 90-day expiry is self-enforced only.
