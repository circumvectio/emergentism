## W-new — Single-Signer Availability Risk (NEW · flag)

**Frame:** The organism's ability to *act* is gated on the founder's
wallet being reachable and operable. Today there is no key-sharding, no
threshold scheme, no time-locked fallback. If the founder is unavailable
(travel, illness, lost keys, compromised device), every pending approval
either expires (no action) or blocks indefinitely.

**Why this is a weakness and not a threat:** It is a property of the
current architecture, not an external force. The organism deliberately
concentrates signing authority; that is the constitutional choice. But the
*operational expression* of that choice has gaps that should be named
honestly.

**Candidate remediations (not decisions):**

- Time-locked fallback signer (secondary wallet that becomes valid after
  N days of primary wallet inactivity) — requires constitutional amendment
- Shamir-style k-of-n sharding across founder-controlled devices —
  preserves K2 but removes single-device SPOF
- Documented expire-rather-than-act policy — acceptable for non-urgent
  decisions, unacceptable for time-critical ones
- Founder-signed advance-approval envelopes for pre-specified surfaces
  (e.g. pre-sign N routine operations with explicit scope) — narrow
  delegation that still respects per-action binding

**Why it belongs in SWOT now:** Before Sprint-A, the question was
theoretical — the organism didn't yet sign for real. Now that the K2 path
runs end-to-end, the availability of the signer becomes the next
binding constraint.

**Weight:** NEW, flagged for founder discussion. Do not attempt
remediation without a signed constitutional directive.
