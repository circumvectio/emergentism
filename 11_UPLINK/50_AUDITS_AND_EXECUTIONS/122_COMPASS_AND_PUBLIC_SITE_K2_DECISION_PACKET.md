## Required corrections before any public build or corpus-wide canon mutation

### C1 — Scope every generator at point of use

Receipt 121 correctly narrows G1 to complementary couplings after an auditable
AND/OR (`σ`) check and register-types its claims. Its one-sentence compression
must carry that scope or link immediately to it. It may not state unqualified
"a factor at a pole is death" as a universal cosmological law.

### C2 — Keep the Compass's authority rule typed

`00_THE_COMPASS.md` says one natural person signs every irreversible act. That
is a private-DAV rule. Any public-DAV-facing rendering must instead state that a
complete valid bound PRISM decision receipt from at least two natural-person
councilors binds the exact public consequence; PRISM records/verifies only and
software/agents do not sign, authorize, or execute on their own.

### C3 — Keep conjectural claims visibly conjectural

The Compass's Ω/syntropy bearing is a wager, and G2 plus the recurrence/AND
welds are open or conjectural. No public page may present those as established
or as an empirical outcome. Each must retain its tier and a concrete falsifier.

### C4 — Replace performed openness with auditable openness

A public falsifier count, if built, must be calculated from a versioned source
ledger with date, source location, resolution state, and a count that can reach
zero. A static counter, an unverifiable private-artifact state, or a counter
forced nonzero is not evidence of openness.

### C5 — Preserve source/build provenance

Before any public presentation, place the Compass prototype and its source under
the active owner lane with a reproducible local build command, pinned runtime,
and content hash. A private hosted artifact URL alone is not a repository-backed
or independently verifiable public artifact.

### C6 — Do not turn lateral recurrence into convergence-as-proof

The public IA may show six registers laterally, but must label recurrence as one
interpretive frame across registers—not independent confirmation, a summit, or
proof by repetition.
