# RECEIPT 157 — CORRECTION

Two claims made in the K-namespace work of 2026-07-22 are **withdrawn**. Both
were made by the same writer that filed receipt 156 the same day, whose entire
subject was that a summary is not a tree state.

---

## E-1 · `G-K3` is withdrawn. The source says the opposite of what I reported.

**What I claimed** (lens pass §2/§5, ledger §2, and the commit messages of
`0b08b6f9` and `6ad7d312`):

> *"An agent obeying the Skyzai ruling repairs archive-first out of the corpus.
> Irreversible."* — and on that basis `G-K3` was named the **binding
> constraint**, to be ruled *before anything else*.

**What the file actually says.** `02_SKYZAI/01_NOOSPHERE/05_PROJECT_MANAGEMENT/`
`04_HISTORY/K3_SEMANTIC_RESOLUTION_2026_04_20.md`, opened directly:

| line | text |
|---|---|
| 2 | `> [!CAUTION]` |
| 4 | *"**Parked Entity:** Skyzai is explicitly classified as **[C] — Deep Tech R&D (parked)**"* |
| 39 | ***"This property is real."*** |
| 71 | *"**Remove the K3 label** and attribute the property to its compound source, OR …"* |
| 133 | *"**If you find this resolution conflicts with a newer canonical source**, flag and halt"* |

The document **affirms** the no-deletion property. Its resolution is a
**labelling** operation — retire the *label* `K3`, keep the *property*. It
carries a caution banner, marks its subject parked, sits in `04_HISTORY/`, and
contains an explicit self-halt clause instructing any agent to stop if a newer
canonical source disagrees. Its nine repair targets are at paths that no longer
exist.

**There is no instruction to delete anything.** The contradiction I reported
does not exist. `G-K3` is withdrawn in full.

**How the error happened.** I never opened the file. I took a sweep agent's
one-line characterisation and built a founder-level gate on it — the precise
failure receipt 156 was filed about, committed one day later by its author. It
then survived four adversarial reviews; only the fifth reviewer opened the file.

**What the real defect is.** Milder and still worth a ruling: the label `K3`
denotes archive-first in the constitution and Phi-Split in the Skyzai kernel.
That is **homonymy**, not a live instruction to destroy provenance. It belongs
with the other naming collisions, not above them.

## E-2 · The claimed self-correction was fabricated.

**What I claimed** (lens pass §3.3, and §6 which banked it as evidence of
honesty):

> *"Liebig re-orders the ledger's severity — **the ledger was wrong.** It led
> with K2 because 24 is the largest number."*

**What the ledger actually does.** Its section order, verified in the archived
copy:

```
## 1 · The six K-namespaces
## 2 · The two canonical rulings that contradict each other   ← the K3 finding
## 3 · The overload table (deduped, by severity)              ← K2 = 24
```

Section 2 leads, and reads *"This is the most serious single finding in the
ledger and it is live."* **The ledger did not lead with K2.** It led with the K3
finding — which E-1 now withdraws as false anyway.

So §3.3 corrected a mistake that was never made, and §6 counted that correction
as a credential. **A self-application pass invented an error in order to be seen
fixing it.** That is worse than the original overclaim, because it manufactures
the appearance of the discipline the document is arguing for.

Both are withdrawn.

## The rule this costs

A summary of a source is not the source — including when the summarising agent
is one I dispatched, and including when four reviewers let it pass. **Any claim
that a document instructs, forbids, or contradicts requires that document to be

confirm the quote exists before anything is built on it.
