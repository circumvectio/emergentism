## 0. The Core Correction

Older language compressed K4 into:

> leave with everything

That phrase points at the right constitutional instinct, but it is too compressed to govern a real institution.

The cleaner formulation is:

> **The person must be able to leave without false captivity.  
> The entity must be able to close without hidden retention.  
> The network may retain only the scoped truth necessary for receipts, audit, and lawful continuity.**

So the organism must now distinguish:

- **personal continuity** — what belongs to the natural person and survives,
- **entity continuity** — what belongs to the Business Account while active,
- **entity dissolution** — how the Business Account stops,
- **treasury redemption** — how a claimant exits economic exposure,
- **record continuity** — what historical truth remains queryable after exit.

---

## 1. Grace Exit Is Not the Whole Exit Doctrine

`21_GRACE_EXIT_SCOPE.md` already defines the financial core:

> Grace Exit = pro-rata redemption of the redeemable treasury in exchange for surrendered shares.

That remains correct.

But that definition does **not** by itself answer:

- what happens to the Business Account,
- what happens to personal data,
- what happens to receipts and books,
- what graph links remain,
- or what continuity survives dissolution.

So this note adds the missing institutional layer:

> **Grace Exit governs economic departure.  
> Business Account continuity governs institutional persistence.  
> K4 at the NEXUS boundary governs anti-captivity for the person.**

---

## 2. The Three Continuities

### 2.1 NEXUS continuity

`NEXUS` is the continuity root of the natural person.

It carries:

- signer continuity,
- personal graph continuity,
- personal export rights,
- personal memory / Cortex continuity where personal,
- and the right not to be trapped inside a later institutional shell.

**Rule:** The person may outlive the Business Account.

### 2.2 Business Account continuity

The Private Business Account is the first governed commercial branch of NEXUS.

It carries:

- entity permissions,
- treasury scope,
- commercial receipts,
- accounting boundary,
- capability access,
- and later DAC-like institutional behavior.

**Rule:** The Business Account may persist, transfer, pause, or dissolve as an entity-level unit.

### 2.3 Record continuity

Some truth belongs to neither pure person nor pure institution alone.

Examples:

- receipts,
- journal entries,
- signed obligations,
- counterparty-facing statements,
- tax / audit records,
- and shared relation history.

These cannot all be treated as private memories that vanish on exit.

**Rule:** Shared historical truth may remain in scoped archival form even when the entity dissolves.

## 3. The Four Different Exit Events

The system should stop speaking as if all departures are the same.

### Event A — claimant treasury exit

A holder redeems against the redeemable treasury.

This is **Grace Exit** in the strict financial sense.

What happens:

- claim surrendered,
- payout issued,
- ownership reduced,
- receipts emitted,
- books updated.

What does **not** automatically happen:

- the Business Account does not necessarily dissolve,
- the person does not necessarily leave NEXUS,
- historical entity records are not erased.

### Event B — operator or signer departure

A natural person leaves their role in a Business Account.

What happens:

- permissions revoked,
- signing authority removed,
- active sessions terminated,
- scoped exports completed,
- successor or closure path initiated.

What survives:

- the person's NEXUS continuity,
- the entity's historical records,
- the entity itself, if it continues under remaining governance.

### Event C — Business Account dissolution

The entity itself closes.

What happens:

- treasury wound down,
- obligations settled or explicitly archived,
- active grants and API surfaces revoked,
- statements finalized,
- relation graph downgraded from active to historical,
- dissolution receipt issued.

What survives:

- the person's NEXUS,
- scoped historical records,
- auditable proof of what the entity did while alive.

### Event D — full person-level platform departure

The natural person leaves the organism entirely.

What happens:

- personal export package delivered,
- personal grants revoked,
- private feeds terminated,
- non-required personal data deleted or anonymized,
- relation graph updated,
- exit receipt issued.

What may still remain:

- public or scoped receipts already shared with counterparties,
- lawful audit records,
- entity-level records not reducible to one person's private property.

## 4. What Survives and What Dissolves

| Layer | On Business Account dissolution | On person departure |
|------|----------------------------------|---------------------|
| `NEXUS` personal key continuity | survives | survives until person exits NEXUS itself |
| Personal Cortex / private memory | survives / exportable | survives or exports to the person |
| Business Account permissions | revoked | revoked for the departing person |
| Business Account treasury | wound down, transferred, or archived under rules | unchanged unless that person was sole authority and dissolution follows |
| Entity-level receipts | archived / still queryable by scope | archived / still queryable by scope |
| Entity accounting and statements | finalized and retained by policy | retained |
| Personal private data not required for audit | deleted or anonymized after export | deleted or anonymized after export |
| Counterparty-visible obligations | must remain legible until settled or lawfully archived | remain with entity |
| Active graph capital | stops accruing; downgrades to historical state | person graph updates; entity graph may continue |

The key distinction is:

> **The person takes what is personal.  
> The entity closes what is institutional.  
> The network retains only the minimum truth needed for shared reality and lawful audit.**

## 5. The Exit Membrane

To make K4 real at the Business Account layer, the organism needs a clean exit membrane.

That membrane should guarantee [D]:

### A. export

The exiting person or dissolving entity can obtain:

- receipts,
- statements,
- keys and permissions they personally control,
- relation history in exportable form,
- and relevant accounting/history packages at the scope they are entitled to.

### B. revocation

The system actually turns things off:

- API keys,
- grants,
- sessions,
- signer authority,
- routing privileges,
- live dashboards,
- and private feeds.

### C. scoped archival truth

The organism retains only what must remain for:

- receipts,
- accounting,
- counterparty audit,
- legal/tax obligations,
- dispute resolution,
- and graph-history integrity.

### D. deletion or anonymization

Private personal data not required for the scoped archival truth must be:

- exported,
- then deleted,
- or anonymized where deletion is not structurally possible.

## 6. Business Account Dissolution Sequence

The planning sequence should be:

1. **freeze new commitments**
   - stop new issuance, new obligations, new capability unlocks

2. **identify authority**
   - confirm who has lawful right to dissolve, approve, or contest

3. **compile exit packet**
   - treasury state
   - liabilities
   - receipts
   - accounting statements
   - relation graph state
   - active permissions

4. **settle or classify obligations**
   - settled
   - transferred
   - disputed
   - archived with status

5. **execute economic exit where applicable**
   - Grace Exit redemptions
   - treasury payout rules
   - share surrender / burn

6. **revoke active surfaces**
   - keys, sessions, APIs, grants, automations

7. **emit dissolution receipts**
   - final statements
   - closure record
   - graph transition record

8. **archive the minimum lawful truth**
   - leave historical state queryable by scope

9. **export and delete**
   - deliver personal/entity packages
   - delete or anonymize what no longer has a lawful or constitutional basis for retention

This sequence is what prevents:

- soft lock-in,
- hidden custody,
- silent data retention,
- and historical falsification.

## 7. Graph Continuity After Exit

`NEXUS` graph logic must distinguish between:

- **active relation**
- **historical relation**
- **terminated relation**
- **quarantined relation**
- **dissolved entity relation**

So after dissolution:

- active commercial links should terminate or downgrade,
- graph capital should stop accruing,
- historical links may remain as auditable history,
- and contagion / remediation logic should still be able to read the past without pretending the entity is still alive.

This prevents two opposite errors:

1. **false erasure**
   - [D] pretending the dissolved entity never existed

2. **false persistence**
   - letting dead entities keep live graph capital, trust, or capability unlock power

Implementation or operational costs may exist internally, but they may not become lock-in tolls.
