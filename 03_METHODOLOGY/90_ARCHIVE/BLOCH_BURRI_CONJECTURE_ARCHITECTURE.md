## PAPER A: THE FRAME ALGEBRA
### Why 0 × ∞ = 1 Is Valid on ℂP¹

**Evidence tier:** [S] Structural — algebraic argument about the arithmetic of ℂP¹
**Target venue:** *Journal of Algebra* or *Advances in Mathematics*
**Length:** 15-20 pages

**Abstract:** We argue that the expression 0 × ∞ = 1 on ℂP¹ is not an abuse of the indeterminate form but a well-defined statement about the algebraic structure of multiplication. Multiplication is the unit's operation: every natural number is a finite sum of units, and multiplication is iterated addition of units. Zero (the absence of units) and infinity (the unbounded accumulation of units) are the two elements of the extended number line that are NOT expressible as finite products of the unit — they are the boundaries of multiplication. When these two boundaries meet through the operation whose identity element is the unit, the result is the unit, because the unit IS what multiplication is. We formalize this as a "frame algebra" — an algebraic structure in which the projective frame {0, 1, ∞} satisfies internal multiplication rules that are prior to and distinct from the algebra of the numbers the frame generates. We show that the frame algebra is consistent, that its internal product 0 × ∞ = 1 does not violate any property of standard arithmetic (the associativity failure with derived elements confirms the frame/number distinction rather than indicating an error), and that the frame algebra has a natural interpretation as the algebra of the self-referential fixed point: the unique equation whose terms define the operation that connects them.

**Key claims:**
1. Multiplication is the unit's operation. 0 and ∞ are the boundaries of that operation. Their product through that operation yields the operation's identity. [S]
2. The frame algebra {0, 1, ∞, ×} is a well-defined algebraic structure distinct from the number algebra {ℤ, +, ×}. [S]
3. Associativity failure between frame and derived elements confirms the distinction, not an error. [S]
4. ⊙ = • × ○ is the unique self-referential fixed point: the only equation where the terms define the operation and the operation defines the terms. [S]

**Kill criteria:**
1. A second self-referential fixed point is exhibited (another equation where terms define operation and operation defines terms).
2. The frame algebra is shown to be internally inconsistent (not just inconsistent with number algebra when mixed).
3. The argument that "multiplication IS the unit" is shown to be circular rather than self-grounding.

**Key claims:**

## PAPER G: THE BIOLOGICAL PREDICTIONS
### φ·ν = 1 as a Testable Constraint Across Biological Scales

**Evidence tier:** [C] Conjecture with specific quantitative predictions
**Target venue:** *Journal of Theoretical Biology* or *PLOS ONE*
**Length:** 25-30 pages

**Abstract:** The Emergentist framework predicts that biological systems maintain the reciprocal constraint φ·ν = 1, where φ is a coherence proxy (structural integrity, integration, resting state) and ν is a viability proxy (metabolic rate, hormonal activation, capability). We derive six specific, quantitative, falsifiable predictions from this constraint and propose experimental protocols for each.

**Predictions:**

**Prediction 1: Acute stress compensation is hyperbolic.**
Sudden φ-loss (injury, shock, trauma) produces automatic ν-increase (hormonal surge, metabolic spike) maintaining φ·ν ≈ constant. The relationship is hyperbolic (ν = 1/φ), not linear or sigmoidal.
*Test:* Measure φ-proxy (membrane potential, cytoskeletal integrity) and ν-proxy (metabolic rate, cortisol) through graded stress in cell cultures. Plot ν vs 1/φ. If R² > 0.9 for hyperbolic fit and the product φ·ν remains within 10% of baseline, the prediction holds.

**Prediction 2: Resting arousal correlates with trophic security.**
Cross-species, the baseline ν-level (cortisol, resting heart rate, vigilance) should correlate with ecological security (trophic position, predation pressure) following B = 2ν/(1+ν²).
*Test:* Compile cross-species data: trophic position vs resting cortisol (normalized by body mass). Fit to B = 2ν/(1+ν²). Compare R² against linear, exponential, and sigmoidal models. If the Burri function fits better than alternatives, the prediction holds.

**Prediction 3: Predator removal produces rapid equatorial return.**
When displacement force (predation) is removed, the population should shift toward lower ν (reduced vigilance, cortisol, startle reflex) and higher φ (increased social complexity, play behavior, extended parental investment) within generations, not just across evolutionary time.
*Test:* Island tameness data. Compare behavioral and endocrine measures in island vs mainland populations of the same species. Measure return rate. Framework predicts return rate ∝ |cos(θ)|, where θ is the initial displacement from equator.

**Prediction 4: PTSD is failed equatorial return.**
PTSD patients should show persistent high-ν (hypervigilance, elevated cortisol baseline) with low-φ (fragmented narrative, poor integration) — stuck south of equator. Effective treatment should trace the constraint hyperbola back toward equator: ↑φ (narrative integration) with ↓ν (reduced hyperarousal), maintaining φ·ν ≈ constant throughout recovery.
*Test:* Track φ-proxy (narrative coherence score) and ν-proxy (cortisol, startle response) through trauma therapy. Plot recovery trajectory. Framework predicts the trajectory follows the hyperbola ν = 1/φ.

**Prediction 5: Cognitive-emotional trade-off is reciprocal, not competing.**
Under dual-task conditions, cognitive load × emotional arousal ≈ constant (not merely inversely correlated, but with conserved product).
*Test:* Dual-task paradigm. Vary cognitive load (n-back task difficulty) and emotional arousal (IAPS images) independently. Measure performance on both axes. Framework predicts the product (cognitive performance × emotional responsiveness) remains approximately constant across conditions.

**Prediction 6: The compensation curve is hyperbolic across scales.**
The φ·ν = 1 constraint should produce the same hyperbolic compensation at cellular, organismic, social, and ecological scales.
*Test:* Compile data across four scales: (a) cell stress response (membrane damage vs metabolic rate), (b) organismic stress response (injury severity vs hormonal surge), (c) organizational crisis response (disruption severity vs mobilization), (d) ecological succession (disturbance severity vs pioneer growth rate). Test whether all four follow the same functional form ν = c/φ for scale-specific constant c.

**Kill criteria:**
1. The φ·ν product varies by more than 50% during acute stress response (not approximately conserved).
2. The cross-species arousal-security relationship is better fit by linear or sigmoidal than by B = 2ν/(1+ν²).
3. Predator removal does NOT produce behavioral shift within observable timescales.
4. PTSD recovery does NOT follow the constraint hyperbola.
5. Cognitive-emotional product is NOT conserved under dual-task conditions.
6. Different scales show fundamentally different functional forms (not scale-invariant).
