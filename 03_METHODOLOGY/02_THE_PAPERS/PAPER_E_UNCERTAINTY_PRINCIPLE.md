## 1. Individual means a declared system `[S/I]`

D3 assigns a quantum state to an individuated system `S`:

```text
ρ_S⪰0,             Trρ_S=1.
```

The boundary of `S` must be named. It may be a particle, field mode, composite
system, or subsystem; “individual” does not mean a classical point-particle,
separability, or a pure state.

The same state generates different probability distributions for different
measurements:

```text
p(k|ρ_S,M)=Tr(ρ_SE_k).
```

That is the selected D3 content. A written state assignment is an actual D4
inscription referring to it.

## 2. Position and momentum across D2–D4 `[A/I]`

For a nonrelativistic particle on `ℝᵈ`, D2 supplies position and momentum as
operators. On a suitable common domain,

```text
[X_i,P_j]=iℏδ_ijI,
σρ(X_i) · σρ(P_i) ≥ 1/2 |Tr(ρ[X_i,P_i])| = ℏ/2
```

D3 supplies both state-conditioned distributions for states in the relevant
domains with finite variances. It does **not** supply one simultaneously sharp
classical phase-space point `(x,p)`. The lower bound follows from
noncommutativity; in the usual position/momentum representation, the two
wavefunctions are Fourier transforms of one another.

So the sentence “D3 has no momentum” is false if it means the observable or
distribution is absent. Its defensible content is:

> **D2 defines momentum as an observable; D3 supplies its state-conditioned
> distribution; D4 is where a declared apparatus produces one spacetime-indexed
> outcome, momentum-transfer event, clock correlation, and record.**

This distinction clarifies what changes at the D3→D4 interface. It does not
solve or replace the Heisenberg–Robertson relation.

The viable extension is a `[C]` **time-seam conjecture**: a stationary or
constraint-satisfying D3 quantum state may yield D4 experienced dynamics
relationally through clock correlations and records. This is compatible with
known relational-time constructions but not derived by the D-registers.
Momentum and state entropy exist at D3 as an observable distribution and a
functional; actual momentum transfer and a directed entropy-production history
require D4. A model must recover standard clock and transfer statistics before
it can claim more.

## 3. The typed measurement interface `[A/I]`

For a quantum instrument `{𝓘_k}`,

```text

ρ'_k  = 𝓘_k(ρ)/p_k                 when p_k>0.
```

D2 contains the instrument specification. D3 contains `ρ` and the
instrument-conditioned predicted distribution. D4 contains the actual
preparation, apparatus, interaction, outcome token, time, provenance, and
custody. `μ₃` is the Emergentist name for this state-to-record boundary `[I]`;
it is not a measurement operator or a fundamental-collapse mechanism.

## 4. What this says about quantum gravity `[C]`

General relativity treats spacetime geometry as dynamical. Quantum general
relativity also has a predictive low-energy effective-field-theory regime.
Accordingly, “there is no quantum gravity at D4” is not a valid statement of
current physics, and the D-registers do not explain why a complete ultraviolet
theory remains open.

The narrower Emergentist conjecture is worth testing:

> A D3 quantum state and a single approximately classical D4 spacetime record
> are distinct types. Any proposed quantum-gravity bridge must show how the
> latter is recovered from the former without identifying a probability model
> with one realized history.

This is a research constraint, not a theory. It becomes a result only if a
model derives a quantitative discriminator, recovers general relativity and
low-energy quantum-gravity effective theory in their domains, and outperforms
rivals.

## 5. The Burri-chart fence

The reciprocal identity `φν=1` is not the uncertainty relation. For specially
parameterized minimum-uncertainty Gaussian or squeezed states, one may define
reciprocal dimensionless widths whose product is one. That is a coordinate
choice on a restricted equality family. It does not derive noncommutativity,
the Robertson bound, `ℏ`, quantum measurement, the D3 ordinal, or gravity.
