# The Unit as Frame: From Euclid's Monad to the 2019 SI

## Abstract

The history of zero is the canonical story of a boundary-behavior promoted to arithmetic citizenship, with one residue (0/0) marking where the promotion cannot complete. This paper assembles the parallel and neglected history of **one**, and argues it runs in the opposite direction. Greek arithmetic did not count the unit among the numbers: Euclid defines the monad as "that by virtue of which each of the things that exist is called one" and number as "a multitude composed of units" — the unit is the *measure* of number, not a number. The demotion of this role-distinction is historically datable, with Stevin's *L'Arithmétique* (1585) as its sharpest single document, and positional-decimal practice as its vehicle: by modernity, 1 is an unmarked point on the line. But the frame-character did not disappear; it migrated into measurement practice and was rediscovered operationally three times — in the ratio character of all measurement (the unit is stipulated, not measured), in dimensional analysis (laws must be invariant under change of units: Buckingham's theorem makes unit-independence a derivation engine), and in the 2019 redefinition of the SI (all seven base units now fixed by stipulating exact values of natural constants — the frame pinned to invariants rather than artefacts). Reading this arc through the frame/operand distinction of the companion paper yields a structural thesis: number systems have three boundary items, not two, and the third has been hidden in plain sight precisely because measurement works so well. The reading licenses no revision to arithmetic; its payoff is historiographic and conceptual — it explains why "1 is excluded from the primes," unit-invariance requirements, and stipulated-exactness clauses share one shape.

## 1. Introduction: an asymmetry in the historiography

Zero has biographies; one has a rap sheet. The literature on zero's promotion — Brahmagupta's rules, the positional placeholder, the residual indeterminacy of 0/0 — is rich and standard. The unit's converse trajectory (from explicitly *not a number* to unmarked number, while its original role quietly re-housed itself in metrology) has, to our knowledge, no comparable connected treatment. ## 2. The monad as measure (the role-distinction stated)

Euclid, *Elements* VII, Definitions 1–2 (Heath): the monad defined as that by which each existing thing is called one; number as multitude composed of monads. The unit is what counting is *done with*, not what is counted — presupposed by every number, element of none. Aristotle's gloss makes the role explicit: the one is the measure, and the measure is not among the measured in the same register (*Metaphysics*, Iota — verify the standard passage, c. 1052b–1053a, "to be one is to be a measure of a multitude"). This was a maintained position, not naivety: the "one is not a number" tradition persists through late antiquity and the medieval arithmetic textbooks. ## 3. The demotion (datable)

Two forces flatten the monad into a point on the line. Conceptually, Stevin's *L'Arithmétique* (1585) argues outright that the unit is a number — that number is continuous quantity and 1 divides like any other ["l'unité est nombre" — verify exact locus and wording]. Practically, positional-decimal computation makes the distinction unaffordable: an algorithm has no use for an item that is counted-with but not counted. By the time the number line becomes the default picture of number, the unit's frame-role is invisible — exactly the way zero's placeholder role once hid zero's operand pathologies.

## 4. The operational return: three rediscoveries

**4.1 Measurement is ratio to a stipulated unit.** Every measurement report has the form "x = n · [u]". The unit itself is not measured; it is *fixed* — the one quantity in the system exact by fiat. This is the monad's definition wearing modern clothes.

**4.2 Dimensional analysis.** Physical laws must be invariant under change of units; Buckingham (1914) turns this invariance into a derivation method (the Π-theorem: any physical relation is expressible in dimensionless combinations). The unit is precisely what equations must not depend on — the operational signature of a frame, not a datum. [Cite Buckingham, *Physical Review* 4: 345–376; Bridgman's *Dimensional Analysis* for the conceptual development — verify.]

**4.3 The 2019 SI.** The revised SI defines all seven base units by stipulating exact numerical values of seven constants (c, h, e, k_B, N_A, ΔνCs, K_cd) — stipulation made total and explicit, and relocated from artefacts (the prototype kilogram) to invariants of nature. [Cite BIPM *SI Brochure*, 9th ed., 2019.] The frame is not eliminated by precision metrology; it is pinned ever more deliberately.

## 5. The structural reading

Against the frame/operand criteria of the companion paper (constitutivity; composition-pathology; stratified cure): the unit is constitutive (no counting or measuring without it); its pathology is self-measurement (asking the unit's value *in its own system* is empty — 1 [u] per [u]; circular-definition episodes in metrology are this signature); and the cure is stratification — a stipulated stratum (definitions of units) held apart from the measured stratum (everything else), with the 2019 SI as the cleanest historical instance of the two strata being formally separated. Three boundary items, one signature: 0 promoted with a residue, ∞ tamed by compactification with a residue, 1 demoted and then operationally re-erected in metrology.

## 6. What the reading does not license

No revision to arithmetic (1 is a perfectly good field element; the paper is about *roles*, not algebra). No metaphysics of number. No priority claim over the measurement-theory literature — the thesis is a *connection*: the monad tradition, the Stevin demotion, and the metrological stipulation practice are one arc, visible when the frame/operand lens is applied. Payoffs are bounded and historiographic: why 1's exclusion from the primes is convention with a structural rationale; why unit-invariance is a derivation engine rather than a bookkeeping rule; why the SI's stipulated-exactness clauses have the form they do.

## 7. Conclusion

One was a frame before it was a number, and measurement never stopped treating it as one. The unit's history completes the boundary-item picture the companion paper sketches: the interesting items of a number system are the ones it cannot treat as ordinary citizens without losing the system — and the most successfully hidden of these is not zero or infinity but the item every count silently leans on.
