## Abstract

Is judged organizational prospect *multiplicative* — such that near-zero standing on one underlying dimension collapses the whole, regardless of strength elsewhere — or compensatory, as additive aggregation assumes? We test three pre-registered propositions with blind raters and archival outcomes. **P1 (dimensionality):** ratings of diverse organizational vignettes organize into two dominant, interpretable factors — internal *coherence* (consistency of purpose, integrity of structure, low self-contradiction) and external *viability* (resource throughput, adaptability, survival margin) — rather than one general factor or three-plus of comparable variance. **P2 (composition):** the *product* of recovered factor scores predicts documented five-year outcomes better than the sum, the maximum, or either factor alone, under cross-validated model comparison. **P3 (collapse asymmetry):** vignettes engineered to pair high coherence with near-zero viability, and the reverse, are both rated near-zero on overall prospect — the signature of non-compensatory composition. Raters (N ≈ 300) are blind to the framework and to outcomes; the outcome criterion is archival and rater-independent. Each proposition carries a pre-committed kill condition, and a clean failure on any is informative and publishable. A win establishes the modest claim only: two-factor multiplicative structure exists in evaluative judgment and tracks outcomes — not any stronger ontological reading.

## 1. Background

Two literatures meet here, and the study should be written as their intersection rather than as a novel framework. First, **information integration**: Anderson's program established that human judgment composes cues by identifiable algebraic rules — adding, averaging, multiplying — with multiplying appearing where one cue gates another (verify: N. H. Anderson, *Foundations of Information Integration Theory*, 1981; multiplicative integration findings). Second, **noncompensatory judgment and conjoint structure**: lexicographic and conjunctive models, and the axiomatics of multiplicative conjoint measurement (verify: Krantz, Luce, Suppes & Tversky, *Foundations of Measurement* Vol. 1, 1971, polynomial conjoint measurement; Gigerenzer on noncompensatory heuristics; venture-capital screening studies showing deal-breaker logic). The open question this study targets: for *whole-organization* prospect judgments, (a) is the cue space effectively two-dimensional along a coherence/viability split, and (b) is the composition rule of the gating (multiplicative) type, and (c) does the multiplicatively composed judgment track real outcomes better than compensatory alternatives — three claims that are separable and separately falsifiable.

## 2. Propositions and kill conditions (pre-registered)

- **P1.** EFA on a split half, CFA on the other: two factors with interpretable loadings (coherence items vs viability items), each explaining non-trivial variance, factor correlation |r| < 0.5. **Kill:** a third factor of comparable eigenvalue/variance, or a single general factor (two-factor CFA not preferred by fit indices). A third factor specifically wounds the framework's keystone two-factor wager — recorded as such.
- **P2.** Out-of-sample (k-fold by vignette): outcome ~ f(F1, F2) compared across f ∈ {F1·F2, F1+F2, max, min, F1 only, F2 only, full interaction model}. **Kill:** additive ≥ multiplicative on cross-validated error/AIC. (Note: min() is the sharpest rival — multiplicative and minimum composition are easily confused; the engineered-collapse vignettes in P3 are designed to separate them, and the model set includes both.)
- **P3.** Planned contrasts: engineered high-C/near-zero-V and high-V/near-zero-C vignettes both rated ≤ scale midpoint − 1 SD on global prospect. **Kill:** mid-scale ratings (compensation observed).

## 3. Method

**Targets.** 60 vignettes of real, anonymized organizations/projects with documented 5-year outcomes (survival/failure/acquisition + performance class), drawn from archival sources balanced across sector, size, and era; outcomes coded by two coders blind to ratings (2 person-days). Plus 6 engineered-collapse vignettes (Appendix B pattern) used only for P3. Vignettes are ~150 words, scrubbed of outcome leakage (no tense cues, no fame cues) and pre-tested for recognizability (recognized targets replaced).

**Raters.** N ≈ 300 (Prolific), each rating a random subset of ~12 vignettes on the 14-item instrument + 2 global-prospect items; ≥ 25 ratings per vignette per item. Attention checks; framework-blind (no tier vocabulary, no φ/ν language anywhere in materials).

**Instrument (Appendix A, summary).** Seven coherence-flavored items (e.g., "Different parts of this organization seem to work toward the same goal"; "Its stated strategy and its actual activities match"; "Internal contradictions are rare/minor") and seven viability-flavored items (e.g., "It has the resources to keep operating through a bad year"; "It adapts when conditions change"; "Demand for what it does is robust"), all 7-point agree scales, order-randomized; two global items ("Overall, this organization will be in good shape in five years"; "I would bet on this organization's survival"). Final wording frozen after pre-test for floor/ceiling and item-total behavior.

**Analysis.** As registered: split-half EFA/CFA (P1); factor scores → cross-validated model comparison (P2); planned contrasts (P3); rater random effects throughout; power by simulation before launch (the driver is vignettes × ratings-per-vignette; budget sized accordingly).

## 5. Discussion (branches pre-committed)

Win on all three: evaluative judgment of organizations carries a two-factor, gate-like (multiplicative) structure that tracks outcomes — relevant to screening instruments (VC, grants, accreditation) and to theories of organizational health; explicitly **not** established: any claim that organizations *are* two-factor multiplicative systems (judgment structure ≠ system ontology — the bridge is future work and is marked). Loss on P1: the two-factor lens fails at the first empirical gate; the source framework records the wound. Loss on P2 only: dimensions exist but compose compensatorily in judgment — the multiplicative ethic loses its empirical tooth at the judgment layer. Loss on P3 only: no gating signature; multiplicative fit in P2, if present, is an averaging artifact — say so.
