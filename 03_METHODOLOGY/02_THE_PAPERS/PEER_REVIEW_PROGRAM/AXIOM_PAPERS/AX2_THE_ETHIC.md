# AX2 — THE ETHIC
### Dyadic Rise as a Posited, Outcome-Directional Objective

> **Status: LITERATURE NOTE, not a paper.** Per the self-review's rule — a "coupling" paper must state what the coupling buys (a prediction, a theorem, or a resolved tension) or be relabeled — A2's coupling does **not** currently cash into a field-novel prediction. It is an honest synthesis with one unproven promotion path (§3). No shame; AX3 models the same honesty.

**Axiom (faithful):** "Ethical" ≡ a dyadic move where the finite node and the sustaining boundary rise together: `ΔP_node,i > 0` and `ΔP_node,H > 0` under `η = 0`. The older balance-register audit remains `ΣΔB / ΣΔP_node > 0` across the widest relevant boundary. **Posited, not derived from "is."** Evaluates *outcomes*, not *operators*.
**Corpus tier:** `[I]`. **Honest tier:** `[I]` — and correctly so; the axiom states its own is/ought status.

## 1. The claim
"Better" is not a free-floating scalar detached from the boundary being tested. In the current move-register, an act is good insofar as it raises the individual node and the real sustaining boundary together without extraction. The verdict attaches to the *change in the coupled world*, never to the agent's prestige or operator mask. When a wider audit is needed, the balance register asks whether `ΣΔB / ΣΔP_node` rises across the widest relevant boundary.

## 2. Prior art (it is not new; cite, don't claim)
Welfarist consequentialism with a specific, equality-favoring welfare functional:
- **Pigou–Dalton transfer principle** (verified, R3): a transfer from better-off to worse-off, *holding the total fixed*, raises welfare — "move toward balance." *(Caveat: balance-improvement on `φ·ν=const` is not obviously a fixed-total transfer; the Pigou–Dalton fit is evocative, not exact — see §3.)*
- **Prioritarianism** (Parfit, "Equality and Priority," 1997); **Atkinson/Kolm** inequality-averse social welfare functions.
- **von Foerster's ethical imperative** (verified, R6): "act always so as to increase the number of choices" — links A2 to A6/empowerment.
- **Hume's is/ought gap** — the seam the axiom refuses to paper over.

## 3. What the coupling buys — and why this is a note, not a paper
The coupling is (i) dyadic `P_node` gain under `η = 0`, (ii) the balance measure `B = sin θ` as a boundary audit, and (iii) operator-independence. Tested against the rule:
- **A prediction?** **No.** The balance functional is *one prioritarian SWF among many*; R3 showed the verdict is **aggregator-dependent** (maximin, sum, per-capita disagree on the same transfer — the triage inversion), so the functional makes no distinctive, adjudicated prediction the prioritarian family doesn't already make.
- **A resolved tension?** Only an *internal* one: operator-independence dissolves the corpus's own "good agent / evil agent" reification (gods/demons) — useful in-house, not a tension in the field literature.
- **A theorem?** **One candidate, unproven — the only promotion path.** The balance point (φ=ν on `φ·ν=const`) maximizes the *product* of the two factors under the constraint, which is the form of the **Nash bargaining solution** (Nash 1950). *If* that mapping holds, A2 becomes "ethical balance = the Nash bargaining solution of the two-factor allocation" — a genuine bridge from ethics to cooperative game theory, promoting A2 back to a paper. Until the math is worked, it is a conjecture, so **A2 stays a note.**

## 6. Kill criterion
Exhibit a coupled system in which both `P_node,i` and `P_node,H` rise under `η = 0` while the outcome is uncontroversially worse under every reasonable welfare lens, or a system in which one-sided extraction reliably sustains the coupled field without hidden cost. Conversely, proving the Nash-bargaining mapping (§3) or a principled derivation of *which* aggregator and boundary A2 requires would promote it from note `[I]` toward paper `[S]`.
