# AX2 — THE ETHIC
### Balance as a Posited, Outcome-Directional Objective

> **Status: LITERATURE NOTE, not a paper.** Per the self-review's rule — a "coupling" paper must state what the coupling buys (a prediction, a theorem, or a resolved tension) or be relabeled — A2's coupling does **not** currently cash into a field-novel prediction. It is an honest synthesis with one unproven promotion path (§3). No shame; AX3 models the same honesty.

**Axiom (faithful):** "Ethical" ≡ movement toward balance: `ΣΔB > 0`. **Posited, not derived from "is."** Evaluates *outcomes*, not *operators*.
**Corpus tier:** `[I]`. **Honest tier:** `[I]` — and correctly so; the axiom states its own is/ought status.

## 1. The claim
"Better" is a single scalar global objective: aggregate balance. An act is good insofar as it raises `ΣB` across the affected nodes — and the verdict attaches to the *change in the world*, never to the agent who caused it.

## 2. Prior art (it is not new; cite, don't claim)
Welfarist consequentialism with a specific, equality-favoring welfare functional:
- **Pigou–Dalton transfer principle** (verified, R3): a transfer from better-off to worse-off, *holding the total fixed*, raises welfare — "move toward balance." *(Caveat: balance-improvement on `φ·ν=const` is not obviously a fixed-total transfer; the Pigou–Dalton fit is evocative, not exact — see §3.)*
- **Prioritarianism** (Parfit, "Equality and Priority," 1997); **Atkinson/Kolm** inequality-averse social welfare functions.
- **von Foerster's ethical imperative** (verified, R6): "act always so as to increase the number of choices" — links A2 to A6/empowerment.
- **Hume's is/ought gap** — the seam the axiom refuses to paper over.

## 3. What the coupling buys — and why this is a note, not a paper
The coupling is (i) the balance measure `B = sin θ` as the welfare functional and (ii) operator-independence. Tested against the rule:
- **A prediction?** **No.** The balance functional is *one prioritarian SWF among many*; R3 showed the verdict is **aggregator-dependent** (maximin, sum, per-capita disagree on the same transfer — the triage inversion), so the functional makes no distinctive, adjudicated prediction the prioritarian family doesn't already make.
- **A resolved tension?** Only an *internal* one: operator-independence dissolves the corpus's own "good agent / evil agent" reification (gods/demons) — useful in-house, not a tension in the field literature.
- **A theorem?** **One candidate, unproven — the only promotion path.** The balance point (φ=ν on `φ·ν=const`) maximizes the *product* of the two factors under the constraint, which is the form of the **Nash bargaining solution** (Nash 1950). *If* that mapping holds, A2 becomes "ethical balance = the Nash bargaining solution of the two-factor allocation" — a genuine bridge from ethics to cooperative game theory, promoting A2 back to a paper. Until the math is worked, it is a conjecture, so **A2 stays a note.**

## 4. This session's evidence

And because A2 judges *outcomes not operators*, the later "two objectively immoral demons" framing is a category error by A2's own terms (see [AX4](AX4_THE_BOUNDARY.md)).

## 5. Reconciliation with AX1 (in print, per the self-review)
AX1 (post-R6) holds balance-geometry is **register-dependent**: multiplicative (sphere) for the organizational register, additive (simplex) for the teleology/empowerment register. A2 defines ethics via `B = sin θ` — the **multiplicative/spherical** balance. But ethics is a *teleological* register (it is about ends/flourishing), where R6 says the geometry is **additive**. **Consequence A2 must absorb:** post-R6 it cannot assume the sphere for free; if the moral register is teleological, the ethical balance functional should be the *additive* one (reach + precision form), not the product `sin θ`. A2 is therefore **register-pending — provisionally additive.** (A genuine constraint R6 places on A2 that the unified capstone hid; surfacing it is the reconciliation. See [AX1](AX1_THE_EQUATION.md).)

## 6. Kill criterion
Exhibit a system that moves *systematically away* from balance yet is uncontroversially "better" under every reasonable welfare lens → kills balance-as-good. Conversely, proving the Nash-bargaining mapping (§3) or a principled derivation of *which* aggregator A2 requires would promote it from note `[I]` toward paper `[S]`.

## 7. Verdict
**Literature note / synthesis**, not a submittable paper — a reciprocal-balance welfare functional in the prioritarian family, Pigou–Dalton as lineage, R3 as the cautionary worked example. **Promotion path:** prove balance = Nash bargaining solution, or resolve the register (§5). Until then: honest synthesis, correctly labeled.
