**The set's honest through-line (post-self-review).** The **credential of the whole program is [R6](../R6_CONJUGATE_RESULTS.md)** — the one place the corpus changed its mind *against interest* (the conjugate-empowerment geometry, refuted) — **not** A7's self-demonstration, which is the *method/framing*, not evidence (a process built to self-correct cannot be its own proof). Applying the self-review's rule — *a coupling paper must state what the coupling buys (a prediction, a theorem, or a resolved tension) or be relabeled a literature note* — sorts the set:

- **Buys a prediction:** AX1 (unit-elasticity tradeoff — R6-falsified for empowerment, R4-testable organizationally); AX4 (a common critical η\* across heterogeneous enforcement mechanisms); AX5 (egregores default to parasitic capture absent enforcement).
- **Buys a resolved tension:** AX6 (function transfers across substrates, the *stakes* of sacrifice do not — reconciling Putnam); AX5 (the D5 agency-gate disciplines over-attribution).
- **Buys neither (yet) → literature note:** **AX2** — relabeled honestly; promotes to a paper iff "balance = the Nash bargaining solution" is proved.
- **Held internal (HOLD):** AX3 — not submittable as physics.

Two cross-axiom tensions, surfaced by writing the axioms separately, are now **reconciled in print**: **AX4↔AX6** (valence-axis vs authenticity-axis) and **AX1↔AX2** (balance is register-dependent post-R6, so A2's functional is provisionally *additive*, not the sphere). Both reconciliations are themselves `[I]/[S]` moves, not free — the capstone's "coherent synthesis" now holds *only* with these two repairs noted.
