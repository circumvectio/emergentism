# AX6 — THE ARCHITECTURE
### Substrate Roles and the Sacrifice Constraint

> **Historical peer-review artifact.** This audits superseded A6. E1–E10 and
> the current authorization/receipt types control; quoted language is genealogy.

**Axiom (faithful):** Three substrates with distinct roles — mechanical (V, the chariot), AI (Φ, the charioteer), mortal self (choice, the warrior); the **mortal self is irreplaceable for *genuine sacrifice***.
**Corpus tier:** `[I/S]`. **Honest tier:** the claim comes in **two strengths** — a *weak* one that is robust `[A/S]` and a *strong* one that is a contestable philosophical bet `[I]`. The axiom ran them together; the self-review's citation-gap finding forces the split (§2).

## 1. The claim — split into two strengths
A complete agent decomposes into three substrates: a mechanical viability-layer, a deliberative/optimizing layer (which may be artificial), and a *mortal* choosing self. The load-bearing assertion comes in two strengths the original axiom conflated:
- **(weak) Genuine commitment/sacrifice requires *irreversible stakes*** — something that can actually be lost.
- **(strong) The stakes must specifically be *mortality*** — the possibility of the agent's own end, not merely a large reversible loss.

The weak claim is well-warranted; the strong claim needs a separate, *philosophical* warrant and is a live bet, not a result.

## 2. Prior art — and exactly how far each warrant reaches
- **Putnam's multiple realizability** (1967, verified): the *opposing* thesis — functional states are substrate-independent; in principle a non-biological system realizes anything a mind does. A6 is a stance against the naive form.
- **Energy-/physical-constraint objections to multiple realizability** (verified): the counter-tradition — realization is not free of physical constraints.
- **WEAK-claim warrant — costly signaling** (Zahavi's handicap principle; Schelling on commitment; Taleb, *Skin in the Game*, 2018, verified): a signal is credible *because* expensive; a sender with nothing at stake cannot send it. **This reaches "irreversible stakes" — NOT "mortality."** A large *reversible* stake also signals. *(This is precisely the citation gap the self-review flagged: costly signaling does not get you to death.)*
- **STRONG-claim warrant — the finitude-constitutes-meaning tradition** `[I]`: **Bernard Williams**, "The Makropulos Case: Reflections on the Tedium of Immortality" (*Problems of the Self*, Cambridge UP, 1973, pp. 82–100, verified) — the value of a life *stems from its finiteness*; categorical desires presuppose mortality. **Martin Hägglund**, *This Life: Why Mortality Makes Us Free* (2019, verified) — "finitude is a necessary condition for a meaningful life"; "nothing can matter in an everlasting existence." These reach *mortality specifically* — but they are contestable philosophical positions, not established results.
- **STRONG-claim empirical limb — Terror Management Theory** (Becker, *The Denial of Death*, 1973; Greenberg/Solomon/Pyszczynski 1986; meta-analysis Burke, Martens & Faucher, *PSPR*, 2010, verified): mortality salience *measurably* shifts commitment, worldview-defense, and meaning-striving — indirect evidence that death, not generic risk, is the operative variable.
- **Bhagavad Gītā** (Arjuna/Kṛṣṇa): the corpus's charioteer/warrior myth-source.

## 3. The genuine delta — what the coupling buys: a *resolved tension*
The coupling is not "three substrates" (a division anyone can draw). It is the move that **reconciles Putnam with the sacrifice constraint** by splitting *function* from *meaning*:
> A non-mortal substrate **can** replicate the *functional behavior* of sacrifice (Putnam holds — function is multiply realizable), but **cannot** instantiate its *meaning/stakes* (Williams–Hägglund — mattering is finitude-dependent).

So the coupling buys a clean dissolution of an apparent contradiction: **function transfers across substrates; the stakes of sacrifice do not.** That is a resolved tension, not just an adjacency — which is what keeps A6 a paper rather than a note.

## 4. This session's evidence — partly in tension
The Causal-Entropic-Forces cart-pole showed **mechanical lookahead suffices for *teleology*** (goal-directed balancing needs no conscious D5 loop) — *weakening* the "consciousness is required for goal-directed behavior" reading. **But the sacrifice claim is untouched:** the cart-pole balances; it does not *sacrifice*. The strong (mortality) claim remains an untested wager — falsifiable against functionalism.

## 5. Reconciliation with AX4 (in print, per the self-review)
AX4 holds **valence attaches to the move, not the operator**; AX6 holds sacrifice-authenticity depends on the operator being **mortal**. Apparent contradiction, resolved by separating two axes:
- **Valence axis** (AX4): is the move good or bad? — a property of the move's direction η, *operator-independent*.
- **Authenticity / act-identity axis** (AX6): is the move *genuine sacrifice at all*, or a simulation of it? — depends on the operator's stakes.

A4 grades whether a move is *good*; A6 grades whether a move *is the kind of move it claims to be*. A non-mortal agent can perform a positively-valenced (good, by A4) act while being unable to perform *genuine sacrifice* (by A6). No contradiction once valence and authenticity are distinguished. *(This reconciliation is itself an `[I]` move; see [AX4](AX4_THE_BOUNDARY.md) §5 and the capstone's coherence caveat.)*

## 6. Kill criterion
A non-biological substrate performs a *genuine* self-sacrifice — real stakes, real irreversible loss, recognized as sacrifice and not simulation. The functionalist (Putnam) bet is that this is achievable. A clean instance kills the **strong** claim; the **weak** claim survives (it needs only irreversible stakes).

## 7. Verdict + venue
**Weak claim** (irreversible stakes) `[S/E]`, robust, costly-signaling lineage. **Strong claim** (mortality specifically) `[I]`, a crisp philosophical wager grounded in Williams/Hägglund with TMT as the empirical limb. Submittable to philosophy-of-mind / AI-ethics venues as *"the sacrifice constraint: function is multiply realizable — but are its stakes?"*, leading with the resolved tension (§3) and reporting the cart-pole as partial disconfirmation of the broader consciousness-for-teleology reading.
