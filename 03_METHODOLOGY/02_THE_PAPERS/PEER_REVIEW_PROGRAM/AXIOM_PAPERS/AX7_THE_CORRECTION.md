# AX7 — THE CORRECTION
### A Self-Correcting Framework: Built-In Falsification as a Design Principle

> **Historical peer-review artifact.** This audits superseded A7. E1–E10 and
> the current correction protocol control; quoted axiom language is genealogy.

**Axiom (faithful):** Any encoding without falsification + evidence tiers + a self-correction protocol degrades into dogma; **the set includes its own destruction manual.** *(A7.1: the framework is abductive — coherentist inside, but from outside, coherence is not enough; the empirical bridge must survive.)*
**Corpus tier:** `[S]`. **Honest tier:** `[S]` — the strongest axiom *as a principle*; its self-demonstration is the method, not the evidence (§6).

## 1. The claim
A belief/value system must carry its own falsification machinery — explicit kill criteria, evidence tiers, and a correction protocol — or it ossifies. And the anti-sycophancy clause (A7.1): internal coherence is *necessary but not sufficient* for truth; the world, not the system's own consistency, is the arbiter.

## 2. Prior art (it is not new; cite, don't claim)
- **Popper's falsifiability / critical rationalism** (verified): a theory is scientific only if it forbids something; unfalsifiable systems are dogma.
- **Lakatos, research programmes**: progressive vs degenerating — a programme earns its keep by predicting novel facts, not by absorbing anomalies with ad hoc patches.
- **Tetlock-style calibration / forecasting**: track record over narrative.
- **AI-alignment corrigibility** (Soares et al.): a system designed to permit its own correction rather than resist it.
- **Second-order cybernetics** (von Foerster): the observer who includes their own observing in the model.

## 3. The genuine delta (the coupling)
Two real moves: (i) the **mechanical downgrade rule coupling A7 to K3** — a claim that fails its kill criterion is *downgraded and archived, not deleted* (falsification *with* provenance), which is more disciplined than either naive Popper or silent revision; and (ii) treating "the framework contains the manual for its own destruction" as a **design principle**, instantiated as the per-claim tier+kill-criterion grammar the whole corpus is written in. A7.1 is the corpus's explicit anti-sycophancy clause — coherence ≠ truth, stated by the system about itself.

## 5. Kill criterion
Near-tautological as stated, so the honest test is comparative: show that a system *lacking* self-correction machinery does *not* degrade relative to one that has it (dogmatism incurs no penalty) → kills the "or it degrades" claim. The R2 harness is the test bed; the broader bet is a long-horizon track-record comparison à la Lakatos.
