# R4 — Is Evaluated Productivity Multiplicative? Preregistration Outline

**Working title (external):** *Two-Factor Structure and Multiplicative Composition in Lay Judgments of Organizational Viability*
**Status:** Outline v1, 2026-06-10. This is the owed blind-human study. Requires panel budget before execution.
**Internal source:** the keystone finite-node wager (`P_node = Φ × V`). This study is the corpus's highest-stakes external test: it is the one designed to be able to *lose*.

---

## 1. The claim, externalized

Internal form: productive capacity factors into exactly two conjugate components — call them, in external language, **coherence** (internal consistency, integrity of structure, low self-contradiction) and **viability** (resource throughput, adaptability, survival margin) — and composes **multiplicatively**: near-zero on either factor collapses the whole, and no surplus on one compensates a zero on the other.

Externalized as three testable propositions:

- **P1 (dimensionality):** Blind raters' judgments of diverse target systems organize into two dominant, near-orthogonal factors interpretable as coherence and viability — not one general factor, not three-or-more of comparable variance.
- **P2 (composition):** The **product** of recovered factor scores predicts an independent outcome criterion better than the sum, the max, or either factor alone (model comparison by cross-validated error and information criteria).
- **P3 (collapse asymmetry):** Vignettes engineered to be high-coherence/near-zero-viability and high-viability/near-zero-coherence are both rated near-zero on overall prospect — the signature of multiplicative, not additive, composition.

## 2. Design sketch

- **Targets:** 60 short vignettes of real organizations/projects (anonymized, historical, with known 5-year outcomes) — balanced across sectors and outcome classes. Outcome criterion: documented survival/performance at horizon (archival, independent of raters).
- **Raters:** N ≈ 300 (Prolific or equivalent), fully blind to the framework and to outcomes; each rates a subset on ~14 mixed items (7 coherence-flavored, 7 viability-flavored, plus 2 global-prospect items). Item wording avoids framework vocabulary entirely; item pool pre-tested for floor/ceiling.
- **Analysis:** Exploratory + confirmatory factor analysis (split-half); P2 via pre-registered model comparison: outcome ~ f(F1, F2) for f ∈ {product, sum, max, single-factor, full interaction}; P3 via planned contrasts on the engineered vignettes.
- **Power:** Simulate before launch; commit to N and item counts in the registration.

## 3. Kill criteria (pre-committed)

- A third factor with eigenvalue/variance comparable to the second → P1 fails → the two-factor wager takes a direct, recorded hit.
- Additive or single-factor models predict ≥ as well as multiplicative under cross-validation → P2 fails → the multiplicative ethic loses its empirical tooth; internal doctrine must demote the composition claim from structural to heuristic.
- Engineered-collapse vignettes rated mid-scale → P3 fails → the "either-zero kills" signature is not present in lay judgment.

A clean loss on all three is maximally informative and will be published as such. A win on all three does **not** establish the internal ontology; it establishes that the two-factor multiplicative structure exists in evaluative judgment and tracks outcomes — the modest, defensible version.

## 4. Honest limitation to carry in the paper

Rater judgments + archival outcomes test the structure in *evaluation*, not in the systems themselves. Bridging from "judges compose multiplicatively and the product tracks outcomes" to any stronger ontological claim is future work and will be marked as such.
