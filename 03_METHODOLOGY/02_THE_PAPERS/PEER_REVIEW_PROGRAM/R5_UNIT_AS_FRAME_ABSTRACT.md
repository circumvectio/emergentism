## Abstract (extended)

The history of zero is well told: a boundary-behavior promoted to first-class arithmetic citizenship, with one residue (0/0) marking where the promotion cannot complete. This paper tells the parallel and neglected history of **one** — and argues it runs in the opposite direction.

Greek arithmetic did not count the unit among the numbers. Euclid defines the monad as "that by virtue of which each of the things that exist is called one," and number as "a multitude composed of units" (*Elements* VII, Def. 1–2): the unit is the *measure* of number, not a number — a frame, in the sense of the companion paper. This was not naivety but a role distinction, maintained through the medieval arithmetic tradition. Its demotion is datable: Simon Stevin's *L'Arithmétique* (1585) argued squarely that the unit is a number, and positional-decimal practice made the promotion irresistible. By the modern era, 1 sits on the number line as just another point, its frame-character invisible.

But the frame-character did not disappear; it migrated into measurement practice, where it was rediscovered operationally three times. (1) Every measurement report is a ratio to a unit; the unit itself is not measured but *stipulated* — the one quantity in the system with exact value by fiat. (2) Dimensional analysis (Buckingham 1914) rests on the requirement that physical laws be invariant under change of units: the unit is precisely what equations must *not* depend on — the signature of a frame, not a datum. (3) The 2019 revision of the SI completed the arc: all seven base units are now defined by fixing exact numerical values of natural constants — stipulation made explicit and total, the frame pinned to invariants of nature rather than artefacts.

The thesis: **the unit is a boundary object of the same structural kind as zero and infinity** — constitutive of the operation space (no counting or measuring without it), pathological when asked to be measured by itself, and managed historically by the same device of stratification (the stipulated stratum vs. the measured stratum). Zero's history is a promotion story with a residue; one's is a demotion story with a return. Together they suggest that number systems have not two special items but three, and that the third has been hidden in plain sight precisely because measurement works so well.

The paper claims history and structure, not metaphysics. Deliverables: (i) a documented arc Euclid → Stevin → Buckingham → SI-2019 with primary sources; (ii) the structural reading via the frame/operand criteria of the companion paper; (iii) a bounded discussion of what the reading does and does not license (it licenses no revision to arithmetic; it reframes why "1 is not prime" conventions, unit-invariance requirements, and stipulated-exactness clauses share a shape).
