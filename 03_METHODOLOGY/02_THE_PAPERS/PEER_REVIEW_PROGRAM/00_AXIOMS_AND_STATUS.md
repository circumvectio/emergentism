## PART 3 — The weltanschauung in one honest paragraph

Existence is the fold between a void (0) and a totality (∞), held open at a self-dual centre (1, *finity*) where coherence and viability stand in reciprocal balance. To be a determinate thing is to hold a latitude on that sphere; to act well is to move toward balance and route order down the scarcity gradient; to govern well is to refuse extraction, preserve exit, and build in your own correction. **The math of the centre is real and elementary; that reality is the centre is a wager; the ethics is a posited direction, not a derived fact; and the cosmology is honest conjecture.** The framework's deepest commitment is not any of its claims but A7 — that it states its own tier, names its own kill criteria, and dissolves rather than ossifies. By that commitment it is, today, an unusually self-honest synthesis whose truth against the world remains to be tested — and whose next move is the experiment, not the restatement. Its *coherence*, however, is not free: the per-axiom papers ([AXIOM_PAPERS/](AXIOM_PAPERS/README.md)) surfaced two cross-axiom tensions — **AX4↔AX6** (valence vs authenticity) and **AX1↔AX2** (balance is register-dependent, so the ethic is provisionally *additive*, not spherical) — that hold together only once explicitly reconciled, and those reconciliations are interpretive moves, not theorems. And the program's credential is the one refutation it accepted against interest (**R6**), not the self-narrative that it self-corrects.
