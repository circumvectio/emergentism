# Core Narrative — Coherence Check (2026-06-10)

The founder restated the load-bearing narrative and asked to confirm it is "coherent and consistent." Three distinct questions hide in that — and they get three different answers:

- **Coherent** (internally consistent as a grammar)? — **Largely yes.** The model hangs together.
- **Consistent with what this session established** (experiments + grounding)? — **No, in two specific places.** The restatement reverts corrections the corpus's own results already made.
- **True** (corresponds to the world)? — **Unestablished**, because the empirical program is unrun; and where we *did* test, two pieces failed.

Coherence is not truth. A self-consistent grammar can be coherently wrong (the corpus says this itself). Below, each element of the restatement, mapped.

## The one correction that matters most

The restatement's **"2 gods objectively ethical / 2 demons objectively immoral, exhausting the game space"** is the single place where the confident narrative contradicts both the corpus's own canon (Paper III F-1) and this session's experiments (R7/R8/R9). The refined, supported claim is:

> **Cooperation (the gods) is collectively optimal but never the default; it is sustained only by bounded conditional enforcement (the fenced demons). The four moves are gods = cooperation + demons = enforcement — not 2-good + 2-evil. "Objective" means model-objective (an inherited premise), not world-objective.**

That is *more* impressive than "2 good, 2 evil," not less — it's the major-evolutionary-transitions result, and it's why the corpus's fenced demons are load-bearing rather than tolerated evil. Reverting to "objectively immoral demons" throws away the corpus's own best result.

## Verdict

The narrative is a coherent grammar; the math kernel is correct (and elementary); the structure hangs together. But "coherent and consistent" cannot be upgraded to "true," and the restatement re-inflates two claims the session's own work corrected (the gods/demons ethics; the sphere geometry) and leans on one that's empirically weak (D0≡D6 physics). The corpus is at its strongest when it states the *refined* result and its honest tier — not the confident headline. The decisive move remains the unrun experiment, not a tighter restatement.
