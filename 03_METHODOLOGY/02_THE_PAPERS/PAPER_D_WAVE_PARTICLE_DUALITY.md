# Paper D: Wave-Particle Correspondence

Quantum theory predicts interference, discrete detection records, uncertainty
relations, and Bell-inequality violations. Those results stand independently
of Emergentism.

The former paper treated wave and particle as Titan factors, identified
uncertainty products with `φν=1`, described measurement as the glyph product,
and said Bell outcomes emerge at a μ-limit. These were interpretive inflations.
The complete prior text remains recoverable from Git blob
`717e9f12eb4573c2e399c39eab374d2fec9cf865`.

## Established floor `[A]`

- Quantum states and observables are governed by the relevant formalism, not by
  the Burri chart.
- Position/momentum uncertainty is an inequality whose lower bound and
  saturation conditions are state-dependent; it is not the chart identity
  `φν=1`.
- Bell tests rule out the relevant local-hidden-variable class under their
  assumptions. They do not prove D5, physical retrocausality, or a shared
  coherence field.
- Everett has no fundamental collapse; collapse or actualization in other
  interpretations is interpretation-specific.

## D3 state / D4 record boundary `[A/I]`

The quantum state and declared measurement generate probabilities at D3; an
actual preparation, interaction, detector outcome, and stable record belong to
D4. Assigning those roles to D3/D4 is an Emergentist crosswalk `[I]`. D5 remains
agent-represented counterfactual content, not quantum branch space. `μ₃` supplies
no measurement operator or collapse mechanism, `χ` is an agent selector, and no
quantum conclusion licenses a universal dimensional ontology.

The quantum-interpretation and Burri-sphere comparisons are removable. Their
deletion must leave both the D3 state calculation and the agent calculus intact.

## Kill criterion

The correspondence is retired for any scope in which it adds no discriminating
prediction, obscures the standard formalism, or induces a reader to treat
D3/D4/D5 as extra spacetime dimensions.

*Physics supplies the territory. The Burri inset is optional notation.*
