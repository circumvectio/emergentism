# PAPER 3: THE REFLEXIVE DEMON

**Status:** Stub scaffold.

**Program Home:** [`The D5 Landauer Barrier Program`](README.md)

**Depends On:** [`Paper 1`](PAPER_1_THE_D5_LANDAUER_BARRIER.md), [`Paper 2`](PAPER_2_WORLDLINE_FORESIGHT_IS_PHYSICAL.md)

## Thesis

An actual D4 selector does not observe a fixed future and then choose from it.
Its D4 model token represents D5 alternative histories, and the acts of
observing, modeling, communicating, or controlling can change the system whose
alternatives are being predicted.

## Role In The Program

This paper owns the game-theory and control-theory problem:

```text
Predict actor branches.
Act on the prediction.
Actors and environment respond.
The predicted branch set changes.
Prediction becomes part of the system it predicts.
```

This is why the “D5 demon” is only a name for a limit on D4 modeling of D5
content. Even a hypothetical high-capacity selector must include its own
modeling and action effects in the modeled field.

## Required Sections

1. Define observer inclusion: the selector is inside the branch system.
2. Model action as branch-space intervention, not passive observation.
3. Handle strategic actors who respond to being predicted.
4. Distinguish useful robust foresight from impossible total foresight.
5. Connect reflexivity to A7 correction: predictions must be corrigible.

## Kill Criteria

- The paper assumes a static branch tree after the selector acts.
- The paper treats all actors as passive particles when the claim requires strategic agents.
- The paper uses reflexivity as mysticism rather than as a formal intervention problem.
