# Paper O: Epistemic Asymmetry and Macro-Constraint

## The crack

The former paper called opacity from below “Strong Emergence,” called
legibility from above “Weak Emergence,” and elevated that contrast into an
Asymmetry Theorem. That language was too strong. Failure to predict or reduce a
higher-level pattern may reflect incomplete information, computational cost,
poor variables, or an immature theory. It does not establish ontological
irreducibility.

The complete pre-repair text remains recoverable from Git blob
`18d28c8cd46a415f54b45b97e9c835829bb58cce`. It is provenance, not current
authority.

## The gold seam

The defensible result is a candidate macro-constraint test:

```text
K_X(x' | x)          lower-level transition law
G_C(x' | x,y) >= 0   gate or weight under macro-context C
K_X^C                normalize(K_X * G_C)

support(K_X^C) ⊆ support(K_X)
```

A higher-level context may restrict or reweight lower-law-admissible
trajectories. It may not manufacture a forbidden micro-transition. Its causal
status is earned only when the context is perturbable and improves a fair,
costed intervention or prediction test:

```text
EI_baseline = max(EI_micro_fair, EI_coarse_null, EI_domain)
Cost_C      = measurement + memory + control + model
              + labor + erasure + entropy_export
W_C         = EI_macro - EI_baseline - Cost_C
```

`W_C > 0` at a declared grain is evidence for a useful causal-emergence
witness at that grain. It is not proof of strong emergence, a new physical
force, or a universal D-register.

## Relation to the μ ladder

Each proposed `D_n → μ_n → D_{n+1}` transition is a candidate emergence
crossing. Until a reduction or an affirmative irreducibility argument succeeds,
its reduction status is `currently_unreduced`. A successful reduction
reclassifies the crossing as weak emergence without destroying the D0–D6
scaffold as a translation grammar.

D4 carries actual means, performed actions, receipts, outcomes, and records.
D5 carries possible contents: modeled futures, admissible options, rankings,
and selections. The D5→D4 commitment bridge is not a μ-crossing, collapse, or
quantum measurement.

## Surviving claim

> Macro descriptions can sometimes become more interventionally useful than
> available micro descriptions after fair baselines and all relevant costs are
> paid. Epistemic opacity motivates the test; it never settles the ontology.

## Kill criteria

This scoped claim fails for a proposed context when:

1. perturbing or removing `C` does not change the outcome distribution;
2. the macro model wins only by blinding the micro baseline;
3. its advantage disappears after measurement, control, labor, memory,
   erasure, or entropy-export costs are counted;
4. it requires a micro-transition forbidden by the lower-level law; or
5. a simpler domain mechanism predicts or controls equally well.
