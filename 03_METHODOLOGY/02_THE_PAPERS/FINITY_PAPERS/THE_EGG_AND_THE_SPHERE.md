# THE EGG AND THE SPHERE

## A tiered correspondence

> **[金] Kintsugi seam — correspondence is not generation.** The predecessor
> said the egg generates the sphere, that both readings are necessary, and
> promoted the emblem `0×∞=1` into a structural generative core. This successor
> retains the useful comparison while making every dependency optional.

This paper compares two vocabularies for reciprocal symmetry: the Finity
Papers' sphere language and Minoru Suda's egg/fold language. It is a
correspondence study, not an owner of Emergentist doctrine. Removing the egg
reading leaves the analytic core and operational Emergentist calculus intact.

## 1. Shared analytic core `[A]`

For `x∈ℝ₊`, let `I(x)=1/x`. Then:

1. `I` has the unique positive fixed point `x=1` and `I'(1)=-1`.
2. `E(x)=(log x)²` is invariant under `I`, is minimized uniquely at `x=1`,
   and diverges as `x→0⁺` or `x→∞`.
3. `u=(x-1)/(x+1)` carries reciprocal inversion to `u↦-u`.
4. `x`, `s=log x`, and `u=tanh(s/2)` are coordinate descriptions of the same
   involution on their stated domains.
5. With the selected substitution `x=tan(θ/2)`, inversion corresponds to
   `θ↦π-θ`, and `B=sin θ=sech(log x)` is maximal at `x=1`.

These identities do not establish an egg ontology, a spherical universe, a
phase dynamics, or an ethic.

## 2. Two optional readings `[I]`

| Reading | Metaphor | What it emphasizes |
|---|---|---|
| sphere | fixed point, reflection, equatorial symmetry | structural relations in the selected chart |
| egg/fold | compression, inversion, unfolding | a process-oriented interpretation of the same relations |

The two readings may illuminate one another. Neither is necessary, neither
generates the other, and neither upgrades the analytic identities. Their
compatibility is an interpretive proposal `[I]`.

The Titan glyph `•   ⊙   ○`, sometimes written `1=0×∞`, is an emblem. In ordinary
field arithmetic `0·∞` is not well formed because infinity is not a field
element; in limit calculus it may be an indeterminate form. The emblem is never a proof of genesis or a
law of reality.

## 3. Attributed and empirical extensions `[B/C]`

The source corpus attributes to Suda a reciprocal-symmetry program containing
measurement protocols, a continuous half-twist proposal, a phase loop, and a
safe-inversion protocol. Attribution is `[B]` where the cited record supports
it. Whether any proposal describes a real physical, biological, cognitive, or
social process is separately testable `[C]`.

A valid experiment must state:

- the operational variables and units;
- intervention, comparator, and outcome;
- the predicted advantage over a simpler reciprocal model;
- preregistered failure conditions;
- the source and independence status of the evidence.

Convergence of vocabulary is not independent confirmation when the two
accounts share sources, prompts, transformations, or post-hoc selection.

## 4. Value boundary

Reciprocal symmetry, `B`, and `η_observed` do not derive value. `η_observed`
can report a transfer only after payer, beneficiary, boundary, baseline,
horizon, and cost are named. Emergentist moral or ethical classification comes
from the independent bearer-complete Justice envelope, not from the egg,
sphere, fixed point, or sign of an axis.

## 5. Adoption rule

A correspondence may enter the wider framework only when it:

1. names its evidence tier and source;
2. separates identity, interpretation, and empirical hypothesis;
3. supplies a discriminator and kill criterion for empirical claims;
4. preserves the ordinary arithmetic boundary around `0`, `1`, and `∞`;
5. makes no ethical inference without the independent Justice postulate; and
6. can be removed without changing the operational Burri calculus.

## Kill criteria

The correspondence fails if:

- the proposed empirical extensions add no predictive value over their nulls;
- the source programs are shown not to be independent where independence is
  claimed;
- the mapping requires `0×∞=1` as arithmetic;
- either metaphor is treated as a necessary generator of the other; or
- a value verdict is inferred from symmetry alone.

## Standing

The reciprocal identities are established mathematics `[A]`. The comparison
is an interpretive lens `[I]`. Its attributed history is `[B]`. Its generative
and real-world extensions remain conjectural `[C]`.
