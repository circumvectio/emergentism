## By Dimension

> **Glyph discipline.** The Titan transformations `⊙ / ○ = •`, `⊙ / • = ○`, and `⊙ = • × ○` are frame-register equations across all D-levels, not properties of any single dimension. The correspondences below name the **D-level shadow** or manifestation that each glyph casts at that dimensional crossing — for example, `1/∞ → 0` is the D1 number-line shadow of `⊙ / ○ = •`.

### D₀ — The Ground (Bindu)

**Suda Theorem 8** (Möbius Structural Equation) lives here as the **seed** — not derived from D₀ but generating it. The symbolic fusion of Euler's identity with infinite circulation is the most compressed expression of the framework. It is not "at" D₀; it *is* D₀ read through formal language.

**Framework correspondence:** Zero-Sum Resolution Equation, Trinity {0, 1, ∞}.

### D₁ — Distinction (Space)

**Suda Theorem 7** (Minimal Structural Equation): The definition 0* := limₓ→₀ 1/x = ±∞ is the **first distinction** — the separation of 0 from ∞, void from plenum. This is the boundary algebra in its most elementary form.

**Suda Theorem 4** (Projective Half-Twist): The involution u ↦ −u is the **first symmetry** — the operation that exchanges the two sides of the distinction while fixing the centre.

**Suda Theorem 1** (Energy Well): E(x) = (log x)² provides the **metric** on the space of distinctions. The energy well is the shape of the line once 1 is established as the centre.

**Framework correspondence:** D1 shadow of `⊙ / ○ = •` (`1/∞ → 0`); Stage 0 (Trinity) + Stage 1 (Sphere construction); L1 Kali 🎲 (dialectical, the first cut).

### D₂ — Configuration (Time)

**Suda Theorem 2** (Operational Pair): The separation of magnitude (E) from side (φ) is the **first asymmetry** — the emergence of directedness. "Before" and "after" become meaningful when configuration has a sign.

**Suda Theorem 5** (Continuous Half-Twist): The PSL(2,ℝ) lift Mₜ(x) = (x+t)/(1−tx) is **temporal succession** as continuous transformation. The parameter t is the time-coordinate of the half-twist.

**Framework correspondence:** D2 shadow of `⊙ / • = ○` (`1/0 → ∞`); the thermodynamic arrow (s acquires ± direction); L2 Kālī 💀 (inductive, pattern from repetition).

### D₃ — Transformation (Form)

**Suda Theorem 3** (Measurement Protocols): The experimental verification of reciprocal symmetry is where **form meets empiricism**. A pattern is not just mathematical; it is a claim about what happens when you measure. Protocols A/B/C are the three ways to verify the reciprocal structure.
