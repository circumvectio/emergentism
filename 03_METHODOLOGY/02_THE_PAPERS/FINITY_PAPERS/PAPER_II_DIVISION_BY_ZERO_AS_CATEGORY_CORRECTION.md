# Division by Zero — Domain and Category Boundary

> **[金] Crack:** the predecessor correctly noticed that different algebras
> handle boundary expressions differently, but then said the Titan frames
> define operations and treated projective extension as corrected field
> division. **Repair:** name the domain before evaluating the expression.

## 1. Field arithmetic `[A]`

In a field, division by zero is undefined because `0` has no multiplicative
inverse. Ordinary `0` and `1` remain elements, numbers, and operands. Their
identity roles do not remove them from the field.

## 2. Projective and meromorphic extension `[A]`

The Riemann sphere `ℂP¹=ℂ∪{∞}` adds a projective point. A meromorphic function
such as `z↦1/z` extends to the sphere with `0↦∞` and `∞↦0`. In this declared
context, one may use the convention that `N/0` approaches or maps to `∞` for
nonzero `N` through the corresponding projective/meromorphic extension.

That convention is not field division made valid. It changes the codomain and
the structure. Direction-sensitive real limits can also distinguish `+∞` and
`−∞`; the projective statement must not erase the chosen context.

The field quotient `0/0` remains undefined. `0/0` may be an indeterminate
**limit form** only after functions and an approach path are declared.
Analytic “indeterminate forms” describe limits, not new arithmetic values.

## 3. Alternative total algebras `[A]`

Wheel-like structures can totalize division by changing familiar algebraic
laws. Their behavior is valid inside those explicitly defined systems and
cannot be imported into a field without paying the axiomatic cost. No totalized
result proves an apophatic Ground or resolves Gödel, Tarski, or the Liar.

## 4. Titan boundary `[I]`

Emergentism selects `{0,1,∞}` as symbolic boundary roles. They do not define
the operations, generate the number system, or force an ontology. The emblem
`1=0×∞` is not arithmetic; infinity is not a field element, so this is not a
field product. `0·∞` may be an indeterminate limit form. Forcing a totalized
product to equal `1` while retaining the usual rules breaks associativity.

The durable lesson is methodological: many paradoxes dissolve when types and
domains are made explicit. That is a useful lens, not evidence that every
paradox has the same solution.
