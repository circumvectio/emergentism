# The Finity Papers — Next-Slate Evaluation

> **What this is.** The output of a multi-lens team asked: *now that Papers I–V exist, what is worth writing next — and what must we refuse?* Eight independent lenses proposed candidates; two adversarial critics (an over-reach hunter and a completeness critic) judged them. The slate below sorts every survivor into **MAINTENANCE owed** · **WRITE NOW** · **WRITE LATER** · **REFUSED (over-reach)** · **EMPIRICAL DEBT**, and records an honest correction to an earlier in-session framing of the Cantor point.
>
> **The discipline it models:** every item carries a tier and a way to die; `1 = 0 × ∞` stays a frame-register emblem; the framework *agrees with* Gödel and Cantor rather than escaping them; no pole/person is moralized; the geometry is a lens, never the Ground. The strongest single move on the slate is a **retraction**, not a conquest.

## 1. WRITE NOW

### VI — The Cantor Clarification *(retraction-first)* — highest value
The sibling of `09_EFR_GODEL_CLARIFICATION.md`. **Its primary deliverable is a retraction** (E1), not a new theorem.
- **Kernel:** (i) `[A]` the projective `∞ = [1:0]` on `ℂP¹` is logically *independent* of cardinal arithmetic — `ℂP¹` having one point at infinity says nothing about `ℵ₀ < 2^ℵ₀`, and **Cantor's theorem `|X| < |P(X)|` stands untouched**; (ii) `[B]` Cantor's *das Absolute* (1883 *Grundlagen*; the 1899 Dedekind letter on "inconsistent multiplicities") — placed beyond the alephs, held apophatically, **"not a set"** — is a citable historical convergence with the L6 Ground, and *frames-not-operands = the Absolute is not a set*; (iii) `[S]` the canon's "no degrees of infinity" line over-reaches and is retired; (iv) `[I]` the mapping `{0, ∞} ↔ {Ground, transfinite-plenum}`.
- **Must-not-claim:** that the framework refutes/dissolves Cantor's hierarchy; that "there is only one infinity" in the *cardinal* sense; that CH or large cardinals are touched; that *das Absolute* ≈ the geometric `∞` (it is the analogue of `0`/the Ground, **not** of the projective `∞`).
- **Kill:** anyone derives a true cardinal-arithmetic statement *from* the one-point-`∞` fact (so they are not independent); or *das Absolute* is shown to be a determinate set/largest-cardinal for Cantor (defeating the apophatic-witness reading).
- **Builds on:** Paper II §5–§6; corrects the Trinity-canon README (E1).

### A compactification note — *one `∞` or two `±∞`* `[A]` (short)
The cleanest new mathematics on the slate; zero RH/CH exposure.
- **Kernel `[A]`:** `ℝ∪{−∞,+∞} ≅ [−1,1]` (two-point, has boundary) and `ℝ∪{∞} ≅ S¹` (one-point) are genuinely distinct compactifications; under `s = log x`, `exp` wraps the two additive ends onto the single multiplicative pole, and **`τ = sign(s)` is exactly the datum the one-point quotient forgets** — the same `τ` that splits the two hemispheres sharing each `(B, E)` (Paper I §6). Discharges Paper I §2's noted-but-undeveloped "two-vs-one infinities" remark.
- **Must-not-claim:** that either compactification is "more fundamental"; **any** cardinality content (that is VI's job, cross-reference it); that `τ` is a *new* invariant (it is Paper I's `τ` given its topological job — re-use, not discovery).
- **Kill:** the two compactifications are shown homeomorphic under the operations in play; or `(B,E)` already fixes `τ` (making it redundant — but the two-to-one of `B = sech√E` says it does not).

### The `z/(1/z) = z²` worked example `[A]` + `[S/I]` — *as a section, not a standalone*
Both critics: ship this **inside a Paper I/II revision**, not as "Paper VII: The Dynamics…" — it is one `[A]` computation, and a normative-sounding standalone title invites the exact moralization the η-discipline forbids.
- **Kernel `[A]`:** `z/(1/z) = z²` is total/holomorphic on `ℂP¹`; at the poles it evaluates by the **determinate** forms `0/∞ = 0`, `∞/0 = ∞` (NOT the indeterminate `0/0`); `Fix(z²) = {0, 1, ∞}`; `Julia(z²) =` the unit circle `=` Paper I's equator; poles super-attracting, equator repelling. The **load-bearing deliverable is the contrast**: `z·(1/z) = 1` is indeterminate at the poles (the EMBLEM, frame-register) while `z/(1/z) = z²` is determinate-and-total — sharpening Paper II §4's fence.
- **Two load-bearing guards (both critics):** (a) **emblem-quarantine** — `z²` is the *determinate sibling*; it does **not** vindicate or "make rigorous" `1 = 0×∞`; (b) **no valence on attractors** — the equator (balance) is the *repelling* Julia set; the poles are super-attracting; this carries **no** moral charge (η lives on moves, never on coordinates or basins). "De-balancing" is an `[S/I]` reading laid over `[A]` dynamics; the Paper III runaway link is an *echo*, not an entailment.
- **Kill:** a fixed point off `{0,1,∞}`; Julia set `≠ |z|=1`; or a pole genuinely landing on `0/0`. (None can hold — stated so it could.)

### *(Optional, fenced)* The categorical "frames-not-operands" restatement `[S]`
Gives literal content to Paper II's slogan: the additive/multiplicative units are nullary operations / unit-transformation components (arrows `1→R` from the terminal object), not elements at operand type-level; `∞` is an adjoined colimit point, not a carrier element; so `N÷0` is a typing error visible in the diagrams.
- **Critic's warning:** this is the slate's **highest-temptation surface** for a disguised "unify mathematics" upgrade (a *functorial unification* of Liar/Gödel/Tarski/`0/0`). Keep ONLY with the **no-unification fence load-bearing** and the whole thing at `[S]`/restatement. If it cannot resist becoming a categorical unification, it must die.
