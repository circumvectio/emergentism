## 1. Emergence as the structure of being `[I]`

### 1.1 The bidirectionality of emergence

Emergence is standardly discussed in two flavors:

- **Strong emergence:** Lower-level interactions generate fundamentally new, irreducible properties at higher levels (bottom-up).
- **Weak emergence:** Higher-level systemic structures constrain, organize, and explain the behavior of lower-level components (top-down).

The framework's claim is that these are **not competing accounts** but the two directions of traversal of a single M√∂bius/phase-dynamic loop `[I]`. The bottom-up direction is the upward arc `E ‚Üí M ‚Üí L ‚Üí I ‚Üí Q` (Suda's ontology; see `SUDA_ONTOLOGICAL_RESONANCE_MAP.md`); the top-down direction is the downward arc `Q ‚Üí I ‚Üí L ‚Üí M ‚Üí E`. What appears as "strong" from the lower level appears as "weak" from the higher level, because the loop is single-sided: there is no absolute inside or outside, only local orientations `[I]`.

This is not a claim that emergence is "explained away" by the loop. It is a claim that emergence **is** the loop ‚ÄĒ the structure by which existence unfolds from itself, neither purely bottom-up nor purely top-down, but both simultaneously `[I]`.

### 1.2 Dasein as emergent being

Heidegger's *Dasein* ‚ÄĒ being-there, being-in-the-world ‚ÄĒ is standardly read as existential phenomenology. The framework reads it as **emergent ontology**: Dasein is not a substance that happens to be in a world; it is the **loop itself** localized ‚ÄĒ the phase-dynamic structure `E‚ÜíM‚ÜíL‚ÜíI‚ÜíQ‚ÜíE` instantiated at the scale of a finite cognizer `[I]`.

On this reading, the "there" of Dasein is not a spatial location but a **phase-position**: the place in the loop where the upward and downward traversals intersect, where the world enters cognition and cognition re-enters the world. The equator `‚äô` (`x = 1`, `s = 0`) is the structural image of this intersection: the locus where the small-large exchange leaves the loop fixed, where being and knowing are in maximal equilibrium `[I]`.
