# Paper A · Frame algebra

## Abstract

Emergentism distinguishes ordinary operands from selected boundary-frame roles.
This type distinction prevents projective notation, arithmetic, agency, and
causation from being silently fused. It is a modeling contract, not a theorem
that three symbols generate reality.

## 1 · Types

```text
Number          ordinary finite arithmetic value
ProjectivePoint point of an explicitly chosen compactification
TitanFrame      opaque term {0_T,1_T,∞_T}; no arithmetic/coercion
Agent           embodied causal system
Action          actual attempted transition
```

Ordinary `0` and `1` are numbers and may be operands. Projective `∞` is not an
ordinary real or complex operand. `TitanFrame={0_T,1_T,∞_T}` is instead an
opaque selected type rendered with familiar labels. `add_T`, `sub_T`, `mul_T`,
and `div_T` are undefined. The numeric/projective neighbor obeys its own
mathematics; inversion `x↦1/x` fixes both `+1` and `−1`.

## 2 · Reciprocal chart `[A]`

For `θ∈(0,π)`, `φ=cot(θ/2)` and `ν=tan(θ/2)` give `φν=1` and
`φ+ν≥2`. These are analytic chart identities. They do not establish empirical
conservation, ontology, ethics, or a finite-node product law.

## 3 · Emblem boundary `[I]`

The triadic composite glyph is an emblem for mediated relation among three
frames. It does not define Titan multiplication or division. In field
arithmetic the similarly printed numeric expression with infinity is not well
formed because infinity is not a field element; the corresponding limit form
may be indeterminate. The emblem cannot be used in any derivation.

## 4 · Operators `[S/I]`

The seven named positions are a chosen game vocabulary: four signed transfer
directions plus three frame roles. The movers act; the frames bound or orient a
description. This partition is exhaustive only for the declared signed plane
and its chosen frames, not for all transformations, games, or reality.
