## 1. Three distinct work surfaces

### 1.1 Analytic model surface

The reciprocal chart supplies elementary identities under declared
definitions. It does not supply the topology of reality, the D ladder, node
power, ethics, teleology, or institutional design.

### 1.2 Translation surface

Evidence tiers, typed registers, Rosetta comparisons, source attribution, and
kill criteria make cross-domain proposals inspectable. Translation never
transfers proof or upgrades evidence.

### 1.3 Application surface

Anti-capture practices, Justice conditions, accountable authorization, receipt
separation, and Grace Exit are declared normative or design commitments. They
must be tested in their application domains. They are not consequences of the
reciprocal chart.

## 2. Typed inventory

### Analytically settled `[A]`

- the reciprocal-chart identities and inequalities stated by the current
  formula owner;
- standard mathematics and native scientific results cited under their actual
  assumptions; and
- type checks showing that a model, interpretation, observation, and norm are
  not the same kind of claim.

### Selected inside the framework `[S/I]`

- D0–D6 as a structural/interpretive scaffold;
- the evidence and translation disciplines;
- an operator-free Titan metaframe as symbolic vocabulary; and
- Justice and anti-capture commitments as chosen constraints.

Selection makes these usable within Emergentism. It does not make them
necessary features of nature.

### Empirically open `[C]`

- each ontic reading of a `μ` interface;
- universal real-world fit of `P_node=ΦV` against rival AND-class models;
- the five force–freedom correspondence;
- model-mediated foresight effects beyond ordinary decision-theoretic
  baselines;
- Egregoreotype candidates under intervention and carrier turnover; and
- comparative effects of Justice/anti-capture practices in real institutions.

The ontology of consciousness also remains open. Actual experiences, neural
correlates, and reports are D4 events; represented counterfactual content may
be D5. No D-register assignment proves a fundamental consciousness theory.

### Technically deferred

- credible native-domain bridge laws and recovery maps;
- preregistered comparisons with strong rival models;
- held-out predictions and independent replication; and
- long-horizon tests of institutional persistence, correction, and exit.

### Dead as derivations

- chart identity as empirical conservation law;
- product uniqueness;
- missing reduction as proof of irreducibility;
- literal D6/D0 identity or self-similar closure;
- quantum interpretations as extra dimensions;
- physical retrocausality inferred from modeled futures;
- Titan arithmetic; and
- renormalization, mediation, ethics, or ontology derived from a glyph.

## 3. Renormalization correspondence withdrawn

The live Titan display is operator-free:

```text
•     ⊙     ○
0_T   1_T   ∞_T
```

There is no Titan multiplication and therefore no Titan renormalization
theorem. The Casimir effect and quantum-field-theoretic renormalization are
native physical and mathematical procedures. Their use of boundaries,
regularization, or finite differences does not establish a general
Emergentist composition law.

D0 is a singleton object-level boundary role described by the metaframe; D6 is
a non-positive exit marker. The non-`μ` edge `r₆:D6↝D0` is interpretive and
does not identify the endpoints, prove recurrence, or close reality.

One research heuristic survives at `[I]`: a process can be expensive to
predict prospectively and easier to compress retrospectively. That contrast
does not establish strong emergence. Every proposed macro-cause still owes a
lower-level baseline, intervention, cost ledger, recovery rule, reduction
status, prediction, and kill criterion.

## 4. Mediation as a research subject

Mediation is not forced by a Titan expression. It is a recurring empirical and
normative subject in systems where representations, institutions, or
intermediaries affect access to a source or another bearer.

A specific mediation claim must identify the mediator, affected bearers,
custody, consent, costs, benefits, authorization, alternatives, and contest or
exit path. `etaObserved` is descriptive; a Justice judgment requires the
separate normative owner.

## 5. Program-level kill criteria

Withdraw or narrow a proposed bridge when:

1. it adds no held-out predictive or explanatory value over a native-domain
   baseline;
2. a rival model achieves the same result with fewer auxiliary assumptions;
3. the mapping cannot recover the source-domain distinctions it claims to
   preserve;
4. an intervention fails to change the predicted outcome; or
5. the proposal survives only by changing its tier, target, or mapping after
   the result is known.
