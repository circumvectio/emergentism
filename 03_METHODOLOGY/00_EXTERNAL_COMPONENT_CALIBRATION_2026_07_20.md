# External-neighbor crosswalk

## Verdict

The Compass now has an **attributed external-neighbor crosswalk**. The
integrated Emergentist synthesis remains **externally uncalibrated and
unvalidated**. Literature contact found formal neighbors, empirical analogues,
unresolved composites, tensions, and one refuted historical identification.

This is comparison, not calibration: an outside body of work can support,
bound, oppose, or fail to address a local claim, but the local claim has not
thereby been measured against an independently specified target. A resemblance
never transfers proof through the Rosetta, and no collection of precedents
substitutes for a novel, preregistered discriminator.

## Method

Each cluster was compared with a primary paper, formal source, or authoritative
institutional statement. For every match the audit asked five questions:

1. What external result actually exists?
2. Which typed Emergentist claim does it touch?
3. What stronger reading does it not license?
4. Does it corroborate, converge, leave unresolved, create tension, or refute?
5. What test could move the surviving claim?

`[A]` marks analytic or established formal results inside named systems. `[B]`
marks adequately custodied sources, observations, measurements, experiments,
tests, and built artifacts; rows qualify whether contact is external,
independent, replicated, or local. `[I]` marks the translation. `[C]` marks the
untested Emergentist extension. These are evidence types, not a prestige ladder.

## Assembly protocol — standing on giants without stealing their warrant

The dimensional scaffold is an **address system**, not a proof engine. A result
from another thinker or science may be placed into it only through this
five-step interface:

1. state the outside result in its native definitions and domain;
2. separate theorem, observation, interpretation, and historical attribution;
3. name the exact D-register role and type-preserving correspondence;
4. record what proof and evidence do **not** transfer through that placement;
5. require a discriminator before the assembled whole gains a stronger tier.

If removal of the outside result leaves the local claim unchanged, it was
decoration. If the local translation changes the outside theorem's type, it was
appropriation. If several inherited results fit one scaffold, that is useful
compression `[I]`, not independent confirmation of the scaffold.
