## Why This Document Exists

The philosophical remainder now has a clean `closed / open / deferred` split.

The science stack needs the same treatment.

This document does **not** say which sciences are "finished."
It says something narrower:

- which scientific translation surfaces are already canonically stabilized
- which are still active bridge problems
- which remain deferred because they require stronger instruments, longer timelines, or a deeper formal breakthrough

---

## The Rule

In this document:

- **Closed** means the framework has a stable public register or stable canonical rule for that lane
- **Open** means the lane is live and materially affects the framework's current scientific standing
- **Deferred** means the lane is real but not yet in a position for near-term closure

So:

- closed does **not** mean complete
- open does **not** mean confused
- deferred does **not** mean ignored

## By Lane

### 1. Formal and Foundational Sciences

**Closed** as a public surface.

The framework knows where these disciplines live:

- D0-D1
- pure mathematics
- topology
- algebra
- invariance

This is the most stable part of the scientific stack.

### 2. Biology and Life Science

**Closed** as a translation register.
**Open** as a unification claim.

What is closed:

- the translation discipline
- the vocabulary order
- the rule against smuggling frontier theory through biology

What is open:

- whether the framework's deeper unification claim over these biological vocabularies is actually right
- whether cross-scale invariance appears in data

### 3. Neuroscience and Cognitive Science

**Established disciplines govern the actual D4 application domain.**
**Open** where the framework makes stronger cross-register claims.

What is stable:

- actual organisms, cognition, control processes, observations, and reports are
  D4 carriers or events studied by biology, neuroscience, cognitive science,
  phenomenology, and related disciplines; no one discipline defines D4

What remains live:

- framework-specific tests of the candidate `μ₄:D4→D5` emergence of
  counterfactual-representation capacity
- measurement of integration/selection structures

### 4. Strategy, Economics, and Institutional Design

**Established as public methods for strategic phenomena.**
**Open** as a claim about the D5 content type.

This is currently the strongest public science surface for the framework because:

- the register is clean
- the disciplines are established
- the variables can be translated without mystification

What remains open:

- whether typing represented counterfactuals as D5 predicts more than a looser
  coalition of strategic sciences
- whether AI alignment and human cooperation data actually support the deeper geometry

### 5. D6 and the Axiomatic Boundary

**Closed** as a boundary rule.
**Open** only in whether D4 practices and institutions honor the boundary
discipline.

The important clarification is now the opposite of what this file previously implied.

D6 is not a missing public register still waiting to be completed.
D6 marks where this scaffold stops asserting positive content.

So what is now closed is:

- the rule that `D5` is the highest positive content type in this scaffold
- the rule that `D6` is an exit/nonclosure marker rather than a discipline
- the anti-idolatry correction to the whole science stack

What remains open is:

- whether actual practices produce their declared D4 outcomes
- whether a living institution can preserve the D6 boundary rule without
  turning it into positive doctrine
- whether the anti-capture posture survives success

### 6. Physics

**Deferred** at the highest level.

This is the most important distinction in the science stack.

The framework has:

- real mathematics
- some real physics adjacencies
- an active research program

It does **not** yet have:

- a completed physical bridge
- a derived Standard-Model-grade structure
- a closed dimensional program

So physics remains the great deferred lane rather than the current proof surface for the framework.
