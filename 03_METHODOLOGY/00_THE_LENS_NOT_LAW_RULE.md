# The Lens-Not-Law Rule
### Standing discipline against over-claim regeneration

**Tier `[S]`** (a discipline on claims, not a new doctrine). Opened 2026-06-10. Extends [`02_EPISTEMOLOGY/01_EVIDENCE_TIERS/00_THE_HONEST_POSITION.md`](../02_EPISTEMOLOGY/01_EVIDENCE_TIERS/00_THE_HONEST_POSITION.md) and [`00_CANONICAL_CLAIM_MATRIX.md`](00_CANONICAL_CLAIM_MATRIX.md).

## Why this exists
The framework's over-claims ("fifth force," "unifies the sciences," "verified 92%," "creatively destroys the Big Four") are **not lapses — they are endogenous.** A lens that fits every domain becomes unfalsifiable in proportion to its scope: a failure in one domain is always absorbable as "that register has a different geometry." Scope buys immunity; immunity *feels like* confirmation; and confirmation wearing a beautiful seven-fold symmetry is, from the inside, indistinguishable from profundity. So the architecture **secretes grandiosity** — which is why it needed three separate immune systems (A7, Paper P, the Serpent). A one-time scrub does not hold; the next extension of the lens regenerates the over-claim. This rule is the antibody **made standing.**

## The rule
> **Every cathedral-scope claim wears its evidence tier inline, on the surface a reader actually sees — and carries the "lens, not law" frame unless it is `[A]` or `[B]`.**

A *cathedral-scope* claim is any claim that reaches across domains (physics ↔ biology ↔ ethics ↔ theology) or asserts unification, fundamental status, proof, or conquest. The tier must be **visible at the point of the claim**, not buried in a footnote or a frontmatter field — because the failure mode is a reader absorbing a `[C]` as an `[A]`.

## The trigger watchlist (these words demand a visible tier or a fence)
| Trigger | The discipline |
|---|---|
| **physics-reserved**: *force, field, fundamental law* (esp. F5 / ektropy) | say *organizing principle / selection pressure*; mark `[I]`. "Force" is reserved. |
| **unification**: *unifies the sciences, theory of everything, grand unified, proves* | say *lens / reading*; mark `[I]`. It unifies vocabulary, not mechanism. |
| **confirmation**: *verified, confirmed, demonstrated, established* on a model-internal or unrun result | forbidden unless an external receipt exists. Model-internal ⇒ `[S]`; unrun ⇒ `[C]`. |
| **named magnitudes**: *R\* ≈ 1.5, "85–92%", a threshold/percentage* without a live, reproducible receipt | `[C]`, and say "a direction, not a measurement." |
| **conquest**: *destroys / replaces [incumbent]* | a venture's `[C]` thesis; fence to the ventures lane, not the framework surface. |

## The test (apply before any cathedral claim ships to a live surface)
1. Is the tier **visible at the claim**, not just in metadata?
2. Does the framing claim only what the tier supports (no `[C]` dressed as `[A]`)?
3. On a *public* surface, is it labelled lens-not-law?

If any answer is no → fence it (`[I]/[C]`) or relocate it. The README front door, the book, and the public site are the surfaces this matters most.

## The recursion guard (learned 2026-06-10)
**Audits of over-claims are themselves prone to the over-claim error — in both directions.** A grep for "fifth force" first returned **0** (a false "all clean," a tooling bug), then **31** (a false "it's everywhere"), and the truth was **~0 live** after classifying *asserted vs. disavowed* — most hits were the corpus naming a dead claim to mark it dead. **Never act on a raw count.** Classify asserted-vs-fenced before you "fix" anything, or you will corrupt the honest disavowals while congratulating yourself for tidying.

## The one escape hatch (lens → law)
A cathedral claim graduates from lens to law by **exactly one** path: a **cross-domain novel prediction that pays out** — something the equation makes you look for in a domain you'd never have predicted it from, and that is then *there*. R4 (does coherence×viability beat additive models at predicting real outcomes?) is the corpus's only attempt. Until an experiment like it lands, every cathedral claim stays a lens, and this rule keeps it honestly labelled as one. **Refinement makes the corpus honest; only the experiment makes it true.**
